# 12-Month NEET PG Plan

This is a realistic rank-oriented plan built around:

- 1 major first read
- 2 serious revisions
- regular PYQs and QBank
- GT analysis and weak-area correction
- an aggressive last 3 months

If your exam timeline is shorter, compress proportionally but keep the same sequence.

---

## Global Rules

- Study 2 subjects in parallel only when one is major and one is minor.
- Solve MCQs immediately after finishing a topic.
- Revise within 24 hours, 7 days, and 30 days.
- Write fewer notes, revise more often.
- Every Sunday: test + error analysis + next week planning.
- Every month: weak-subject audit.

## Daily Study Model

- 1 block: core learning
- 1 block: MCQs
- 1 block: revision
- 1 short block: PYQs or error notebook

Target baseline:

- 8 to 10 effective study hours if full-time
- 5 to 7 effective study hours if internship is active

## Month 1

Focus:

- Build system and start strong with pre-clinical subjects

Subjects:

- Anatomy
- Physiology
- Biochemistry

Goals:

- Finish first read of these subjects
- Start concise notes
- Solve topic-wise MCQs
- Build your first weak-topic list

Deliverables:

- Notes ready in subject folders
- MCQ logs started
- One weekly mini test

## Month 2

Focus:

- Complete para-clinical base

Subjects:

- Pathology
- Pharmacology
- Microbiology

Goals:

- Finish first read
- Start image-heavy and table-heavy revision
- Solve MCQs aggressively

Deliverables:

- Antidote list
- Culture/stain/test list
- Neoplasia, inflammation, hematology revision notes

## Month 3

Focus:

- Finish remaining shorter subjects

Subjects:

- Forensic Medicine
- PSM
- ENT
- Ophthalmology

Goals:

- Finish first read
- Solve PYQs for these subjects
- Start one-liner notebook

Deliverables:

- Vaccine and health program sheet
- ENT and ophthal image notes
- One subject test each week

## Month 4

Focus:

- Start major clinical subjects

Subjects:

- Medicine
- Respiratory Medicine

Goals:

- Cover core systems thoroughly
- Learn ECG, ABG, acid-base, electrolytes

Deliverables:

- Emergency medicine cheatsheets

## Month 5

Focus:

- Surgical core

Subjects:

- Surgery
- Orthopedics
- Radiology
- Anesthesia

Goals:

- Build strong conceptual understanding
- Track instruments, trauma, staging, imaging signs

Deliverables:

- Instruments and staging notebook
- Fracture and trauma revision sheet
- 1 GT + full analysis

## Month 6

Focus:

- Women and child health block

Subjects:

- Obstetrics and Gynecology
- Pediatrics

Goals:

- Strengthen obstetric emergencies and neonatology
- Solve integrated clinical questions

Deliverables:

- Labor and CTG sheet
- Vaccination and milestones sheet
- 1 GT + one weak-area review

## Month 7

Focus:

- Short subjects completion and first-read closure

Subjects:

- Dermatology
- Psychiatry
- Revision of missed topics from previous months

Goals:

- Finish all untouched areas
- Close backlog
- Complete all first-read pending items

Deliverables:

- Full syllabus coverage status audit
- Updated weak-subject ranking
- 2 GTs this month

## Month 8

Focus:

- First full revision begins

Method:

- Fast subject-wise revision
- Heavy PYQ integration
- High-yield only notes expansion

Priority order:

- Pathology
- Pharmacology
- Microbiology
- PSM
- Medicine
- Surgery
- OBG

Goals:

- Reduce notes to revision-ready format
- Strengthen repeated PYQ topics

Deliverables:

- One revision sheet per subject
- 2 to 3 GTs with deep analysis

## Month 9

Focus:

- Continue first revision and mixed practice

Method:

- Combine major and minor subjects in weekly cycles
- Practice image-based and integrated questions

Goals:

- Finish revision 1 of all subjects
- Improve attempt quality and reduce silly errors

Deliverables:

- Error notebook becomes active daily tool
- 3 GTs

## Month 10

Focus:

- Second revision plus test-heavy phase

Method:

- Rapid revision
- Strong emphasis on volatile facts, images, one-liners

Goals:

- Finish revision 2
- Improve GT scores and consistency

Deliverables:

- 4 GTs
- Subject-wise error trend tracking
- Refined exam strategy

## Month 11

Focus:

- Rank improvement phase

Method:

- Daily revision plus alternate-day test review
- Repeated revision of weak zones
- No new major source additions

Goals:

- Maximize retention
- Eliminate repeat mistakes
- Stabilize performance

Deliverables:

- 4 to 6 GTs
- Last-45-days sheet ready
- Final golden-topics file ready

## Month 12

Focus:

- Final revision and exam execution

Method:

- High-yield only
- Tables, images, PYQs, error notebook, one-liners
- Rapid cycles

Goals:

- Protect confidence
- Avoid burnout
- Enter exam with a stable attempt plan

Deliverables:

- Last-15-days list
- Last-7-days list
- Exam-day attempt framework finalized

---

## Weekly Structure

Use this every week:

- 5 days: core study + MCQs
- 1 day: revision + PYQs
- 1 day: test + analysis + planning

## GT Rules By Phase

- Months 1 to 3: 1 GT every 3 to 4 weeks
- Months 4 to 7: 1 to 2 GTs per month
- Months 8 to 10: 3 to 4 GTs per month
- Months 11 to 12: 1 GT every 5 to 7 days if analysis quality stays high

## What Not To Do

- Do not keep switching sources.
- Do not make full textbook notes.
- Do not keep solving questions without reviewing mistakes.
- Do not postpone revision waiting for syllabus completion.
- Do not judge preparation by study hours alone.

## End-Of-Month Review Questions

- Which subjects improved?
- Which topics remain weak?
- Which mistakes are repeating?
- Is GT performance rising?
- Are revisions getting faster?
- Is negative marking under control?
