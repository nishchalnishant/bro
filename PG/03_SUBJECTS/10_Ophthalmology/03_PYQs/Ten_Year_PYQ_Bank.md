# Ten-Year PYQ Bank: Ophthalmology (2014-2024)

This bank focuses on high-yield clinical vignettes for Cataract, Glaucoma, and Retina, with emphasis on IBQs.

## 1. Lens & Anterior Segment

### [Clinical Vignette] - Cataract
**Question**: An 80-year-old male presents with gradual, painless loss of vision. Examination shows an opacified lens. What is the surgery of choice with the smallest incision?
- **Key Point**: **Phacoemulsification**. (2014, 2018, 2023).

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Sunflower Cataract**: Seen in Wilson's disease.
- **Oil Droplet Cataract**: Seen in Galactosemia.
- **Snowflake Cataract**: Seen in Diabetes Mellitus.
- **Vossius Ring**: Pigment on lens after trauma.

## 2. Glaucoma & Neuro-Ophthalmology

### [Clinical Vignette] - Acute Glaucoma
**Question**: A patient presents with sudden, intense eye pain, headaches, and colored halos around lights. Diagnosis?
- **Key Point**: **Acute Angle Closure Glaucoma**. (Immediate treatment: IV Mannitol, Pilocarpine, Laser PI).
- **Year Repeated**: 2015, 2019, 2024.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Argyn-Robertson Pupil**: Reacts to accommodation but not to light (Neurosyphilis - "Prostitutes pupil").
- **Adie's Pupil**: Tonic pupil; Slow reaction.
- **Marcus-Gunn Pupil**: RAPD (Relative Afferent Pupillary Defect).
- **Gold Standard Tonometer**: **Goldmann Applanation Tonometer**.

## 3. Retina & Optic Nerve

### [Clinical Vignette] - CRAO
**Question**: A 70-year-old male presents with sudden, painless, total loss of vision in one eye. Fundus shows a **"Cherry Red Spot"** at the fovea. Diagnosis?
- **Key Point**: **Central Retinal Artery Occlusion (CRAO)**.
- **Year Repeated**: 2016, 2021, 2024.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Blood-and-thunder Fundus**: Central Retinal Vein Occlusion (CRVO).
- **Hard Exudates**: Seen in Diabetic Retinopathy.
- **Roth's Spots**: Seen in Infective Endocarditis.
- **Retinoblastoma**: Most common primary intraocular tumor of childhood (Flexner-Wintersteiner rosettes).

## 4. Ocular Refraction & Muscles

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2024 High Yield
- **Myopia**: Nearsightedness; Corrected by **Concave lens**.
- **Hypermetropia**: Farsightedness; Corrected by **Convex lens**.
- **Presbyopia**: Age-related loss of accommodation.
- **3rd Nerve Palsy Triad**: Ptosis, Pupil Dilation, Outward & Downward gaze.
