# Ten-Year PYQ Bank: ENT (Otolaryngology) (2014-2024)

This bank focuses on high-yield clinical vignettes for the Ear, Nose, and Throat, with emphasis on IBQs.

## 1. Otology (Ear)

### [Clinical Vignette] - Otitis Media
**Question**: A 5-year-old child presents with high fever, ear pain, and a bulging, red tympanic membrane. Diagnosis?
- **Key Point**: **Acute Suppurative Otitis Media (ASOM)**. Most common organism: *Streptococcus pneumoniae*.
- **Year Repeated**: 2014, 2018, 2023.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Otosclerosis Test**: Negative Rinne; Gelle's Test (Negative).
- **Meniere's Disease Triad**: Vertigo, Tinnitus, SNHL (Fluctuating).
- **Acoustic Neuroma**: Cerebellopontine angle tumor; Affects CN VIII.
- **CSOM (Cholesteatoma)**: Attico-antral disease; Requires Mastoidectomy.

## 2. Rhinology (Nose)

### [Clinical Vignette] - Epistaxis
**Question**: An 18-year-old male presents with recurrent, massive epistaxis and a nasal mass. Diagnosis?
- **Key Point**: **Juvenile Nasopharyngeal Angiofibroma (JNA)**. (Note: Biopsy is contraindicated; CT showing "Holman-Miller sign" is diagnostic).
- **Year Repeated**: 2015, 2019, 2024.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Little's Area**: Site of 90% of epistaxis (Kissellbach's Plexus).
- **Antrochoal Polyp**: Unilateral; Arises from Maxillary sinus.
- **Ethmoidal Polyp**: Bilateral; Multipeeled-grape appearance.

## 3. Laryngology (Throat & Larynx)

### [Clinical Vignette] - Vocal Cord Palsy
**Question**: A 35-year-old female undergoes Thyroid surgery. Post-operatively, she presents with hoarseness and inability to produce high-pitched sounds. Which nerve is injured?
- **Key Point**: **External Branch of Superior Laryngeal Nerve** (supplies Cricothyroid).
- **Year Repeated**: 2016, 2021, 2024.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Quinsy**: Peritonsillar abscess; Uvula pushed to the opposite side.
- **Killian's Dehiscence**: Site of Zenker's Diverticulum (Weak spot in Pharynx).
- **Laryngomalacia**: Most common cause of congenital stridor; Omeg-shaped epiglottis.
- **Tracheostomy level**: 2nd/3rd tracheal rings.
