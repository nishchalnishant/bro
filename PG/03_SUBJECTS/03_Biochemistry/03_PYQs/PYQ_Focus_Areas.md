# PYQ Focus Areas for Biochemistry

Based on an audit of recent NEET PG and INI-CET papers (2021-2024), prioritize these core high-yield clusters:

## 1. Molecular Diagnostics Surge
- **PCR interpretation**: Identifying the size of a band on a gel or the role of specific primers.
- **Blotting**: Case-based questions where SNOW DROP is needed to identify the technique.
- **DNA Repair Disorders**: Strong focus on the mechanism of repair missed in Xeroderma Pigmentosum or Lynch Syndrome.

## 2. Inborn Errors of Metabolism (Visual/Odor cues)
- **Classic Odors**: Musty (PKU), Maple Syrup (MSUD), Cabbage (Tyrosinemia), Sweaty feet (Isovaleric acidemia).
- **Classic Signs**: Black urine (Alkaptonuria), Oil drop cataract (Galactosemia), Cherry red spot (Storage diseases - although shared with Pediatrics).

## 3. Metabolic Rate-Limiting Steps
- Direct recall of enzymes for Heme synthesis (ALA Synthase), Urea Cycle (CPS-1), and Cholesterol (HMG-CoA Reductase).

## 4. Vitamin Clinical Correlation
- **Megaloblastic Anemia**: Differentiating B12 vs Folate using Methylmalonic Acid and Neuro symptoms.
- **Hypervitaminosis**: Specifically Vitamin A (Pseudotumor cerebri) and Vitamin D (Hypercalcemia).

## 5. Genetic Pedigrees
- **Modes of Inheritance**: Interpreting horizontal (AR) vs vertical (AD) vs mother-to-son (XLR) patterns.
- **Mitochondrial inheritance**: Often tested via a pedigree where every child of an affected mother is affected.

## 6. Energy Yield Calculations
- Ensuring you can calculate the net ATP gain for a 16-carbon or 18-carbon Fatty Acid chain correctly.
- Energy gain from the Malate-Aspartate vs Glycerol-3-Phosphate shuttle.
- Rate-limiting enzymes.
- Inborn errors.
- Molecular techniques.
- Lipoproteins and bilirubin patterns.
