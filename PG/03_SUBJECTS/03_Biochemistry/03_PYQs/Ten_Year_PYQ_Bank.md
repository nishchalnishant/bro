# Ten-Year PYQ Bank: Biochemistry (2014-2024)

This bank focuses on enzyme kinetics, metabolic pathways, and molecular biology diagnostic techniques.

## 1. Metabolism (Carbs, Lipids, Proteins)

### [Clinical Vignette] - Glycogen Storage Disease
**Question**: A 2-year-old child presents with hepatomegaly, hypoglycemic seizures, and doll-like facies. Laboratory findings show hyperuricemia and lactic acidosis. Which enzyme is deficient?
- **Key Point**: **Glucose-6-Phosphatase** (Von Gierke's Disease Type I).
- **Year Repeated**: 2014, 2018, 2021, 2024.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Key Enzyme of Glycolysis**: Phosphofructokinase-1 (PFK-1).
- **Site of Urea Cycle**: Mitochondria and Cytoplasm of Liver cells.
- **Rate-limiting enzyme of HMP Shunt**: G6PD.
- **Ketone Body Synthesis Site**: Mitochondria of the Liver.

## 2. Molecular Biology & Genetics

### [Clinical Vignette] - Diagnostic Blotting
**Question**: An investigator wants to detect a specific **DNA** sequence in a patient's sample. Which blotting technique should be used?
- **Key Point**: **Southern Blotting**. [Mnemonic: SNOW DROP - South:DNA, North:RNA, West:Protein].
- **Year Repeated**: 2015, 2019, 2024.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **PCR Steps**: Denaturation -> Annealing -> Extension.
- **Translation start codon**: AUG (Methionine).
- **Lesch-Nyhan Syndrome**: Deficiency of HGPRT (X-linked recessive).
- **Okazaki fragments**: Formed on the **Lagging strand** during DNA replication.

## 3. Vitamins & Minerals

### [Clinical Vignette] - Wernicke-Korsakoff Syrup
**Question**: An alcoholic patient presents with ataxia, confusion, and ophthalmoplegia. Which vitamin deficiency is responsible?
- **Key Point**: **Vitamin B1 (Thiamine)**.
- **Year Repeated**: 2016, 2021, 2023.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Vitamin B12 absorption**: Requires **Intrinsic Factor** (from Parietal cells).
- **Scurvy**: Vitamin C deficiency (Defective Collagen Hydroboxylation).
- **Zinc Deficiency**: Acrodermatitis enteropathica.
- **Copper accumulation**: Wilson's disease (ATP7B mutation).

## 4. Clinical Biochemistry & Enzymes

### [Clinical Vignette] - Enzyme Inhibition
**Question**: A drug increases the **Km** of an enzyme without changing the **Vmax**. What type of inhibition is this?
- **Key Point**: **Competitive Inhibition**.
- **Year Repeated**: 2014, 2017, 2022.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2024 High Yield
- **Marker for Acute Pancreatitis**: Serum Amylase & Lipase.
- **G-protein signaling**: cAMP is the second messenger for Gs.
- **Ketosis Marker**: Acetone (breath) and Acetoacetate (urine).
- **Highest caloric value fuel**: Lipids (9 kcal/g).
