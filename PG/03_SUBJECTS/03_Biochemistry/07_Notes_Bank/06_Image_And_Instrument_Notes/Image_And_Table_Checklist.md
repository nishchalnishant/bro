# Image And Table Checklist: Biochemistry

Biochemistry IBQs (Image-Based Questions) often focus on diagnostic molecular techniques and clinical signs of IEMs.

## 1. Molecular Biology Images
1. **PCR Gel Electrophoresis**: Identifying bands (e.g., presence of a mutation vs normal).
2. **Southern/Western Blot Gels**: Identifying DNA/Protein patterns and confirmatory markers (e.g., HIV p24/gp120).
3. **DNA Sequencing (Sanger)**: Reading a sequencing gel from bottom to top (5' to 3').
4. **DNA Repair Diagrams**: Recognizing the steps of NER vs BER vs Mismatch repair.
5. **Karyotyping**: Recognizing trisomies (21, 18, 13) or Turner's/Klinefelter's.

## 2. Metabolic Cycle Charts
1. **Michaelis-Menten & Lineweaver-Burk Plots**: Differentiating inhibition types visually.
2. **TCA Cycle**: High-yield stops (Enzymes and energy production).
3. **Urea Cycle**: Differentiating Mitochondrial vs Cytosolic steps.
4. **HMP Shunt**: Identifying the G6PD gatekeeper step.
5. **Electron Transport Chain (ETC)**: Identifying inhibitors (Cyanide, Rotenone, CO) based on their complex number.

## 3. Clinical Signs of Inborn Errors (IEM)
1. **Oil Drop Cataract**: Classic for Galactosemia.
2. **Cherry Red Spot on Macula**: Tay-Sachs, Niemann-Pick, Gaucher.
3. **Mental Retardation + Fair Skin**: Classical PKU.
4. **Self-mutilation (Biting fingers/lips)**: Lesch-Nyhan Syndrome.
5. **Downward Lens Dislocation (Ectopia Lentis)**: Homocystinuria.

## 4. Biochemical Crystals/Urinalysis
1. **Black Urine on standing**: Alkaptonuria.
2. **Maple Syrup odor**: MSUD.
3. **Hexagonal Crystals in urine**: Cystinuria.
4. **Cystine Crystals**: "Flat, hexagonal plates".
