# 100+ High-Yield One-Liners for Biochemistry

Rapid-fire facts for the final revision push.

## General & Enzymes
1. **Competitive Inhibition**: Km increases, Vmax unchanged.
2. **Non-competitive Inhibition**: Km unchanged, Vmax decreases.
3. **Suicidal Inhibition**: Aspirin, Allopurinol.
4. **Km**: Inversely proportional to enzyme affinity.
5. **Isoenzymes**: LDH1 (Heart), LDH5 (Liver), CK-MB (Heart).
6. **Rate-Limiting (Glycolysis)**: PFK-1.
7. **Rate-Limiting (FA Synthesis)**: Acetyl-CoA Carboxylase.
8. **Rate-Limiting (Cholesterol)**: HMG-CoA Reductase.
9. **Allosteric Activator (PFK-1)**: Fructose-2,6-bisphosphate.
10. **Metabolic Alkalosis**: Seen in severe vomiting.

## Carbohydrate Metabolism
11. **Aerobic Glycolysis**: 32 ATP (Malate-Aspartate).
12. **Anaerobic Glycolysis**: 2 ATP.
13. **TCA Cycle**: 10 ATP per Acetyl-CoA.
14. **Von Gierke (Type I)**: Glucose-6-Phosphatase deficiency.
15. **Pompe (Type II)**: Lysosomal Maltase deficiency (Cardiomegaly).
16. **McArdle (Type V)**: Muscle Phosphorylase deficiency.
17. **Cori Cycle**: Muscle Lactate to Liver Glucose.
18. **G6PD Deficiency**: Heinz bodies and Bite cells.
19. **Essential Fructosuria**: Fructokinase deficiency (Benign).
20. **Galactosemia**: Oil drop cataract (GALT deficiency).

## Lipid Metabolism
21. **Palmitate oxidation (16C)**: 106 ATP.
22. **Carnitine Shuttle**: Transports FA into mitochondria.
23. **Ketone bodies**: Acetone, Acetoacetate, Beta-hydroxybutyrate.
24. **Liver**: Produces Ketone bodies but cannot use them.
25. **LDL**: The primary carrier of cholesterol to tissues.
26. **HDL**: Performs reverse cholesterol transport.
27. **Apo B100**: Marker for LDL/VLDL.
28. **Apo A1**: Marker for HDL.
29. **Chylomicrons**: Present in milky plasma after a fatty meal.
30. **HMG-CoA Synthase**: Rate limiting for Ketogenesis.

## Protein & Amino Acids
31. **Urea Cycle**: CPS-1 is rate-limiting.
32. **OTC Deficiency**: Most common urea cycle defect (X-linked).
33. **Phenylketonuria (PKU)**: Phenylalanine Hydroxylase deficiency.
34. **PKU Odor**: Musty / Mousy odor.
35. **Alkaptonuria**: Homogentisate Oxidase; Black urine.
36. **Albinism**: Tyrosinase deficiency.
37. **Maple Syrup Urine Disease**: Branched chain amino acid ketoaciduria.
38. **Homocystinuria**: Cystathionine Synthase deficiency.
39. **Hartnup Disease**: Tryptophan transport defect (Pellagra).
40. **Essential Amino Acids**: PVT TIM HALL.

## Vitamins & Minerals
41. **B1 (Thiamine)**: Transketolase cofactor.
42. **B3 (Niacin)**: Derived from Tryptophan.
43. **B12**: Requires Intrinsic Factor for absorption.
44. **B6**: Required for ALA Synthase (Heme).
45. **Vitamin C**: Required for Collagen cross-linking.
46. **Vitamin D**: Active form is 1,25-DHCC.
47. **Vitamin K**: Required for Gamma-carboxylation.
48. **Zinc**: Deficiency causes Acrodermatitis enteropathica.
49. **Copper**: Deficient in Menkes disease; Extra in Wilson disease.
50. **Selenium**: Part of Glutathione Peroxidase.

## Molecular Biology
51. **Stop Codons**: UAA, UAG, UGA.
52. **Start Codon**: AUG (Methionine).
53. **Z-DNA**: Left-handed double helix.
54. **Topoisomerase**: Relieves DNA supercoiling.
55. **SNOW DROP**: Southern/DNA, Northern/RNA, Western/Protein.
56. **PCR**: Millions of copies of DNA in hours.
57. **Xeroderma Pigmentosum**: NER defect.
58. **HNPCC**: Mismatch repair defect.
59. **Degeneracy**: Multiple codons for one amino acid.
60. **Wobble**: Third base pairing flexibility.
