# Genetics And Clinical Biochemistry

## 1. Genetics & Modes of Inheritance

### Quick Reference Table
| Mode of Inheritance | Key Feature | Examples |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Autosomal Dominant** | No skipped generations; Male = Female | Marfan, Achondroplasia, Huntington |
| **Autosomal Recessive** | Skips generations; Horizontal | **Cystic Fibrosis, Most IEMs** |
| **X-linked Recessive**| Mothers to sons; No male-to-male | Hemophilia, G6PD, Duchenne (DMD) |
| **Mitochondrial** | Mothers to **ALL** children | MELAS, LHON |

- **Trinucleotide Repeat Disorders**: Huntington (CAG), Fragile X (CGG), Myotonic Dystrophy (CTG).

## 2. Molecular Diagnostics (High Yield)

### Blotting Techniques (Recap)
- **Southern Blot**: Detects DNA (mutations, deletions).
- **Northern Blot**: Detects RNA (gene expression).
- **Western Blot**: Detects Proteins (confirmatory test for HIV).

### Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR)
- **Components**: Template DNA, Primers, Taq Polymerase, dNTPs.
- **Steps**: Denaturation (Heat) -> Annealing (Cool) -> Extension (Elongation).
- **Variants**: 
    - **RT-PCR**: Detects RNA (converts to cDNA).
    - **qPCR**: Real-time quantification of DNA.

## 3. Clinical Biochemistry

### Heme Synthesis & Porphyrias
- **Rate-Limiting Enzyme**: **ALA Synthase** (requires Vitamin B6).
- **Acute Intermittent Porphyria (AIP)**: Deficiency of Porphobilinogen Deaminase. Symptoms: Painful abdomen, Polyneuropathy, Port-wine urine.
- **Porphyria Cutanea Tarda (PCT)**: Deficiency of Uroporphyrinogen Decarboxylase. Most common; photosensitivity.

### Bilirubin Metabolism
- **Unconjugated Hyperbilirubinemia**: Gilbert's (mild), Crigler-Najjar (severe).
- **Conjugated Hyperbilirubinemia**: Dubin-Johnson (Black liver), Rotor syndrome.

### Clinical Markers
- **Renal**: **Cystatin C** (Earliest marker), Creatinine, BUN.
- **Liver**: ALT (Specific), AST (Heart/Liver), ALP (Biliary obstruction).
- **Cardiac**: Troponin I/T (Gold standard), CK-MB (Re-infarction), BNP (Heart failure).

## 4. Tumor Markers
- **AFP**: Hepatocellular carcinoma, Yolk sac tumor.
- **PSA**: Prostate cancer.
- **CA-125**: Ovarian cancer.
- **CA-19-9**: Pancreatic cancer.
- **Calcitonin**: Medullary thyroid carcinoma.
