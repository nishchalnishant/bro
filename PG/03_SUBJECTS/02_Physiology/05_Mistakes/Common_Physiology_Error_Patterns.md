# Common Physiology Error Patterns: The "Increase/Decrease" Trap Log

Physiology is full of directional traps. Use this log to avoid common confusions.

## 1. Renal & Fluid Traps
- **ADH vs Urea**: ADH increases water reabsorption in the collecting duct **AND** increases urea reabsorption (via UT1) in the **Medullary** collecting duct.
- **PTH vs Phosphate**: PTH increases Calcium reabsorption but **decreases** (inhibits) Phosphate reabsorption in the PCT.
- **Aldosterone vs Potassium**: Aldosterone increases Na+ reabsorption but **decreases** K+ (increases secretion).

## 2. Respiratory Traps
- **Compliance vs Elasticity**: High compliance means easy to stretch (e.g., Emphysema); high elasticity means easy to recoil (e.g., Fibrosis). They are **inversely** related.
- **Bohr vs Haldane Effect**: 
    - **Bohr**: CO2/H+ affecting O2 affinity (Shift Right at tissues).
    - **Haldane**: O2 affecting CO2 affinity (Shift Left at lungs).

## 3. CVS Traps
- **Afterload vs Stroke Volume**: Increased Afterload **decreases** SV.
- **Preload vs Stroke Volume**: Increased Preload **increases** SV (up to a point - Starling's Law).
- **Sympathetic Effect on Heart**: Increases HR (Inotropy) **AND** increases speed of relaxation (Lusitropy).

## 4. Endocrine & GIT Traps
- **Dopamine vs Prolactin**: Dopamine **decreases** Prolactin release. (Antipsychotics that block dopamine cause hyperprolactinemia).
- **Insulin vs Potassium**: Insulin shifts K+ **into** cells (decreases serum K+). This is why it's used in Hyperkalemia management.
- **Secretin vs Bicarb**: Secretin increases watery, bicarb-rich pancreatic juice but **decreases** gastric acid secretion.

## 5. Nervous System Traps
- **RMP vs K+**: If ECF K+ increases (Hyperkalemia), the RMP becomes **less negative** (closer to threshold), making the cell *more* excitable initially, then refractory.
- **Myopia Correction**: Myopia needs a **minus** (Concave) lens; Hyperopia needs a **plus** (Convex) lens.
- **LMN vs UMN**:
    - **UMN**: Increased tone (Spastic), Increased reflexes.
    - **LMN**: Decreased tone (Flaccid), Decreased reflexes, Atrophy, Fasciculations.

- Knowing the formula but not the meaning.
- Reading graphs backward.
- Ignoring compensation in acid-base.
- Mixing endocrine hormone action with receptor type.
