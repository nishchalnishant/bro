# Graphs, Formulas, And One-Liners

## 1. Master Formula Bank

### Renal & Fluids
- **Renal Clearance (Cx)**: `(Ux * V) / Px` (U: urine conc, V: urine flow rate, P: plasma conc).
- **GFR (Inulin Clearance)**: ~125 mL/min.
- **Renal Plasma Flow (RPF - PAH Clearance)**: ~625 mL/min.
- **Filtration Fraction (FF)**: `GFR / RPF` (~20%).
- **Anion Gap**: `Na+ - (Cl- + HCO3-)`. (Normal: 8-12 mEq/L).
- **Osmolality**: `2[Na+] + [Glucose]/18 + [BUN]/2.8`.

### Respiratory & CVS
- **Minute Ventilation**: `TV x Respiratory Rate`.
- **Alveolar Ventilation**: `(TV - Dead Space) x Respiratory Rate`.
- **Starling's Law of the Heart**: `CO = HR x SV`.
- **Mean Arterial Pressure (MAP)**: `Diastolic BP + 1/3 Pulse Pressure`.
- **Ejection Fraction (EF)**: `(EDV - ESV) / EDV`.

## 2. Graph Interpretation Keys

### Pressure-Volume (PV) Loops
- **Width**: Represents Stroke Volume.
- **Top Right Corner**: Aortic valve closure.
- **Bottom Left Corner**: Mitral valve closure.
- **Area under the curve**: Represents Cardiac Work.

### Lung Function Curves
- **Obstructive Disease (e.g., Asthma)**: Shifted to the **Left**, wider (Increased FRC/RV). Low FEV1/FVC ratio.
- **Restrictive Disease (e.g., Fibrosis)**: Shifted to the **Right**, narrow/small (Decreased TLC/VC). Normal or high FEV1/FVC ratio.

### Nerve Action Potential
- **Depolarization**: Opening of Voltage-gated **Na+** channels.
- **Repolarization**: Opening of Voltage-gated **K+** channels.
- **Hyperpolarization**: Slow closure of K+ channels.

## 3. High-Yield One-Liners & Constants
1. **Total Body Water**: ~60% of body weight (ICF 2/3, ECF 1/3).
2. **Resting Membrane Potential (RMP)**: Influenced most by **K+** permeability.
3. **Threshold Potential**: ~ -55 mV for nerve.
4. **Blood pH**: 7.35 - 7.45.
5. **Surfactant**: Reduces surface tension; prevents alveolar collapse.
6. **Bicarb Filtered**: 90% reabsorbed in the **PCT**.
7. **Glucose Threshold**: Tmax for glucose is ~375 mg/min (Filtered load > threshold leads to glucosuria).
8. **JVP waves**: 
    - **a wave**: Atrial contraction.
    - **c wave**: Bulging of tricuspid during ventricular contraction.
    - **v wave**: Venous filling.
9. **Heart Sounds**:
    - **S1**: Closure of AV valves.
    - **S2**: Closure of Semilunar valves.
10. **Refractory Period**: Absolute (no stimulus triggers AP) vs Relative (suprathreshold stimulus can).

## One-Liners
- Type II muscle fibers fatigue faster.
- Surfactant is mainly from type II pneumocytes.
- Aldosterone acts on late distal tubule and collecting duct.
- ADH acts via `V2` receptor.
