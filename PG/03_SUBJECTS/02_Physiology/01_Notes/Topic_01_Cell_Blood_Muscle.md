# Cell, Blood, And Muscle Physiology

## 1. Cell Physiology & Transport

### Membrane Transport
- **Simple Diffusion**: Gases (O2, CO2), lipids, steroid hormones.
- **Facilitated Diffusion**: Glucose via GLUT (except GLUT-4 which is insulin-dependent), Aquaporins.
- **Primary Active Transport**: **Na+-K+ ATPase** (3 Na+ out, 2 K+ in). Electrogenic, maintains RMP. 
- **Secondary Active Transport**: 
    - **SGLT-1/2**: Sodium-Glucose Co-transport (Symport).
    - **Na+-Ca2+ Exchanger**: Antiport.

### Secondary Messengers (Very High Yield)
- **cAMP Pathway (Gs)**: Glucagon, ACTH, PTH, TSH, ADH (V2).
- **IP3-DAG Pathway (Gq)**: Oxytocin, ADH (V1), GnRH, TRH. (mnemonic: "GOAT-HAG")
- **cGMP Pathway**: ANP, Nitric Oxide (Vasodilation).
- **Tyrosine Kinase**: Insulin, Growth factors (IGF-1).

## 2. Blood Physiology

### Hemoglobin-Oxygen Dissociation Curve
- **Right Shift (Decreased Affinity)**: **CADET**, face Right!
    - **C**: CO2 (Increase).
    - **A**: Acidosis (Decrease pH) - **Bohr Effect**.
    - **D**: 2,3-DPG (Increase).
    - **E**: Exercise.
    - **T**: Temperature (Increase).
- **Left Shift (Increased Affinity)**: Fetal Hemoglobin (HbF), CO poisoning, Hypothermia.

### Coagulation Cascade
- **Extrinsic Pathway**: Tissue Factor -> Factor VII. (Tested by **PT**).
- **Intrinsic Pathway**: 12 -> 11 -> 9 -> 8. (Tested by **aPTT**).
- **Common Pathway**: 10 -> 5 -> 2 (Prothrombin) -> 1 (Fibrinogen).
- **Vitamin K Dependent**: II, VII, IX, X, Protein C & S.

### RBC Indices & Anemia
- **MCV**: 80-100 fL (Microcytic < 80, Macrocytic > 100).
- **Erythropoietin**: Site of production is **Peritubular interstitial cells of Kidney**. Stimulated by Hypoxia.

## 3. Muscle & Nerve Physiology

### Molecular Basis of Contraction
- **Skeletal Muscle**: Ca2+ binds to **Troponin C** -> moves Tropomyosin -> Myosin binds Actin.
- **Smooth Muscle**: Ca2+ binds to **Calmodulin** -> activates **MLCK** (Myosin Light Chain Kinase). NO Troponin in smooth muscle.
- **Cardiac Muscle**: Calcium-induced Calcium Release (CICR) via Ryanodine receptors (RyR2).

### Nerve Fiber Types (Erlanger-Gasser)
- **Type A-alpha**: Largest, fastest. Motor to skeletal muscle, Proprioception.
- **Type A-delta**: Sharp pain, Temperature.
- **Type C**: Smallest, slowest, **Unmyelinated**. Slow dull pain, Post-ganglionic autonomics.

## 4. Clinical Pearls
- **Myasthenia Gravis**: Antibodies against Ach receptors at NMJ. Post-junctional.
- **Lambert-Eaton**: Antibodies against voltage-gated Ca2+ channels. Pre-junctional.
- **Anion Gap**: Na+ - (Cl- + HCO3-). Normal = 8-12 mEq/L.
- **Osmolality**: (2 x Na+) + (Glucose / 18) + (BUN / 2.8). Normal = 285-295 mOsm/kg.
