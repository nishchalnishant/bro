# GIT, Endocrine, CNS, And Reproductive Physiology

## 1. Gastrointestinal Physiology (GIT)

### Gut Hormones Master Table
- **Gastrin**: Produced by **G-cells** (Antrum). Increases H+ secretion and growth of gastric mucosa.
- **CCK**: Produced by **I-cells** (Duodenum/Jejunum). Contraction of Gallbladder, Pancreatic enzyme secretion.
- **Secretin**: Produced by **S-cells**. Increases pancreatic HCO3- secretion (**Nature's Antacid**).
- **GIP**: Produced by **K-cells**. Stimulates insulin release (Incretin effect).

### Digestion & Absorption
- **Iron**: Absorbed in the **Duodenum**.
- **Folate**: Absorbed in the **Jejunum**.
- **Vitamin B12**: Absorbed in the **Terminal Ileum** (requires Intrinsic Factor from Parietal cells).

## 2. Endocrine & Reproductive Physiology

### Mechanisms of Hormone Action
- **Intracellular/Nuclear (Steroids)**: Cortisol, Aldosterone, Estrogen, Testosterone, **Thyroid Hormone (T3/T4)**, Vitamin D.
- **Surface Receptors (G-proteins)**: (See Topic 01 for detail - cAMP/IP3).
- **Inositol Triphosphate (IP3)**: GnRH, TRH, GHRH, Angiotensin II.

### Endocrine Axis & Feedback
- **Growth Hormone**: Stimulated by GHRH, inhibited by Somatostatin. Acts via **IGF-1** from the liver.
- **Prolactin**: Inhibited by **Dopamine** (Prolactin-Inhibiting Factor). 
- **Menstrual Cycle**:
    - **Follicular Phase**: Estrogen dominant. High FSH.
    - **Ovulation**: Triggered by **LH Surge** (Positive feedback by Estrogen).
    - **Luteal Phase**: Progesterone dominant.

## 3. CNS & Special Senses

### Sleep Stages & EEG
- **N1 (Light sleep)**: Theta waves.
- **N2 (Deep sleep)**: **Sleep spindles** and **K-complexes** (Bruxism occurs here).
- **N3 (Slow wave)**: Delta waves (Night terrors, Sleepwalking).
- **REM (Paradoxical)**: Beta waves. Dreaming, **muscle atonia**, penile tumescence.

### Motor Circuits (Direct vs Indirect)
- **Direct Pathway**: Stimulates movement (D1 receptors).
- **Indirect Pathway**: Inhibits movement (D2 receptors).
- **Parkinson's**: Loss of Dopaminergic neurons in Substantia Nigra -> Overactive Indirect pathway.

### Refractive Errors
- **Myopia (Nearsighted)**: Eyeball too long. Focus in front of retina. Correct with **Concave** lens.
- **Hyperopia (Farsighted)**: Eyeball too short. Focus behind retina. Correct with **Convex** lens.

## 4. Clinical Pearls
- **Zollinger-Ellison Syndrome**: Gastrinoma -> multiple peptic ulcers.
- **Diabetes Insipidus**: Lack of ADH (Central) or ADH-resistance (Nephrogenic). Dilute urine.
- **Phaeochromocytoma**: Adrenal medulla tumor (Catecholamines). Classic triad: HA, Palpitations, Sweating.
