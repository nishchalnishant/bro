# CVS, Respiratory, And Renal Physiology

## 1. Cardiovascular System (CVS)

### Cardiac Cycle Master Table
- **Atrial Contraction (a wave)**: S4 heart sound (if pathologically stiff).
- **Isovolumetric Contraction**: S1 (Closure of Mitral/Tricuspid). Highest oxygen consumption.
- **Ventricular Ejection**: Aortic valve opens.
- **Isovolumetric Relaxation**: S2 (Closure of Aortic/Pulmonary). Lowest volume (ESV).
- **Rapid Ventricular Filling**: S3 (Physiological in kids, Pathological in HF).

### Pressure-Volume (PV) Loops
- **Increased Afterload**: Thinner, taller loop. Decreased Stroke Volume.
- **Increased Preload**: Wider loop. Increased Stroke Volume.
- **Increased Contractility**: Taller loop. Increased Stroke Volume and Ejection Fraction.

### EKG Interpretation
- **P-wave**: Atrial depolarization.
- **PR-Interval**: 0.12 - 0.20s. Delay in AV node.
- **QRS**: Ventricular depolarization (<0.12s).
- **T-wave**: Ventricular repolarization.

## 2. Respiratory System

### Lung Volumes & Capacities
- **Static Volumes**: TV, IRV, ERV, RV. (**RV cannot be measured by spirometry**).
- **Capacities**: 
    - **FRC**: ERV + RV. Volume in lungs after normal expiration. 
    - **TLC**: IRV+TV+ERV+RV.
- **Dead Space**: Anatomical (conducting zone) vs Physiological (Anatomical + Alveolar).

### Compliance & Diffusion
- **Surfactant**: Produced by **Type II Pneumocytes**. Reduces surface tension. Increased in smaller alveoli.
- **Hb-Affinity (Recap)**: **Bohr Effect** (Tissue: CO2/H+ -> Shift Right). **Haldane Effect** (Lung: O2 -> Displace CO2 -> Shift Left).

## 3. Renal Physiology & Acid-Base

### Glomerular Filtration (GFR) Master Table
- **Afferent Constriction**: Decreased GFR, Decreased RPF. (e.g., NSAIDs).
- **Efferent Constriction**: Increased GFR, Decreased RPF. (e.g., Angiotensin II).
- **Afferent Dilation**: Increased GFR, Increased RPF. (e.g., Prostaglandins).
- **Efferent Dilation**: Decreased GFR, Increased RPF. (e.g., ACE inhibitors).

### Acid-Base Compensation (High Yield)
| Primary Disorder | Primary Abnormality | Compensation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Metabolic Acidosis** | Low HCO3- | Low PCO2 (**Winter's Formula**) |
| **Metabolic Alkalosis** | High HCO3- | High PCO2 (Hypoventilation) |
| **Respiratory Acidosis** | High PCO2 | High HCO3- (Renal retention) |
| **Respiratory Alkalosis** | Low PCO2 | Low HCO3- (Renal excretion) |

- **Winter's Formula**: Expected PCO2 = (1.5 x HCO3-) + 8 +/- 2.

## 4. Clinical Correlations
- **Starling's Law**: Stroke volume increases in response to an increase in preload (Initial stretch).
- **Cushing Reflex**: Increased ICP -> HTN, Bradycardia, Respiratory irregularity.
- **Renin-Angiotensin System**: Renin is secreted by **Juxtaglomerular cells** in response to low BP/NaCl.
