# Physiology PYQ Focus Areas for Physiology

Based on an audit of NEET PG, INI-CET, and FMGE recall sessions (2021-2024), prioritize these core focus clusters:

## 1. Graphical Analysis (The "IBQ" Core)
- **Pressure-Volume Loops**: Effect of various valvular lesions (Aortic Stenosis, Mitral Regurgitation) on the loop shape.
- **Spirometry & Flow-Volume Loops**: Differentiating Obstructive vs Restrictive patterns.
- **JVP Waveforms**: Identifying "Cannon a waves" or "Prominent y descent" in clinical scenarios.

## 2. Numerical Calculations (Recent Surge)
- **Renal Calculations**: Clearance, GFR, and Filtration Fraction.
- **Respiratory Numericals**: Alveolar gas equation and Winter's formula (Acid-Base).
- **EKG Math**: Determining heart rate from an EKG strip.

## 3. Regulatory Mechanisms (Direct Recall)
- **Baroreceptor Reflex**: Response to hemorrhage vs carotid sinus massage.
- **Cushing's Reflex**: The classic triad associated with raised ICP.
- **RAAS System**: Sites of action of ACE inhibitors and ARBs at a physiological level.

## 4. Endocrine Axes
- **Thyroid Feedbacks**: Interpreting TSH vs T3/T4 for primary vs secondary hyper/hypothyroidism.
- **Menstrual Cycle Events**: Correlation between Basal Body Temperature, LH surge, and ultrasound findings.

## 5. Neurophysiology
- **Basal Ganglia Circuitry**: Direct vs indirect pathways in the context of Parkinson's Disease.
- **Sleep Architecture**: Identifying the stage based on EEG waveforms.
- **Visual Pathway Defects**: Mapping the lesion to the specific field defect (e.g., optic chiasm -> bitemporal hemianopia).

## Repeated Themes
- Cardiac cycle and ECG.
- Respiratory graphs and oxygen transport.
- Clearance and acid-base.
- Hormone receptor-second messenger.
- Special senses and CNS localization basics.

## PYQ Method
- Solve graph PYQs separately.
- Keep one error list for formulas and one for physiology logic.
