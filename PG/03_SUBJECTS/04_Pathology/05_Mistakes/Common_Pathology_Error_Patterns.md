# Common Pathology Error Patterns: The Histology "Look-Alike" Trap

Pathology is a visual subject. Use this log to avoid common morphological and marker-based confusions.

## 1. The "Owl-Eye" Confusion
- **Error**: Thinking every "Owl-eye" is CMV.
- **Fact**: 
    - **Reed-Sternberg Cells** (Hodgkin Lymphoma): Large cell with mirror-image nuclei, each with a prominent nucleolus (**Owl-eye appearance**).
    - **CMV Inclusion**: Single, large intranuclear inclusion with a clear halo.
    - **Aschoff Body (Anitschkow cells)**: "Caterpillar cells" are seen, but some sections look like "Owl-eyes."

## 2. CD Marker Traps
- **CD15 / CD30**: Positive in Hodgkin Lymphoma; Negative in Non-Hodgkin.
- **CD5**: Positive in T-cells **BUT ALSO** positive in Mantle Cell Lymphoma and CLL (B-cell cancers).
- **CD34**: Marker for **Blasts** (Stem cells); if negative, it's likely a mature cell lesion.

## 3. Amyloid Differentiators
- **Error**: Confusing AL with AA amyloid.
- **Fact**: 
    - **AL**: Light Chain; Primary; Plasma cell dyscrasias.
    - **AA**: Amyloid Associated; Secondary; Chronic inflammation (TB, Osteomyelitis).

## 4. Nephritic vs Nephrotic EM Findings
- **Subepithelial Humps**: PSGN (Nephritic).
- **Subendothelial Deposits**: MPGN Type I (Nephritic/Nephrotic).
- **Intramembranous Deposits**: MPGN Type II (Dense Deposit Disease).
- **Effacement of Foot Processes**: Minimal Change Disease (Nephrotic).

## 5. Genetic Translocation Confusion
- **t(8;14)**: Burkitt (c-MYC).
- **t(11;14)**: Mantle Cell (Cyclin D1).
- **t(14;18)**: Follicular (Bcl-2).
- **Trap**: These all involve Chromosome 14 (IgH locus). Always identify the *other* chromosome.

## 6. Histological Bodies Traps
- **Psammoma Bodies vs Schaumann Bodies**:
    - **Psammoma**: Laminated calcium (Papillary Ca).
    - **Schaumann**: Laminated calcium + protein (Sarcoidosis).
- **Negri Bodies vs Guarnieri Bodies**:
    - **Negri**: Rabies.
    - **Guarnieri**: Smallpox (Variola).
- Knowing pathology but not classic morphology.
- Confusing overlapping renal syndromes.
- Missing image clue.
