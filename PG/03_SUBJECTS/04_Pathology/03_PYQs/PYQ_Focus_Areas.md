# PYQ Focus Areas for Pathology

Based on an audit of NEET PG, INI-CET, and FMGE recall sessions (2021-2024), prioritize these core focus clusters:

## 1. Clinical-Pathological Vignettes
- **The "Case-Study" Shift**: Recent exams rarely ask direct definitions. Instead, they present a clinical scenario (e.g., "A child with periorbital edema and cola-colored urine...") and ask for the **Electron Microscopy** finding (Subepithelial humps).
- **MI Timeline**: Detailed histology and complications (Free wall rupture vs Dressler syndrome).

## 2. Image-Based Histology (IBQ)
- **H&E Slide Mastery**: Recognize classic patterns: "Starry sky" (Burkitt), "Keratin pearls" (Squamous Ca), "Fried egg" (Seminoma), "Coffee bean nuclei" (Brenner/Granulosa).
- **Gross Specimen Identification**: "Nutmeg liver" (Chronic venous congestion), "Bread and butter" (Fibrinous pericarditis), "Shingle-like" (Intestinal TB).

## 3. Immunohistochemistry (IHC) & CD Markers
- **Marker Banks**: Inevitable questions on IHC markers for GIST (CD117), Mesothelioma (Calretinin), and Breast Ca (ER/PR/HER2).
- **Hematology CD Markers**: CD34 for blasts, CD15/CD30 for Reed-Sternberg cells, CD19/20 for B-cells.

## 4. Molecular Pathology & Genetics
- **Translocations**: Mastery of t(8;14), t(14;18), t(9;22), and t(15;17).
- **Genetic Syndromes**: Li-Fraumeni (p53), Von Hippel-Lindau (VHL), and FAP (APC).

## 5. General Pathology Fundamentals
- **Amyloidosis**: Typing (AL vs AA) and staining properties.
- **Inflammation Mediators**: Identifying the specific role of IL-6 (Acute phase), LTB4 (Chemotaxis), and IL-10 (Anti-inflammatory).
- **Apoptosis**: Differentiating the Intrinsic vs Extrinsic pathways.

## 6. Renal Pathology Focus
- **GN Master Class**: PSGN, Minimal Change, Membranous, and RPGN are consistently tested every 2 years in rotation.
- Anemia and leukemia.
- Renal pathology.
- Histology images.
- Inflammation and necrosis.
