# Ten-Year PYQ Bank: Pathology (2014-2024)

This bank contains 30+ of the most frequently repeated "Banker" questions from NEET PG and INI-CET, categorized by system.

## 1. General Pathology: Cell Injury & Inflammation

### [Clinical Vignette] - Reversible vs Irreversible Injury
**Question**: A 65-year-old male with a history of MI presents with acute chest pain. Tissue biopsy shows cell swelling and blebbing. What is the pathognomonic feature indicating the injury has become **Irreversible**?
- **Key Point**: **Amorphous densities in mitochondrial matrix**; RUPTURE of lysosomes; Pyknosis/Karyolysis.
- **Year Repeated**: 2016, 2019, 2023.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Apoptosis Marker**: Annexin-V (binds to Phosphatidylserine).
- **Psammoma Bodies**: Seen in **P**apillary thyroid ca, **S**erous cystadenoma of ovary, **M**eningioma, **M**esothelioma.
- **Macrophage in Brain**: Microglia.
- **Macrophage in Liver**: Kupffer cells.

## 2. Hematology

### [Clinical Vignette] - Iron Deficiency Anemia
**Question**: A patient presents with microcytic hypochromic anemia. Laboratory results show low Ferritin and high TIBC. What is the most sensitive indicator of Iron Deficiency?
- **Key Point**: **Serum Ferritin level**. (2022 NEET PG).

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Starry Sky Appearance**: Burkitt's Lymphoma (t(8;14)).
- **Reed-Sternberg Cells**: Hodgkin Lymphoma (CD15+, CD30+).
- **Auer Rods**: AML (specifically M3 - Promyelocytic).
- **Bence-Jones Proteins**: Multiple Myeloma (Light chains).

## 3. Systemic Pathology

### [Clinical Vignette] - Renal (Nephrotic Syndrome)
**Question**: A 5-year-old boy presents with periorbital edema and massive proteinuria. Electron microscopy shows **Effacement of foot processes**. Diagnosis?
- **Key Point**: **Minimal Change Disease (MCD)**. Best treated with Steroids. (Repeated every 2 years).

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Aschoff Bodies**: Rheumatic Heart Disease (Anitschkow cells - "Caterpillar" appearance).
- **Barrett's Esophagus**: Metaplasia of Squamous to **Columnar with Goblet cells**.
- **Councilman Bodies**: Viral Hepatitis (Apoptotic hepatocytes).
- **Negri Bodies**: Rabies (Intracytoplasmic).

## 4. Genetics & Neoplasia

### [Vignette] - Li-Fraumeni Syndrome
**Question**: A family shows multiple members with early-onset breast cancer, sarcomas, and brain tumors. Which gene is mutated?
- **Key Point**: **TP53** (Guardian of the Genome).

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Guardian of Genome**: p53.
- **Philadelphia Chromosome**: t(9;22) in CML (BCR-ABL).
- **Tumor Marker for Liver Ca**: Alpha-fetoprotein.
- **Tumor Marker for Pancreas Ca**: CA 19-9.
