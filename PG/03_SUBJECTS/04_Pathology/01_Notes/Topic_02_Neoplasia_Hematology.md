# Neoplasia And Hematology

## 1. Neoplasia: The Biology of Cancer

### Genes of Cancer (Very High Yield)
- **Oncogenes (Gain of function)**:
    - **RAS**: Most common mutated oncogene in human tumors.
    - **MYC**: Burkitt Lymphoma (c-MYC), Neuroblastoma (n-MYC).
    - **HER2/neu**: Breast and Gastric cancer.
- **Tumor Suppressor Genes (Loss of function)**:
    - **RB**: "Governor of Cell Cycle". Retinoblastoma, Osteosarcoma.
    - **TP53**: "Guardian of the Genome". Li-Fraumeni Syndrome. Most common target of genetic alteration in cancer.
    - **APC**: Familial Adenomatous Polyposis (FAP).
    - **BRCA1/2**: Breast and Ovarian cancer.

### Hallmarks & Metastasis
- **Warburg Effect**: Cancer cells shift to aerobic glycolysis (Glucose hungry).
- **Seeding of Body Cavities**: Classic for Ovarian cancer.
- **Lymphatic Spread**: Classic for Carcinomas.
- **Hematogenous Spread**: Classic for Sarcomas (Exceptions: RCC, HCC, Follicular Thyroid Ca, Choriocarcinoma spread by blood).

## 2. Hematology: Red Cell Disorders

### Anemia Lab Differentiation
| Feature | Iron Deficiency | Anemia of Chronic Disease | Sideroblastic |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ferritin** | Low | **High** | High |
| **TIBC** | **High** | Low | Low |
| **Serum Iron** | Low | Low | High |
| **Marrow Iron** | Absent | Present | Ring Sideroblasts |

- **Megaloblastic Anemia**: Hypersegmented neutrophils. B12 (High MMA) vs Folate (Normal MMA).
- **Sickle Cell**: Resistance to *P. falciparum*. H-shaped vertebrae. Crew-cut appearance on X-ray.

## 3. Hematology: White Cell Disorders

### CD Markers Master List (Must Know)
- **All Lymphocytes**: CD45.
- **B-cells**: CD19, CD20, CD21 (EBV receptor).
- **T-cells**: CD3 (all), CD4 (helper), CD8 (cytotoxic).
- **NK-cells**: CD16, CD56.
- **Myeloid/Blasts**: **CD34** (Stem cell), CD33, CD13, **MPO** (Auer Rods).

### Leukemia & Lymphoma Patterns
- **AML**: Auer Rods (M3 - APL), t(15;17) responds to ATRA.
- **CLL**: Smudge cells. Most common leukemia in elderly.
- **CML**: **t(9;22)** BCR-ABL (Philadelphia chromosome); Low LAP score.
- **Hodgkin Lymphoma**: **Reed-Sternberg (RS) cells** (**Owl-eye**). CD15+, CD30+.
- **Burkitt Lymphoma**: **t(8;14)**. **Starry sky appearance** (Macrophages eating apoptotic cells).

## 4. Clinical Pearls
- **Tumor Lysis Syndrome**: Hyperkalemia, Hyperuricemia, Hyperphosphatemia, **Hypocalcemia**.
- **Paraneoplastic Syndrome**: 
    - **PTHrP**: Squamous cell Ca Lung (Hypercalcemia).
    - **ACTH**: Small cell Ca Lung (Cushing).
    - **Erythropoietin**: RCC (Polycythemia).
