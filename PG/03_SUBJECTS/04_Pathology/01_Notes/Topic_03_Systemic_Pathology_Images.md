# Systemic Pathology: Organ-Specific Diseases

## 1. Cardiovascular System (CVS)

### Myocardial Infarction (MI) Histology Timeline
- **0-30 mins**: No change.
- **4-12 hours**: **Wavy fibers**. Beginning of coagulation necrosis.
- **12-24 hours**: Contraction band necrosis.
- **1-3 days**: Heavy Neutrophilic infiltrate.
- **3-7 days**: Macrophage infiltrate. **Risk of free wall rupture** (Max weakening).
- **10 days - 2 weeks**: Granulation tissue.
- **7 weeks+**: Dense Fibrous Scar.

- **Rheumatic Heart Disease**: **Aschoff bodies** (Fibrinoid necrosis + Anitschkow cells - "Caterpillar cells").
- **Libman-Sacks Endocarditis**: Steroids-associated; small vegetations on **both sides** of valves.

## 3. Respiratory Pathology

### Lung Carcinoma
- **Adenocarcinoma**: Most common in non-smokers and females. Peripheral location.
- **Squamous Cell Ca**: **Smoking** linked. Central location. **Keratin pearls** and intercellular bridges.
- **Small Cell Ca**: Neuroendocrine. Highly aggressive. **Azzopardi effect** (DNA deposition on vessels).
- **Mesothelioma**: Asbestos exposure. **Psammoma bodies** and calretinin positive.

## 4. GIT & Liver
- **Barrett's Esophagus**: Intestinal metaplasia (Stratified squamous -> **Columnar with Goblet cells**).
- **Alcoholic Liver Disease**: **Mallory-Denk bodies** (Ubiquitinated keratin).
- **Wilson's Disease**: Copper accumulation. **Kayser-Fleischer rings** in cornea.

## 5. Clinical Morphology One-Liners
- **Reed-Sternberg Cells**: Hodgkin Lymphoma.
- **Negri Bodies**: Rabies (intracytoplasmic).
- **Psammoma Bodies**: Papillary thyroid ca, Menigioma, Mesothelioma, Serous papillary ovary ca. (mnemonic: "P-M-M-S")
- **Call-Exner Bodies**: Granulosa cell tumor of ovary.
- **Schiller-Duval Bodies**: Yolk sac tumor.
