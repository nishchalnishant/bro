# General Pathology: Cell Injury & Inflammation

## 1. Cell Injury & Adaptation

### Necrosis Types (High Yield)
- **Coagulative**: Most common; seen in **Ischemia/Infarction** (except brain). Architecture preserved.
- **Liquefactive**: Seen in **Brain Infarcts** and **Abscesses**. Architecture lost.
- **Caseous**: Cheese-like; classic for **Tuberculosis**. Granuloma present.
- **Fat Necrosis**: Seen in **Acute Pancreatitis** (Saponification with Calcium).
- **Fibrinoid**: Seen in **Immune-mediated vasculitis** and Antiphospholipid syndrome.

### Apoptosis: Programmed Cell Death
- **Intrinsic Pathway (Mitochondrial)**: Triggered by DNA damage. Involves **Caspase 9**. Regulated by Bcl-2 (Anti-apoptotic) and Bax/Bak (Pro-apoptotic).
- **Extrinsic Pathway (Death Receptor)**: Triggered by FAS or TNF. Involves **Caspase 8**.

### Calcification
- **Dystrophic**: Deposition in **Dead/Dying tissues**. Normal serum Calcium. (e.g., Atherosclerosis, Valve calcification).
- **Metastatic**: Deposition in **Normal tissues**. Elevated serum Calcium. (e.g., Hyperparathyroidism).

## 2. Inflammation & Repair

### Acute Inflammation
- **Events**: Vasodilation -> Increased permeability -> Emigration of Neutrophils.
- **Mediators**: Histamine (early), Prostaglandins (pain/fever), Leukotrienes (chemotaxis).
- **Chemotaxis order**: C5a, LTB4, IL-8, Bacterial products. (mnemonic: "C-L-I-B")

### Chronic Inflammation & Granulomas
- **Granuloma Components**: Epithelioid cells (Modified Macrophages), Giant cells (Langhans), Lymphocytes.
- **Cases**: 
    - **TB**: Caseating granuloma.
    - **Sarcoidosis**: Non-caseating granuloma; Asteroid/Schaumann bodies.
    - **Leprosy**: TT (epithelioid) vs LL (foamy macrophages).

## 3. Amyloidosis Master Table
- **AL (Amyloid Light Chain)**: Derived from plasma cells; Primary amyloidosis / Myeloma.
- **AA (Amyloid Associated)**: Derived from SAA protein; Secondary to chronic inflammation (TB, RA).
- **A-beta**: Alzheimer's disease.
- **Transthyretin (ATTR)**: Familial amyloid polyneuropathy / Senile systemic.

- **Staining**: Congo Red -> **Apple-green birefringence** under polarized light.

## 4. Hemodynamics
- **Thrombosis (Virchow's Triad)**: Endothelial injury, Stasis key, Hypercoagulability.
- **Infarction**: White (Anemic) in solid organs vs Red (Hemorrhagic) in loose tissues/dual blood supply (Lung, SI).
- **Shock Stages**: Non-progressive -> Progressive (Lactic acidosis) -> Irreversible.
