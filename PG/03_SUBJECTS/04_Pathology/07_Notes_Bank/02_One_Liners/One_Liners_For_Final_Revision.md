# 100+ High-Yield One-Liners for Pathology

Rapid-fire morphology and marker facts.

## General pathology
1. **Coagulative Necrosis**: Most common; seen in solid organ infarcts (except brain).
2. **Liquefactive Necrosis**: Brain infarcts and abscesses.
3. **Casous Necrosis**: TB; cheese-like appearance.
4. **Fibrinoid Necrosis**: Immune-mediated vasculitis.
5. **Apoptosis (Intrinsic)**: Involves Caspase 9 and Bcl-2 family.
6. **Apoptosis (Extrinsic)**: Involves Caspase 8 and Death Receptors (Fas).
7. **Dystrophic Calcification**: Dead/Dying tissues; Normal Serum Calcium.
8. **Metastatic Calcification**: Normal tissues; High Serum Calcium (Hyperparathyroidism).
9. **Metaplasia**: Barrett's Esophagus (Squamous to Columnar).
10. **Amyloid Staining**: Congo Red -> Apple-green birefringence.
11. **Primary Amyloidosis**: AL type (Plasma cell dyscrasias).
12. **Secondary Amyloidosis**: AA type (Chronic inflammation).
13. **Chemotaxis Mediators**: C5a, LTB4, IL-8.
14. **Acute Phase Reactant**: IL-6.
15. **Granuloma Components**: Epithelioid cells (Modified macrophages).

## Neoplasia
16. **TP53**: Most common mutated gene in human cancer.
17. **RB Gene**: 13q14; governor of cell cycle.
18. **RAS**: Most common mutated oncogene overall.
19. **c-MYC**: Burkitt Lymphoma t(8;14).
20. **n-MYC**: Neuroblastoma (poor prognosis).
21. **HER2/neu**: Breast cancer marker; poor prognosis, responds to Trastuzumab.
22. **Hallmark of Cancer**: Evading growth suppressors and enabling replicative immortality.
23. **Warburg Effect**: Aerobic glycolysis in cancer cells.
24. **Hematogenous Spread**: Carcinomas (RCC, HCC, Follicular Thyroid).
25. **Seeding**: Ovarian cancer spread pattern.
26. **Tumor Lysis Syndrome**: Hyperuricemia, Hyperkalemia, Low Calcium.
27. **PSA**: Prostate-specific antigen tumor marker.
28. **AFP**: High in HCC and Yolk sac tumor.
29. **CA-125**: Ovarian cancer marker.
30. **Vimentin**: Marker for Mesenchymal tumors (Sarcomas).

## Hematology
31. **Iron Deficiency Anemia**: Low Ferritin, High TIBC.
32. **Anemia of Chronic Disease**: High Ferritin, Low TIBC.
33. **Megaloblastic Anemia**: Hypersegmented Neutrophils.
34. **Sickle Cell Disease**: Point mutation (Glu -> Val) in beta chain.
35. **Howell-Jolly Bodies**: Nuclear remnants seen post-splenectomy.
36. **Thalassemia**: Target cells; Microcytic hypochromic.
37. **Spherocytes**: Hereditary Spherocytosis; AIHA.
38. **Auer Rods**: Pathognomonic for AML (M3).
39. **Philadelphia Chromosome**: t(9;22) CML (BCR-ABL).
40. **CLL**: Smudge cells; most common elderly leukemia.
41. **Reed-Sternberg Cells**: CD15+, CD30+; Hodgkin Lymphoma.
42. **Starry Sky Appearance**: Burkitt Lymphoma.
43. **Bence-Jones Proteins**: Multiple Myeloma (Free light chains in urine).
44. **Rouleaux Formation**: Multiple Myeloma (High ESR).
45. **G6PD Deficiency**: Heinz bodies and Bite cells.

## Systemic Pathology
46. **Aschoff Bodies**: Rheumatic Heart Disease.
47. **Anitschkow Cells**: Caterpillar nuclei in RHD.
48. **Wavy Fibers**: Earliest histology change in MI (4-12 hours).
49. **Libman-Sacks Endocarditis**: SLE; both sides of valves involved.
50. **Nutmeg Liver**: Chronic Passive Congestion of liver.
51. **Mallory-Denk Bodies**: Alcoholic liver disease.
52. **Councilman Bodies**: Viral hepatitis apoptosis.
53. **Ground-glass Hepatocytes**: HBV (HBsAg).
54. **Subepithelial Humps (EM)**: PSGN.
55. **Minimal Change Disease**: Effacement of foot processes (EM).
56. **Kimmelstiel-Wilson Nodules**: Diabetic glomerulosclerosis.
57. **Psammoma Bodies**: Papillary thyroid ca, Meningioma, Mesothelioma.
58. **Keratin Pearls**: Squamous cell carcinoma.
59. **Barrett's Esophagus**: Goblet cells present.
60. **Signet Ring Cells**: Gastric Adenocarcinoma (Diffuse).
61. **Krukenberg Tumor**: Gastric Ca metastatic to Ovaries.
62. **Call-Exner Bodies**: Granulosa cell tumor of ovary.
63. **Schiller-Duval Bodies**: Yolk sac tumor.
64. **Negri Bodies**: Rabies (Intracytoplasmic).
65. **Homer Wright Rosettes**: Neuroblastoma.
66. **Birbeck Granules**: Langerhans Cell Histiocytosis (Tennis racket).
67. **Charcot-Leyden Crystals**: Asthma.
68. **Orphan Annie Eye Nuclei**: Papillary Thyroid Ca.
69. **Medullary Thyroid Ca**: Amyloid stroma; Calcitonin secretor.
70. **Pheochromocytoma**: VMA in urine; Rule of 10s.
71. **Wilms Tumor**: Associated with WAGR syndrome.
72. **RCC**: Clear cell type is most common; VHL association.
73. **Osteosarcoma**: Sunburst pattern; Codman's triangle.
74. **Ewing Sarcoma**: Onion skinning periosteal reaction.
75. **Giant Cell Tumor**: Soap bubble appearance on X-ray.
76. **Pemphigus Vulgaris**: Intraepidermal blisters; Acantholysis (Fishnet IF).
77. **Bullous Pemphigoid**: Subepidermal blisters (Linear IF).
78. **Lichen Planus**: Wickham's striae; Saw-tooth rete ridges.
79. **Astroid Bodies**: Sarcoidosis.
80. **Schaumann Bodies**: Sarcoidosis.
81. **Crohn's Disease**: Transmural inflammation; Skip lesions; Non-caseating granulomas.
82. **Ulcerative Colitis**: Mucosal inflammation; Continuous; Lead-pipe colon.
83. **Auerbach Plexus**: Deficient in Achalasia cardia.
84. **Virchow's Node**: Left supraclavicular node (Gastric Ca).
85. **Sister Mary Joseph's Nodule**: Periumbilical node (Gastric Ca).
86. **Hirschsprung Disease**: Aganglionic segment in rectum.
87. **Meckel's Diverticulum**: Rule of 2s; Vitelline duct remnant.
88. **Zenker's Diverticulum**: Pulsion diverticulum in esophagus.
89. **Mallory-Weiss Syndrome**: Longitudinal mucosal tear (Alcoholics).
90. **Budd-Chiari Syndrome**: Hepatic vein obstruction.
91. **Alpha-1 Antitrypsin Deficiency**: PAS positive diastase resistant globules.
92. **Primary Biliary Cirrhosis**: Anti-mitochondrial antibody positive.
93. **Primary Sclerosing Cholangitis**: Beads-on-a-string appearance.
94. **Hemochromatosis**: Bronze diabetes; Iron accumulation (Prussian Blue).
95. **Antiphospholipid Syndrome**: Fibrinoid necrosis; Recurrent abortions.
96. **DIC**: Consumptive coagulopathy; Schistocytes.
97. **TTP**: Deficiency of ADAMTS13; Pentad of symptoms.
98. **von Willebrand Disease**: Most common inherited bleeding disorder.
99. **Hemophilia A**: Factor VIII deficiency.
100. **Antley-Bixler Syndrome**: Associated with POR deficiency.
