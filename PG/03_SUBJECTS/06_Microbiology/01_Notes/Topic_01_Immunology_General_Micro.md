# Immunology And General Microbiology

## 1. General Microbiology: Sterilization & Media

### Sterilization Parameters (Very High Yield)
- **Autoclave (Moist Heat)**: 121°C for 15 mins at 15 psi. Mechanism: Denaturation of proteins. **Biological Indicator**: *Geobacillus stearothermophilus*.
- **Hot Air Oven (Dry Heat)**: 160°C for 2 hours. Mechanism: Oxidative damage. **Biological Indicator**: *Bacillus atrophaeus* (formerly *B. subtilis*).
- **Ionizing Radiation (Cold Sterilization)**: Gamma rays. Used for disposable syringes, catheters.
- **Glutaraldehyde (2%)**: Used for endoscopes, bronchoscopes.

### Culture Media Types
- **Basal**: Peptone water, Nutrient agar.
- **Enriched**: Blood agar, Chocolate agar.
- **Selective**: Lowenstein-Jensen (TB), Thayer-Martin (Gonococcus), TCBS (Vibrio).
- **Differential**: MacConkey (LF vs NLF).

## 2. Immunology

### Hypersensitivity Master Table
| Type | Name | Mediator | Clinical Examples |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **I** | Immediate / Anaphylactic | **IgE** | Asthma, Hay fever, Anaphylaxis |
| **II** | Cytotoxic | IgG / IgM | Erythroblastosis fetalis, Goodpasture's |
| **III** | Immune Complex | Ag-Ab Complex | SLE, Arthus reaction, Serum sickness |
| **IV** | Delayed | **T-cells** | TB skin test (Mantoux), Contact dermatitis |

### HLA & Disease Associations
- **HLA-B27**: Ankylosing spondylitis, Reiter's syndrome.
- **HLA-DR3 / DR4**: Type 1 Diabetes Mellitus.
- **HLA-DR4**: Rheumatoid Arthritis.
- **HLA-B51**: Behcet's disease.

### Immunoglobulins (Ig)
- **IgG**: Most abundant; **Crosses placenta**.
- **IgA**: Present in secretions (Milk, Tears, Saliva); J-chain.
- **IgM**: Largest (Pentamer); **Earliest** to appear in infection.
- **IgE**: Allergic reactions and Parasitic infections.
- **IgD**: B-cell surface marker.

## 3. Lab Safety & Microscopy
- **Biosafety Level 2 (BSL-2)**: Most diagnostic labs.
- **Biosafety Level 3 (BSL-3)**: *M. tuberculosis*, HIV.
- **Biosafety Level 4 (BSL-4)**: Ebola, Marburg (Viral Hemorrhagic Fevers).
- **Dark Ground Microscopy**: Used for *Treponema pallidum*.
- **Fluorescence Microscopy**: Auramine-Rhodamine for TB.
