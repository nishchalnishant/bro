# Stains, Media, Vaccines, & One-Liners

## 1. Master Identification Key for Media & Stains

### Selected Culture Media
| Organism | Selective Media | Key Colony Feature |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **M. tuberculosis** | Lowenstein-Jensen (LJ) | Rough, Tough, Buff (R-T-B) |
| **Vibrio cholerae** | **TCBS** | **Yellow** colonies (Sucrose fermenter) |
| **C. diphtheriae** | Loeffler's / Tellurite | Black colonies on Potassium Tellurite |
| **N. gonorrhoeae** | Thayer-Martin | Enriched Chocolate agar + Antibiotics |
| **Bordetella pertussis**| **Regan-Lowe** / Bordet-Gengou | Mercury-drop appearance |
| **H. influenzae** | Chocolate Agar (X & V factors) | **Satellitism** around Staph aureus |
| **Legionella** | **BCYE** | Ground glass appearance |
| **Campylobacter** | Skirrow's Medium | Seagull-wing morphology |
| **Leptospira** | EMJH / Fletcher's | Dinger's ring |

### High-Yield Stains
- **Gram Stain**: Crystal Violet (Primary) -> Safranin (Counter).
- **Ziehl-Neelsen (Acid Fast)**: Carbol Fuchsin (Heat) -> Methylene Blue.
- **Albert's Stain**: For *C. diphtheriae* (Metachromatic granules).
- **India Ink**: For *Cryptococcus* (Capsule).
- **Giemsa**: Malaria, Chlamydia, Borrelia.

## 2. National Immunization Schedule (2023 Updates)
- **At Birth**: BCG (ID), OPV-0 (Oral), HepB-0 (IM).
- **6, 10, 14 Weeks**: Pentavalent (DPT+HepB+Hib), Rotavirus (Oral), PCV, fIPV.
- **9 Months**: MR-1, Vitamin A, JE-1 (in endemic areas), PCV-Booster.
- **16-24 Months**: MR-2, DPT-Booster-1, OPV-Booster.

## 3. Rapid-Recall One-Liners
1. **Largest Virus**: Poxvirus.
2. **Smallest Virus**: Parvovirus.
3. **Smallest Bacteria**: Mycoplasma (Lacks cell wall).
4. **Bull's eye appearance**: *Entamoeba histolytica* nucleus.
5. **Safety-pin appearance**: *Yersinia pestis* (Plague).
6. **Medusa head colony**: *Bacillus anthracis*.
7. **Swarming motility**: *Proteus*, *Vibrio parahemolyticus*.
8. **Stalactite growth**: *Yersinia pestis* in ghee broth.
9. **Satellitism**: *H. influenzae* growth with *S. aureus*.
10. **Dinger's Ring**: *Leptospira* in EMJH medium.
11. **Nagler Reaction**: For *C. perfringens* (Alpha toxin).
12. **Elek's Gel Precipitation**: For *C. diphtheriae* toxin.
13. **Weil-Felix Test**: For Rickettsial infections (OX-19, OX-K).
14. **Paul-Bunnell Test**: For Infectious Mononucleosis (EBV).
15. **Stormy Clot Fermentation**: *C. perfringens* in litmus milk.
16. **Friedlander's bacillus**: *Klebsiella pneumoniae*.
17. **Rice-water stools**: Cholera (Vibrio).
18. **Pea-soup diarrhea**: Typhoid (Salmonella).
19. **Rose-spots on skin**: Typhoid.
20. **Hand-foot-mouth disease**: Coxsackie A16.
