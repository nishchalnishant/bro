# PYQ Focus Areas for Microbiology

Based on an audit of NEET PG, INI-CET, and FMGE recall sessions (2021-2024), prioritize these core focus clusters:

## 1. Virology Surge (Post-Pandemic)
- **Hepatitis Serology**: This is the #1 repeating theme in INI-CET. Master the interpretation of Acute vs Chronic vs Window period.
- **DNA/RNA Virus Classification**: Rapidly identifying if a virus is DNA (Pox, Herpes, HepB) or RNA (HIV, Influenza) is a high-value one-liner.
- **Inclusion Bodies**: Matching Cowdry Type A, Negri bodies, and Henderson-Patterson bodies to their diseases.

## 2. General Microbiology & Sterilization
- **Sterilization Parameters**: Focus on Autoclave (121°C) vs Hot air oven (160°C). Recent questions have focused on "Biological Indicators."
- **Culture Media (IBQs)**: Identifying selective media (LJ for TB, TCBS for Cholera) from images.

## 3. Immunology & Hypersensitivity
- **Hypersensitivity Types**: Clinical cases (e.g., patient developing rash after penicillin IV) and matching them to Type I-IV.
- **HLA Associations**: HLA-B27 (Ankylosing spondylitis) is evergreen.

## 4. Parasitology Morphology (IBQs)
- **Egg Identification**: Mastering the "Bile-stained" (Ascaris, Trichuris, Taenia) vs "Non-bile stained" (Hookworm, Pinworm).
- **Protozoal diagnostic clues**: Quadrinucleated cyst (Entamoeba), Banana gametocyte (P. falciparum).

## 5. Applied Microbiology & HIC
- **Biomedical Waste (BMW) 2016**: Identifying which bag (Yellow, Red, Blue, White) is used for specific clinical waste.
- **MRSA/VRE**: Understanding the gene (mecA, vanA) and the drug of choice.

## 6. Mycology Clues
- **Aspergillus vs Mucor**: Differentiating the branching angle (Acute 45° vs Obtuse 90°) on H&E slides.
