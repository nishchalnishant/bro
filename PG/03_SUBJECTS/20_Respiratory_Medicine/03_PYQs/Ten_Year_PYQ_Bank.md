# Ten-Year PYQ Bank: Respiratory Medicine (2014-2024)

This bank focuses on TB protocols, Lung cancers, and PFT interpretation.

## 1. Tuberculosis (NTEP Core)

### [Clinical Vignette] - MDR-TB
**Question**: A patient already on intensive phase of TB treatment fails to convert his sputum to negative at the end of 2 months. What is the current drug of choice for **MDR-TB** (Rifampicin resistant)?
- **Key Point**: **Bedaquiline-based regimen** (Short Oral Regimen). [2022 NTEP Update].
- **Year Repeated**: 2014, 2018, 2021, 2024.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Most common site for Extrapulmonary TB**: Lymph nodes.
- **Ghon Focus**: Primary TB lesion in the lower part of the upper lobe or upper part of the lower lobe.
- **Miliary TB**: Hematogenous spread (Millet seed appearance on X-ray).

## 2. Lung Cancer & Pleural Diseases

### [Clinical Vignette] - Lung Cancer
**Question**: A 60-year-old heavy smoker presents with a peripheral lung mass. Biopsy shows large cells with prominent nucleoli. What is the most common histological type of lung cancer in **Non-Smokers**?
- **Key Point**: **Adenocarcinoma**.
- **Year Repeated**: 2016, 2021, 2024.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Pancoast Tumor**: Apical lung cancer; Causes Horner's Syndrome.
- **Small Cell Carcinoma**: Associated with paraneoplastic syndromes (ACTH, SIADH).
- **Mesothelioma**: Associated with **Asbestos** exposure.

## 3. Obstructive & Restrictive Lung Diseases

### [Clinical Vignette] - PFT Interpretation
**Question**: A patient with chronic breathlessness has a Pulmonary Function Test (PFT). **FEV1 is 40% Predicted**, **FVC is 80% Predicted**, and **FEV1/FVC is 0.5**. Diagnosis?
- **Key Point**: **Obstructive Lung Disease** (e.g., Bronchial Asthma or COPD).
- **Year Repeated**: 2015, 2019, 2023.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2024 High Yield
- **Sarcoidosis**: Non-caseating granulomas; Bilateral hilar lymphadenopathy.
- **Silicosis**: Egg-shell calcification of lymph nodes.
- **Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA)**: Gold Standard investigation is Polysomnography.
- **Light's Criteria**: Used to distinguish Exudative vs Transudative pleural effusion.
