# Image And Table Checklist: Anatomy

Master these images to score 10/10 in the Image-Based Question (IBQ) section.

## 1. Neuroanatomy (The "IBQ" King)
1. **Circle of Willis**: Identifying PICA, AICA, and Basal artery on MRA.
2. **Internal Capsule**: Identifying the Genu vs Posterior limb on axial MRI.
3. **Midbrain Section**: Identifying "Mickey Mouse" sign (Crus cerebri).
4. **Pons Section**: Identifying the facial colliculus and trigeminal nerve exit.
5. **Medulla Section**: Identifying the olives and pyramids.
6. **Ventricular System**: Identifying the Foramen of Monro and Cerebral Aqueduct.

## 2. Histology Slides (High Yield)
1. **Appendix**: Identifying the continuous ring of lymphoid follicles.
2. **Duodenum**: Identifying Brunner's glands (submucosa).
3. **Ileum**: Identifying Peyer's patches (lamina propria/submucosa).
4. **Trachea**: Identifying C-shaped hyaline cartilage.
5. **Thymus**: Identifying Hassall's corpuscles (medulla).
6. **Spleen**: Identifying white pulp around central arteriole.
7. **Bone**: Identifying Haversian systems.
8. **Stomach**: Identifying deep gastric pits (Pylorus) vs branched glands (Fundus).

## 3. Radiology (CT & X-Ray)
1. **CT Thorax (T4 level)**: Arch of aorta, Bifurcation of trachea.
2. **CT Abdomen (L1 level)**: SMA/SMV origin, Hila of kidneys.
3. **X-Ray Joint**: Identifying Scaphoid fracture (Snuffbox).
4. **X-Ray Knee**: Identifying the epiphyseal plates (Age estimation).
5. **Shoulder Dislocation**: Anterior vs Posterior (Light-bulb sign).

## 4. Gross Anatomy Diagrams
1. **Brachial Plexus**: Identifying the Posterior Cord and its branches.
2. **Inguinal Canal**: Identifying the Inferior Epigastric vessels.
3. **Perineum**: Identifying the Ischial Spine and Pudendal nerve.
4. **Heart**: Identifying the LAD vs RCA in a posterior view.
5. **Ureter Course**: Point of crossing the common iliac artery.

## 5. Embryology Models
1. **Pharyngeal Arches**: Identifying the 1st vs 2nd arch in a 5-week embryo.
2. **Gut Rotation**: Identifying the physiological herniation stages.
3. **Placenta**: Cross-section of umbilical cord (2 arteries, 1 vein).
