# Common Anatomy Error Patterns: The "Trap" Log

Avoid these high-frequency exam traps.

## 1. Nerve Supply Exceptions (The most common trap)
- **Palate Muscle Trap**: All muscles of the palate are supplied by the Pharyngeal Plexus (Vagus) **EXCEPT** Tensor Veli Palatini (**V3**).
- **Tongue Muscle Trap**: All muscles of the tongue are supplied by the Hypoglossal (XII) **EXCEPT** Palatoglossus (**Vagus**).
- **Larynx Muscle Trap**: All intrinsic muscles of the larynx are supplied by the Recurrent Laryngeal Nerve **EXCEPT** Cricothyroid (**External Laryngeal N.**).
- **Forearm Muscle Trap**: All muscles of the flexor compartment of the forearm are supplied by the Median nerve **EXCEPT** Flexor Carpi Ulnaris and the medial half of FDP (**Ulnar**).

## 2. Relations & Layout
- **Femoral Sheath Trap**: It contains the Femoral Artery, Vein, and Canal, but **NOT the Femoral Nerve**. The nerve is lateral to the sheath.
- **Porta Hepatis Trap**: Contents from posterior to anterior are **Portal vein -> Hepatic artery -> Bile duct**.
- **Cavernous Sinus Trap**: The Abducens nerve (`VI`) is **inside** the sinus (next to the ICA), while others (`III, IV, V1, V2`) are in the lateral wall.

## 3. Embryology Confusions
- **Third vs Fourth Pouch**: Third pouch gives **Inferior** Parathyroid (paradoxically!). Fourth gives Superior.
- **Cleft vs Pouch**: 1st Cleft is the External Auditory Meatus; 1st Pouch is the Middle Ear Cavity.
- **Microglia**: These are the only neuroglial cells derived from **Mesoderm** (not neuroectoderm).

## 4. Histology Misidentifiers
- **Peyer's Patches vs Brunner's Glands**: Peyer's are in the **Ileum**; Brunner's are in the **Duodenum**.
- **Serous vs Mucous**: Parotid is **Purely Serous**; Sublingual is **Mostly Mucous**; Submandibular is **Mixed**.

## 5. Clinical Mislocations
- **Erb-Duchenne vs Klumpke**: Erb is Upper Trunk (`C5-C6`) -> Waiter's tip. Klumpke is Lower Trunk (`C8-T1`) -> Claw hand.
- **Fracture Humerus**:
    - Neck: Axillary N.
    - Midshaft: Radial N.
    - Supracondylar: Median/Brachial Artery.
    - Medial Epicondyle: Ulnar N.
- Remembering muscle but not nerve supply.
- Remembering nerve but not root value.
- Knowing structure but forgetting relation.
- Knowing embryology fact but not clinical anomaly.
- Recognizing histology organ family but not exact slide.

## Fix Method
- For every missed question, add one anchor fact and one differentiator.
- Example:
  spleen has central arteriole, lymph node does not.
- Example:
  carpal tunnel does not contain flexor carpi radialis tendon inside the tunnel proper in the same way candidates often assume.
