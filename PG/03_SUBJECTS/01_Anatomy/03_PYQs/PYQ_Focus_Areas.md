# Anatomy PYQ Focus Areas for Anatomy

Based on analysis of NEET PG, INI-CET, and FMGE papers from 2021-2024, focus on these recurring clusters:

## 1. Neuroanatomy (The "New" Favorite)
- **Brainstem Sections**: Identifying the level based on nuclei (e.g., Facial colliculus in Pons).
- **Ventricles/CSF**: Questions on the path of flow and sites of obstruction.
- **Circle of Willis**: Identifying specific arteries in a MRA or diagram.

## 2. Head & Neck Clinicals
- **Thyroid Surgery**: The relationship between arteries and nerves.
- **Tongue**: Integrated questions on taste, sensation, and motor supply.
- **Salivary Glands**: Secretomotor pathways (Otic and Pterygopalatine ganglia).

## 3. Thorax & Abdomen
- **Diaphragm Openings**: The "T8, T10, T12" contents are evergreen.
- **Liver Segments**: Couinaud classification and blood supply.
- **Hernias**: Detailed anatomy of the inguinal canal and Hesselbach's triangle.

## 4. Limbs (The "Old" Standard)
- **Brachial Plexus**: Still a top-tier topic, especially Erb's and Klumpke's.
- **Hand Spaces**: Clinical scenarios involving infections of the hand.
- **Nerve Testing**: "How to test Median nerve?" vs "Ulnar nerve?" clinical scenarios.

## 5. Recent Image-Based Trends
- **Histology**: Differentiators like Brunner glands, Peyer's patches, and Hassall's corpuscles.
- **Gross Images**: Identified structures in the Pelvis (internal iliac branches).
- **Radiology**: CT sections of the Thorax/Abdomen at high-yield levels (e.g., L1).
- Nerve injury after fracture or dislocation.
- Brachial plexus root value and lesion pattern.
- Direct vs indirect inguinal hernia.
- Cranial nerve palsy and cavernous sinus.
- Branchial arch derivatives.
- Histology slide identification.
- Internal capsule blood supply.
- Femoral triangle and cubital fossa contents.
- Bronchopulmonary segments and mediastinum.
- Pelvic supports and pudendal nerve.

## PYQ Review Method
- Group PYQs by these buckets:
  upper limb, lower limb, thorax, abdomen, head-neck, neuroanatomy, embryology, histology.
- Track not only the answer, but the exam’s favorite distractor.
- Convert repeated PYQs into one-liners and place them in revision sheet.

## Most Repeated Facts
- Surgical neck of humerus -> axillary nerve.
- Neck of fibula -> common peroneal nerve.
- Corneal reflex -> `V1` afferent, `VII` efferent.
- Superior orbital fissure -> `III`, `IV`, `V1`, `VI`.
- Third pouch -> inferior parathyroid and thymus.
- Hassall corpuscles -> thymus.
