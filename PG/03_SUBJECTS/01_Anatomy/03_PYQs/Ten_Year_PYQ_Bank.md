# Ten-Year PYQ Bank: Anatomy (2014-2024)

This bank focuses on core neuroanatomy, neurovascular relations, and high-yield clinical correlations.

## 1. General Anatomy & Embryology

### [Clinical Vignette] - Branchial Arches
**Question**: A child presents with a congenital defect of the mandible and ear ossicles (Malleus, Incus). Which branchial arch is involved?
- **Key Point**: **1st Branchial Arch** (Mandibular arch). Nerve: Trigeminal (V).
- **Year Repeated**: 2015, 2019, 2024.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Derivative of 2nd arch**: Stapes, Styloid process, Lesser horn of hyoid. Nerve: Facial (VII).
- **Pharyngeal pouch 3**: Inferior parathyroid and Thymus.
- **Umbilical vein (Left)**: Becomes the **Ligamentum teres**.
- **Foramen Ovale closure**: Becomes **Fossa ovalis**.

## 2. Upper & Lower Limb

### [Clinical Vignette] - Nerve Injury
**Question**: A patient with a mid-shaft humeral fracture presents with "Wrist Drop." Which nerve is most likely damaged?
- **Key Point**: **Radial Nerve** (Radial groove of humerus).
- **Year Repeated**: 2014, 2017, 2021, 2023.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Claw Hand**: Ulnar nerve injury.
- **Ape Thumb Deformity**: Median nerve injury.
- **Foot Drop**: Common Peroneal Nerve injury.
- **Trendelenburg Sign**: Weakness of **Gluteus medius/minimus** (Superior Gluteal Nerve).

## 3. Abdomen, Thorax & Pelvis

### [Clinical Vignette] - Diaphragmatic Openings
**Question**: At which vertebral level does the **Inferior Vena Cava (IVC)** pass through the diaphragm?
- **Key Point**: **T8** (Vena Cava Opening). [T10: Esophagus, T12: Aorta].
- **Year Repeated**: 2016, 2022.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Direct vs Indirect Inguinal Hernia**: Lateral vs Medial to Inferior Epigastric Artery.
- **Triangle of Calot**: Bound by Cystic duct, Common Hepatic duct, and Liver surface. (Contains Cystic Artery).
- **Left Recurrent Laryngeal Nerve**: Hooks around the **Arch of Aorta**.

## 4. Neuroanatomy

### [Clinical Vignette] - Cavernous Sinus
**Question**: A patient with an infection of the "danger area of the face" presents with ophthalmoplegia and loss of sensation over the forehead. Which structure is involved?
- **Key Point**: **Cavernous Sinus**. Nerves involved: III, IV, VI, V1, V2.
- **Year Repeated**: 2016, 2020, 2024.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Broca's Area**: Brodmann 44, 45 (Frontal Lobe - Speech production).
- **Poliomyelitis**: Affects **Anterior Horn Cells** (Motor).
- **Circle of Willis**: Formed by Internal Carotid and Vertebrobasilar systems.
- **Blood supply to Visual Cortex**: Posterior Cerebral Artery.
