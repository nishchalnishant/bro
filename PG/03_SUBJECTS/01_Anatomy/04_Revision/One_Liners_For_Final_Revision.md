# 100+ High-Yield One-Liners for Anatomy

Rapid-fire facts for the final revision push.

## General & Embryology
1. **Clavicle**: Most common bone to break; first to ossify.
2. **Growing end**: Opposite to the direction of the nutrient foramen.
3. **Primary cartilaginous joint**: Epiphyseal plate.
4. **Secondary cartilaginous joint**: Symphysis pubis (Midline).
5. **Neural Crest**: Gives rise to Melanocytes, Adrenal medulla, and Schwann cells.
6. **Mataesthesial**: Mesoderm gives rise to Microglia.
7. **Pharyngeal Arch 1**: Supplied by Mandibular nerve (V3).
8. **Pharyngeal Arch 2**: Supplied by Facial nerve (VII).
9. **Midgut rotation**: 270 degrees anti-clockwise around Superior Mesenteric Artery.
10. **Meckel's Diverticulum**: Remnant of Vitellointestinal duct.

## Upper Limb
11. **Serratus Anterior**: Winging of scapula (Long thoracic nerve).
12. **Deltoid**: Major abductor from 15-90 degrees (Axillary nerve).
13. **Supraspinatus**: Initiates abduction (0-15 degrees).
14. **Biceps Brachii**: Main supinator of the flexed forearm.
15. **Radial Nerve**: Injury in spiral groove causes Wrist Drop.
16. **Ulnar Nerve**: Injury at elbow causes Claw Hand.
17. **Median Nerve**: Nerve of the "Hand of Benediction".
18. **Scaphoid**: Most commonly fractured carpal bone.
19. **Lunate**: Most commonly dislocated carpal bone.
20. **Rotator Cuff**: SITS (Supraspinatus, Infraspinatus, Teres minor, Subscapularis).

## Lower Limb
21. **Sartorius**: Longest muscle; tailor's muscle.
22. **Gluteus Maximus**: Extender of the hip; supplied by Inferior Gluteal N.
23. **Superior Gluteal Nerve**: Injury causes Trendelenburg sign.
24. **Sciatic Nerve**: Largest nerve in the body.
25. **Common Peroneal Nerve**: Neck of fibula fracture causes Foot Drop.
26. **Tibial Nerve**: Passes behind the medial malleolus.
27. **Ligamentum Teres**: Carries blood supply to the head of the femur in children.
28. **ACL**: Prevents anterior displacement of the tibia.
29. **Popliteus**: Unlocking muscle of the knee.
30. **Great Saphenous Vein**: Formed by the union of the dorsal venous arch and medial marginal vein.

## Thorax
31. **Angle of Louis (T4/T5)**: Landmark for arch of aorta and tracheal bifurcation.
32. **Azygos Vein**: Arches over the right lung root.
33. **SA Node**: Pace maker; located at the junction of SVC and RA.
34. **AV Node**: Located in the Triangle of Koch.
35. **LAD**: Most common artery involved in Myocardial Infarction.
36. **Right Main Bronchus**: Shorter, wider, and more vertical (Aspiration site).
37. **Thoracic Duct**: Largest lymphatic vessel; drains into left brachiocephalic vein.
38. **Left Recurrent Laryngeal N**: Arches under the Arch of Aorta.
39. **Phrenic Nerve**: Supplies the diaphragm (C3, C4, C5).
40. **T12 Opening**: Aorta, Thoracic duct, Azygos vein pass through.

## Abdomen & Pelvis
41. **Celiac Trunk (T12)**: Supplies the foregut.
42. **SMA (L1)**: Supplies the midgut.
43. **IMA (L3)**: Supplies the hindgut.
44. **Porta Hepatis**: Portal vein is the most posterior content.
45. **Caudate Lobe (Segment I)**: Independent venous drainage to IVC.
46. **Tail of Pancreas**: Contains most of the Islets of Langerhans; lies in the lienorenal ligament.
47. **Portal Vein**: Formed by the union of SMV and Splenic vein.
48. **Hesselbach's Triangle**: Site of direct inguinal hernia.
49. **Appendix**: Supplied by the appendicular artery (branch of ileocolic).
50. **Waldeyer's Fascia**: Connective tissue posterior to the rectum.

## Head & Neck
51. **Stapedius**: Smallest muscle in the body.
52. **Facial Nerve**: Passes through the stylomastoid foramen to exit the skull.
53. **Ciliary Ganglion**: Parasympathetic supply to the sphincter pupillae.
54. **Chorda Tympani**: Carries taste from the anterior 2/3 of the tongue.
55. **Glossopharyngeal (IX)**: Carries taste from the posterior 1/3 of the tongue.
56. **External Laryngeal N**: Supplies the cricothyroid muscle.
57. **Kiesselbach's Plexus**: Most common site of epistaxis (Little's area).
58. **Cavernous Sinus**: Abducens nerve (VI) is the most medial content.
59. **Lacrimal Gland**: Secretomotor supply via the Pterygopalatine ganglion.
60. **Dangerous Area of Face**: Communication of facial vein with cavernous sinus.

## Neuroanatomy
61. **Internal Capsule**: Charcot's artery of hemorrhage (Lenticulostriate branches of MCA).
62. **Broca's Area (44, 45)**: Motor speech area in the frontal lobe.
63. **Wernicke's Area (22)**: Sensory speech area in the temporal lobe.
64. **Corpus Callosum**: Largest commissural fiber tract.
65. **Thalamus**: Relay center for all sensory pathways except olfaction.
66. **Cerebral Aqueduct**: Connects 3rd and 4th ventricles.
67. **Medial Medullary Syndrome**: Contralateral hemiplegia and ipsilateral tongue palsy.
68. **Lateral Medullary Syndrome (Wallenberg's)**: Loss of pain/temperature on ipsilateral face and contralateral body.
69. **PICA**: Artery involved in Wallenberg syndrome.
70. **Upper Motor Neuron Lesion**: Results in spasticity and hyperreflexia.
