# Anatomy MCQ Strategy And Topics

## Core Rule
- Anatomy MCQs are rarely random if you master five buckets:
  nerve injury, artery/vein relations, boundaries/contents, embryology derivatives, histology images.

## Topic Priority Order
1. Brachial plexus and nerve injuries.
2. Cranial nerves and foramina.
3. Neuroanatomy and blood supply.
4. Inguinal canal and abdomen.
5. Embryology derivatives.
6. Histology and image recognition.
7. Lower-limb nerves and pelvis.
8. Thorax mediastinum and coronary supply.

## MCQ Attempt Framework
- First identify the domain:
  nerve, artery, embryology, histology, osteology, imaging.
- Then identify the asked variable:
  root value, function, relation, lesion, derivative, content, blood supply.
- Eliminate look-alike options by one anchor fact.

## High-Frequency MCQ Patterns
- Fracture followed by nerve palsy.
- Hernia relation to inferior epigastric vessels.
- Cavernous sinus content confusion.
- Foramen-content mismatch.
- Histology slide between lymph node, spleen, thymus.
- Branchial arch and pouch derivative mismatch.

## Daily Practice System
- `20` region-wise questions after each topic.
- `10` image questions every third study day.
- `25` mixed revision questions every weekend.
- Wrong questions go immediately into mistake log.

## Accuracy Targets
- First pass:
  `55 to 60%`.
- Second pass:
  `70 to 75%`.
- Final revision phase:
  `80%+`.

## Common Traps
- Mixing median with ulnar claw signs.
- Confusing direct vs indirect hernia.
- Confusing `III`, `IV`, `VI` palsies.
- Confusing inferior vs superior parathyroid derivatives.
- Confusing PCT vs DCT and spleen vs lymph node histology.
