# Anatomy High-Yield Notes

Use this file as the subject dashboard. Read this first, then go into the topic files.

## What Anatomy Gives You In NEET PG
- Direct factual MCQs: nerves, arteries, foramina, embryology derivatives, histology, surface anatomy.
- Clinical-anatomy MCQs: nerve injury after fracture, referred pain, hernia, brainstem lesion, pelvic floor damage.
- Image-based MCQs: histology slides, bones, foramina, brain sections, gross specimens, radiological anatomy.

## How To Study Anatomy For Exam Use
- Learn structure with relation, blood supply, nerve supply, action, and clinical correlation together.
- Convert every region into tables: contents, boundaries, branches, derivatives, root values.
- Prioritize repeated high-yield anatomy over low-yield descriptive detail.
- Revise diagrams actively: redraw, label, then test recall.

- Anatomical position, planes, movement terms, and side/position relationships.

## Cranial Nerves Essentials
- `I`: olfaction.
- `II`: vision.
- `III`, `IV`, `VI`: eye movement and pupillary function.
- `V`: sensory of face and muscles of mastication.
- `VII`: facial expression, taste anterior `2/3`.
- `VIII`: hearing and balance.
- `IX`, `X`: pharynx, larynx, gag reflex.
- `XI`: SCM and trapezius.
- `XII`: tongue movements.
- Know nuclei, foramina, parasympathetic components, lesion signs, and reflex associations.

## Very Common Clinical Correlations
- Surgical neck of humerus: axillary nerve.
- Midshaft humerus: radial nerve.
- Medial epicondyle: ulnar nerve.
- Supracondylar fracture: median nerve/brachial artery.
- Neck of fibula: common peroneal nerve.
- Posterior hip dislocation: sciatic nerve.
- Cavernous sinus thrombosis: ophthalmoplegia.
- Uncal herniation: CN III compression.
