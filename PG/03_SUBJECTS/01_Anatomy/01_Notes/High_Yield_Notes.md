# Anatomy High-Yield Notes

## General Anatomy
- Anatomical position, planes, movements, and common terminology.
- Epiphysis, metaphysis, periosteum, and ossification centers are repeatedly tested.

## Upper Limb
- Brachial plexus: roots, trunks, cords, branches, and classic nerve palsies.
- Axillary artery parts, cubital fossa contents, rotator cuff, and carpal tunnel.

## Lower Limb
- Femoral triangle, adductor canal, sciatic nerve, and gluteal muscles.
- Hip joint stability, knee ligaments, foot arches, and common nerve injuries.

## Thorax
- Mediastinum divisions, heart borders, coronary artery supply, and bronchopulmonary segments.
- Intercostal neurovascular bundle and surface marking of pleura and lungs.

## Abdomen And Pelvis
- Portal-systemic anastomosis, inguinal canal, retroperitoneal relations, and peritoneal spaces.
- Uterus supports, pelvic diaphragm, pudendal nerve, and ischioanal fossa.

## Head, Neck, And Neuroanatomy
- Cranial nerves, cavernous sinus contents, triangles of neck, and branchial arch derivatives.
- Internal capsule, ventricles, blood supply of brain, and spinal cord tracts.

## Embryology And Histology
- Pharyngeal arches, pouches, clefts, gut rotation, and fetal circulation.
- Histology of epithelium, cartilage, bone, lymphoid organs, and endocrine glands.

## Image Targets
- Foramina and transmitted structures.
- Arterial supply, venous drainage, and nerve root values.
