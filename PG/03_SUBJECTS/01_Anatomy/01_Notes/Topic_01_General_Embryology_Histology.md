# General Anatomy, Embryology, And Histology

## 1. General Anatomy

### Core Definitions
- **Anatomical position**: Reference for all descriptions.
- **Planes**:
    - **Median**: Equal R/L halves.
    - **Coronal**: Anterior/Posterior.
    - **Transverse/Axial**: Superior/Inferior.

### Bones & Ossification
- **Growing end rule**: Nutrient artery enters opposite to the growing end. (e.g., In the humerus, the growing end is the upper end).
- **Ossification Types**:
    - **Intramembranous**: Flat bones of skull, mandible (mostly), clavicle (medial part). Lack a cartilage model.
    - **Endochondral**: Most long bones.
- **Bone Cells**:
    - **Osteoblasts**: Build bone (derived from mesenchymal stem cells).
    - **Osteoclasts**: Resorb bone (derived from monocyte-macrophage lineage).

### Joints
- **Fibrous**: Sutures (skull), Syndesmotic (inferior tibiofibular), Gomphosis (teeth).
- **Cartilaginous**:
    - **Primary**: Synchondrosis (Epiphyseal plate, 1st Rib-Sternum). Temporary.
    - **Secondary**: Symphysis (Pubic symphysis, IV disc). Permanent, midline.
- **Synovial**:
    - **Plane**: Acromioclavicular.
    - **Hinge**: Elbow, Ankle.
    - **Pivot**: Atlanto-axial, Radio-ulnar.
    - **Saddle**: 1st CMC joint.
    - **Condylar**: Knee (Modified hinge/condylar).
    - **Ball-and-Socket**: Shoulder, Hip.

## 2. Embryology

### Early Development & Twinning
- **Fertilization**: In Ampulla of the fallopian tube.
- **Implantation**: Days 6-10 (window of implantation).
- **Twinning Timings (High Yield)**:
    - **Day 0-3**: Dichorionic, Diamniotic (DCDA).
    - **Day 4-8**: Monochorionic, Diamniotic (MCDA).
    - **Day 8-13**: Monochorionic, Monoamniotic (MCMA).
    - **>Day 13**: Conjoined twins.

### Germ Layer Derivatives (Advanced)
- **Ectoderm (Surface)**: Epidermis, Enamel, Anterior Pituitary, Lens, Inner Ear.
- **Neuroectoderm (Neural Tube)**: CNS, Retina, Pineal gland, Posterior Pituitary.
- **Neuroectoderm (Neural Crest)**: **MOTEL PASS**
    - **M**: Melanocytes.
    - **O**: Odontoblasts.
    - **T**: Tracheal cartilage.
    - **E**: Enterochromaffin cells.
    - **L**: Laryngeal cartilage.
    - **P**: Parafollicular cells (C-cells) of Thyroid.
    - **A**: Adrenal Medulla.
    - **S**: Schwann cells.
    - **S**: Spinal/Sympathetic ganglia.
- **Mesoderm**: Muscle, Bone, Connective Tissue, Heart, Kidney, Gonads, Dermis.
    - **Exception**: Microglia are mesodermal.
- **Endoderm**: Epithelial lining of GIT/Respiratory, Liver, Pancreas, Follicular cells of Thyroid.

### Pharyngeal Apparatus (Master Table)
| Arch | Nerve | Muscles | Skeletal / Ligaments |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1st (Mandibular)** | V3 | Mastication, Mylohyoid, Ant. Digastric, Tensor vela palatini, Tensor tympani | Meckel cartilage, Malleus, Incus, Mandible, Sphenomandibular ligament |
| **2nd (Hyoid)** | VII | Facial expression, Stapedius, Stylohyoid, Post. Digastric | Reichert cartilage, Stapes, Styloid, Lesser horn of hyoid |
| **3rd** | IX | Stylopharyngeus | Greater horn of hyoid |
| **4th & 6th** | X (RLN/SLN) | Cricothyroid (4th), Laryngeal intrinsic (6th), Pharyngeal constrictors | Thyroid, Cricoid, Arytenoid, Corniculate, Cuneiform cartilages |

### Pouches & Clefts
- **1st Pouch**: Middle ear, Tubotympanic recess.
- **2nd Pouch**: Palatine tonsil stroma.
- **3rd Pouch**: **Inferior** Parathyroid + Thymus (Third = Thymus).
- **4th Pouch**: **Superior** Parathyroid + Ultimobranchial body.
- **1st Cleft**: External acoustic meatus.

## 3. Histology

### Epithelium Identification
- **Simple Squamous**: Endothelium (vessels), Mesothelium (pleura/peritoneum), Alveoli.
- **Simple Cuboidal**: Thyroid follicles, Renal tubules (PCT/DCT).
- **Simple Columnar**: Stomach, Intestines, Gallbladder.
- **Pseudostratified Ciliated Columnar**: Respiratory tract (Trachea, Bronchi).
- **Transitional (Urothelium)**: Urinary bladder, Ureter, Renal pelvis. (Keys: Umbrella cells).
- **Stratified Squamous Non-keratinized**: Esophagus, Vagina, Cornea.
- **Stratified Squamous Keratinized**: Skin (Epidermis).

### Organ-Specific Slide Keys
- **Duodenum**: Brunner glands in the submucosa.
- **Ileum**: Peyer patches in the lamina propria/submucosa.
- **Appendix**: Continuous ring of lymphoid follicles.
- **Colon**: No villi, many goblet cells, Taenia coli (longitudinal muscle).
- **Trachea**: C-shaped hyaline cartilage rings.
- **Liver**: Hexagonal lobules with central vein, portal triads (Hepatic artery, Portal vein, Bile duct).
- **Pancreas**: Islets of Langerhans (Endocrine) + Acini (Exocrine). No striated ducts.

## 4. One-Liners & Clinical Correlation
- **Treacher Collins Syndrome**: Failure of 1st Arch neural crest migration.
- **DiGeorge Syndrome**: Failure of 3rd and 4th pouches (Thymic hypoplasia + Hypocalcemia).
- **Thyroglossal Duct Cyst**: Midline swelling, moves with protrusion of tongue.
- **Branchial Cyst**: Lateral neck swelling (Remnant of cervical sinus), does NOT move with deglutition.
- **Ligamentum Arteriosum**: Remnant of Ductus Arteriosus (Left 6th arch).
- **Meckel Diverticulum**: Remnant of Vitellointestinal duct; "Rule of 2s".
