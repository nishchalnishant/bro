# General Anatomy, Embryology, And Histology

## 1. General Anatomy

### Core Definitions
- Anatomical position is the reference for all descriptions.
- Median plane divides body into equal right and left halves.
- Coronal plane divides anterior and posterior parts.
- Transverse plane divides superior and inferior parts.

### Movements
- Flexion and extension.
- Abduction and adduction.
- Medial and lateral rotation.
- Pronation and supination.
- Inversion and eversion.
- Opposition and reposition.

### Bones
- Long, short, flat, irregular, sesamoid, pneumatic bones.
- Nutrient artery enters opposite to growing end.
- Growing end rule is repeatedly asked in osteology.

### Joints
- Fibrous:
  suture, syndesmosis, gomphosis.
- Cartilaginous:
  primary and secondary.
- Synovial:
  plane, hinge, pivot, condylar, saddle, ball-and-socket.

### Ossification
- Intramembranous:
  flat bones of skull and clavicle part.
- Endochondral:
  most long bones.
- Important comparison:
  intramembranous lacks cartilage model.

## 2. Embryology

### Early Development
- Fertilization occurs in ampulla.
- Cleavage produces morula then blastocyst.
- Implantation normally in upper posterior uterine wall.
- Bilaminar disc forms epiblast and hypoblast.
- Gastrulation forms ectoderm, mesoderm, endoderm.

### Germ Layer Derivatives
- Ectoderm:
  CNS, PNS, epidermis, enamel, adrenal medulla.
- Mesoderm:
  muscle, bone, connective tissue, cardiovascular system, kidney, gonads.
- Endoderm:
  epithelial lining of GIT and respiratory tract, liver, pancreas, thyroid follicular cells.

### Pharyngeal Apparatus
- First arch:
  maxilla, mandible, malleus, incus, muscles of mastication, nerve `V3`.
- Second arch:
  stapes, styloid process, lesser horn of hyoid, facial expression muscles, nerve `VII`.
- Third arch:
  greater horn of hyoid, stylopharyngeus, nerve `IX`.
- Fourth and sixth arches:
  laryngeal cartilages, pharyngeal/laryngeal muscles, nerves `X`.

### Pouches And Clefts
- First pouch:
  middle ear cavity and auditory tube.
- Second pouch:
  palatine tonsil epithelium.
- Third pouch:
  inferior parathyroid and thymus.
- Fourth pouch:
  superior parathyroid and ultimobranchial body.
- First cleft:
  external auditory meatus.
- Second to fourth clefts normally disappear.

### Gut Rotation
- Midgut herniates then rotates total `270` degrees anticlockwise around SMA.
- Failure can produce malrotation or volvulus.
- Vitellointestinal duct remnant causes Meckel diverticulum.

### Fetal Circulation
- Umbilical vein carries oxygenated blood.
- Ductus venosus bypasses liver.
- Foramen ovale bypasses lungs.
- Ductus arteriosus connects pulmonary trunk to aorta.

## 3. Histology

### Epithelium
- Simple squamous:
  alveoli, endothelium, mesothelium.
- Simple cuboidal:
  thyroid follicles, kidney tubules.
- Simple columnar:
  stomach, intestine, gallbladder.
- Pseudostratified ciliated columnar:
  trachea.
- Transitional:
  urinary bladder.

### Cartilage
- Hyaline:
  articular surfaces, tracheal rings.
- Elastic:
  epiglottis, pinna.
- Fibrocartilage:
  intervertebral disc, pubic symphysis.

### Bone
- Osteoblasts build bone.
- Osteoclasts resorb bone.
- Osteocytes lie in lacunae.
- Woven bone is immature; lamellar bone is mature.

### Lymphoid Organs
- Lymph node:
  cortex with follicles, medulla with cords/sinuses.
- Spleen:
  white pulp around central arteriole, red pulp sinusoids.
- Thymus:
  lobulated, cortex/medulla, Hassall corpuscles.

### Endocrine Histology
- Thyroid:
  follicles with colloid.
- Parathyroid:
  chief cells and oxyphil cells.
- Pituitary:
  acidophils, basophils, chromophobes.

### High-Yield Slide Differentiators
- PCT has eosinophilic cells and brush border.
- DCT has clearer lumen and less brush border.
- Ileum has Peyer patches.
- Duodenum has Brunner glands.
- Colon has many goblet cells and no villi.

## 4. One-Liners To Memorize
- First arch syndrome includes Treacher Collins and Pierre Robin patterns.
- Inferior parathyroid comes from third pouch and migrates farther than superior parathyroid.
- Meckel diverticulum is a true diverticulum.
- Hassall corpuscles identify thymus.
- Transitional epithelium identifies urinary tract lining.
