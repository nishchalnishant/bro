# Head, Neck, Neuroanatomy, And Pelvis

## Head And Neck
- Cranial nerves, scalp, face, triangles of neck, salivary glands, and thyroid relations.
- Cavernous sinus and foramina with transmitted structures are essential.

## Neuroanatomy
- Cerebral cortex, internal capsule, ventricles, brainstem nuclei, spinal tracts, and cerebellum.
- Blood supply of brain and cranial nerve lesions should be integrated clinically.

## Pelvis And Perineum
- Pelvic walls, supports of uterus, perineal pouches, pudendal nerve, and ischioanal fossa.
- Male and female perineal anatomy are often tested through procedures or injuries.

## Must Learn
- Cranial nerve nuclei/functions.
- Internal capsule blood supply.
- Foramina contents.
- Supports of uterus.
