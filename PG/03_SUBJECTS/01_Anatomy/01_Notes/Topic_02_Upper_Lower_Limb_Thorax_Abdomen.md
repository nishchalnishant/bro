# Upper Limb, Lower Limb, Thorax, And Abdomen

## Upper Limb
- Brachial plexus, rotator cuff, cubital fossa, carpal tunnel, and arterial supply.
- Nerve injuries with muscle weakness and sensory loss are frequent.

## Lower Limb
- Femoral triangle, adductor canal, gluteal region, sciatic nerve, and knee ligaments.
- Hip blood supply, foot arches, and common fracture-nerve correlations.

## Thorax
- Mediastinum, pleura, lungs, bronchopulmonary segments, and coronary circulation.
- Surface anatomy of lung borders and heart sounds has clinical value.

## Abdomen
- Peritoneum, mesenteries, portal venous system, inguinal canal, and retroperitoneal organs.
- Applied anatomy includes hernia, portosystemic anastomosis, and referred pain.

## Must Learn
- Root values and muscle actions.
- Coronary artery dominance.
- Inguinal canal boundaries.
- Portocaval sites.
