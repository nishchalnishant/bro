# Ten-Year PYQ Bank: Internal Medicine (2014-2024)

This bank focuses on core diagnosis, ECG interpretation, and 2023-2024 Management Guidelines.

## 1. Cardiology

### [Clinical Vignette] - Acute MI Management
**Question**: An 60-year-old male presents with retrosternal chest pain for 2 hours. ECG shows **ST elevations in V1-V4**. What is the most important next step?
- **Key Point**: **Immediate PCI (Primary Angioplasty)** or Fibrinolysis (if PCI not available within 120 mins).
- **Year Repeated**: 2014, 2017, 2021, 2024.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **JVP Waveform**: 'a' wave (Atrial contraction), 'v' wave (Venous filling).
- **Beck's Triad**: Muffled heart sounds, Hypotension, Raised JVP (Cardiac Tamponade).
- **DOC for SVT**: Adenosine.
- **Duroziez's Sign**: Seen in Aortic Regurgitation.

## 2. Neurology & Endocrinology

### [Clinical Vignette] - Neurological Localization
**Question**: A patient presents with Broca's aphasia and right-sided upper limb weakness. Which artery is involved?
- **Key Point**: **Left Middle Cerebral Artery (MCA)** - Superior division.
- **Year Repeated**: 2015, 2019, 2023.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **DOC for Diabetic Nephropathy (Early)**: ACE Inhibitors (e.g., Enalapril).
- **Whipple's Triad**: Hypoglycemic symptoms, Low plasma glucose, Relief with glucose (Insulinoma).
- **Dawn Phenomenon**: Early morning hyperglycemia due to GH surge.

## 3. Pulmonology & Nephrology

### [Clinical Vignette] - COPD (GOLD 2023 Update)
**Question**: A 65-year-old smoker presents with chronic cough and breathlessness. **FEV1/FVC < 0.7**. What is the current Step 1 treatment for Group B patients?
- **Key Point**: **LAMA or LABA** (or combination). [Note: ICS only used if Eosinophils >300].
- **Year Repeated**: 2016, 2021, 2023.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Egg-shell calcification**: Seen in **Silicosis**.
- **Nephritic Syndrome Triad**: Hematuria, Hypertension, Azotemia.
- **Muddy Brown Casts**: Seen in Acute Tubular Necrosis (ATN).

## 4. Gastroenterology & Hematology

### [Clinical Vignette] - Chronic Liver Disease
**Question**: A patient with cirrhosis presents with hematemesis. Which score is used to assess the 3-month survival/prognosis for transplant?
- **Key Point**: **MELD Score** (Model for End-Stage Liver Disease). [Parameters: Creatinine, Bilirubin, INR].
- **Year Repeated**: 2018, 2022, 2024.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2024 High Yield
- **DOC for Wilson's Disease**: D-Penicillamine.
- **Auer Rods**: Pathognomonic for AML.
- **Philadelphia Chromosome**: t(9;22) in CML.
- **Schilling Test**: Used for Vitamin B12 absorption.
