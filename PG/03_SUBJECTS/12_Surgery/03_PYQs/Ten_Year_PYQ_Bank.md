# Ten-Year PYQ Bank: General Surgery (2014-2024)

This bank focuses on core surgical pathology, trauma management, and pathognomonic visual signs.

## 1. Abdominal Surgery (GI & Hepatobiliary)

### [Clinical Vignette] - Intestinal Obstruction
**Question**: A 40-year-old female presents with colicky abdominal pain, vomiting, and constipation. X-ray shows **Dilated small bowel loops with multiple air-fluid levels**. What is the most common cause of small bowel obstruction in adults?
- **Key Point**: **Post-operative Adhesions**.
- **Year Repeated**: 2014, 2018, 2021, 2024.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Courvoisier's Law**: Palpable gallbladder + Jaundice ≠ Gallstones (usually Pancreatic head cancer).
- **Rovsing's Sign**: Pressure on LLQ causes pain in RLQ (Acute Appendicitis).
- **DOC for Acute Cholecystitis**: Laparoscopic Cholecystectomy.
- **Glass-bead appearance**: Seen in Gallstone Ileus.

## 2. Breast & Endocrine Surgery

### [Clinical Vignette] - Breast Cancer
**Question**: A 50-year-old woman presents with a hard, painless lump in the upper outer quadrant of her breast. Examination shows **Skin tethering (Peau d'orange)**. What is the most common type of breast cancer?
- **Key Point**: **Invasive Ductal Carcinoma (NOS)**.
- **Year Repeated**: 2015, 2019, 2023.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Most common benign breast tumor**: Fibroadenoma ("Breast Mouse").
- **Orphan Annie Eye Nuclei**: Papillary Thyroid Carcinoma.
- **Berry's Ligament**: Landmark for identifying Recurrent Laryngeal Nerve during Thyroidectomy.

## 3. Trauma & Fluids (ATLS 10th Update)

### [Clinical Vignette] - Tension Pneumothorax
**Question**: A victim of a RTA presents with respiratory distress, tracheal deviation to the opposite side, and absent breath sounds on one side. What is the immediate life-saving procedure?
- **Key Point**: **Needle Thoracocentesis** (2nd intercostal space) followed by ICD (Chest tube).
- **Year Repeated**: 2016, 2020, 2024.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Lethal Triad of Trauma**: Acidosis, Coagulopathy, Hypothermia.
- **Rule of 9 (Burns)**: Hand is 1%; Anterior Trunk is 18%.
- **Parkland Formula**: 4 ml x weight (kg) x % BSA burn (Fluid given over 24 hrs).

## 4. Urology & Vascular Surgery

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2024 High Yield
- **Triad of Renal Cell Ca**: Hematuria, Flank pain, Palpable mass.
- **Varicocele**: "Bag of worms" appearance (usually on the Left side).
- **Intermittent Claudication**: Peripheral Arterial Disease (Sign of Limb Ischemia).
- **Trendelenburg Test**: For Varicose Veins (Saphenous competence).
