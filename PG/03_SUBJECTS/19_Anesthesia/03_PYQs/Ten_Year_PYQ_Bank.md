# Ten-Year PYQ Bank: Anesthesia (2014-2024)

This bank focuses on anesthetic agents, emergency intubation protocols, and high-yield pharmacologic facts.

## 1. Intravenous & Inhalational Anesthetics

### [Clinical Vignette] - Infusion Sensation
**Question**: An IV anesthetic agent is injected, and the patient reports a feeling of "Propofol-induced pain" at the injection site. Which agent has the fastest induction and recovery, commonly used for day-care surgery?
- **Key Point**: **Propofol**. (2014, 2018, 2023).

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **DOC for Day-Care Surgery**: Propofol.
- **DOC for Head Injury**: Thiopentone (Reduces ICP).
- **Agent causing Dissociative Anesthesia**: **Ketamine**. (Causes ↑ ICP, ↑ BP, Bronchodilation).
- **Steal Phenomenon**: Seen with **Isoflurane** (Coronary steal).

## 2. Local Anesthesia

### [Clinical Vignette] - LA Toxicity
**Question**: After a local anesthetic block, a patient develops perioral numbness and metallic taste. What is the mechanism of action of local anesthetics?
- **Key Point**: **Na+ channel blockage** (Inhibits influx).
- **Year Repeated**: 2015, 2019, 2024.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Smallest fiber affected first**: Type B and Type C (Sympathetic and Pain).
- **Agent with highest systemic toxicity**: Bupivacaine.
- **EMLA cream**: Combination of Lidocaine and Prilocaine.

## 3. General Anesthesia & Procedures

### [Clinical Vignette] - Intubation
**Question**: During an emergency intubation, the anesthesiologist uses the **Mallampati Classification** to assess the airway. If only the Soft Palate is visible, what is the Grade?
- **Key Point**: **Grade III**. (Grade I: Pillars, Uvula, Palate visible; Grade IV: Only Hard Palate).
- **Year Repeated**: 2016, 2021, 2024.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Difficult Airway Predictor**: Thyromental distance < 6 cm.
- **Triad of GA**: Analgesia, Amnesia, Muscle Relaxation.
- **Magill's Circuit**: Mapleson A (Best for spontaneous breathing).
- **Bain's Circuit**: Mapleson D (Best for controlled ventilation).

## 4. Muscle Relaxants & Emergencies

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2024 High Yield
- **Succinylcholine**: Only depolarizing muscle relaxant (Causes fasciculations). **Malignant Hyperthermia** risk.
- **DOC for Malignant Hyperthermia**: **Dantrolene**.
- **Hoffmann Elimination**: Seen with **Atracurium** and Cis-atracurium (Safe in renal/liver failure).
- **Color of Oxygen Cylinder**: Black body, White shoulder.
- **Color of Nitrous Oxide Cylinder**: Blue.
