# Ten-Year PYQ Bank: Orthopedics (2014-2024)

This bank focuses on high-yield clinical vignettes for Fractures, Tumors, and Systemic conditions, with emphasis on IBQs.

## 1. Fractures & Dislocations

### [Clinical Vignette] - Shoulder Injury
**Question**: An 18-year-old male falls on an outstretched hand and presents with a "Squared-off" shoulder. Diagnosis?
- **Key Point**: **Anterior Dislocation of Shoulder**. Most common nerve injured: **Axillary Nerve**.
- **Year Repeated**: 2014, 2018, 2023.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Dinner Fork Deformity**: Colles' Fracture (Distal radius - Dorsal displacement).
- **Garden Spade Deformity**: Smith's Fracture (Distal radius - Volar displacement).
- **Monteggia Fracture**: Proximal Ulna fracture + **Radial head** dislocation.
- **Galeazzi Fracture**: Distal Radial fracture + **Radio-ulnar joint** dislocation.

## 2. Orthopedic Tumors (IBQ Core)

### [Clinical Vignette] - Osteosarcoma
**Question**: A 15-year-old boy presents with pain around the knee. X-ray shows **Sunray spicules** and **Codman's triangle**. Diagnosis?
- **Key Point**: **Osteosarcoma**.
- **Year Repeated**: 2015, 2019, 2024.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Onion-Skin Appearance**: Ewing's Sarcoma (t(11;22)).
- **Soap-Bubble Appearance**: Giant Cell Tumor (GCT / Osteoclastoma).
- **Ground-Glass Appearance**: Fibrous Dysplasia.
- **Night Pain relieved by Aspirin**: Osteoid Osteoma (Characteristic "Nidus").

## 3. Pediatric & Systemic Orthopedics

### [Clinical Vignette] - Osteomyelitis
**Question**: A 10-year-old boy presents with sudden on-set high fever and localized bone pain. X-ray is initially normal. What is the most sensitive early investigation?
- **Key Point**: **MRI** (or Ultrasound/Bone Scan if MRI not available).
- **Year Repeated**: 2016, 2021, 2024.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Housemaid's Knee**: Pre-patellar bursitis.
- **Clergyman's Knee**: Infra-patellar bursitis.
- **Boutonniere Deformity**: Seen in Rheumatoid Arthritis (Central slip rupture).
- **Swan Neck Deformity**: Seen in Rheumatoid Arthritis (Volar plate laxity).

## 4. Orthopedic Tests (Rapid Recall)
- **Thomas Test**: For Fixed Flexion Deformity of Hip.
- **Trendelenburg Test**: For Gluteus Medius weakness.
- **Lachman Test**: Most sensitive for **ACL** tear.
- **McMurray's Test**: For Meniscal tear.
- **Finkelstein's Test**: For De Quervain's Tenosynovitis.
