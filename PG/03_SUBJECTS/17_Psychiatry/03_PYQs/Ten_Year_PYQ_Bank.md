# Ten-Year PYQ Bank: Psychiatry (2014-2024)

This bank focuses on core symptom recognition, Schizophrenia, Mood disorders, and High-yield Drug management.

## 1. Schizophrenia & Psychotic Disorders

### [Clinical Vignette] - Schneider's First Rank Symptoms
**Question**: A patient reports hearing a "running commentary" of his own thoughts. Which of the following is a classic Schneider's First Rank Symptom?
- **Key Point**: **Auditory Hallucinations (3rd person)**; Thought Insertion; Delutional Perception.
- **Year Repeated**: 2014, 2018, 2023.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Best Prognostic Factor**: Late onset, female gender, positive symptoms.
- **Atypical Antipsychotic for Resistant cases**: Clozapine (Requires WBC monitoring for Agranulocytosis).
- **EPS (Extrapyramidal Symptoms)**: Caused by **D2 blockage** (Typical antipsychotics like Haloperidol).

## 2. Mood Disorders (Depression & Bipolar)

### [Clinical Vignette] - Lithium Toxicity
**Question**: A patient on Lithium for Bipolar Disorder presents with tremors, polyuria, and confusion. Which cardiac anomaly is associated with Lithium intake during pregnancy?
- **Key Point**: **Ebstein's Anomaly**.
- **Year Repeated**: 2015, 2019, 2024.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **DOC for Depression**: SSRIs (e.g., Fluoxetine).
- **DOC for Acute Mania**: Lithium / Valproate.
- **Bipolar I vs II**: I requires one manic episode; II requires Hypomania + Depression.
- **Dysthymia**: Chronic low-grade depression (≥ 2 years).

## 3. Personality & Anxiety Disorders

### [Clinical Vignette] - OCD
**Question**: A 25-year-old male feels compelled to check the door lock 10 times before leaving his house, despite knowing it is locked. Diagnosis and DOC?
- **Key Point**: **Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)**. DOC: **Fluoxetine** (SSRIs) in high doses or Clomipramine.
- **Year Repeated**: 2016, 2021, 2024.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Antidote for Benzodiazepine Overdose**: Flumazenil.
- **Defense Mechanism - Splitting**: Seen in **Borderline Personality Disorder**.
- **Defense Mechanism - Reaction Formation**: Expressing the opposite of a forbidden impulse (e.g., a child who hates a parent becoming overly caring).

## 4. Miscellaneous (Sleep, Eating, Substance)

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2024 High Yield
- **Delirium Tremens**: Severe alcohol withdrawal (72-96 hours after last drink).
- **Wernicke Encephalopathy Triad**: Confusion, Ataxia, Ophthalmoplegia (B1 deficiency).
- **Narcolepsy**: Tetrad - Day sleepiness, Cataplexy, Sleep paralysis, Hypnagogic hallucinations.
- **Highest mortality psychiatric disorder**: Anorexia Nervosa.
