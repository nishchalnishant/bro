# Ten-Year PYQ Bank: Forensic Medicine (2014-2024)

This bank focuses on core IPC sections, injury patterns, and thanatology.

## 1. Legal Procedures & Medical Law

### [Clinical Vignette] - Professional Negligence
**Question**: A surgeon operates on the wrong limb. Legal action is taken. Which legal doctrine applies when the "thing speaks for itself"?
- **Key Point**: **Res Ipsa Loquitur**. (2015, 2021, 2024).

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **IPC 304A**: Punishment for causing death by negligence.
- **IPC 375**: Definition of Rape.
- **IPC 320**: Grievous hurt (includes permanent loss of sight/hearing, emasculation).
- **Hanging (IPC)**: Section 300 (Murder) or 306 (Abetment of suicide).

## 2. Thanatology (Science of Death)

### [Clinical Vignette] - Post-Mortem Interval
**Question**: During an autopsy, the body shows stiffening of joints starting from the jaw and moving downwards. What is this phenomenon?
- **Key Point**: **Rigor Mortis**. (2014, 2018, 2023).

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Algor Mortis**: Post-mortem cooling of the body.
- **Livor Mortis**: Post-mortem staining/lividity.
- **Adipocere Formation**: Saponification of fat (occurs in moist/warm environments).
- **Mummification**: Desiccation of tissues (occurs in dry/hot environments).

## 3. Toxicology (Forensic)

### [Clinical Vignette] - Chronic Poisoning
**Question**: A 45-year-old worker in a battery factory presents with abdominal colic and a blue line on his gums. Diagnosis?
- **Key Point**: **Plumbism (Lead Poisoning)**. Characteristic "Burtonian line". (2016, 2022 INI-CET).

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Arsenic Poisoning**: Mee's lines on nails. Aldrich-Mee's.
- **Mercury Poisoning**: Minamata disease.
- **Strychnine Poisoning**: Risus Sardonicus.
- **Organophosphate antidote**: Atropine (Muscarinic) and Pralidoxime (Nicotinic).

## 4. Traumatology & Sexual Offenses

### [Clinical Vignette] - Firearm Injuries
**Question**: An entrance wound shows **Tattooing** and **Smudging**. What is the likely range of fire?
- **Key Point**: **Intermediate range** (Tattooing indicates unburnt gunpowder). (2017, 2020, 2024).

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Chop Wound**: Caused by heavy-bladed weapons (Cleaver, Sword).
- **Contusion**: Bruise (extravasation of blood).
- **Laceration**: Tearing of tissues.
- **Virginity sign**: Intact Hymen (not absolute).
