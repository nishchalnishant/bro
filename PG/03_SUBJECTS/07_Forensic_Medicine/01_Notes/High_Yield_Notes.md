# Forensic Medicine High-Yield Notes

## Legal Medicine
- Consent, negligence, medical ethics, medical records, and doctor-patient responsibilities.
- IPC, CrPC, evidence law, and medicolegal reporting basics.

## Thanatology
- Time since death, rigor mortis, lividity, putrefaction, and postmortem findings.
- Cause, manner, and mechanism of death should be distinguished clearly.

## Injuries
- Abrasion, bruise, laceration, incised, stab, firearm, and blast injuries.
- Regional injury patterns and age of bruise are commonly asked.

## Asphyxia And Thermal Deaths
- Hanging, strangulation, drowning, suffocation, burns, electrocution, and lightning.

## Sexual Offences And Toxicology
- Sexual offences, examination protocol, hymen facts, and evidence preservation.
- Common poisons, signs, fatal doses, and antidotes.
