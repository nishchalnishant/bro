# General Pharmacology, ANS, And CNS

## 1. General Pharmacology

### Pharmacokinetics & Dynamics
- **Zero-order Kinetics**: Constant **amount** eliminated (e.g., Ethanol, Aspirin at high dose, Phenytoin).
- **First-order Kinetics**: Constant **fraction** eliminated. Most drugs follow this.
- **Half-life (t1/2)**: Time to reach steady state (~4-5 half-lives).
- **Potency**: Amount required for an effect (shifts curve left).
- **Efficacy**: Maximum effect possible (shifts curve up).

### Receptor Signaling (Very High Yield)
| G-Protein | Signaling Pathway | Key Receptors |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Gs** | Stimulates Adenylate Cyclase -> cAMP | **Beta-1, Beta-2**, D1, V2, H2 |
| **Gi** | Inhibits Adenylate Cyclase | **M2**, Alpha-2, D2 |
| **Gq** | Phospholipase C -> IP3/DAG/Ca | **M1, M3**, Alpha-1, H1, V1 |

## 2. Autonomic Nervous System (ANS)

### Cholinergic Drugs
- **Direct Agonists**: Pilocarpine (Drug of Choice for Acute Congestive Glaucoma).
- **Anticholinesterases**:
    - **Neostigmine**: Preferred for Myasthenia Gravis.
    - **Edrophonium**: Tensilon test (Diagnosis of Myasthenia).
    - **Physostigmine**: Antidote for Atropine poisoning.
- **Organophosphate Poisoning**: Management includes **Atropine** (Antimuscarinic) and **Pralidoxime** (Cholinesterase regenerator).

### Adrenergic Drugs
- **Alpha-1**: Vasoconstriction.
- **Beta-1**: Heart (Increase rate/force).
- **Beta-2**: Vasodilation/Bronchodilation.
- **Alpha-blockers**: Prazosin (First dose phenomenon - syncope).
- **Beta-blockers**: Propranolol (Non-selective; avoid in Asthma).

## 3. Central Nervous System (CNS)

### Antiepileptic Drugs (AEDs) - MOA & Side Effects
- **Phenytoin**: Na+ channel blocker. **S/E**: Gingival hyperplasia, Hirsutism, Fetal hydantoin syndrome.
- **Valproate**: Multiple MOA. **S/E**: Weight gain, Hepatotoxicity, Neural tube defects in fetus. **Drug of Choice for most Generalized Epilepsy**.
- **Ethosuximide**: T-type Ca+ channel blocker. **Drug of Choice for Absence Seizures**.
- **Carbamazepine**: **Drug of Choice for Trigeminal Neuralgia**.

### Parkinson's Disease
- **Levodopa + Carbidopa**: Gold standard. Carbidopa prevents peripheral conversion.
- **Side Effects**: "On-off" phenomenon, Dyskinesias.

### Antipsychotics & Antidepressants
- **Antipsychotics**: Typical (Haloperidol - Extrapyramidal S/E) vs Atypical (Clozapine - Agranulocytosis risk, Weight gain).
- **Antidepressants**: SSRIs (Fluoxetine - Most common) vs TCAs (Amitriptyline - Anticholinergic S/E).
- **Lithium**: Drug of choice for Bipolar. Narrow therapeutic index; causes Ebstein's anomaly in fetus.
