# General Pharmacology, ANS, And CNS

## General Pharmacology
- Pharmacokinetics, receptor theory, potency vs efficacy, and adverse drug reactions.
- Drug interactions and dose-response relationships are high yield.

## ANS Drugs
- Sympathomimetics, sympatholytics, cholinergic agonists, anticholinergics, and NMJ blockers.
- Learn prototype drug, mechanism, uses, and adverse effects.

## CNS Drugs
- Antiepileptics, sedative-hypnotics, antipsychotics, antidepressants, opioids, and anesthetic agents.

## Must Learn
- Receptor-action pairs.
- Antiepileptic adverse effects.
- Antipsychotic side effects.
- Organophosphate management.
