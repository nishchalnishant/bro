# CVS, Endocrine, Respiratory, And GIT Drugs

## 1. Cardiovascular System (CVS)

### Antiarrhythmics (Vaughan-Williams Classification)
- **Class I (Na+ Channel Blockers)**:
    - **Ia**: Procainamide, Quinidine (Increase APD).
    - **Ib**: Lidocaine (Shorten APD). **Drug of Choice for Post-MI Ventricular Arrhythmia**.
    - **Ic**: Flecainide (No effect on APD).
- **Class II (Beta-Blockers)**: Propranolol, Esmolol (Rapid action).
- **Class III (K+ Channel Blockers)**: **Amiodarone** (Most effective; S/E: Pulm fibrosis, Thyroid dysfunction), Sotalol.
- **Class IV (Ca+ Channel Blockers)**: Verapamil, Diltiazem.

### Heart Failure & Hypertension
- **Digoxin**: Inhibits Na/K ATPase. **S/E**: Yellow vision (Xanthopsia), PVCs. **Antidote**: Digibind.
- **Hypertension in Pregnancy**: **Nifedipine** (preferred), Methyldopa, Labetalol. (Mnemonic: "He Loves My Neonate" -> Hydralazine, Labetalol, Methyldopa, Nifedipine).
- **ACE Inhibitors**: Enalapril. **S/E**: Dry cough (Bradykinin), Angioedema, Teratogenic.

## 2. Endocrine Pharmacology

### Diabetes Mellitus (Latest Trends)
- **Metformin**: MOA (Activates AMP-activated protein kinase). S/E: Lactic acidosis.
- **SGLT2 Inhibitors (Dapagliflozin)**: MOA (Inhibit glucose reabsorption in kidneys). S/E: UTI/Genital candidiasis.
- **GLP-1 Agonists (Liraglutide)**: MOA (Incretin mimetic). Weight loss benefits.
- **DPP-4 Inhibitors (Sitagliptin)**: Weight neutral.

### Thyroid & Bone
- **Antithyroid**: Propylthiouracil (PTU) preferred in 1st trimester. Methimazole in 2nd/3rd.
- **Bisphosphonates (Alendronate)**: MOA (Inhibit osteoclasts). S/E: Erosive esophagitis (Take with water, remain upright).

## 3. Respiratory Pharmacology

### Bronchial Asthma (GINA 2023 Guidelines)
- **Preferred Reliever**: **Low-dose ICS-Formoterol** (Single Maintenance and Reliever Therapy - SMART).
- **Maintenance**: Inhaled Corticosteroids (ICS) + LABA.
- **SABA (Salbutamol)**: Now used with ICS even for mild cases.

### COPD (GOLD 2023 Guidelines)
- **Group A**: Bronchodilator.
- **Group B**: LABA + LAMA.
- **Group E**: Triple therapy (LABA + LAMA + ICS).

## 4. Gastrointestinal Tract (GIT)

### Anti-secretory & Antacids
- **PPIs (Omeprazole)**: MOA (Irreversible inhibition of H/K ATPase). Drug of choice for GERD and Peptic Ulcer.
- **H2 Blockers**: Ranitidine, Cimetidine (S/E: Gynecomastia).

### Anti-emetics
- **5-HT3 Blockers (Ondansetron)**: MOA (Chemoreceptor trigger zone inhibition). **Drug of choice for Chemotherapy-induced vomiting**.
- **Aprepitant (NK1 Blocker)**: Used for delayed vomiting.
