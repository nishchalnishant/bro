# 50 Must-Do MCQ Themes for Pharmacology

Mechanisms, side effects, and Drugs of Choice (DOC) dominate the Pharmacology section.

1. **Zero-Order vs First-Order Kinetics**: Identifying drugs that follow zero-order (Ethanol, Phenytoin).
2. **Receptor G-Protein Coupling**: Matching receptors (Alpha, Beta, M, H) to their G-proteins (Gs, Gi, Gq).
3. **Enzyme Inducers vs Inhibitors**: Recognizing drug interactions (e.g., Rifampin inducing Warfarin metabolism).
4. **Adrenaline and its Receptors**: Physiological effects at low vs high doses (Beta-2 vs Alpha-1).
5. **Organophosphate Poisoning**: Triple therapy (Atropine, Pralidoxime, Midazolam).
6. **Myasthenia Gravis Management**: Differentiating diagnosis (Edrophonium) vs treatment (Pyridostigmine).
7. **Glaucoma Drugs**: Mechanism of Pilocarpine, Beta-blockers, and Latanoprost.
8. **Antiepileptic Drug Selection**: Drug of Choice for Absence (Ethosuximide) vs Generalized (Valproate).
9. **Phenytoin Side Effects**: Gingival hyperplasia, Hirsutism, and Fetal Hydantoin Syndrome.
10. **Valproate Toxicology**: Hepatotoxicity and Weight gain.
11. **Parkinson's Therapy**: Role of Levodopa/Carbidopa and the "On-Off" phenomenon.
12. **Antipsychotic Metabolic S/E**: Weight gain and Diabetes risk with Atypicals (Clozapine).
13. **Lithium Toxicity**: Nephrogenic DI and Ebstein's anomaly.
14. **Typical Antipsychotic S/E**: Extrapyramidal symptoms (EPS) and neuroleptic malignant syndrome.
15. **Antiarrhythmic Classification**: Matching drugs to Vaughan-Williams classes (I-IV).
16. **Digoxin Mechanism & Toxicity**: Inhibition of Na/K ATPase and Xanthopsia (Yellow vision).
17. **Antihypertensives in Pregnancy**: Safety of Nifedipine, Labetalol, and Methyldopa.
18. **ACE Inhibitor Side Effects**: Dry cough (Bradykinin) and Angioedema.
19. **Statin Mechanism**: HMG-CoA Reductase inhibition and Rhabdomyolysis risk.
20. **Warfarin vs Heparin**: Monitoring (PT/INR vs aPTT) and Antidotes (Vit K vs Protamine).
21. **Newer Anticoagulants**: Dabigatran (Direct Thrombin) and Rivaroxaban (Factor Xa).
22. **Insulin Profiles**: Difference between Rapid (Lispro) and Long-acting (Glargine).
23. **GLP-1 Agonists vs SGLT2 Inhibitors**: Cardiovascular and weight loss benefits.
24. **Corticosteroid Side Effects**: Cushingoid features, Hyperglycemia, and Cataracts.
25. **Antithyroid Selection in Pregnancy**: PTU (1st Trim) vs Methimazole (After).
26. **Bisphosphonates**: Administration instructions and Jaw Osteonecrosis risk.
27. **Asthma Step-wise Therapy**: SMART approach with Low-dose ICS-Formoterol (GINA 2023).
28. **COPD Management**: Role of LAMA vs LABA (GOLD 2023).
29. **Proton Pump Inhibitors (PPIs)**: Long-term risks (B12 deficiency, Osteoporosis).
30. **Anti-emetic Choice**: 5-HT3 blockers (Ondansetron) for Chemo-induced vomiting.
31. **Beta-Lactam Mechanisms**: Penicillins vs Cephalosporins (Cell wall inhibition).
32. **Cephalosporin Generations**: 3rd Gen (Ceftriaxone) for Meningitis/Typhoid.
33. **Aminoglycoside Toxicity**: Ototoxicity (Vestibular/Cochlear) and Nephrotoxicity.
34. **Tetracycline Side Effects**: Teeth discoloration and Photosensitivity.
35. **Macrolide Interactions**: Inhibition of Cyt P450 (Erythromycin).
36. **Fluoroquinolone Risks**: Tendon rupture and QT prolongation.
37. **Tuberculosis (DOTS)**: Dosing of HRZE and optic neuritis (Ethambutol).
38. **Antiretroviral Therapy (ART)**: NRTIs (Zidovudine - Anemia) vs PIs.
39. **Antifungal Mechanisms**: Ergosterol synthesis (Azoles) vs Pore formation (Amphotericin).
40. **Malaria Prophylaxis vs Treatment**: Use of Chloroquine and Artemisinin (ACT).
41. **Cancer Chemo Toxicity (Alkylating)**: Hemorrhagic cystitis (Cyclophosphamide).
42. **Antimetabolite S/E**: Methotrexate (Marrow suppression) and Folinic acid rescue.
43. **Microtubule Inhibitor Neurology**: Peripheral neuropathy with Vincristine.
44. **Monoclonal Antibodies**: Trastuzumab (HER2) and Imatinib (Bcl-Abl).
45. **General Anesthesia Stages**: Inhaled (Halothane) vs IV (Propofol).
46. **Local Anesthesia**: Addition of Adrenaline to prolong action.
47. **General Poisoning Antidotes**: N-Acetylcysteine for Paracetamol.
48. **Metal Chelation**: Dimercaprol for Arsenic/Mercury/Lead.
49. **Therapeutic Drug Monitoring (TDM)**: Drugs requiring level checks (Digoxin, Lithium, Phenytoin).
50. **Clinical Drug Development**: Difference between Phase I, II, III, and IV trials.
