# Ten-Year PYQ Bank: Pharmacology (2014-2024)

This bank contains 30+ high-yield "Banks" focused on mechanism-based clinical vignettes and guideline-sensitive drug choices.

## 1. Neuro-Pharmacology (ANS & CNS)

### [Clinical Vignette] - Organophosphate Poisoning
**Question**: A farmer is brought to the ER with pinpoint pupils, salivation, and bradycardia. What is the mechanism of the drug used to reverse the **muscle weakness**?
- **Key Point**: **Pralidoxime** (Oximes) - Reactivates acetylcholinesterase. (Note: Atropine only treats muscarinic symptoms, not the muscle weakness).
- **Year Repeated**: 2015, 2018, 2021, 2024.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **DOC for Trigeminal Neuralgia**: Carbamazepine.
- **DOC for Status Epilepticus**: Lorazepam (IV).
- **DOC for Absence Seizure**: Ethosuximide.
- **Side Effect of Valproate**: Neural tube defects, Hepatotoxicity.

## 2. Cardiovascular & Renal

### [Clinical Vignette] - Digoxin Toxicity
**Question**: A patient on treatment for heart failure presents with nausea and yellow vision. ECG shows "hockey stick" ST depression. Diagnosis?
- **Key Point**: **Digoxin Toxicity**. (Treat with Digibind).
- **Year Repeated**: 2014, 2017, 2022.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **ACE Inhibitor Side Effect**: Dry cough (due to Bradykinin).
- **Loop Diuretic Mechanism**: Inhibits Na-K-2Cl symporter (Thick ascending limb).
- **DOC for Anaphylactic Shock**: Adrenaline (IM 1:1000).

## 3. Endocrine & Metabolic

### [Clinical Vignette] - Diabetes Mellitus (Guideline Update)
**Question**: A diabetic patient with Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) and Heart Failure needs a new oral hypoglycemic agent. Which drug is preferred?
- **Key Point**: **SGLT2 Inhibitors** (e.g., Empagliflozin). [2023 Update].
- **Year Repeated**: 2021, 2023.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **DOC for Hyperthyroidism in 1st Trimester**: PTU (Propylthiouracil).
- **DOC for Diabetes Insipidus**: Desmopressin.
- **Side effect of Metformin**: Lactic acidosis.

## 4. Antimicrobials & Chemotherapy

### [Clinical Vignette] - TB Therapy (NTEP Update)
**Question**: A patient on DOTS therapy for TB presents with orange-colored urine. Which drug is responsible?
- **Key Point**: **Rifampin**.
- **Year Repeated**: 2016, 2019, 2024.

### [One-Liners] - 2014-2020 High Yield
- **Gray Baby Syndrome**: Chloramphenicol.
- **Red Man Syndrome**: Vancomycin.
- **DOC for MRSA**: Vancomycin.
- **DOC for Syphilis**: Benzathine Penicillin G.
