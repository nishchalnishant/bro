# Common Pharmacology Error Patterns: The Enzyme & Receptor Traps

Pharmacology is highly volatile. Use this log to avoid common conceptual and factual confusions.

## 1. The "Enzyme Master" Confusion (Most Common Trap)
- **Error**: Confusing an Enzyme Inducer with an Inhibitor. 
- **Fact**: 
    - **Inducers (Decrease drug levels)**: "G-P-R-S Cell" -> **G**riseofulvin, **P**henytoin, **R**ifampin, **S**moking, **C**arbamazepine.
    - **Inhibitors (Increase drug levels/toxicity)**: "VITAMIN K" -> **V**alproate, **I**soniazid, **T**rimethoprim, **A**miodarone, **M**acrolides (except Azithromycin), **I**traconazole, **N**epelivine, **K**etoconazole / **G**rapefruit juice.

## 2. Teratogenic Drug Traps
- **Error**: Thinking all ACE inhibitors are safe in early pregnancy.
- **Fact**: They are **Teratogenic** (Renal agenesis).
- **Phenytoin**: Fetal Hydantoin Syndrome.
- **Warfarin**: Fetal Warfarin Syndrome (Stippled epiphysis). Use **Heparin** instead.
- **Valproate**: Neural Tube Defects (Highest risk).

## 3. Receptor Coupling Confusions
- **M2 vs M3**:
    - **M2**: Heart (Gi - Inhibitory).
    - **M3**: Smooth muscle/Glands (Gq - Stimulatory).
- **Alpha-1 vs Alpha-2**:
    - **Alpha-1**: Post-synaptic (Gq - Constriction).
    - **Alpha-2**: Pre-synaptic (Gi - Inhibition of release).

## 4. Anti-TB Side Effects
- **Hepatotoxicity**: P-Z-H (Pyrazinamide > Z > H). **Ethambutol is NOT hepatotoxic**.
- **Peripheral Neuropathy**: Caused by **Isoniazid** (H). Treat with **Vitamin B6 (Pyridoxine)**.
- **Visual Changes**: **Ethambutol** (Optic neuritis).

## 5. Leukemia Treatment Confusions
- **CML**: **Imatinib** (DOC).
- **APL (M3)**: **ATRA** (All-trans retinoic acid).
- **CLL**: **Fludarabine** / Ibrutinib.

## 6. Disulfiram-like Reaction
- **Error**: Missing the "hidden" drugs that Cause this.
- **Fact**: **Metronidazole** (most classic), **Cefotetan**, **Cefoperazone**, **Procarbazine**, **Chlorpropamide**.
