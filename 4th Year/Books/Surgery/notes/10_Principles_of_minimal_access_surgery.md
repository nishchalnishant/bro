# Chapter 10: Principles of minimal access surgery

## Learning Objectives

- Understand the principles and various approaches to minimal access surgery (MAS).
- Grasp the advantages and distinct limitations of MAS techniques compared with open surgery.
- Have a systematic approach to the perioperative assessment and care of these patients.
- Understand the evolution, advantages, and drawbacks of robotic surgery.
- Appreciate novel advances such as augmented reality and artificial intelligence in surgical practice.

---

## 1. Definition and History

**Minimal Access Surgery (MAS)** aims to accomplish traditional surgical therapeutic goals with minimal somatic and psychological trauma. 
- *History:* Experimental laparoscopy was performed in 1901 by Kelling on a dog. The boom in the field occurred post-1987 when Mouret performed the first video-laparoscopic cholecystectomy.

---

## 2. Minimal Access Approaches

1. **Laparoscopy:** Rigid endoscope introduced into the peritoneal cavity (requiring pneumoperitoneum).
2. **Thoracoscopy (VATS):** Access via intercostal spaces. Relies heavily on double-lumen endotracheal tubes to isolate the operative lung. Does not typically require high-pressure gas insufflation.
3. **Single-Incision Laparoscopic Surgery (SILS):** Instrumentation enters via a single multichannel port (usually umbilical). Theoretically 'scarless' but steeper learning curve, less triangulation, and debatable clinical superiority.
4. **Endoluminal/Natural Orifice Surgery (NOTES):** Rigid or flexible scopes accessing through natural orifices (e.g., Transanal Endoscopic Microsurgery). NOTES aims for totally scarless transgastric/transcolonic surgery, but safe physiological closure of the pierced viscus remains challenging.
5. **Perivisceral Endoscopy:** Creation of working spaces where no natural cavity exists. E.g., Retroperitoneoscopy (kidney, aorta), Subfascial Endoscopic Perforator Surgery (veins).
6. **Arthroscopy:** Minimal access inside joint spaces (Knee, shoulder, wrist, etc.).
7. **Hybrid Approaches:** 
   - *Totally Endoscopic:* Synthesizing two modalities (e.g., laparo-endoscopic colon polyp resection) or utilizing navigational marking prior to minimally invasive resection.
   - *Open and Endoscopic (HALS):* Hand-Assisted Laparoscopic Surgery utilizes a hand port to allow tactile feedback and blunt dissection while maintaining pneumoperitoneum. Rapidly resolving due to better total-laparoscopic skills.

---

## 3. Surgical Trauma and Advantages of MAS

The bulk of the trauma in open surgery arises from creating adequate exposure (incisions and rigid retractors). 

**Advantages of MAS:**
- Dramatically diminished wound size and associated wound pain.
- Reduction in systemic wound complications (infection, dehiscence, incisional herniation).
- Improved rapid mobility (decreasing atelectasis and DVT risks).
- Reduced internal heat and evaporative fluid loss.
- Decreased mechanical bowel handling minimizes adynamic ileus and long-term adhesions.
- Magnified, highly-illuminated surgical view.

---

## 4. Limitations of Standard Straight-Stick MAS

1. **Lack of 3D Vision:** Standard monitors are 2D, removing natural depth perception and steepening the learning curve.
2. **Increased Operative Time:** Complex instrument maneuvers take longer, potentially worsening intraoperative risks. Conversion to 'open' is a sign of *sound judgment*, not a complication.
3. **Haemostasis Challenges:** Small pools of blood heavily absorb light and obscure vision. Control requires high coordination and specific energy devices.
4. **Loss of Tactile Feedback (Haptics):** Inability to simply 'feel' for pulses, tumor borders, or distinct tissue tension. Laparoscopic Ultrasound (LUS) aims to mitigate this functionally.
5. **Tissue Extraction:** Malignant specimens require intact extraction to prevent seeding (ensured by fetching in specialized endoscopic specimen bags), negating some elements of minimal access if the extraction wound needs large extension.

## 5. Robotic Surgery

Robots create a human-machine interface combining computational advantages with manual control. Currently dominated by **Teleoperated (Master-Slave)** systems like the *da Vinci Xi/SP*, *Versius*, *Senhance*, and *Hugo*.

### 5.1 Advantages of Robotic Systems
- **3D High-Definition Vision:** Provides true stereoscopic depth perception via the surgical console.
- **Manoeuvrability and Dexterity:** Instruments possess an 'EndoWrist' simulating seven degrees of freedom.
- **Motion Scaling and Tremor Suppression:** The computer translates large external hand sweeps into precise internal micromovements while filtering out physiological tremor.
- **Ergonomics:** The surgeon sits comfortably at a dedicated console, radically reducing physical fatigue specifically in demanding or lengthy/deep-pelvis cases.

### 5.2 Disadvantages
- Exorbitant initial capital purchase and ongoing consumable costs.
- Bulky equipment heavily occupies theater space.
- Complete blockade of tactile feedback for the console surgeon.
- Prolonged learning curve and setup/docking times.

## 6. Perioperative Planning 

### 6.1 Preparation
- **History/Examination:** Assess cardiopulmonary fitness due to the physiological impact of pneumoperitoneum and steep positioning. Severe COPD or recent ischemia may contraindicate MAS. Check for previous abdominal surgical scars (adhesions).
- **Prophylaxis:** Increased venous stasis (e.g., from pneumoperitoneum and reverse Trendelenburg positioning) mandates meticulous VTE prophylaxis (LMWH, pneumatic calf compression).
- **Consent:** Patients must be counseled that MAS procedures explicitly carry the risk of *conversion to open surgery*. Emphasize specific risks like inadvertent organ/vessel injury and postoperative shoulder-tip pain.

---

## 7. General Intraoperative Principles & Problems

- **Access and Adhesions:** Redo surgeries require safe open (Hasson) entry techniques. Adhesions are teased away visually.
- **Vascular Injury (Surgical Emergency):** Usually caused during initial trocar entry or dissecting major vessels. Control with grasp pressure or adjacent viscera immediately. Call for help, consider extra ports, or execute an emergency *conversion to open* if control feels unattainable. In robotic cases, the bedside assistant must be adept at rapidly un-docking the robot to allow the primary surgeon access.
- **Trocar Site Bleeding:** Frequently masks as persistent dripping. Can be resolved post-procedure using mass figure-of-eight transfascial closure sutures or temporarily stented by inserting and inflating a Foley catheter.
- **Electrosurgery Hazards:** Monopolar diathermy is the main culprit due to insulation failure, direct coupling, or capacitive coupling. Bipolar energy or ultrasonic shears are safer alternatives in narrow anatomical pockets.

---

## 8. Postoperative Care

The recovery pathway aims for early mobilization and oral intake (Enhanced Recovery After Surgery - ERAS protocols).

- **Nausea:** Very common (up to 50%). Opiate sparing regimens and antiemetics (Ondansetron) are best.
- **Shoulder-Tip Pain:** Referred pain from diaphragmatic irritation by residual $CO_{2}$ gas or blood. Highest at 24 hours, relieved by simple analgesics like paracetamol.
- **Analgesia:** Avoid heavy opioids where possible to prevent nausea and ileus. Use local anesthetic port-site infiltration and routine Paracetamol / NSAIDs.
- **Oral Intake & Mobility:** Patients can usually mobilize and tolerate light diet within 4-6 hours post-operatively in non-gut anastomotic operations.

## 9. Further Developments in MAS

### 9.1 Augmented Reality (AR)
Fusing computerized images onto real-time surgical fields.
- **Indocyanine Green (ICG) Fluorescence:** Under near-infrared light, ICG allows surgeons to clearly visualize biliary anatomy, evaluate bowel perfusion, or map lymphatic sentinel nodes. Easily integrated into standard HD and Robotic cameras.
- **Navigational Bronchoscopy / Thoracoscopy:** Reconstructed 3D CT maps act as GPS to guide biopsy of small distal lung nodules or place fiducial markers for subsequent robotic resection.

### 9.2 Artificial Intelligence
AI platforms tracking surgical workflow hold the future potential to warn of deviating techniques, identify at-risk anatomy via deep learning, provide real-time metabolic tissue profiling (e.g., *iKnife* using REIMS), and augment ultra-realistic dynamic simulator training.
