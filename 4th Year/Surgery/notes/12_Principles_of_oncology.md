# Chapter 12: Principles of oncology

## Learning Objectives

- Understand the biological hallmarks and nature of cancer.
- Appreciate the major causative factors: genetic predisposition vs. environmental exposure.
- Grasp the multidisciplinary management of cancer encompassing prevention, screening, and active treatment.
- Understand the principles behind surgical, radiotherapeutic, and systemic therapies.
- Distinguish between curative intent, palliative care, and end-of-life care.

---

## 1. What is Cancer?

### 1.1 History and Cellular Origin
Coined by Hippocrates based on the crab-like extensions of tumors. Rudolf Virchow established that cancer is a disease of cells (*omnes cellula e cellula*). Modern oncology hinges on understanding the DNA level mutations first conceptualized after Watson and Crick.

### 1.2 The Hallmarks of Cancer (Hanahan & Weinberg)
Cancer cells must acquire a series of characteristics to survive, divide, and spread:
1. **Establish an autonomous lineage:** Escaping normal physiological checks. Mutation of proto-oncogenes into active **oncogenes** drives unremitting cell proliferation. Multiple mutations in key 'driver' genes are almost universally required (e.g., the classic Vogelstein colorectal adenoma-carcinoma sequence).
2. **Obtain replicative immortality:** Normal cells undergo finite divisions due to telomere shortening (Hayflick limit). Cancer cells exploit **telomerase** to rebuild telomeres, achieving immortality.
3. **Evade apoptosis:** Inactivating tumor suppressor genes like *p53* allows malignant cells to evade programmed cellular self-destruction.
4. **Acquire angiogenic competence:** A tumor cannot grow beyond 1 mm without constructing its own blood supply (angiogenesis) via secreted growth factors like VEGF.
5. **Acquire ability to invade:** Chemical degradation of the stromal basement membrane (collagenases, proteases) and cellular decoupling (Epithelial-Mesenchymal Transition - EMT) allows cells to slip into local tissues.
6. **Disseminate and implant:** Crossing the endothelium into circulation. The 'Seed and Soil' hypothesis (Paget) dictates that metastases only develop when a circulating tumor cell finds a distant tissue bed highly conducive to its growth.
7. **Evade detection/elimination:** Tumors downregulate immune markers or directly inhibit T-cell activation to evade destruction by the host's immune system.
8. **Change energy metabolism (Warburg Effect):** Cancer cells bypass normal mitochondrial oxidative phosphorylation, relying heavily on less efficient aerobic glycolysis to produce lactate, aiding survival in hypoxic tumor microenvironments.

### 1.3 Tumor Growth Kinetics
Tumors follow **Gompertzian growth**: Early growth is aggressive and exponential, slowing down substantially as the tumor mass outgrows its vascular supply and internal competition increases. Thirty doublings ($10^9$ cells) produce a clinically palpable 1 cm tumor.

## 2. The Causes of Cancer: Nature vs Nurture

### 2.1 Environmental Causes
Highly pervasive risk factors requiring aggressive public health intervention:
- **Tobacco:** Causes 80% of lung cancers and strongly associated with head/neck and bladder cancers.
- **Viral / Bacterial:** HPV (Cervical, Anal, Head/Neck), EBV (Lymphoma), HBV/HCV (Hepatocellular Carcinoma), *H. pylori* (Gastric cancer).
- **Physical/Chemical:** UV radiation (Melanoma), ionizing radiation (Leukaemia/Thyroid), Asbestos (Mesothelioma).

### 2.2 Inherited Cancer Syndromes
Highly penetrant germline mutations mandating familial surveillance and prophylactic surgeries:
- **FAP (APC gene):** Total penetrance for colorectal cancer; indicates prophylactic panproctocolectomy.
- **Lynch Syndrome/HNPCC (MMR genes):** Colorectal and endometrial cancers.
- **BRCA 1 & 2:** Severely elevates lifetime risk of Breast and Ovarian cancers.
- **RET (MEN 2A/2B):** Medullary thyroid carcinomas, phaeochromocytomas.
- **TP53 (Li-Fraumeni):** Devastating early-age sarcomas, breast cancers, brain tumors, and leukaemias.

## 3. The Management of Cancer

Management lies along a continuum:
Prevention $\rightarrow$ Screening $\rightarrow$ Diagnosis $\rightarrow$ Staging $\rightarrow$ Treatment $\rightarrow$ Palliation / Rehabilitation.

### 3.1 Screening
Aims to detect disease in an asymptomatic population to improve overall survival by treating earlier stage disease. 
**Crucial Testing Biases:**
- *Lead time bias:* Artefactual inflation of survival time simply because the disease was detected earlier, without actually delaying the eventual time of death.
- *Length bias:* Screening naturally favors finding slow-growing indolent tumors, as aggressive fast-growing variants arise and present symptomatically entirely *between* screening rounds.
- *Selection bias:* Healthier, more affluent people usually volunteer for screening programs.

### 3.2 Diagnosis and Staging
Accurate histological diagnosis and grade (e.g., Gleason Score in prostate cancer) combined with exact anatomical mapping via the **TNM Classification system** (Tumor, Nodes, Metastases) and cross-sectional imaging (CT / MRI / PET).
Emerging staging technologies include detecting circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) to precisely identify postoperative minimal residual disease.

### 3.3 The Multidisciplinary Team (MDT)
A consensus-driven panel ensuring the patient's pathology, radiology, surgical readiness, and oncological plan align strictly with best practice guidelines, avoiding idiosyncratic single-doctor management.

## 4. Modalities of Treatment

### 4.1 Principles of Cancer Surgery
- **Diagnosis/Staging:** Laparoscopy for occult peritoneal spread, sentinel lymph node biopsies.
- **Cure (Primary removal):** Meticulous total excision of the primary with draining lymphatics.
- **Metastasectomy:** Resection of isolated oligo-metastases (e.g., liver resection for CRC, lung resection for renal cell carcinoma) can be curative.
- **Palliation:** Bypass procedures or cytoreduction to eliminate pain/bleeding despite uncontrolled distant disease.

### 4.2 Radiotherapy
Ionizing radiation creates oxygen free radicals that induce devastating single and double-stranded DNA breaks. Works best against dividing cells and requires adequate tissue oxygenation (hypoxic cores resist radiation). Administered as external beam, Intensity Modulated Radiation Therapy (IMRT), stereotactic targeted regimens, or internal Brachytherapy.

### 4.3 Systemic Anti-Cancer Therapies
1. **Cytotoxic Chemotherapy:** Exploits the fraction of rapidly dividing cells (selective toxicity). Employs drugs like spindle poisons (Taxanes), antimetabolites (5-FU), cross-linkers (Cisplatin), and topoisomerase inhibitors. 
2. **Hormonal Treatments:** Starving hormone-sensitive tumors (Tamoxifen / Aromatase inhibitors in breast cancer; androgen deprivation in prostate).
3. **Targeted Treatments ('Personalized Medicine'):** Directly interrupting known molecular drivers. Highly mutation specific. (e.g., *Imatinib* inhibiting mutated c-KIT in GIST; *Vemurafenib* inhibiting BRAF V600E in melanoma).
4. **Immunotherapy / Checkpoint Inhibitors:** Stripping the tumor's invisible cloak by blocking 'off-switches' on host T-Cells (PD-1 inhibitors like Pembrolizumab). Can precipitate severe auto-immune side effects (colitis, hypophysitis).

*Combined Treatment Principles:* Using synergistic agents with distinct physical modes of action and strictly non-overlapping toxicities to stamp out nascent resistant tumor clones safely.

## 5. Oncological Emergencies
- **Spinal Cord Compression:** Requires immediate steroids and urgent radiotherapy/neurosurgical decompression.
- **Neutropenic Sepsis:** Immediate, aggressive broad-spectrum intravenous antibiotics.
- **Severe Immune Side-Effects:** Endocrine/pulmonary acute failure secondary to immunotherapy.

---

## 6. Palliation and End of Life
- **Palliative Care:** Long-term symptom relief throughout what can be years of chronic cancer care (managing effusions, preventing bone fractures with bisphosphonates, rigorous pain management, nutrition, psychological support). 
- **End-of-Life Care:** Distinctly handles the final months/weeks emphasizing comfort, family support, and spirituality ensuring a 'good death'.
