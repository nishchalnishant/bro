## DAY 1 — Pre-Clinical Core

### Anatomy
- Radial nerve → wrist drop; axillary nerve (surgical neck humerus) → deltoid loss; common peroneal → foot drop
- Meckel's: **2%**, 2 feet from IC valve, 2 inches long, 2:1 M:F, presents <2 yrs
- Erb's palsy C5-C6 (waiter's tip) vs Klumpke's C8-T1 (claw + Horner's)
- Portal vein = SMV + splenic vein, behind **neck of pancreas**
- Left RLN loops around **arch of aorta**; Right RLN around **subclavian artery**

### Physiology
- Phase 2 plateau = Ca²⁺ influx = CCB target; Phase 0 = rapid Na⁺ influx
- GFR 125 mL/min; RPF 650 mL/min; FF **20%**
- P50 = 26 mmHg; 2,3-DPG ↑ → right shift → ↓O₂ affinity (more O₂ release)
- Obstructive: FEV1/FVC <70%, TLC↑ / Restrictive: FEV1/FVC >80%, TLC↓
- Cushing reflex = HTN + bradycardia + irregular breathing = **raised ICP**

### Biochemistry
- Gaucher's = glucocerebrosidase ↓ (most common LSd) / Niemann-Pick = sphingomyelinase ↓ (cherry red spot) / Tay-Sachs = Hex-A ↓ (cherry red, **no HSM**)
- PKU = phenylalanine hydroxylase ↓; Alkaptonuria = homogentisic acid oxidase ↓ (dark urine, ochronosis)
- PFK-1 = rate-limiting enzyme of glycolysis
- HbA1c measures glycemic control over **last 3 months**
- Diabetes: fasting ≥126 mg/dL; random ≥200 mg/dL

### Pathology
- Virchow's triad: stasis + hypercoagulability + endothelial injury → DVT
- Reed-Sternberg cells (owl-eye) = **Hodgkin's lymphoma**
- Psammoma bodies: papillary thyroid Ca, meningioma, serous cystadenoCA ovary, mesothelioma
- Ghon focus = primary TB (subpleural, lower upper lobe); Ranke complex = Ghon + hilar node calcification
- Amyloid: Congo red stain → **apple-green birefringence** under polarized light

## DAY 2 — Pre-Clinical Applied

### Pharmacology
- Class IA antiarrhythmics: Na⁺ channel blockers + ↑refractory period (quinidine, procainamide, disopyramide)
- Succinylcholine CI: hyperkalemia, burns, crush injury, denervation; antidote for **organophosphate** = atropine + pralidoxime
- ACE inhibitors CI in bilateral renal artery stenosis; cause cough (bradykinin) → switch to ARB
- Warfarin reversal: Vit K (slow) + FFP (fast); reversal for heparin: **protamine sulfate**
- Most teratogenic: thalidomide (flipper limbs), valproate (NTDs), isotretinoin (cardiac/CNS); all are Category X

### Microbiology
- Gram +ve cocci in clusters = Staph; chains = Strep; pairs = Pneumococcus
- Ghon focus (primary TB), cavitation (secondary); AFB: Ziehl-Neelsen stain (red on blue)
- Obligate intracellular: Chlamydia, Rickettsia; Obligate aerobe: M. tuberculosis; Anaerobe: Clostridium, Bacteroides
- Widal test: rises from **2nd week** of typhoid fever; positive titre: >1:80 O antigen (in endemic areas)
- VDRL = non-treponemal screening; FTA-ABS = treponemal confirmatory (stays positive for life)

### Forensic Medicine
- Rigor mortis: starts 2–6h, complete 12h, passes 24–48h; **Nysten's law**: starts in jaw, ends in legs
- Hypostasis/lividity: appears 2–4h, fixed 6–12h (can't be moved after fixation)
- McEwen's sign (scaphoid abdomen) in air embolism; **Diatom test**: gold standard to prove drowning
- Vitreous humor: most resistant to decomposition → best for alcohol estimation post-mortem
- Thanatology = study of death; **Autopsy**: medico-legal = coroner's; hospital = clinical

### SPM / Community Medicine
- Sensitivity = TP/(TP+FN); Specificity = TN/(TN+FP); PPV depends on prevalence
- PHC: 1 per 30,000 (plains), 20,000 (hilly); CHC: 1 per **1,00,000**; Anganwadi: 1 per 1,000
- Herd immunity threshold: measles **>92–95%** (highest); polio >80–85%
- MMR: maternal deaths per **1,00,000 live births** (India ~97); IMR: deaths per 1,000 live births (India ~28)
- BCG at birth (intradermal, 0.05–0.1 mL, right arm); Measles at **9 months**

## DAY 3 — Clinical (Medicine + Surgery)

### General Medicine
- CURB-65: Confusion, Urea >7, RR ≥30, BP <90/60, age ≥65; score **≥2** = admit
- Troponin rises **3–6h** in STEMI, peaks 24h, normalizes 7–14 days
- STEMI: ST elevation ≥2 contiguous leads / NSTEMI: troponin +ve, no ST elevation (subendocardial)
- eGFR CKD: G1 ≥90, G2 60–89, G3a 45–59, G3b 30–44, G4 15–29, G5 <15 mL/min/1.73m²
- Hypertensive emergency: BP >180/120 + end-organ damage; lower by **25% in first hour** only
- AEIOU TIPS (coma causes): Alcohol, Epilepsy, Insulin, Opiates, Uremia; Trauma, Infection, Psychiatric, Stroke
- Osler's nodes = painful (immune-complex), infective endocarditis / Janeway = painless (embolic)

### General Surgery
- Courvoisier's law: palpable GB + painless jaundice → NOT stones → **periampullary carcinoma**
- Charcot's triad (cholangitis) + BP↓ + confusion = **Reynold's pentad**
- Ranson's criteria ≥3 = severe pancreatitis; Hartmann's = sigmoid colectomy + end colostomy
- Indirect inguinal hernia: lateral to inferior epigastric, patent processus vaginalis, young males
- AAA surgery: diameter **>5.5 cm** or rapid growth >1 cm/year
- CBD normal: ≤6 mm; post-cholecystectomy up to 8 mm
- Glasgow Coma Scale max = **15** (E4+V5+M6); severe head injury ≤8

## DAY 4 — Clinical (OBG, Pediatrics, Psychiatry)

### OBG
- Preeclampsia = BP ≥140/90 + proteinuria (≥300 mg/24h) after 20 weeks
- Eclampsia Rx: MgSO4 Pritchard — 4g IV + 5g IM each buttock loading; antidote: **calcium gluconate**
- Placenta previa = painless bright-red bleeding; Abruptio = painful, dark blood, woody hard uterus (DO NOT do PV in previa)
- PPH: >500 mL vaginal / >1000 mL CS; most common cause: **uterine atony**; first Rx: oxytocin 10 IU IM
- Bishop score ≥8 = favourable cervix; AFI normal 5–25 cm; oligohydramnios <5 cm
- Down syndrome triple test: ↑hCG, ↓AFP, ↓estriol; GDM screening: 75g OGTT at **24–28 weeks**
- HELLP = Hemolysis + Elevated LFTs + Low Platelets; Rx = **delivery**

### Pediatrics
- Social smile = **6 weeks**; Sits without support = 6 months; Walks = 12–15 months
- Single word with meaning = 1 yr; 2-word sentences = 2 yr; sentences = 3 yr
- Birth weight doubles by **5 months**, triples by 1 yr, quadruples by 2 yr
- Pyloric stenosis: 2–8 weeks, projectile non-bilious vomiting, olive mass, hypochloremic hypokalaemic metabolic **alkalosis**; Rx: Ramstedt's pyloromyotomy
- Kawasaki: fever >5 days + 4/5 criteria; Rx: **IVIG + aspirin** (coronary artery aneurysm risk)
- Croup = barking cough, steeple sign, dexamethasone + nebulized epi / Epiglottitis = H.flu B, thumb sign, tripod posture
- Febrile seizures: 6m–6yr; LP mandatory if <12 months

### Psychiatry
- Schizophrenia: ≥2 symptoms for **≥6 months** (delusions, hallucinations, disorganized speech, negative sx)
- Bipolar I = manic episode ≥7 days; Bipolar II = hypomania + major depression
- Clozapine = treatment-resistant schizophrenia; risk: **agranulocytosis** → weekly WBC
- Lithium: therapeutic 0.6–1.2 mEq/L; toxicity >1.5 (tremor, ataxia, confusion)
- ECT: DOC for severe depression + suicidal ideation, catatonia, treatment-resistant depression
- SIGECAPS for MDD (≥5 for ≥2 weeks): Sleep, Interest, Guilt, Energy, Concentration, Appetite, Psychomotor, Suicidal
- OCD = ego-dystonic (resists obsessions) vs OCPD = ego-syntonic (personality trait, no resistance)

## DAY 5 — Surgical Specialties + Radiology

### Dermatology
- Auspitz sign = psoriasis (capillary loop bleeding); Nikolsky = pemphigus vulgaris + TEN + SSSS
- Wickham's striae = Lichen Planus (4Ps: Purple, Polygonal, Pruritic, Papules)
- Pemphigus vulgaris: intraepidermal, IgG vs desmoglein 3, Nikolsky+, oral lesions first
- Bullous pemphigoid: subepidermal, IgG vs BP180/230, Nikolsky−, elderly
- Leprosy: PB (1–5 patches) → rifampicin + dapsone × 6m; MB (≥6) → RFP + dapsone + clofazimine × 12m
- ABCDE melanoma: Asymmetry, Border, Color, Diameter >6mm, Evolution

### Orthopedics
- Colles' = distal radius, FOOSH, dinner-fork deformity; Scaphoid = anatomical snuffbox tenderness, AVN risk
- Pott's fracture = bimalleolar ankle / Pott's disease = spinal TB (gibbus)
- Looser's zones = osteomalacia (pathognomonic); Salter-Harris V (crush) = worst prognosis
- Compartment syndrome: pressure >30 mmHg → **fasciotomy**
- Trendelenburg gait = gluteus medius weakness; pelvis drops to opposite (normal) side
- Osteoporosis: T-score ≤-2.5, normal mineralization vs Osteomalacia: ↓mineralization, ↑ALP, Looser's zones

### ENT
- Rinne −ve (BC>AC) = conductive HL; Weber lateralizes to **worse ear** in conductive, better ear in SNHL
- Cholesteatoma: pars flaccida perforation, squamous epithelium, bone erosion; Rx: mastoidectomy
- Meniere's: episodic vertigo + fluctuating SNHL + tinnitus + aural fullness (endolymphatic hydrops)
- Epistaxis: 90% anterior (Kiesselbach's/Little's area); 10% posterior (sphenopalatine artery)
- Audiogram: normal <25 dB; profound >90 dB

### Ophthalmology
- POAG: painless, open angle, C/D >0.6; first-line Rx: **timolol** (topical beta-blocker)
- Acute angle-closure: severe pain + haloes + mid-dilated fixed pupil; emergency: IV acetazolamide + pilocarpine; definitive: **laser iridotomy**
- CRAO = cherry red spot (sudden painless vision loss) vs CRVO = blood-and-thunder fundus
- Diabetic retinopathy PDR = neovascularization; Rx: **pan-retinal photocoagulation**
- Trachoma (Chlamydia) = most common **preventable** blindness worldwide; Cataract = most common **treatable** blindness in India

### Anesthesia + Radiology
- Ketamine = dissociative, maintains airway + BP; DOC for **hemorrhagic shock** induction
- Malignant hyperthermia: succinylcholine/volatile agents + ryanodine receptor mutation; Rx: **dantrolene**
- Spinal (into CSF, L3–L4, fast dense block) vs Epidural (outside dura, catheter, slow onset)
- Ground-glass opacity on HRCT: pulmonary edema, PCP, COVID-19
- Coffee bean sign = sigmoid volvulus; Double bubble = duodenal atresia; String sign of Kantor = Crohn's
- Subdural = crescent-shaped, crosses sutures (bridging veins) / Epidural = biconvex, does NOT cross sutures (MMA)
- CT head without contrast = **first** investigation for intracranial hemorrhage
