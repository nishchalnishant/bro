## Growth and Development

### Anthropometric Milestones

#### Weight
| Age | Weight Gain | Milestone |
|-----|------------|-----------|
| 0-6 months | **700g/month** | — |
| 6-12 months | **500g/month** | — |
| 5 months | — | **Birth weight doubles** |
| 1 year | — | **Birth weight triples** (~9-10 kg) |
| 2 years | — | **Birth weight quadruples** (~12 kg) |
| 3-10 years | 2 kg/year | — |

- Average birth weight: **2.8-3.2 kg** (WHO reference)

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Birth weight doubles at 5 months; triples at 1 year; quadruples at 2 years; birth weight formula >1 year = Age (years) × 2 + 8 kg**

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Height at 1 year ≈ 75 cm (birth 50 cm + 25 cm in year 1); head circumference at birth = 33-35 cm; HC = chest circumference at 6-9 months**

#### Height
- Birth: ~50 cm
- **+25 cm in first year** (75 cm at 1 year)
- +12 cm in second year (~87 cm)
- +6 cm/year from age 3-10 years

#### Head Circumference
- At birth: **33-35 cm** (average 34 cm)
- **+2 cm/month for first 3 months** → 40 cm at 3 months
- +1 cm/month for months 4-6
- +0.5 cm/month for months 7-12
- 47 cm at 1 year; 48-49 cm at 2 years

### Developmental Milestones

| Age | Gross Motor | Fine Motor | Language | Social |
|-----|------------|------------|---------|--------|
| **3 months** | Head control (prone lifts head 45°), diminishing Moro | Fisted hand opens, swipes at objects | Cooing, social smile | **Social smile (6 weeks onset)** |
| **6 months** | Rolls, sits with support, bears weight | Palmar grasp, transfers hand-to-hand | Babbling ("ba, da, ga") | Stranger anxiety begins |
| **9 months** | Crawls, stands with support, pulls to stand | **Pincer grasp developing** (inferior pincer) | "Mama/dada" nonspecific | Separation anxiety, waves bye-bye |
| **12 months** | Walks with one hand held, cruising | **Neat pincer grasp**, releases | "Mama/dada" specific, 1-2 words | Points, gives objects |
| **18 months** | Walks independently, runs (broad-based), walks upstairs | Tower of **3 cubes**, scribbles | **10 words**, follows 2-step commands | Domestic mimicry |
| **24 months** | Runs well, kicks ball, walks upstairs 2 feet/step | Tower of **6 cubes** | **50+ words, 2-word phrases** | Parallel play |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Social smile = 6 weeks; head control = 3-4 months; sits without support = 6-7 months; pincer grasp = 9-10 months; walks independently = 12-15 months**

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Language milestones — 1 word meaningful = 12 months; 2-word phrases = 24 months; 50+ words = 24 months; follows 2-step commands = 18 months**

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Tower of cubes — 2 cubes = 15 months; 3 cubes = 18 months; 6 cubes = 2 years; 9-10 cubes = 3 years; draw circle = 3 years; draw cross = 4 years**

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Social smile = 6 weeks; Pincer grasp = 9-10 months; 50 words + 2-word phrases = 2 years; follows 2-step commands = 18 months

### Denver Developmental Screening Test (DDST-II)
- Tests 125 items across 4 domains: Personal-social, Fine motor-adaptive, Language, Gross motor
- **Delay**: Failure to achieve item achieved by 90% of age peers
- **Caution**: Failure to achieve item achieved by 75-90%

### Developmental Red Flags
- No social smile by **3 months**
- No head control by **4 months**
- Not sitting independently by **9 months**
- Not standing by **12 months**
- Not walking by **18 months**
- No words by **18 months**
- No 2-word phrases by **24 months**
- Any loss of previously acquired skills at any age (regression = urgent evaluation)

### Universal Immunization Programme (UIP) Schedule — India

| Age | Vaccines |
|-----|---------|
| **Birth** | **BCG** (0.1 mL intradermal left deltoid), **OPV-0**, **HepB-0** (within 24 hours) |
| **6 weeks** | DPT-1, IPV-1, Hib-1, PCV-1, Rotavirus-1, HepB-1 |
| **10 weeks** | DPT-2, IPV-2, Hib-2, PCV-2, Rotavirus-2, HepB-2 |
| **14 weeks** | DPT-3, IPV-3, Hib-3, PCV-3, Rotavirus-3 |
| **9 months** | **MR** (measles-rubella), **JE-1** (endemic areas) |
| **16-24 months** | DPT booster-1, OPV booster, MR-2, JE-2, Hep A-2 |
| **5-6 years** | DPT booster-2 |
| **10 years** | TT/Td |
| **16 years** | TT/Td |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: BCG = birth, intradermal, LEFT arm; OPV-0 and HepB-0 = within 24 hours of birth; MR = 9 months; MMR not in NIS (India) — MR only in NIS**

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Measles vaccine age = 9 months (NIS India); MMR = 12-15 months (IAP private); Varicella = 15 months (IAP); Hep A = 2 doses at 12 months and 18 months (IAP)**

### Cold Chain
- **Freeze-sensitive vaccines** (must NOT freeze): DPT, TT, HepB, Hib, PCV, IPV, Rabies
- **Freeze-tolerant vaccines**: OPV, BCG, MMR, Rotavirus, Varicella
- Optimal temperature: **+2°C to +8°C** (refrigerator)
- OPV: Stored at -20°C (freezer) or +2 to +8°C

### Key AEFI (Adverse Events Following Immunization)
| Vaccine | AEFI |
|---------|------|
| **BCG** | Keloid, lymphadenitis, BCG-itis (disseminated in immunocompromised) |
| **DPT** | Fever, local redness (common); **Febrile convulsions**, anaphylaxis, encephalopathy (rare) |
| **OPV** | **VAPP** (Vaccine-Associated Paralytic Polio): 1 in 750,000 first doses |
| **MMR/MR** | Rash, fever, mild thrombocytopenia; febrile seizure (5-12 days post-MMR) |
| **Rotavirus** | **Intussusception** (slight increased risk, especially Rotashield — withdrawn) |

### Contraindications to Vaccination
- **BCG**: Immunocompromised (HIV with CD4 <200, SCID, steroid therapy)
- **Live vaccines** (MMR, OPV, BCG, Rotavirus, Varicella): Immunodeficiency, pregnancy
- **DPT**: Previous anaphylaxis to component; encephalopathy within 7 days of previous dose

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: OPV contraindicated in immunocompromised; BCG at birth left arm intradermal; VAPP = OPV

## Neonatology

### APGAR Score

| Parameter | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|-----------|---|---|---|
| **A**ppearance (color) | Blue/pale all over | Body pink, extremities blue | Pink all over |
| **P**ulse (heart rate) | Absent | <100 bpm | ≥100 bpm |
| **G**rimace (reflex irritability) | No response | Grimace | Cry/cough/sneeze |
| **A**ctivity (muscle tone) | Limp | Some flexion | Active motion |
| **R**espiration | Absent | Weak/irregular | Good, crying |

- Assessed at **1 minute** (resuscitation need) and **5 minutes** (outcome)
- Score 7-10: Normal; 4-6: Moderate asphyxia; 0-3: Severe asphyxia

### Birth Asphyxia — NRP Algorithm
1. **Initial steps** (30 seconds): Warm, dry, stimulate, position, suction if secretions (not routine)
2. **Evaluation** at 30 seconds: Breathing + HR
3. **Positive Pressure Ventilation (PPV)**: If apneic OR HR <100 bpm — 40-60 breaths/min, FiO2 21% (room air) for term
4. **Chest compressions**: If HR <60 after 30 seconds of PPV — 3:1 ratio (3 compressions : 1 breath), 100 events/minute
5. **Medications**: HR <60 after 45-60 seconds of compressions — Epinephrine 0.1-0.3 mL/kg of 1:10,000 IV/IO OR 0.5-1 mL/kg via ETT

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: PPV if HR <100; chest compressions if HR <60; room air for term neonates

### Neonatal Jaundice

**Physiological vs Pathological:**
| Feature | Physiological | Pathological |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Onset | >24 hours | **<24 hours** (always pathological) |
| Duration | <7 days (term), <14 days (preterm) | Prolonged (>2 weeks term, >3 weeks preterm) |
| Peak bilirubin | <12 mg/dL (term), <15 mg/dL (preterm) | Higher |
| Direct bilirubin | <2 mg/dL or <20% of total | Elevated direct = cholestasis |
| Rate of rise | <5 mg/dL/day | >5 mg/dL/day |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Jaundice <24 hours = always pathological (think hemolytic disease); jaundice >2 weeks term = always investigate (think hypothyroidism, biliary atresia)**

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Physiological jaundice peak = day 3-5 term, day 5-7 preterm; Kramer Zone 5 (palms and soles) ≥20 mg/dL = exchange transfusion level**

**Kramer's Zones (clinical estimation):**
- Zone 1: Face/forehead → ~5 mg/dL
- Zone 2: Neck/upper trunk → ~10 mg/dL
- Zone 3: Lower trunk/thighs → ~12-14 mg/dL
- Zone 4: Arms/lower legs → ~15 mg/dL
- Zone 5: Palms/soles → **≥20 mg/dL** (danger zone)

**Treatment Thresholds** (use Bhutani nomogram):
- **Phototherapy**: >95th percentile on nomogram (approximately >12-15 mg/dL in term, earlier in preterm)
- **Exchange transfusion**: TSB ≥25 mg/dL (term), lower for preterm/sick; or after failed phototherapy with rapid rise

**Hemolytic Disease of Newborn:**
- **ABO incompatibility**: Mother O + Baby A or B; DAT weakly positive; less severe; responds to phototherapy
- **Rh disease (Erythroblastosis fetalis)**: Rh-negative mother + Rh-positive baby; **Anti-D prophylaxis** (300 mcg at 28 weeks + within 72h of delivery); hydrops fetalis in severe cases

### Neonatal Sepsis
| Feature | Early-onset (<72h) | Late-onset (>72h) |
|---------|-------------------|--------------------|
| Organisms | **GBS, E. coli**, Listeria | **Coagulase-negative Staph (CONS)**, Klebsiella, Enterococcus |
| Source | Maternal genitourinary | Nosocomial, environment |
| Presentation | Respiratory distress, shock | Subtle — poor feeding, temperature instability |
| Treatment | **Ampicillin + Gentamicin** | **Vancomycin + Gentamicin** (nosocomial) |

- **Gold standard diagnosis**: Blood culture
- Risk factors: Prolonged ROM (>18h), maternal fever, GBS positive, preterm, invasive procedures
- Sepsis screen: CRP, CBC (neutropenia worse than neutrophilia), I:T ratio >0.2, micro-ESR

### Respiratory Distress Syndrome (RDS/HMD)

- **Cause**: **Surfactant deficiency** (type II pneumocytes produce surfactant — predominantly phosphatidylcholine/DPPC)
- **Risk**: Preterm (<36 weeks), maternal DM (hyperinsulinemia delays surfactant), male sex, C-section (no labor hormones)
- **CXR**: Ground-glass opacity, air bronchograms, low lung volumes

**Treatment:**
- **CPAP** (nasal continuous positive airway pressure): First-line respiratory support
- **Surfactant replacement** (beractant/poractant alfa): Intratracheal via ETT; rescue or prophylactic
- **Prevention**: Antenatal corticosteroids (24-34 weeks)

### Necrotizing Enterocolitis (NEC)
- **Bell's staging**: Stage I (suspect), Stage II (definite), Stage III (advanced — perforation)
- **CXR findings**: **Pneumatosis intestinalis** (gas in bowel wall — pathognomonic), portal venous gas, pneumoperitoneum
- **Risk factors**: Prematurity, formula feeding, hypoxia, polycythemia
- **Medical management**: NPO, nasogastric decompression, IV antibiotics (piperacillin-tazobactam/meropenem), TPN
- **Surgical indications**: Perforation, clinical deterioration, pneumoperitoneum

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Pneumatosis intestinalis = NEC; <24h jaundice = always pathological; GBS = early neonatal sepsis

### Congenital Infections (TORCH)

| Infection | Key Features | Diagnosis |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| **T**oxoplasma | Chorioretinitis, hydrocephalus, **intracranial calcifications (diffuse)**, purpura | Sabin-Feldman dye test, PCR |
| **R**ubella | PDA, pulmonary stenosis, cataract, **"salt and pepper" retinopathy**, deafness, "blueberry muffin" rash | IgM |
| **C**MV | Most common congenital infection; **periventricular calcifications**, sensorineural deafness, microcephaly | CMV PCR urine in first 3 weeks |
| **H**SV | Skin/eye/mouth + encephalitis + disseminated; **temporal lobe encephalitis** | HSV PCR CSF |
| **Syphilis** | Snuffles, periostitis, saddle nose, Hutchinson's triad (keratitis/deafness/notched teeth) | VDRL, FTA-ABS |
| **HIV** | Recurrent infections, FTT, lymphadenopathy | PCR (not antibody — maternal antibody persists 18 months) |

### Neonatal Hypoglycemia
- **Definition**: Blood glucose <**45 mg/dL** in term; <**40 mg/dL** in preterm
- **At-risk**: IDM (hyperinsulinism), SGA, preterm, polycythemia, sepsis
- **Treatment**:
  - Asymptomatic: Feeding (breast/formula) + monitoring
  - Symptomatic/severe: IV **10% dextrose 2 mL/kg bolus** → maintenance 6-8 mg/kg/min GIR
  - Persistent: Hydrocortisone, glucagon, diazoxide (hyperinsulinism)

## Respiratory Conditions

### Bronchiolitis
- **Most common cause**: **RSV (Respiratory Syncytial Virus)** — peak Nov-March
- Age: <2 years; peak <6 months
- Features: URTI prodrome → **expiratory wheeze**, crackles, tachypnea, subcostal recession
- **CXR**: Hyperinflation (flattened diaphragm), peribronchial thickening
- **Management**: **Supportive** — oxygen (target SpO2 ≥92%), adequate hydration; NG feeds if unable to breastfeed
- **Do NOT use**: Bronchodilators (salbutamol), steroids, antibiotics, nebulized adrenaline (in community) — NICE guideline
- Indication for hospital: SpO2 <92%, apneas, poor feeding, signs of respiratory failure

**PYQ HOTSPOT: RSV bronchiolitis = commonest in <6 months; treatment = supportive only; ribavirin reserved for immunocompromised/severe cases only (NOT routine)**

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Bronchiolitis vs asthma — bronchiolitis first episode <2 years, no response to bronchodilators; recurrent wheeze >2 episodes in child >2 years = asthma**

### Pneumonia — WHO Classification

**Fast Breathing Thresholds (by age):**
| Age | Fast Breathing Cutoff |
|-----|----------------------|
| **<2 months** | ≥**60 breaths/min** |
| **2-12 months** | ≥**50 breaths/min** |
| **1-5 years** | ≥**40 breaths/min** |

**WHO Classification:**
- Fast breathing: Mild non-severe pneumonia → oral amoxicillin (3 days)
- Chest indrawing: Severe → hospitalise + IV ampicillin + gentamicin
- Danger signs (central cyanosis, severe respiratory distress, unable to drink, convulsions): Very severe

### Croup vs Epiglottitis

| Feature | Croup (LTB) | Epiglottitis |
|---------|------------|--------------|
| Cause | **Parainfluenza** virus | **H. influenzae type b** (Hib) |
| Age | 6 months-3 years | 2-7 years |
| Onset | Gradual | **Rapid** (hours) |
| Fever | Low-grade | **High fever** |
| Stridor | **Inspiratory** ± biphasic | Inspiratory |
| Voice | Barking cough | **Muffled "hot potato" voice** |
| Drooling | No | **Yes** |
| Position | Any | **Tripod** (lean forward, hands on knees) |
| X-ray | **Steeple sign** (subglottic narrowing) | **Thumb sign** (enlarged epiglottis) |
| Throat exam | Can examine | **NEVER examine** throat (may cause complete obstruction) |
| Treatment | Nebulized adrenaline + **dexamethasone** | Airway first (intubation), IV ceftriaxone |

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Do NOT examine throat in epiglottitis; steeple sign = croup; thumb sign = epiglottitis

## Nutrition

### Breastfeeding
- **WHO recommendation**: Exclusive breastfeeding for **6 months** + continue breastfeeding + complementary feeding until **2 years**
- **Colostrum**: First milk (days 1-3); rich in **IgA**, lactoferrin, macrophages, less fat/lactose
- **Breast milk vs Formula**:
  - Breast milk: Higher whey:casein ratio (60:40), more IgA, lactoferrin, HMOs, bioavailable iron, less sodium
  - Formula: Higher casein, added vitamins/minerals

### Complementary Feeding
- Start at **6 months** (not before 4 months)
- Caloric density: 100 kcal/kg/day from 6-12 months; introduce one food at a time
- First foods: Iron-rich (pureed meat, fortified cereals), soft fruits/vegetables

### Protein-Energy Malnutrition

| Feature | SAM (Severe) | MAM (Moderate) |
|---------|-------------|----------------|
| MUAC | **<115 mm** | **115-124 mm** |
| WHZ (Weight-for-height Z-score) | **<-3 SD** | -3 to -2 SD |
| Bilateral pitting edema | Presence = SAM (kwashiorkor) | — |

**Types of SAM:**
- **Marasmus**: Wasting, baggy pants appearance, monkey face, no edema, BMR low
- **Kwashiorkor**: Edema, moon face, flag sign (depigmented hair bands), fatty liver, hypoalbuminemia
- **Marasmic-Kwashiorkor**: Mixed

**Treatment of SAM (10 steps + 2 phases):**
- **Stabilization phase** (days 1-7): **F-75 milk** (75 kcal/100mL), treat infections/hypoglycemia/hypothermia
- **Rehabilitation phase** (weeks 2-6): **F-100 milk** (100 kcal/100mL) or RUTF, then catch-up growth
- **RUTF (Ready-to-Use Therapeutic Food)**: Plumpy'nut — peanut-based, community management

### Micronutrient Supplementation

| Micronutrient | Dose | Schedule |
|--------------|------|---------|
| **Vitamin A** | 100,000 IU <12 months; 200,000 IU ≥12 months | At 9 months + 16-18 months + whenever measles/acute illness |
| **Iron** (prophylaxis) | Term: 1 mg/kg/day from 6 months; Preterm: 2-3 mg/kg/day from 2 months | |
| **Vitamin D** | 400 IU/day from 2 weeks of age | Continue if not sun-exposed; for breastfed infants |
| **Zinc** | 10 mg/day (<6 months: 5 mg/day) × 10-14 days | Added to ORS for diarrhea |

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: MUAC <115 = SAM; F-75 in stabilization phase; F-100 in rehabilitation

## GI Emergencies in Pediatrics

### Intussusception
- **Peak age**: **6-9 months** (range 3 months - 3 years)
- **Type**: **Ileo-colic** (ileocecal valve area — most common)
- **Triad**: Episodic colicky abdominal pain + **currant jelly stool** (blood-stained mucus) + **sausage-shaped mass** in RUQ/epigastrium
- **Pathophysiology**: Proximal bowel telescopes into distal; lymphoid hyperplasia (Peyer's patches) acts as lead point in idiopathic; in older children → rule out polyp/lymphoma as lead point
- **Investigations**:
  - **USG**: **Target sign** (concentric rings, "doughnut sign") — diagnostic
  - Barium/air enema: **Coiled spring sign**; also therapeutic (pneumatic reduction)
- **Treatment**:
  - **Pneumatic/hydrostatic reduction (air/barium enema)**: If no perforation, <24h, stable — success rate 80-90%
  - Surgical reduction: Perforation, failed non-surgical, older child with lead point

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Intussusception = peak 6-9 months; ileocolic type; triad = colicky pain + currant jelly stool + sausage mass; USG target sign = diagnostic**

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Intussusception treatment = air/barium enema reduction if stable and <24 hours; contraindications = perforation, peritonitis, hemodynamic instability**

### Hypertrophic Pyloric Stenosis
- **Age**: **2-4 weeks** (range 2-12 weeks); male:female = **4:1** (firstborn male most common)
- **Features**: **Projectile non-bilious** vomiting (gastric outlet obstruction) after every feed; hungry baby
- **Examination**: **Olive-shaped mass** in epigastrium/right of midline palpable after vomiting; visible peristalsis left to right
- **Investigations**:
  - USG: Pyloric channel length >16mm, muscle thickness >4mm, diameter >14mm
  - Barium: **String sign of Kantor** (narrow elongated pyloric channel)
  - Bloods: **Hypochloremic hypokalemic metabolic alkalosis** (prolonged vomiting of HCl; kidneys reabsorb Na+ at expense of H+ and K+; paradoxical aciduria late)
- **Treatment**: Correct metabolic derangement first → **Ramstedt's pyloromyotomy** (surgical incision of pyloric muscle)

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Pyloric stenosis = 2-4 weeks, male firstborn, projectile NON-bilious vomiting; metabolic alkalosis (hypochloremic hypokalemic); olive mass; string sign barium**

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Pyloric stenosis management = correct electrolytes FIRST, then Ramstedt's pyloromyotomy; do NOT operate in uncorrected alkalosis**

### Hirschsprung's Disease (Congenital Aganglionic Megacolon)
- **Pathology**: Absence of ganglion cells (Auerbach's myenteric + Meissner's submucosal plexus) in distal rectum extending proximally
- **Length**: Short segment (rectosigmoid) most common (75%); total colonic rare
- **Gene**: RET proto-oncogene (10q11.2); associated with Down syndrome
- **Clinical features**:
  - Failure to pass meconium within **48 hours** of birth (neonatal presentation)
  - Chronic constipation, abdominal distension, bilious vomiting
  - **PR examination**: Empty rectum; **explosive squirt of stool** on withdrawal of finger (classic)
- **Investigations**:
  - **Barium enema**: Transitional zone (narrow aganglionic segment → dilated normal bowel)
  - **Rectal suction biopsy**: **Absence of ganglion cells + hypertrophic nerve trunks** — gold standard
  - Anorectal manometry: Absent rectoanal inhibitory reflex (RAIR)
- **Treatment**: **Swenson pull-through** operation (or Soave/Duhamel); temporary colostomy first in sick neonates

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Intussusception target sign USG; currant jelly stool; pyloric stenosis = metabolic alkalosis + olive mass; Hirschsprung = no ganglion cells + explosive stool on PR

## Fever and Infectious Diseases

### Febrile Seizures
- **Simple**: <15 min duration, generalized, single within 24h, in child 6 months-5 years, with fever >38°C
- **Complex**: Duration ≥15 min, focal, or >1 seizure in 24h
- **Recurrence risk**: 30-40%; risk increased if <18 months, family history, low fever at onset
- **No LP unless** meningism or suspicious features

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Simple febrile seizure = <15 min + generalized + single episode + age 6 months-5 years + fever; complex = any of: >15 min, focal, recurs within 24 hours**

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Febrile seizure recurrence = 30%; risk of epilepsy is only marginally increased after simple febrile seizure; LP not routinely indicated**

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Kawasaki disease = CRASH mnemonic — Conjunctivitis + Rash (polymorphous) + Adenopathy (cervical) + Strawberry tongue/lips + Hands (erythema/peeling); fever >5 days; treat with IVIG + aspirin**

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Kawasaki = most common cause of acquired heart disease in children in developed countries; coronary artery aneurysm = feared complication; echo mandatory**

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Nephrotic syndrome in children — most common cause = MCNS (Minimal Change Nephrotic Syndrome); responds to steroids; triad = edema + proteinuria + hypoalbuminemia + hyperlipidemia**

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Reye syndrome = aspirin + viral illness (influenza B or varicella) → fatty liver + encephalopathy + hyperammonemia; avoid aspirin in children <12 years with viral infections**

**PYQ HOTSPOT: G6PD deficiency = X-linked recessive; oxidant stress (primaquine, dapsone, fava beans) → Heinz body hemolytic anemia; G6PD most common enzyme deficiency; Mediterranean and African descent**

### Dengue Fever

**WHO 2009 Classification:**
- **Dengue without warning signs**: Fever 2-7 days, nausea, rash, aches
- **Dengue with warning signs**: Abdominal pain, persistent vomiting, mucosal bleed, liver enlargement, lethargy/restlessness, rising hematocrit with rapid platelet fall
- **Severe dengue**: Severe plasma leakage (shock), severe bleeding, severe organ impairment

**Diagnostics:**
- **NS1 antigen**: Days 1-5 (early)
- **IgM antibody**: Days 5-7 onwards
- **Tourniquet test (Rumpel-Leede)**: ≥10 petechiae in 1 inch² = positive (capillary fragility)
- **Platelet transfusion**: If <10,000/μL (spontaneous bleeding) or <20,000/μL with bleeding

### Typhoid (Enteric Fever)
- **Organism**: Salmonella typhi (S. paratyphi for paratyphoid)
- **Clinical**: Stepladder fever, relative bradycardia, **rose spots** (2-4mm pink macules on trunk, week 2), hepatosplenomegaly, constipation (early) → diarrhea
- **Widal test**: O antibody (1:160) and H antibody (1:160) rise from 2nd week
- **Gold standard**: Blood culture (1st week) → stool/urine culture (2nd-3rd week) → bone marrow (most sensitive)
- **Drug of choice**: **Ceftriaxone** IV (hospitalized); azithromycin/ciprofloxacin for outpatient
- **Complications**: Intestinal perforation (3rd week), hemorrhage, encephalopathy

### Measles
- **3 C's + 1 K**: **Cough, Coryza, Conjunctivitis** + **Koplik's spots** (white spots on buccal mucosa opposite lower molars — pathognomonic, appear 1-2 days before rash)
- **Rash**: Maculopapular, starts behind ears/hairline → spreads downward; lasts 5-6 days; stains then desquamates
- **Complications**: Pneumonia (most common), otitis media, croup, **SSPE** (subacute sclerosing panencephalitis — rare, 7-10 years later), encephalitis, **corneal ulceration + blindness in Vitamin A deficient**
- **Vitamin A**: Give to all cases (reduces mortality); repeat if Vitamin A deficient or signs of deficiency

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Koplik's spots before rash; NS1 early dengue; rose spots = typhoid; ceftriaxone = DOC typhoid

## Hematology

### Thalassemia

| Feature | Alpha Thalassemia | Beta Thalassemia |
|---------|------------------|-----------------|
| Gene | HBA1/HBA2 (chromosome 16) | HBB (chromosome 11) |
| 1 gene deleted | Silent carrier | — |
| 2 genes deleted | Alpha thal minor (trait) | Beta thal minor (trait) |
| 3 genes deleted | **HbH disease** (HbH = β4 tetramers) | — |
| 4 genes deleted | **Hydrops fetalis** (HbBart = γ4; lethal) | — |
| Homozygous | — | **Beta thal major (Cooley's anemia)** |

**Beta Thalassemia Major:**
- HbF predominant (HbA absent), HbA2 elevated
- **Chipmunk facies** (frontal bossing, prominent maxilla), hepatosplenomegaly, growth retardation
- **CXR**: Crew-cut/hair-on-end skull (medullary hyperplasia)
- **Treatment**: Regular blood transfusions (target Hb 10g/dL) + **chelation therapy** (desferrioxamine IV/SC or deferasirox oral) for iron overload
- **Curative**: Bone marrow transplant (HLA-matched sibling)

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Beta thal major = HbA absent + HbF elevated + HbA2 elevated; hair-on-end skull X-ray; chipmunk facies; transfusion dependent from 6 months of age**

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Alpha thal — 3 genes deleted = HbH disease (beta4 tetramers); 4 genes deleted = hydrops fetalis (HbBart = gamma4 tetramers) — INCOMPATIBLE WITH LIFE**

### Sickle Cell Anemia
- **HbS**: Valine substitutes glutamic acid at position 6 of beta chain (GAG → GTG)
- **Sickling triggers**: Low O2, acidosis, dehydration, cold, infection

**Vaso-occlusive Crises:**
- **Painful crisis**: Musculoskeletal pain (ischemia)
- **Acute chest syndrome**: Fever + respiratory symptoms + new CXR infiltrate
- **Stroke**: **Children** most at risk (10%); TCD screening + hydroxyurea/transfusion
- **Dactylitis (hand-foot syndrome)**: First manifestation in infants; swelling of hands/feet
- **Sequestration crisis**: Rapid spleen enlargement + anemia (splenic sequestration)
- **Aplastic crisis**: Parvovirus B19 infection → temporary cessation of erythropoiesis

**Treatment:**
- **Hydroxyurea**: Increases HbF → reduces sickling; reduces crisis frequency
- **Penicillin prophylaxis**: From 2 months (functional asplenia); Pneumovax vaccination
- **Folate supplementation** (chronic hemolysis)
- **Exchange transfusion**: Stroke, acute chest syndrome

### ITP (Immune Thrombocytopenic Purpura)
- **Pathology**: Antiplatelet IgG antibodies → platelet destruction by spleen
- **Clinical**: Often post-viral (1-3 weeks after), **petechiae/purpura without hepatosplenomegaly/lymphadenopathy**
- **CBC**: Isolated thrombocytopenia; normal RBC/WBC
- **Most cases in children**: Acute, self-limiting (resolves within 6 months)
- **Treatment**: Observation if mild (no mucosal bleeding); IVIG (0.8-1 g/kg × 2 days) or steroids for significant bleeding; Rituximab/splenectomy for chronic

### ALL (Acute Lymphoblastic Leukemia)
- **Most common childhood malignancy** (peak 2-5 years)
- **Type**: **Pre-B cell** most common; Common ALL (cALL) — **CD10 (CALLA)** positive
- **Features**: Pallor, fever, bleeding, bone pain, lymphadenopathy, hepatosplenomegaly, **mediastinal mass** (T-ALL)
- **CSF involvement**: CNS disease risk — CNS prophylaxis (intrathecal methotrexate)
- **Treatment**: BFM (Berlin-Frankfurt-Münster) protocol — Induction → Consolidation → Maintenance (2-3 years total)
  - Induction: Vincristine + prednisolone + L-asparaginase ± anthracycline
- **Philadelphia chromosome** t(9;22): Poor prognosis; imatinib added

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Hydrops fetalis = 4 alpha genes deleted; HbF = thal major; CALLA/CD10 = ALL; BFM protocol

## Congenital Heart Disease

### Acyanotic CHD (Left to Right Shunt)

| Defect | Murmur | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| **VSD** | Pan-systolic murmur at LLSE | Most common CHD; small VSDs close spontaneously |
| **ASD** | Wide fixed split S2, ejection systolic at 2nd LICS | Secundum most common; late presentation |
| **PDA** | **Continuous "machinery" murmur** at 2nd LICS | Common in preterm; indomethacin closes PDA; surgical ligation |

- **Eisenmenger's syndrome**: Longstanding L→R shunt → pulmonary hypertension → reversal to R→L shunt → cyanosis; **inoperable**

### Cyanotic CHD

| Defect | Key Feature | Management |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| **Tetralogy of Fallot** | **4 components**: VSD + RVOTO + aorta overriding + RVH; boot-shaped heart; **Tet spells (hypercyanotic)** — squat/knee-chest position, propranolol; **Boot-shaped heart on CXR** | Total correction surgery |
| **TGA (Transposition of Great Arteries)** | Parallel circulations; egg-on-side/egg-on-string heart; **Prostaglandin E1** to keep PDA open; balloon atrial septostomy (Rashkind) | Arterial switch operation (neonatal) |
| **Tricuspid atresia** | No tricuspid valve; left ventricular dominant; **ASD essential for survival** | Glenn shunt → Fontan procedure |
| **TAPVC** | All pulmonary veins drain to RA; obstructed type = emergency | Surgical repair |
| **Truncus arteriosus** | Single great vessel from both ventricles + VSD | Surgery in infancy |

**Tetralogy Spell Management (ABCD):**
- **A**: Assume knee-chest position
- **B**: Beta-blocker (propranolol)
- **C**: Calm the child, O2
- **D**: IV morphine, fluids, phenylephrine

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Boot-shaped heart = TOF; PGE1 = TGA; continuous machinery murmur = PDA; Eisenmenger = inoperable

## Genetics and Metabolic Disorders

### Down Syndrome (Trisomy 21)
- **Karyotype**: Trisomy 21 (94%), Robertsonian translocation (4%), Mosaicism (2%)
- **Features**: Flat facies, epicanthal folds, small ears, **Brushfield spots** (white spots on iris), macroglossia, single palmar crease (**simian crease**), clinodactyly
- **Cardiac defects**: AVSD (commonest in Down) > VSD > ASD; in 40%
- **Other associations**: Duodenal atresia ("double bubble"), Hirschsprung's, hypothyroidism, **Alzheimer's disease** (chromosome 21 = APP gene), **atlantoaxial instability** (screen before activities), ALL
- **Maternal serum screening (1st trimester)**: ↓ PAPP-A + ↑ free β-hCG + NT ≥3mm → combined test
- **2nd trimester quadruple screen**: ↓ AFP + ↓ uE3 + ↑ β-hCG + ↑ inhibin A

### PKU (Phenylketonuria)
- **Inheritance**: Autosomal recessive; PAH gene (phenylalanine hydroxylase) deficiency
- **Mechanism**: Phenylalanine → no conversion to tyrosine → accumulates → toxic to brain
- **Features**: **Fair hair/skin/eyes** (tyrosine → melanin deficient), **musty/mousy odor** (phenylacetate in urine/sweat), intellectual disability, seizures, microcephaly
- **Diagnosis**: **Guthrie test** (neonatal screening, blood spot 48-72h, heel prick)
- **Treatment**: **Phenylalanine-restricted diet** (low Phe formula) lifelong; tyrosine supplementation; Sapropterin (BH4 — cofactor) for mild cases

### Congenital Hypothyroidism
- **Neonatal TSH screening**: Blood spot at **48-72 hours** (Guthrie card simultaneously)
- **TSH >20 mIU/L**: Recall; if confirmed → start levothyroxine **within 2 weeks** of birth
- **Features if untreated**: **Cretinism** — intellectual disability, short stature, coarse features, umbilical hernia, prolonged jaundice, constipation, large tongue, hoarse cry

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Congenital hypothyroidism neonatal screening = heel-prick TSH at 48-72 hours; most common preventable cause of intellectual disability; treatment = levothyroxine within 2 weeks**

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Congenital hypothyroid clinical features: prolonged neonatal jaundice + large fontanelle + umbilical hernia + constipation + hoarse cry + large tongue — think congenital hypothyroid**

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Simian crease + AVSD = Down syndrome; musty odor = PKU; neonatal TSH screen = 48-72h

## Developmental and Behavioral Disorders

### ADHD (Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder)
- **DSM-5 Criteria**: ≥6 symptoms of inattention AND/OR hyperactivity-impulsivity for ≥6 months; <12 years onset; in ≥2 settings; functional impairment
- **Types**: Predominantly inattentive, predominantly hyperactive-impulsive, combined
- **Treatment**:
  - **First-line**: **Methylphenidate** (Ritalin) — stimulant; dopamine/norepinephrine reuptake inhibitor
  - Atomoxetine (non-stimulant): If stimulant not tolerated/tic disorder
  - Behavioral therapy: Always combined with medication
- **Monitoring**: BP, height/weight, tics, sleep

### Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
- **Kanner's syndrome** (classic autism): Social interaction deficit + communication deficit + restricted/repetitive behaviors
- **Triad** (Wing's): Impaired social interaction + impaired communication + restricted repetitive behaviors
- **DSM-5**: Two domains — social communication/interaction deficits + restricted/repetitive behaviors
- **Red flags**: No social smile by 3 months, no babbling/pointing by 12 months, no single words by 16 months, no 2-word phrases by 24 months, loss of language
- **Assessment tool**: **ADOS** (Autism Diagnostic Observation Schedule) — gold standard
- **Treatment**: **Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA)** — evidence-based behavioral therapy; speech/language therapy, occupational therapy; no cure

### Cerebral Palsy (CP)
- **Definition**: **Static encephalopathy** — non-progressive disorder of movement/posture due to lesion in developing brain
- **Types**:
  - **Spastic** (70-80%): Upper motor neuron lesion; increased tone, clonus, exaggerated reflexes
    - Spastic diplegia: Premature, periventricular leukomalacia; legs > arms; scissors gait
    - Spastic hemiplegia: Arm > leg; postnatal stroke
    - Spastic quadriplegia: Most severe; cognitive impairment common
  - **Dyskinetic/Athetoid** (10-15%): Basal ganglia (kernicterus = bilirubin encephalopathy); involuntary movements, preserved intelligence
  - **Ataxic** (5%): Cerebellar; wide-based gait, tremor
  - **Mixed**: Most common in practice
- **GMFCS (Gross Motor Function Classification System)**: Level I-V (I = walks without limitations → V = no voluntary movement)
- **Risk factors**: Prematurity (PVL), HIE, kernicterus, intrauterine infection, genetic
- **Treatment**: Physiotherapy, occupational therapy, speech therapy; baclofen (spasticity), botulinum toxin (focal spasticity), orthopedic surgery; epilepsy management

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: CP = static encephalopathy; dyskinetic CP = kernicterus; ABA = best treatment ASD; methylphenidate = ADHD first-line
