## RHEUMATOLOGY

### Rheumatoid Arthritis

#### ACR/EULAR 2010 Classification Criteria (score ≥6 = RA)

| Domain | Score |
|---|---|
| Joint involvement: 1 large joint | 0 |
| 2-10 large joints | 1 |
| 1-3 small joints | 2 |
| 4-10 small joints | 3 |
| >10 joints (at least 1 small) | 5 |
| Serology: Low positive RF/anti-CCP | 2 |
| High positive RF/anti-CCP | 3 |
| Acute-phase reactants: abnormal CRP or ESR | 1 |
| Duration: ≥6 weeks | 1 |

- **RF (Rheumatoid Factor)**: IgM against IgG Fc; positive in 80% RA; also in other diseases (SLE, Sjogren's, endocarditis)
- **Anti-CCP (Anti-cyclic citrullinated peptide)**: More specific for RA (specificity ~95%); precedes disease by years; associated with erosive disease

#### Steinbrocker Radiological Stages
- Stage I: Periarticular osteoporosis
- Stage II: Joint space narrowing + osteoporosis
- Stage III: Cartilage/bone erosions + deformities
- Stage IV: Fibrous/bony ankylosis

#### RA Features
- Symmetrical small joint (MCPs, PIPs) polyarthritis, morning stiffness >1h
- **Ulnar deviation, swan neck, boutonniere deformities**
- **Baker's cyst** (popliteal), **carpal tunnel syndrome**, atlantoaxial subluxation (C1-C2)
- Extraarticular: Rheumatoid nodules (extensor surfaces), vasculitis, pulmonary (UIP/NSIP pattern), Felty's syndrome (RA + splenomegaly + neutropenia), **Caplan's syndrome** (RA + pneumoconiosis)

#### RA Treatment
- **MTX (Methotrexate)**: First-line DMARD; folate supplementation required; monitor LFTs
- **Hydroxychloroquine**: Mild disease; ophthalmic monitoring (maculopathy)
- **Leflunomide**: Alternative to MTX
- **Sulfasalazine**: Used in combination (COBRA regimen)
- **Biologics** (if inadequate response to 2 DMARDs):
  - Anti-TNF: Etanercept, infliximab, adalimumab, certolizumab, golimumab
  - Anti-CD20: Rituximab
  - Anti-IL-6R: Tocilizumab
  - T-cell costimulation inhibitor: Abatacept
  - JAK inhibitors: Tofacitinib, baricitinib (oral targeted synthetic DMARDs)
- **DAS28 score**: <2.6 = remission; 2.6-3.2 = low activity; 3.2-5.1 = moderate; >5.1 = high activity

### Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE)

#### ACR 1997 Criteria (SOAP BRAIN MD Mnemonic) — ≥4/11 = SLE
- **S**erositis (pleuritis/pericarditis)
- **O**ral ulcers (painless)
- **A**rthritis (non-erosive, ≥2 joints)
- **P**hotosensitivity
- **B**lood disorders (hemolytic anemia, leukopenia, lymphopenia, thrombocytopenia)
- **R**enal (proteinuria >0.5g/day or cellular casts)
- **A**NA positive (most sensitive, ≥95%)
- **I**mmunologic: anti-dsDNA, anti-Sm, antiphospholipid Ab, false + VDRL
- **N**eurological (seizures, psychosis)
- **M**alar rash (butterfly rash — spares nasolabial folds)
- **D**iscoid rash

#### Antibodies in SLE
- **ANA**: Most sensitive (95-99%); also in many other conditions
- **Anti-dsDNA**: Specific for SLE (70%); correlates with **disease activity and nephritis**
- **Anti-Sm (anti-Smith)**: **Most specific** for SLE (25-30%); does NOT correlate with activity
- **Anti-Ro/SSA**: SLE, Sjogren's; associated with neonatal lupus, congenital heart block
- **Anti-La/SSB**: Sjogren's primarily
- **Anti-histone**: **Drug-induced lupus** (procainamide, hydralazine, isoniazid, quinidine)
- **Antiphospholipid antibodies**: APLS overlap

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Most specific antibody in SLE = anti-Sm. Most correlated with disease activity = anti-dsDNA**

#### Lupus Nephritis (ISN/RPS Classification)
| Class | Description |
|---|---|
| I | Minimal mesangial |
| II | Mesangial proliferative |
| III | Focal proliferative (<50% glomeruli) |
| **IV** | **Diffuse proliferative (>50% glomeruli)** — most severe/common, **"wire-loop" lesions** |
| V | Membranous |
| VI | Advanced sclerosing |

Class III/IV: Cyclophosphamide (Eurolupus regimen) or mycophenolate mofetil (MMF) + steroids

#### APLS (Antiphospholipid Syndrome)
- Recurrent thrombosis (arterial/venous) and/or pregnancy morbidity
- **Lab criteria**: Lupus anticoagulant, anticardiolipin IgG/IgM, anti-beta2-glycoprotein I — on **2 occasions ≥12 weeks apart**
- **Paradox**: aPTT prolonged in lab (LA effect) but THROMBOTIC in vivo
- Treatment: **Anticoagulation** (warfarin target INR 2-3; INR 3-4 if recurrent arterial)
- Obstetric APLS: Low-dose aspirin + LMWH

### Gout

- **Monosodium urate (MSU) crystals**: **Negatively birefringent** under polarized light (yellow when parallel to slow vibration axis)
- Hyperuricemia: Uric acid >6.8 mg/dL (above saturation threshold)
- Pseudogout: Calcium pyrophosphate crystals — **positively birefringent** (blue when parallel)

#### Phases
- Asymptomatic hyperuricemia → Acute gouty arthritis → Intercritical period → Chronic tophaceous gout
- **First MTP joint** (podagra) — most classic; also ankle, knee

#### Triggers
- Purine-rich foods (red meat, shellfish), alcohol, dehydration, diuretics, low-dose aspirin, cytotoxic therapy (tumor lysis)

#### Treatment
- **Acute attack**: **Colchicine** (within 36h, most effective early), **NSAIDs** (indomethacin), corticosteroids (if contraindications to above)
- **Do NOT start/stop urate-lowering therapy during acute attack** (may prolong/precipitate flare)
- **Prophylaxis/long-term ULT**:
  - **Allopurinol** (xanthine oxidase inhibitor) — first-line; target uric acid <6 mg/dL (<5 in tophi)
  - **Febuxostat** (selective XOI) — if allopurinol intolerant
  - **Probenecid** (uricosuric) — avoid if renal stones
  - Start ULT with colchicine cover (0.5mg twice daily for 6 months)

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Allopurinol + azathioprine = dangerous interaction (azathioprine toxicity — reduce azathioprine dose by 75%)**

### Spondyloarthropathies

#### Ankylosing Spondylitis (AS)
- **HLA-B27** (>90% of patients)
- Young men, inflammatory back pain (better with activity, worse with rest)
- Sacroiliitis (bilateral, symmetric), ascending spinal involvement
- **Bamboo spine** on X-ray (syndesmophytes — vertical ossification of annulus fibrosus)
- Enthesitis (inflammation at tendon/ligament insertion — Achilles tendon, plantar fascia)
- Extra-articular: **Anterior uveitis** (most common), aortitis, apical lung fibrosis, restrictive lung disease, conduction defects
- Modified New York Criteria: Clinical (back pain ≥3 months, limitation, reduced chest expansion) + radiological (sacroiliitis grade ≥2 bilateral)
- Treatment: **NSAIDs** (first-line), anti-TNF biologics (if NSAID failure), **NO role for DMARDs** for axial disease

#### Reiter's Syndrome (Reactive Arthritis)
- Mnemonic: "**Can't see, can't pee, can't climb a tree**" = **Conjunctivitis + Urethritis + Arthritis**
- Post-infectious: Chlamydia (genitourinary), Salmonella/Shigella/Yersinia/Campylobacter (enteric)
- Skin lesions: **Keratoderma blenorrhagica** (palms/soles), **Circinate balanitis**
- Oral ulcers, nail changes (similar to psoriasis)
- Self-limiting; NSAIDs + antibiotics (for Chlamydia if active infection)

**PYQ HOTSPOT: HLA-B27 positive in 70% of Reiter's. Genitourinary form more common in West, enteric more common in developing countries**

### Vasculitis

#### Giant Cell Arteritis (GCA) / Temporal Arteritis
- Elderly (>50 years), **temporal headache**, scalp tenderness, jaw claudication
- **Most feared complication**: **Anterior ischemic optic neuropathy → blindness** (irreversible)
- Elevated **ESR >50 mm/h** (sometimes >100)
- Diagnosis: **Temporal artery biopsy** (skip lesions — giant cell granulomas)
- 50% association with **Polymyalgia Rheumatica** (shoulder/hip girdle aching, high ESR, normal CK)
- Treatment: **High-dose prednisolone** (40-60 mg/day) — start **immediately** before biopsy if visual symptoms

#### Takayasu's Arteritis (Pulseless Disease)
- Young women, large vessel vasculitis (aorta and branches)
- **Absent pulses** in upper limbs, BP differential >10 mmHg between arms
- Features: Subclavian steal, aortic regurgitation, renal artery stenosis (secondary HTN)
- ESR elevated, angiography (gold standard for diagnosis), MRA/CTA
- Treatment: **Corticosteroids**; MTX or azathioprine as steroid-sparing; stenting/bypass for critical stenosis

#### Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis (GPA / Wegener's)
- **Triad**: Upper respiratory tract (saddle nose deformity, nasal septum perforation, sinusitis) + Lower respiratory (pulmonary nodules, cavitating lesions) + **Necrotizing glomerulonephritis** (crescentic GN)
- **c-ANCA (anti-PR3)** — highly specific
- Biopsy: Necrotizing granulomatous vasculitis
- Treatment: **Cyclophosphamide + prednisolone** (remission induction); rituximab (alternative); azathioprine/MTX (maintenance)

#### Polyarteritis Nodosa (PAN)
- Medium vessel vasculitis
- **p-ANCA negative** (distinguishes from microscopic polyangiitis)
- Renal involvement: Renovascular hypertension, renal infarcts (**NOT glomerulonephritis** — spares glomerular capillaries)
- Associated with **Hepatitis B** (30%)
- Features: Mononeuritis multiplex, testicular pain, livedo reticularis, mesenteric ischemia
- Diagnosis: Angiography (microaneurysms, beading), biopsy
- Treatment: Prednisolone ± cyclophosphamide; **treat HBV** if associated

**PYQ HOTSPOT: PAN spares the lungs and glomeruli (medium vessel). GPA involves both (small vessel + granulomatous)**
