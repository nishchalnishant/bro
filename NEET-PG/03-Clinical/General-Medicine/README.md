# General Medicine

General Medicine covers the diagnosis and management of internal medicine conditions across all organ systems — it is the single highest-weighted subject in NEET PG at ~23 questions per paper and has a rising trend.

## High-Yield Areas

- Cardiology: STEMI diagnostic criteria and management, heart failure (Framingham criteria, NYHA staging), valvular diseases, and ECG patterns
- Endocrinology: DM diagnostic criteria, insulin regimens, thyroid function test interpretation, Cushing's vs Addison's distinction
- Nephrology: CKD staging, nephrotic vs nephritic syndrome, acid-base disorders, and renal tubular acidosis
- Neurology: stroke syndromes (TACS, PACS, LACS), epilepsy classification and drug selection, meningitis CSF findings
- Diagnostic criteria: ADA for DM, modified Duke for IE, ACR for SLE, Rome IV for IBS — direct scorable Q sources

## NEET PG Weight

Avg 23 questions/year (2019–2024). Rising trend — highest weighted subject overall; clinical case-based questions with ECG and investigation data dominate; cardiology and endocrinology together account for ~40% of Medicine questions.
