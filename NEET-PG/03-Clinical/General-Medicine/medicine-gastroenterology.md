## GASTROENTEROLOGY

### GERD and Esophagus

#### Barrett's Esophagus
- **Specialized intestinal metaplasia** (columnar epithelium replaces squamous in distal esophagus)
- Risk factor for **esophageal adenocarcinoma** (30-40x increased risk)
- PPI therapy — mainstay; does NOT reverse Barrett's
- Surveillance endoscopy: Every 3-5 years (no dysplasia), every 6-12 months (LGD), 3 months (HGD)
- Treatment of HGD: **Radiofrequency ablation** (RFA), endoscopic mucosal resection

#### Achalasia
- **Failure of LES to relax** (aperistalsis of esophagus + hypertensive LES)
- Loss of Auerbach's (myenteric) plexus ganglion cells
- **Chagas disease** (Trypanosoma cruzi) = secondary achalasia in endemic areas
- Manometry: **Incomplete LES relaxation + absent peristalsis** (gold standard)
- Barium swallow: **Bird-beak appearance** (smooth tapering)
- CXR: Absent gastric air bubble, widened mediastinum
- Treatment: **Pneumatic balloon dilation**, Heller's myotomy, POEM (Per-oral endoscopic myotomy)

### Peptic Ulcer Disease

#### H. pylori
- Gram-negative curved rod, **urease-positive**
- Causes: **Duodenal ulcer** (>90%), gastric ulcer (75%), MALT lymphoma, gastric cancer
- Diagnosis: **Urea breath test** (non-invasive, best for treatment confirmation); CLO test (rapid urease, from biopsy); serology (cannot confirm eradication); stool antigen test
- **Triple therapy**: PPI + clarithromycin + amoxicillin (14 days)
- **Quadruple therapy** (if clarithromycin resistance): PPI + bismuth + tetracycline + metronidazole

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Duodenal ulcer — pain relieved by food. Gastric ulcer — pain precipitated by food. Both treated with H. pylori eradication + PPI**

#### Zollinger-Ellison Syndrome (ZES)
- **Gastrinoma** (usually in pancreas or duodenum) → excess gastrin → excess HCl
- Multiple/recurrent duodenal ulcers, **secretory diarrhea**, esophagitis
- Associated with **MEN-1** (20-25%)
- Diagnosis: **Fasting serum gastrin >1000 pg/mL** (diagnostic); **secretin stimulation test** (paradoxical rise in gastrin)
- Treatment: High-dose PPI, then surgical resection

#### Meckel's Diverticulum — Rule of 2s
- **2%** of population
- Within **2 feet** of ileocecal valve
- **2 inches** long
- Contains **2 types** of ectopic tissue (gastric > pancreatic)
- Presents in first **2 years** of life
- Most common presentation: **Painless rectal bleeding** (from gastric acid secretion)
- Diagnosis: **Meckel's scan** (Tc-99m pertechnetate — gastric mucosa uptake)

### Inflammatory Bowel Disease

#### Crohn's vs UC Comparison

| Feature | Crohn's Disease | Ulcerative Colitis |
|---|---|---|
| Location | Any GI tract (mouth to anus), especially **terminal ileum** | **Rectum** (always), extends proximally, continuous |
| Pattern | **Skip lesions** | Continuous, diffuse |
| Depth | **Transmural** | **Mucosal** |
| Endoscopy | **Cobblestone**, linear ulcers, aphthous ulcers | Continuous erythema, friability, pseudopolyps |
| Histology | **Non-caseating granulomas** | Crypt abscesses, goblet cell depletion |
| Fistulas | **Yes** (enterocutaneous, perianal) | No |
| Risk of cancer | Slight increase | **High risk** (especially pancolitis >8-10 years) |
| Smoking | **Increases risk** | **Tobacco protective** (nicotine) |
| Backwash ileitis | No | **Yes** (in pancolitis, retrograde from IC valve) |
| Toxic megacolon | Less common | **More common** |
| Serology | ASCA positive | p-ANCA positive |
| Surgery | Not curative (relapse post-resection) | **Colectomy = curative** |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Crohn's = skip, transmural, granulomas, fistulas. UC = continuous, mucosal, rectum always involved**

#### Extraintestinal Manifestations of IBD
- **Parallel disease activity**: Peripheral arthritis, episcleritis, oral aphthous ulcers, erythema nodosum
- **Independent of disease activity**: Ankylosing spondylitis (HLA-B27), PSC (more in UC), pyoderma gangrenosum, uveitis

#### IBD Treatment
- **Mild-moderate UC**: 5-ASA (mesalazine) — topical enemas for left-sided
- **Moderate-severe**: Steroids (prednisolone), then azathioprine/6-MP maintenance
- **Biologics**: Anti-TNF (infliximab, adalimumab), vedolizumab (gut-specific anti-integrin), ustekinumab (anti-IL-12/23)
- **Crohn's**: Similar; **anti-TNF preferred** for fistulizing disease

### Liver Disease

#### Child-Pugh Score

| Parameter | 1 point | 2 points | 3 points |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bilirubin (mg/dL) | <2 | 2-3 | >3 |
| Albumin (g/dL) | >3.5 | 2.8-3.5 | <2.8 |
| PT (INR) | <1.7 | 1.7-2.3 | >2.3 |
| Ascites | Absent | Mild | Moderate-severe |
| Encephalopathy | None | Grade 1-2 | Grade 3-4 |

Class A (5-6): Good surgical risk; Class B (7-9): Moderate risk; Class C (10-15): Poor surgical risk

**MELD Score**: 3.78×ln[Bilirubin] + 11.2×ln[INR] + 9.57×ln[Creatinine] + 6.43
- **MELD >15** = transplant listed; used for organ allocation priority

#### Complications of Cirrhosis

**Varices/Portal Hypertension**:
- Portal pressure gradient >10 mmHg = clinically significant portal hypertension
- Prophylaxis: **Non-selective beta-blockers** (propranolol, carvedilol)
- Acute variceal bleed: **Terlipressin + endoscopic band ligation + ceftriaxone** (prophylactic antibiotics)
- **TIPSS** (Transjugular intrahepatic portosystemic shunt) for refractory bleeding

**Spontaneous Bacterial Peritonitis (SBP)**:
- **PMN count ≥250 cells/mm³** in ascitic fluid = diagnostic (regardless of culture)
- Most common organism: **E. coli, Klebsiella, S. pneumoniae**
- Treatment: **Cefotaxime 2g IV every 8h for 5 days**; **Albumin infusion** (1.5 g/kg on day 1, 1 g/kg on day 3) — reduces HRS
- Prophylaxis: Norfloxacin/ciprofloxacin for prior SBP or ascitic protein <1 g/dL

**Hepatorenal Syndrome (HRS)**:
- **Functional renal failure** in cirrhosis (kidneys normal, splanchnic vasodilation → reduced renal perfusion)
- Type 1 (HRS-AKI): Rapid, Cr doubles to >2.5 mg/dL in <2 weeks
- Type 2 (HRS-CKD): Slower, associated with refractory ascites
- Treatment: **Terlipressin + albumin** (first-line); liver transplant (definitive)
- **Diagnosis of exclusion**: rule out hypovolemia, nephrotoxic drugs, obstruction, parenchymal renal disease

**Hepatic Encephalopathy**:
- Ammonia (NH3) plays central role
- Grades: I (confusion/altered sleep), II (drowsy, asterixis), III (stupor), IV (coma)
- **Asterixis (flapping tremor)** — grade II-III
- Precipitants: GI bleed, infection, constipation, hypokalemia, sedatives, high protein diet
- Treatment: **Lactulose** (reduces NH3 production/absorption); **Rifaximin** (gut-specific antibiotic); dietary protein not restricted (monitor)

#### Wilson's Disease
- Autosomal recessive, **ATP7B gene** mutation (chromosome 13)
- Copper accumulates in liver, brain (basal ganglia), cornea
- **Kayser-Fleischer rings**: Greenish-brown rings at Descemet's membrane (pathognomonic; seen in slit-lamp)
- Labs: **Low ceruloplasmin** (<20 mg/dL), high 24h urinary copper, high serum copper
- **Sunflower cataract** (copper deposition)
- Treatment: **D-penicillamine** (first-line); trientine (if intolerant); zinc (maintenance); liver transplant for fulminant

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Wilson's = KF rings + low ceruloplasmin + hepatic/neuropsychiatric features + Coombs-negative hemolytic anemia**

#### Hemochromatosis
- **HFE gene mutation** (C282Y/H63D); C282Y homozygous = most common
- Iron overload: liver (cirrhosis), pancreas (diabetes), heart (cardiomyopathy), joints (arthropathy), skin (bronze), gonads (hypogonadism)
- "**Bronze diabetes**" = skin bronzing + diabetes + cirrhosis
- Labs: **High serum ferritin, high transferrin saturation (>45%)**; liver biopsy (hepatic iron index)
- Treatment: **Phlebotomy** (500 mL weekly until ferritin <50 μg/L); deferasirox/deferiprone (if phlebotomy not possible)

#### Autoimmune Hepatitis (AIH)
- Young women, associated with other autoimmune diseases
- **Type 1**: Anti-smooth muscle (ASMA), ANA positive
- **Type 2**: Anti-LKM1 (liver-kidney microsomal), anti-LC1
- High IgG, elevated transaminases, interface hepatitis (piecemeal necrosis) on biopsy
- Treatment: **Prednisolone + azathioprine**

#### Primary Biliary Cholangitis (PBC)
- Middle-aged women, fatigue, pruritus, jaundice (late)
- **AMA (Anti-Mitochondrial Antibody)** — specifically **anti-M2** — >95% sensitivity/specificity
- **High ALP**, mild transaminases, high IgM
- Liver biopsy: Florid duct lesion (granulomatous destruction of bile ducts)
- Treatment: **UDCA (ursodeoxycholic acid)**; obeticholic acid (second-line); transplant for end-stage

**PYQ HOTSPOT: PBC = middle-aged woman + pruritus + AMA positive + high IgM**

#### Primary Sclerosing Cholangitis (PSC)
- Young men, **associated with UC** (70-80% of PSC patients have UC)
- **p-ANCA positive** (60-80%)
- Multifocal strictures of intra and extrahepatic bile ducts
- MRCP: **Beaded appearance** of bile ducts (alternating strictures and dilatations)
- High ALP, high IgG4 (in IgG4-PSC — respond to steroids; classic PSC does not)
- Risk of **cholangiocarcinoma** (15% lifetime risk)
- No effective medical treatment; UDCA not proven to reduce mortality; liver transplant

### Pancreatitis

#### Causes — I GET SMASHED Mnemonic
**I**diopathic, **G**allstones (most common in India and worldwide), **E**thanol (most common in Western countries), **T**rauma, **S**teroids, **M**umps (viral), **A**utoimmune, **S**corpion sting, **H**yperlipidemia (TG >1000)/Hypercalcemia, **E**RCP, **D**rugs

**PYQ HOTSPOT: In India, gallstones = most common cause. In West, alcohol = most common**

#### Ranson's Criteria (Severity Assessment)
**At Admission** (5 criteria):
- Age >55
- WBC >16,000/mm³
- Blood glucose >200 mg/dL
- LDH >350 IU/L
- AST >250 IU/L

**At 48 Hours** (5 criteria):
- HCT fall >10%
- BUN rise >5 mg/dL
- Ca²⁺ <8 mg/dL
- PaO2 <60 mmHg
- Base deficit >4 mEq/L
- Fluid sequestration >6L

Score ≥3 = severe pancreatitis; Each criterion scores 1 point

#### Atlanta Classification (2012)
- **Interstitial edematous**: Mild, self-limiting
- **Necrotizing**: Necrosis of pancreas or peripancreatic tissue
- Severity: Mild (no organ failure, no local complications) → Moderate (transient organ failure <48h) → Severe (persistent organ failure >48h)
- Local complications: ANC (acute necrotic collection), WOPN (walled-off necrosis), APF (acute peripancreatic fluid collection), pseudocyst

#### Chronic Pancreatitis
- Causes: Alcohol (most common), tropical (nutritional), hereditary, autoimmune, idiopathic
- Features: Steatorrhea (fat malabsorption), diabetes (late), epigastric pain
- **Ductal stones (calcifications on plain X-ray)** — pathognomonic
- Treatment: **Pancreatic enzyme replacement** (lipase for steatorrhea), pain management, treat diabetes, endoscopic drainage
