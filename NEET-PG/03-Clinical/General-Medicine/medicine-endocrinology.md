## ENDOCRINOLOGY

### Diabetes Mellitus

#### ADA Diagnostic Criteria (Any ONE):
1. **FPG ≥126 mg/dL** (fasting ≥8 hours)
2. **2h PG ≥200 mg/dL** during 75g OGTT
3. **HbA1c ≥6.5%**
4. **Random PG ≥200 mg/dL + symptoms** (polyuria, polydipsia, weight loss)

**Pre-diabetes**: FPG 100-125, 2h PG 140-199, HbA1c 5.7-6.4%

#### Type 1 vs Type 2

| Feature | Type 1 | Type 2 |
|---|---|---|
| Mechanism | Autoimmune beta-cell destruction | Insulin resistance + progressive beta-cell failure |
| Onset | Usually young (<30), acute | Usually older, insidious |
| BMI | Normal/thin | Obese |
| Antibodies | **Anti-GAD, anti-IA2, anti-islet cell, anti-insulin** | Usually negative |
| Ketosis | **Prone (DKA)** | Resistant (HHS more common) |
| C-peptide | **Low/absent** | Normal/elevated |
| Treatment | **Insulin** (mandatory) | Lifestyle + oral agents ± insulin |

#### DKA vs HHS

| Feature | DKA | HHS |
|---|---|---|
| Type | Usually T1DM | Usually T2DM |
| Blood glucose | 250-600 mg/dL | **>600 mg/dL** |
| Ketones | **Present (++++)** | Absent/trace |
| pH | **<7.3** | Normal/near-normal |
| Bicarbonate | **<18 mEq/L** | >15 mEq/L |
| Anion gap | **High** (>12) | Normal |
| Osmolality | Mildly elevated | **>320 mOsm/kg** |
| Consciousness | Variable | **Profound obtundation/coma** |
| Mortality | 1-5% | **5-15%** |

**DKA Management**:
1. **IV Normal Saline** (aggressive resuscitation — 1L in first hour)
2. **IV Regular Insulin** (0.1 unit/kg/h; start only after K⁺ >3.5 mEq/L)
3. **Potassium replacement** (add to IV fluids when K⁺ <5.5; aggressive if <3.5)
4. **Bicarbonate** only if pH <6.9
5. Switch to dextrose when glucose <200 mg/dL (to prevent hypoglycemia while continuing insulin)
6. Transition to SC insulin when anion gap closed and patient eating

**PYQ HOTSPOT: DKA = high anion gap metabolic acidosis + ketones. Insulin drives K+ into cells → must monitor K+ carefully**

#### Dawn vs Somogyi Phenomenon
- **Dawn**: Early morning hyperglycemia due to growth hormone surge (physiological); glucose rises from 3 AM
- **Somogyi**: Morning hyperglycemia after **nocturnal hypoglycemia** (rebound); counter-regulatory response; glucose drops at 3 AM then rises

#### Diabetic Complications
- **Nephropathy**: Stage 1 (hyperfiltration) → 2 (normoalbuminuria) → 3 (microalbuminuria 30-300 mg/day) → 4 (macroalbuminuria) → 5 (ESRD)
- **Retinopathy**: NPDR (microaneurysms, dot/blot hemorrhages, cotton wool spots) → PDR (neovascularization, risk of vitreous hemorrhage, detachment); **macular edema = most common cause of blindness**
- **Neuropathy**: Distal symmetric polyneuropathy (most common — stocking/glove), autonomic (gastroparesis, orthostatic hypotension, bladder, ED), mononeuropathy multiplex

### Thyroid Disorders

#### Hypothyroidism
- Primary (high TSH, low T4): Hashimoto's (anti-TPO, anti-thyroglobulin), iodine deficiency (most common worldwide), post-radioiodine/surgery
- Features: Fatigue, weight gain, cold intolerance, constipation, bradycardia, dry skin, coarse hair, periorbital puffiness, delayed relaxation of reflexes, **carpal tunnel syndrome**
- **Myxedema coma**: Severe hypothyroidism + precipitant (cold, infection, drugs); hypothermia, bradycardia, hypoventilation, altered consciousness
  - Treatment: **IV T3 (liothyronine)** or T4, IV hydrocortisone (adrenal insufficiency may coexist), warming

#### Hyperthyroidism — Graves' Disease
- **TSI (Thyroid-stimulating immunoglobulin) / TRAb** (TSH receptor antibody) — pathognomonic of Graves'
- Features: Weight loss, palpitations, heat intolerance, tremor, diarrhea, oligomenorrhea
- **Specific to Graves'**: **Pretibial myxedema** (infiltrative dermopathy — shin), **thyroid acropachy** (clubbing + periosteal new bone), **exophthalmos/proptosis**
- Treatment: **Carbimazole/propylthiouracil (PTU)**, beta-blockers (symptom control), radioiodine (definitive for most), thyroidectomy
- **PTU preferred in first trimester of pregnancy** (carbimazole — aplasia cutis); carbimazole preferred in second/third trimester (PTU hepatotoxicity)

#### Thyroid Storm
- **Burch-Wartofsky Score**: Temperature, HR, CNS effects, GI dysfunction, AF, precipitating illness → score >45 = thyroid storm
- Treatment: **PTU 600mg loading**, propranolol, hydrocortisone, lugol's iodine (given 1h after PTU — blocks thyroid release), treat precipitant, cooling

#### Thyroid Nodule Evaluation
- **Ultrasound first**: Assess for malignant features (hypoechoic, irregular margins, microcalcifications, taller-than-wide, vascularity)
- **FNAC (Fine Needle Aspiration Cytology)**: For nodules ≥1 cm (or smaller with suspicious features)
- **Bethesda System**: I (non-diagnostic) → VI (malignant)
- **TSH suppressed + hot nodule on scan**: Thyroid adenoma; FNAC less useful (malignancy rare in hot nodules)

### Adrenal Disorders

#### Addison's Disease (Primary Adrenal Insufficiency)
- Low cortisol + low aldosterone + **high ACTH** (feedback)
- Causes: Autoimmune (most common in developed countries), TB (most common worldwide)
- Features: **Hyperpigmentation** (high ACTH/MSH), weakness, fatigue, weight loss, salt craving, **hyponatremia, hyperkalemia, hypoglycemia**, hypotension
- **Waterhouse-Friderichsen syndrome**: Bilateral adrenal hemorrhage (N. meningitidis, DIC)
- Diagnosis: **Short Synacthen test** (0, 30, 60 min cortisol after synthetic ACTH); cortisol <500 nmol/L at 30 min = insufficient
- Treatment: **Hydrocortisone + Fludrocortisone** (primary); prednisolone alone (secondary — no aldosterone deficiency)
- **Addisonian crisis**: IV hydrocortisone 100mg bolus, then IV fluids (normal saline)

#### Cushing's Syndrome

**Causes**:
1. **Iatrogenic** (exogenous steroid use) — most common overall
2. **Pituitary adenoma (Cushing's DISEASE)** — most common endogenous cause; bilateral adrenal hyperplasia; **ACTH-secreting**
3. **Ectopic ACTH** (small cell lung cancer, carcinoid) — very high ACTH, rapid onset
4. **Adrenal adenoma/carcinoma** — low ACTH (autonomous cortisol production)

**Dexamethasone Suppression Test**:
| Test | Cushing's Disease | Ectopic ACTH | Adrenal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Low-dose (1mg overnight) | Does NOT suppress | Does NOT suppress | Does NOT suppress |
| High-dose (8mg overnight) | **Suppresses (>50% reduction)** | Does NOT suppress | Does NOT suppress |
| ACTH levels | Mildly elevated | **Very high** | **Low** |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: High-dose DST suppression = pituitary source (Cushing's disease). No suppression + very high ACTH = ectopic. Low ACTH = adrenal**

#### Conn's Syndrome (Primary Hyperaldosteronism)
- **Aldosterone-producing adenoma** (Conn's) or bilateral adrenal hyperplasia
- Features: **Hypertension + Hypokalemia** (no edema — escape phenomenon) + **Low renin**
- Metabolic alkalosis, weakness, polyuria
- Diagnosis: **Aldosterone:renin ratio >30** (screening); adrenal CT; adrenal vein sampling (lateralization)
- Treatment: Surgical (unilateral adrenalectomy for adenoma); spironolactone/eplerenone (bilateral hyperplasia or surgical contraindication)

#### Pheochromocytoma
**Rule of 10**:
- 10% bilateral, 10% malignant, 10% extra-adrenal (paraganglioma), 10% in children, 10% familial (MEN2, VHL, NF1, SDH mutations)

- Features: **Paroxysmal hypertension** (episodic), headache, sweating, palpitations (pheo triad)
- Diagnosis: **24h urine VMA/metanephrines** (most sensitive); plasma metanephrines (very sensitive); CT/MRI for localization; **MIBG scan** (functional imaging)
- Treatment: **Alpha-blockade FIRST** (phenoxybenzamine/phentolamine) → THEN beta-blockade (to prevent hypertensive crisis from unopposed alpha); **never start beta-blockers first**
- Surgical: Laparoscopic adrenalectomy

#### MEN Syndromes

| Syndrome | Components |
|---|---|
| **MEN 1 (Werner's)** | **3 P's**: Pituitary (prolactinoma most common), Parathyroid (most common manifestation), Pancreas (gastrinoma most common) |
| **MEN 2A (Sipple)** | Medullary thyroid cancer (MTC) + Pheochromocytoma + Parathyroid hyperplasia |
| **MEN 2B** | MTC + Pheochromocytoma + Marfanoid habitus + Mucosal neuromas (NO parathyroid) |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: MEN 1 gene = Menin (tumor suppressor, chromosome 11). MEN 2 = RET proto-oncogene mutation**
**MEN 2A/2B: Thyroidectomy performed prophylactically based on RET mutation type**
