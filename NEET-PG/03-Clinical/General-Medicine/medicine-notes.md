## CARDIOLOGY

### Heart Failure

#### HFrEF vs HFpEF

| Feature | HFrEF (Systolic) | HFpEF (Diastolic) |
|---|---|---|
| EF | <40% | ≥50% |
| Mechanism | Impaired contraction | Impaired relaxation |
| Common cause | IHD, DCM | Hypertension, DM, obesity |
| BNP | Elevated | Elevated |
| Treatment | ACEi/ARB/BB/diuretics/ARNI/SGLT2i | Diuretics, treat cause |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: HFrEF EF <40%, HFpEF EF ≥50%, HFmrEF 40-49%**

#### Frank-Starling Mechanism
- Increased preload → increased stroke volume (up to a point)
- In failing heart: curve is depressed and flattened
- **Sarcomere optimal length: 2.2 microns**

#### Compensatory Mechanisms in Heart Failure
1. **Frank-Starling mechanism** (acute)
2. **Neurohormonal activation**: RAAS, SNS activation
3. **Cardiac remodeling**: ventricular hypertrophy
4. **Tachycardia** (Bowditch/Treppe effect)

#### NYHA Classification

| Class | Symptoms |
|---|---|
| I | No limitation with ordinary activity |
| II | Slight limitation; comfortable at rest |
| III | Marked limitation; comfortable at rest |
| IV | Unable to carry on any activity without discomfort |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: NYHA Class III-IV → consider CRT/ICD implantation in eligible patients**

#### Treatment of Chronic HFrEF
- **ACEi/ARB**: Reduce afterload, prevent remodeling (ramipril, enalapril)
- **Beta-blockers**: Carvedilol, metoprolol succinate, bisoprolol (reduce mortality)
- **Diuretics**: Symptom relief (furosemide for loop, spironolactone for aldosterone antagonism)
- **ARNI (Sacubitril/Valsartan)**: Superior to ACEi (PARADIGM-HF trial); **cannot combine with ACEi** (36-hour washout)
- **SGLT2 inhibitors**: Empagliflozin/dapagliflozin — **reduce HF hospitalizations and CV death** (EMPEROR-Reduced, DAPA-HF trials)
- **Ivabradine**: HR >70 on max beta-blocker, sinus rhythm
- **Hydralazine + Nitrates**: ACEi/ARB intolerant, especially in **Black patients**

**PYQ HOTSPOT: The "Fantastic Four" in HFrEF = ACEi/ARB/ARNI + BB + MRA + SGLT2i**

#### Acute Pulmonary Edema Management
Mnemonic: **LMNOP**
- **L**asix (furosemide IV)
- **M**orphine (reduces preload, anxiolysis) — controversial
- **N**itrates (sublingual/IV — reduce preload)
- **O**xygen (CPAP/BiPAP if needed)
- **P**ositioning (sit upright, legs dependent)

Additional: Dobutamine (inotrope) if cardiogenic shock; Nesiritide (BNP analogue)

### Ischemic Heart Disease / ACS

#### ACS Spectrum

| Feature | STEMI | NSTEMI | Unstable Angina |
|---|---|---|---|
| ECG | ST elevation, new LBBB | ST depression/T-wave changes | Normal or ST depression |
| Troponin | Elevated | Elevated | **Normal** |
| Pain at rest | Yes | Yes | Yes (new or crescendo) |
| Culprit | Complete occlusion | Partial occlusion | Partial occlusion |
| Treatment | **Immediate reperfusion** | Anticoagulation + early PCI | Anticoagulation + PCI (risk-stratified) |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: UA = ACS with normal troponin. NSTEMI = ACS with elevated troponin, no ST elevation**

#### Troponin Timeline
- **Rises**: 3-6 hours post-MI
- **Peaks**: 12-24 hours (Troponin I/T)
- **Normalizes**: 5-14 days
- **CK-MB**: Rises 4-6h, peaks 12-24h, normalizes 48-72h (useful for **reinfarction detection**)

#### ECG Changes in STEMI
- **Hyperacute T waves** (earliest change)
- **ST elevation** (>1mm in 2 contiguous limb leads, >2mm in precordial)
- **Q waves** (irreversible necrosis, ≥0.04s duration, ≥25% R wave height)
- **T wave inversion** (reperfusion)

**PYQ HOTSPOT: New LBBB = STEMI equivalent; Right bundle branch block does NOT mimic STEMI**

#### Localization of MI
| Territory | Leads | Artery |
|---|---|---|
| Anterior | V1-V4 | LAD |
| Lateral | I, aVL, V5-V6 | LCx |
| Inferior | II, III, aVF | RCA |
| Posterior | Tall R in V1-V2 (mirror image) | RCA/LCx |
| RV infarct | V4R | RCA proximal |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: RV infarct = inferior STEMI + hypotension → fluid challenge (NOT nitrates)**

#### Prinzmetal (Variant) Angina
- **Coronary vasospasm** without fixed obstruction
- ST elevation during episode (unlike stable angina)
- Occurs at rest, often at **night/early morning**
- Treatment: **CCB (nifedipine/diltiazem), nitrates**; **beta-blockers CONTRAINDICATED** (can worsen spasm)

#### Dressler's Syndrome
- **Post-MI pericarditis** (2-10 weeks after MI)
- Fever, pleuritic chest pain, pericardial effusion
- **Autoimmune** mechanism (anti-heart antibodies)
- Treatment: **NSAIDs + colchicine**; steroids if refractory

#### Complications of MI — Killip Classification

| Class | Feature | Mortality |
|---|---|---|
| I | No signs of HF | ~6% |
| II | S3 gallop, mild pulmonary rales | ~17% |
| III | Acute pulmonary edema | ~38% |
| IV | Cardiogenic shock | ~81% |

#### Mechanical Complications Timeline

| Complication | Timing | Key Feature |
|---|---|---|
| Free wall rupture | 3-5 days | Sudden death, hemopericardium |
| VSD (interventricular) | 3-5 days | New holosystolic murmur, step-up in RV O2 |
| Papillary muscle rupture (MR) | 3-7 days | Acute severe MR, pulmonary edema |
| LV aneurysm | Weeks-months | Persistent ST elevation, thrombus, arrhythmia |
| LV pseudoaneurysm | Days-weeks | Contained rupture, high risk of rupture |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Free wall rupture = most common cause of death after initial 24h. Papillary muscle most commonly affected = posteromedial (single RCA supply)**

#### GRACE Score
Predicts in-hospital mortality in NSTEMI/UA; variables include: age, HR, BP, creatinine, Killip class, cardiac arrest, ST deviation, troponin elevation

#### Thrombolysis in STEMI
**Indications**: STEMI <12h, no PCI available within 120 minutes
**Contraindications**:
- Absolute: Prior hemorrhagic stroke, ischemic stroke <3 months, active bleeding, aortic dissection, severe uncontrolled HTN, intracranial neoplasm
- Relative: Recent surgery <3 weeks, active peptic ulcer, INR >2

**Agents**: tPA (alteplase), rPA (reteplase), TNK-tPA (tenecteplase)

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Streptokinase = non-fibrin specific, antigenic (cannot repeat within 6 months). tPA = fibrin specific, preferred**

#### PCI vs CABG
| Factor | PCI preferred | CABG preferred |
|---|---|---|
| Anatomy | Single/double vessel disease | Left main disease, 3-vessel disease |
| Diabetics | Less preferred | **Preferred** (FREEDOM trial) |
| EF | Normal | Reduced EF with viable myocardium |
| Urgency | Primary PCI for STEMI | Elective for stable CAD |

### Arrhythmias

#### Atrial Fibrillation

**CHADS-VASC Score** (for stroke risk, decide anticoagulation):
- **C**HF (1), **H**ypertension (1), **A**ge ≥75 (2), **D**iabetes (1), **S**troke/TIA (2), **V**ascular disease (1), **A**ge 65-74 (1), **S**ex female (1)
- Score ≥2 (men) or ≥3 (women) → anticoagulate
- **Score 0 → no anticoagulation; Score 1 (men) → consider**

**HAS-BLED Score**: bleeding risk with anticoagulation

**Rate vs Rhythm Control**:
- Rate control: beta-blockers, CCB (diltiazem/verapamil), digoxin; target HR <80/min at rest
- Rhythm control: cardioversion (electrical/chemical), ablation
- **AFFIRM trial**: Rate = rhythm control in terms of mortality
- **New-onset AF <48h**: Cardiovert directly; **>48h or unknown**: Anticoagulate 3 weeks before and 4 weeks after cardioversion (or TEE to exclude thrombus)

**PYQ HOTSPOT: WPW + AF = DO NOT give AV nodal blocking drugs (digoxin/verapamil/adenosine) → risk of VF via accessory pathway → use procainamide or cardiovert**

#### Ventricular Tachycardia / Ventricular Fibrillation
- **VT (monomorphic)**: Wide complex tachycardia, AV dissociation, capture/fusion beats
- **VF**: Irregular, chaotic → immediate defibrillation
- **Torsades de Pointes**: Polymorphic VT, associated with prolonged QT; treatment = **IV magnesium**; avoid drugs prolonging QT
- **ACLS for pulseless VT/VF**: Defibrillate → CPR → Epinephrine → Amiodarone (or lidocaine)
- **Stable VT**: Amiodarone IV, then cardioversion if no response

#### WPW Syndrome
- **Accessory pathway (Bundle of Kent)** bypasses AV node
- **Delta wave** (slurred upstroke of QRS), short PR interval, wide QRS
- **AVRT**: Orthodromic (narrow complex, most common) vs Antidromic (wide complex)
- **WPW + AF** = Life-threatening (as above)
- Definitive treatment: **Radiofrequency catheter ablation**

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Delta wave = hallmark of WPW. Accessory pathway = Bundle of Kent**

#### Heart Blocks

| Block | ECG Feature | Pacemaker? |
|---|---|---|
| 1st degree | Prolonged PR (>0.20s), all P conducted | No |
| 2nd degree Mobitz I (Wenckebach) | Progressive PR lengthening → dropped beat | Usually no (benign, AV node) |
| 2nd degree Mobitz II | Constant PR, sudden dropped beat | **Yes** (His-Purkinje, unpredictable) |
| 3rd degree (complete) | P and QRS dissociated, escape rhythm | **Yes** (always) |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Mobitz II and 3rd degree = always require pacemaker. Wenckebach = usually benign**

**Pacemaker Indications (other)**:
- Sick sinus syndrome with symptoms
- Bifascicular/trifascicular block with syncope
- Post-MI heart block

#### SVT (AVNRT — most common SVT)
- Narrow complex tachycardia, HR 150-250
- P wave in QRS or just after
- Treatment: **Vagal maneuvers → Adenosine (6mg IV rapid push, can repeat 12mg)**
- **Adenosine**: Half-life 10 seconds, causes transient AV block
- **Contraindicated in WPW (antidromic SVT) and asthma**
- Maintenance: beta-blockers, CCB, ablation

### Hypertension

#### Classification (ACC/AHA 2017 vs older JNC 7)

| Stage | Systolic | Diastolic |
|---|---|---|
| Normal | <120 | <80 |
| Elevated | 120-129 | <80 |
| Stage 1 | 130-139 | 80-89 |
| Stage 2 | ≥140 | ≥90 |

**India uses WHO/JNC 7**: HTN = BP ≥140/90 mmHg

#### Compelling Indications for Antihypertensives

| Condition | Preferred Drug |
|---|---|
| Heart failure (HFrEF) | ACEi/ARB + BB + diuretic |
| Post-MI | ACEi/ARB, BB |
| Diabetes | ACEi/ARB (nephroprotection) |
| CKD with proteinuria | **ACEi/ARB** (first-line) |
| Pregnancy | **Methyldopa** (safest), labetalol, nifedipine |
| Isolated systolic HTN (elderly) | CCB, thiazide |
| Benign prostatic hyperplasia | Alpha-1 blockers (doxazosin) |
| Pheochromocytoma | Alpha-blockers first, then beta |
| Migraine prophylaxis | BB, CCB |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: ACEi/ARB contraindicated in pregnancy (teratogenic - causes renal tubular dysplasia)**

#### Hypertensive Emergency vs Urgency

| Feature | Emergency | Urgency |
|---|---|---|
| BP | Usually >180/120 | >180/120 |
| End-organ damage | **Yes** (brain/heart/kidney) | **No** |
| Treatment | IV drugs (labetalol, nitroprusside, nicardipine) | Oral drugs (amlodipine, clonidine) |
| BP reduction | Lower by 25% in first hour | Gradually over 24-48h |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Hypertensive encephalopathy, aortic dissection, pulmonary edema, eclampsia = emergencies**
**Nitroprusside = cyanide toxicity with prolonged use → use sodium thiosulfate as antidote**

#### Secondary Hypertension

| Cause | Key Feature | Test |
|---|---|---|
| **Conn's syndrome** | Hypokalemia, no edema, low renin | Aldosterone:renin ratio |
| **Cushing's syndrome** | Central obesity, striae, buffalo hump | 24h urine cortisol, low-dose DST |
| **Pheochromocytoma** | Paroxysmal headache/sweating/palpitations | **24h urine VMA/metanephrines** |
| **Renovascular** | Young female (FMD) or atherosclerotic | Duplex ultrasound, MRA, captopril scan |
| **Coarctation of aorta** | Weak femoral pulses, rib notching on CXR | Echo, CT aortography |
| **OSA** | Obese, morning headache, snoring | Polysomnography |

### Valvular Heart Disease

#### Murmur Characteristics

| Valve Lesion | Timing | Location | Radiation | Key Features |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mitral stenosis | Mid-diastolic | Apex | None | **Opening snap** (OS), tapping apex, loud S1, best in left lateral position |
| Mitral regurgitation | Pansystolic | Apex | Axilla | Soft S1, S3, displaced apex |
| Aortic stenosis | Ejection systolic | Aortic area (2nd RICS) | Carotids | **Plateau pulse, slow rising, narrow pulse pressure** |
| Aortic regurgitation | Early diastolic | Left sternal edge | — | **Collapsing/water-hammer pulse, wide pulse pressure** |
| MVP | Mid-systolic click + late systolic murmur | Apex | — | Click moves earlier with standing/Valsalva |
| Tricuspid regurgitation | Pansystolic | Lower sternal border | — | Increases with inspiration (**Carvallo's sign**) |
| Pulmonary stenosis | Ejection systolic | Pulmonary area (2nd LICS) | — | Wide split S2, ejection click |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Opening snap = mitral stenosis. Earlier OS = more severe MS (higher LA pressure)**

#### Mitral Stenosis (Rheumatic)
- Rheumatic fever: Group A Streptococcus → cross-reactive antibodies
- **MVA <1 cm² = critical MS**
- Features: Malar flush, OS, mid-diastolic rumble, Graham Steell murmur (PR due to PAH)
- **S1 loud** (pliable leaflets) → becomes soft when calcified
- Treatment: Diuretics, beta-blockers; **PTMC (balloon valvuloplasty)** if MVA <1.5 cm² with symptoms; surgery for severe/calcified

#### Aortic Regurgitation Signs
- **Corrigan's (water-hammer) pulse**: Abrupt rise and collapse
- **de Musset's sign**: Head bobbing
- **Quincke's sign**: Nail bed pulsation
- **Duroziez's sign**: Systolic/diastolic bruit over femoral
- **Hill's sign**: Popliteal BP > brachial BP by >20 mmHg
- **Traube's sign**: Pistol shot sound over femoral

**PYQ HOTSPOT: AR = wide pulse pressure, diastolic murmur at left sternal edge, best heard sitting forward in expiration**

#### MVP (Most Common Valvular Lesion)
- **Most common** valvular heart disease in developed countries
- Mid-systolic click + late systolic murmur
- **Increases severity** with standing, Valsalva (less preload)
- **Decreases severity** with squatting, leg raise (more preload)
- Myxomatous degeneration of valve leaflets
- Complications: MR, infective endocarditis, arrhythmias, sudden death

### Pericardial Diseases

#### Acute Pericarditis
- **Pleuritic chest pain** (worse supine, better sitting forward)
- **Pericardial friction rub** (3 components: atrial systole, ventricular systole, early diastole)
- **ECG**: Saddle-shaped (concave up) ST elevation in ALL leads except aVR and V1 (which show ST depression); PR depression (**earliest ECG change**)
- Most common cause: **Viral (Coxsackievirus B, echovirus)**
- Treatment: **NSAIDs + Colchicine** (reduces recurrence); steroids only if refractory

**PYQ HOTSPOT: PR depression is the earliest and most specific ECG finding in pericarditis**

#### Cardiac Tamponade
- Compression of heart by pericardial fluid → decreased cardiac output
- **Beck's Triad**: Hypotension + JVD + Muffled heart sounds
- **Pulsus paradoxus**: >10 mmHg fall in SBP with inspiration
- **Kussmaul's sign**: NOT present in tamponade (JVP rises with inspiration in constriction, not tamponade)
- ECG: **Electrical alternans** (alternating QRS amplitude) — pathognomonic
- **Low voltage QRS** on ECG
- Echo: Diastolic collapse of RV (most sensitive) and RA
- Treatment: **Pericardiocentesis** (emergent), surgical window if recurrent

#### Constrictive Pericarditis
- Fibrous/calcified pericardium → impaired diastolic filling
- **Kussmaul's sign IS PRESENT** (JVP rises with inspiration)
- Friedreich's sign: Rapid y-descent (steep)
- Pericardial knock (replaces OS)
- **Pericardial calcification on CXR/CT** — eggshell calcification
- Square root sign (dip-and-plateau) on cardiac catheterization
- Treatment: **Pericardiectomy** (definitive)
- Common causes: TB (worldwide), viral, post-cardiac surgery, radiation

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Tamponade = no Kussmaul's, prominent x-descent; Constriction = Kussmaul's present, prominent y-descent**

## PULMONOLOGY

### COPD

- **Definition**: FEV1/FVC <0.70 (post-bronchodilator) — **fixed ratio**
- Cause: **Cigarette smoking** (most common), biomass fuels, air pollution
- Alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency: Panacinar emphysema, **PiZZ genotype**, affects lower lobes

#### GOLD Staging (based on FEV1 % predicted)
| Stage | FEV1 | Severity |
|---|---|---|
| GOLD 1 | ≥80% | Mild |
| GOLD 2 | 50-79% | Moderate |
| GOLD 3 | 30-49% | Severe |
| GOLD 4 | <30% | Very Severe |

#### Blue Bloater vs Pink Puffer

| Feature | Blue Bloater (Chronic Bronchitis) | Pink Puffer (Emphysema) |
|---|---|---|
| Definition | Cough + sputum ≥3 months/year for ≥2 years | Alveolar destruction, air trapping |
| Appearance | Cyanotic, edematous | Thin, barrel chest, pursed-lip breathing |
| PaO2 | Low | Near normal |
| PaCO2 | Elevated (CO2 retainer) | Normal or low |
| Polycythemia | Present | Absent |
| Cor pulmonale | Common | Less common |
| Hypoxic drive | **At risk — caution with high-flow O2** | Less risk |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Chronic bronchitis = clinical diagnosis (productive cough ≥3 months/year for ≥2 years). Emphysema = pathological diagnosis (alveolar wall destruction)**

#### Cor Pulmonale
- RV hypertrophy/failure due to pulmonary hypertension from lung disease
- ECG: Right axis deviation, P pulmonale (tall peaked P in II), RBBB, S1Q3T3 pattern
- Most common cause: **COPD**

#### COPD Exacerbation Management
- **Controlled O2**: Target SpO2 88-92% (hypoxic drive preservation)
- **Short-acting bronchodilators**: Salbutamol + ipratropium nebulization
- **Systemic corticosteroids**: Prednisolone 40mg for 5 days
- **Antibiotics**: If increased sputum/change in color (amoxicillin-clavulanate, doxycycline, or azithromycin)
- **NIV (BiPAP)**: For respiratory failure (pH <7.35, PaCO2 >6 kPa)

#### LTOT (Long-Term Oxygen Therapy) Criteria
- PaO2 ≤55 mmHg (or SpO2 ≤88%) on room air — 2 measurements 3 weeks apart
- PaO2 56-59 mmHg with cor pulmonale/polycythemia/nocturnal hypoxemia
- Use >15 hours/day; **improves survival**

### Asthma

#### GINA Classification

| Severity | SABA use | Nighttime symptoms | FEV1 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Intermittent | ≤2 days/week | ≤2/month | >80% |
| Mild persistent | >2 days/week, not daily | 3-4/month | >80% |
| Moderate persistent | Daily | >1/week | 60-80% |
| Severe persistent | Continuous | Frequent | <60% |

#### Reversibility Test
- **FEV1 improvement ≥12% AND ≥200 mL** after bronchodilator = significant reversibility (asthma)
- COPD = <12% or <200 mL improvement (fixed obstruction)

#### Status Asthmaticus
- Severe asthma not responding to initial bronchodilators
- Features of life-threatening asthma: SpO2 <92%, silent chest, cyanosis, bradycardia, confusion, PaCO2 rising (normal/high = impending respiratory failure — **normocapnia in asthma is alarming**)
- **Magnesium sulfate IV** (4g over 20 min) for severe/life-threatening
- IV/nebulized salbutamol, IV hydrocortisone, heliox
- **Intubation** if GCS falling, PaCO2 rising despite therapy

### Pneumonia

#### CURB-65 Score (Community-Acquired Pneumonia severity)
- **C**onfusion (new onset)
- **U**rea >7 mmol/L (BUN >19 mg/dL)
- **R**espiratory rate ≥30/min
- **B**lood pressure (SBP <90 or DBP ≤60)
- Age **65** or older

Score 0-1 = outpatient; 2 = hospital admission; 3-5 = consider ICU

**PYQ HOTSPOT: PSI (Pneumonia Severity Index) = more comprehensive; CURB-65 = simpler, bedside tool**

#### CAP Organisms
- **Most common overall**: Streptococcus pneumoniae
- Young adults: Mycoplasma pneumoniae (atypical)
- Elderly/aspiration: Gram-negatives, anaerobes
- Immunocompromised: PCP (Pneumocystis jirovecii), CMV, fungi
- Alcoholics: Klebsiella pneumoniae (currant jelly sputum, upper lobe)
- Bird exposure: Chlamydia psittaci (psittacosis)
- CAP empiric: Beta-lactam + macrolide OR respiratory fluoroquinolone

#### HAP (Hospital-Acquired Pneumonia)
- >48h after admission
- Organisms: **Pseudomonas aeruginosa, MRSA, Acinetobacter, Klebsiella**
- **VAP** (ventilator-associated): >48h after intubation
- Treatment: Broad-spectrum (piperacillin-tazobactam + vancomycin/linezolid for MRSA risk)

#### Atypical Pneumonia Features

| Organism | Key Feature | Diagnosis |
|---|---|---|
| **Mycoplasma pneumoniae** | **Cold agglutinins** (IgM anti-I), bullous myringitis, young adults | Cold agglutinin test, PCR |
| **Legionella pneumophila** | **Hyponatremia**, water coolers/AC, GI symptoms, confusion | **Urinary antigen** (most practical) |
| Chlamydia pneumoniae | Mild, older adults | Serology |
| Coxiella burnetii (Q fever) | Animal exposure (cattle/sheep), hepatitis | Serology (Phase II IgG) |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Legionella = hyponatremia + confusion + water cooler exposure + urinary antigen test**

### Tuberculosis

#### Primary vs Post-Primary TB

| Feature | Primary TB | Post-Primary (Reactivation) TB |
|---|---|---|
| Occurrence | First exposure, any age | Reactivation in adults |
| Location | Mid/lower zones, hilar nodes | **Upper lobe (apical/posterior segments)** |
| Complex | **Ghon complex** (Ghon focus + hilar nodes) | Fibrosis, cavitation |
| Simon's foci | — | **Seeded apical foci** during primary bacteremia |
| Cavitation | No | **Yes (pathognomonic)** |
| Spread | Lymphohematogenous | Contiguous/bronchogenic |

#### Miliary TB
- Hematogenous dissemination
- CXR: Millet seed-like uniform nodules (1-3mm) throughout lung fields
- Most common cause of TB meningitis
- Tuberculin test may be **negative** (anergy)
- Treatment: Same as standard but **longer duration** (9-12 months)

#### DOTS/RNTCP Regimen (India)
**Revised NTEP (National TB Elimination Programme) — Daily regimen**:
- **DS-TB**: 2HRZE/4HR (2 months intensive + 4 months continuation)
- **DR-TB**: Longer regimens based on resistance pattern (bedaquiline-based)

**Drug codes**: H = INH, R = Rifampicin, Z = Pyrazinamide, E = Ethambutol, S = Streptomycin

#### Adverse Effects of TB Drugs
| Drug | Key Side Effect |
|---|---|
| INH | Peripheral neuropathy (give **pyridoxine/Vit B6**), hepatitis, seizures |
| Rifampicin | **Orange discoloration** of fluids, hepatitis, drug interactions (CYP inducer) |
| Pyrazinamide | **Hyperuricemia**, hepatitis, arthralgia |
| Ethambutol | **Optic neuritis** (retrobulbar) — check visual acuity |
| Streptomycin | **Ototoxicity** (vestibular), nephrotoxicity |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Ethambutol = optic neuritis (color vision, then acuity). Streptomycin = NOT used in pregnancy (ototoxicity to fetus)**

#### IRIS (Immune Reconstitution Inflammatory Syndrome)
- Occurs after starting ART in TB-HIV patients (paradoxical worsening)
- Treatment: **NSAIDs**; severe cases — prednisolone

### Pleural Effusion

#### Light's Criteria (Exudate if ANY one met)
1. Pleural fluid protein/serum protein **>0.5**
2. Pleural fluid LDH/serum LDH **>0.6**
3. Pleural fluid LDH >**2/3 upper limit of normal** serum LDH

**Transudate** (<0.5 on all): CHF, cirrhosis, nephrotic syndrome, hypothyroidism

#### Causes by Fluid Type
| Transudate | Exudate |
|---|---|
| CCF (most common bilateral) | Pneumonia (parapneumonic) |
| Cirrhosis (hepatic hydrothorax) | TB (lymphocyte predominant) |
| Nephrotic syndrome | Malignancy |
| Hypothyroidism | Pulmonary embolism |
| Meig's syndrome (ovarian fibroma) | RA, SLE |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Glucose <60 mg/dL in pleural fluid = empyema, TB, RA, esophageal rupture. Amylase elevated = pancreatitis or esophageal rupture**

### Interstitial Lung Disease (ILD)

#### HRCT Patterns

| Pattern | Description | Associated Disease |
|---|---|---|
| **UIP (Usual IP)** | Honeycombing + traction bronchiectasis, basal predominance, subpleural | **IPF** |
| NSIP | Ground-glass + fine reticulation, no honeycombing | CTD-ILD (Scleroderma, PM/DM) |
| DIP | Diffuse ground glass, smokers | DIP |
| COP | Peribronchovascular consolidation, migratory | Organizing pneumonia |
| LIP | Diffuse ground glass, cysts | Sjogren's, HIV |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Honeycombing on HRCT = UIP pattern = IPF. Traction bronchiectasis within honeycombing**

#### IPF Treatment
- **Pirfenidone** (antifibrotic, TGF-β inhibitor) — slows progression
- **Nintedanib** (tyrosine kinase inhibitor) — slows progression
- Neither improves survival significantly; lung transplant is curative
- Steroids: **NOT effective** in IPF (may be harmful)

### Pulmonary Hypertension

#### WHO/Dana Point Classification

| Group | Cause |
|---|---|
| **Group 1 (PAH)** | Idiopathic, heritable, drug/toxin, CTD, portal HTN, HIV, congenital |
| Group 2 | Left heart disease |
| Group 3 | Lung disease/hypoxia |
| Group 4 | Chronic thromboembolic (CTEPH) |
| Group 5 | Multifactorial/unclear |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Group 4 (CTEPH) = only potentially curable with pulmonary thromboendarterectomy**

#### Eisenmenger's Syndrome
- **Left-to-right shunt** (VSD/ASD/PDA) → pulmonary HTN → reversal to right-to-left shunt → **cyanosis**
- Central cyanosis, clubbing, polycythemia
- **Iron deficiency common** (due to erythrocytosis and phlebotomy)
- Most common cause of reversed shunt: **VSD**
- **PDE-5 inhibitors (sildenafil), ERA (bosentan), prostacyclins** — palliation

#### Group 1 PAH Treatment
- CCB (only for vasoreactivity responders on acute testing)
- **PDE-5 inhibitors**: Sildenafil, tadalafil
- **Endothelin receptor antagonists**: Bosentan, ambrisentan
- **Prostacyclins**: Epoprostenol (IV), iloprost (inhaled), treprostinil
- Lung transplant: Definitive

## GASTROENTEROLOGY

### GERD and Esophagus

#### Barrett's Esophagus
- **Specialized intestinal metaplasia** (columnar epithelium replaces squamous in distal esophagus)
- Risk factor for **esophageal adenocarcinoma** (30-40x increased risk)
- PPI therapy — mainstay; does NOT reverse Barrett's
- Surveillance endoscopy: Every 3-5 years (no dysplasia), every 6-12 months (LGD), 3 months (HGD)
- Treatment of HGD: **Radiofrequency ablation** (RFA), endoscopic mucosal resection

#### Achalasia
- **Failure of LES to relax** (aperistalsis of esophagus + hypertensive LES)
- Loss of Auerbach's (myenteric) plexus ganglion cells
- **Chagas disease** (Trypanosoma cruzi) = secondary achalasia in endemic areas
- Manometry: **Incomplete LES relaxation + absent peristalsis** (gold standard)
- Barium swallow: **Bird-beak appearance** (smooth tapering)
- CXR: Absent gastric air bubble, widened mediastinum
- Treatment: **Pneumatic balloon dilation**, Heller's myotomy, POEM (Per-oral endoscopic myotomy)

### Peptic Ulcer Disease

#### H. pylori
- Gram-negative curved rod, **urease-positive**
- Causes: **Duodenal ulcer** (>90%), gastric ulcer (75%), MALT lymphoma, gastric cancer
- Diagnosis: **Urea breath test** (non-invasive, best for treatment confirmation); CLO test (rapid urease, from biopsy); serology (cannot confirm eradication); stool antigen test
- **Triple therapy**: PPI + clarithromycin + amoxicillin (14 days)
- **Quadruple therapy** (if clarithromycin resistance): PPI + bismuth + tetracycline + metronidazole

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Duodenal ulcer — pain relieved by food. Gastric ulcer — pain precipitated by food. Both treated with H. pylori eradication + PPI**

#### Zollinger-Ellison Syndrome (ZES)
- **Gastrinoma** (usually in pancreas or duodenum) → excess gastrin → excess HCl
- Multiple/recurrent duodenal ulcers, **secretory diarrhea**, esophagitis
- Associated with **MEN-1** (20-25%)
- Diagnosis: **Fasting serum gastrin >1000 pg/mL** (diagnostic); **secretin stimulation test** (paradoxical rise in gastrin)
- Treatment: High-dose PPI, then surgical resection

#### Meckel's Diverticulum — Rule of 2s
- **2%** of population
- Within **2 feet** of ileocecal valve
- **2 inches** long
- Contains **2 types** of ectopic tissue (gastric > pancreatic)
- Presents in first **2 years** of life
- Most common presentation: **Painless rectal bleeding** (from gastric acid secretion)
- Diagnosis: **Meckel's scan** (Tc-99m pertechnetate — gastric mucosa uptake)

### Inflammatory Bowel Disease

#### Crohn's vs UC Comparison

| Feature | Crohn's Disease | Ulcerative Colitis |
|---|---|---|
| Location | Any GI tract (mouth to anus), especially **terminal ileum** | **Rectum** (always), extends proximally, continuous |
| Pattern | **Skip lesions** | Continuous, diffuse |
| Depth | **Transmural** | **Mucosal** |
| Endoscopy | **Cobblestone**, linear ulcers, aphthous ulcers | Continuous erythema, friability, pseudopolyps |
| Histology | **Non-caseating granulomas** | Crypt abscesses, goblet cell depletion |
| Fistulas | **Yes** (enterocutaneous, perianal) | No |
| Risk of cancer | Slight increase | **High risk** (especially pancolitis >8-10 years) |
| Smoking | **Increases risk** | **Tobacco protective** (nicotine) |
| Backwash ileitis | No | **Yes** (in pancolitis, retrograde from IC valve) |
| Toxic megacolon | Less common | **More common** |
| Serology | ASCA positive | p-ANCA positive |
| Surgery | Not curative (relapse post-resection) | **Colectomy = curative** |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Crohn's = skip, transmural, granulomas, fistulas. UC = continuous, mucosal, rectum always involved**

#### Extraintestinal Manifestations of IBD
- **Parallel disease activity**: Peripheral arthritis, episcleritis, oral aphthous ulcers, erythema nodosum
- **Independent of disease activity**: Ankylosing spondylitis (HLA-B27), PSC (more in UC), pyoderma gangrenosum, uveitis

#### IBD Treatment
- **Mild-moderate UC**: 5-ASA (mesalazine) — topical enemas for left-sided
- **Moderate-severe**: Steroids (prednisolone), then azathioprine/6-MP maintenance
- **Biologics**: Anti-TNF (infliximab, adalimumab), vedolizumab (gut-specific anti-integrin), ustekinumab (anti-IL-12/23)
- **Crohn's**: Similar; **anti-TNF preferred** for fistulizing disease

### Liver Disease

#### Child-Pugh Score

| Parameter | 1 point | 2 points | 3 points |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bilirubin (mg/dL) | <2 | 2-3 | >3 |
| Albumin (g/dL) | >3.5 | 2.8-3.5 | <2.8 |
| PT (INR) | <1.7 | 1.7-2.3 | >2.3 |
| Ascites | Absent | Mild | Moderate-severe |
| Encephalopathy | None | Grade 1-2 | Grade 3-4 |

Class A (5-6): Good surgical risk; Class B (7-9): Moderate risk; Class C (10-15): Poor surgical risk

**MELD Score**: 3.78×ln[Bilirubin] + 11.2×ln[INR] + 9.57×ln[Creatinine] + 6.43
- **MELD >15** = transplant listed; used for organ allocation priority

#### Complications of Cirrhosis

**Varices/Portal Hypertension**:
- Portal pressure gradient >10 mmHg = clinically significant portal hypertension
- Prophylaxis: **Non-selective beta-blockers** (propranolol, carvedilol)
- Acute variceal bleed: **Terlipressin + endoscopic band ligation + ceftriaxone** (prophylactic antibiotics)
- **TIPSS** (Transjugular intrahepatic portosystemic shunt) for refractory bleeding

**Spontaneous Bacterial Peritonitis (SBP)**:
- **PMN count ≥250 cells/mm³** in ascitic fluid = diagnostic (regardless of culture)
- Most common organism: **E. coli, Klebsiella, S. pneumoniae**
- Treatment: **Cefotaxime 2g IV every 8h for 5 days**; **Albumin infusion** (1.5 g/kg on day 1, 1 g/kg on day 3) — reduces HRS
- Prophylaxis: Norfloxacin/ciprofloxacin for prior SBP or ascitic protein <1 g/dL

**Hepatorenal Syndrome (HRS)**:
- **Functional renal failure** in cirrhosis (kidneys normal, splanchnic vasodilation → reduced renal perfusion)
- Type 1 (HRS-AKI): Rapid, Cr doubles to >2.5 mg/dL in <2 weeks
- Type 2 (HRS-CKD): Slower, associated with refractory ascites
- Treatment: **Terlipressin + albumin** (first-line); liver transplant (definitive)
- **Diagnosis of exclusion**: rule out hypovolemia, nephrotoxic drugs, obstruction, parenchymal renal disease

**Hepatic Encephalopathy**:
- Ammonia (NH3) plays central role
- Grades: I (confusion/altered sleep), II (drowsy, asterixis), III (stupor), IV (coma)
- **Asterixis (flapping tremor)** — grade II-III
- Precipitants: GI bleed, infection, constipation, hypokalemia, sedatives, high protein diet
- Treatment: **Lactulose** (reduces NH3 production/absorption); **Rifaximin** (gut-specific antibiotic); dietary protein not restricted (monitor)

#### Wilson's Disease
- Autosomal recessive, **ATP7B gene** mutation (chromosome 13)
- Copper accumulates in liver, brain (basal ganglia), cornea
- **Kayser-Fleischer rings**: Greenish-brown rings at Descemet's membrane (pathognomonic; seen in slit-lamp)
- Labs: **Low ceruloplasmin** (<20 mg/dL), high 24h urinary copper, high serum copper
- **Sunflower cataract** (copper deposition)
- Treatment: **D-penicillamine** (first-line); trientine (if intolerant); zinc (maintenance); liver transplant for fulminant

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Wilson's = KF rings + low ceruloplasmin + hepatic/neuropsychiatric features + Coombs-negative hemolytic anemia**

#### Hemochromatosis
- **HFE gene mutation** (C282Y/H63D); C282Y homozygous = most common
- Iron overload: liver (cirrhosis), pancreas (diabetes), heart (cardiomyopathy), joints (arthropathy), skin (bronze), gonads (hypogonadism)
- "**Bronze diabetes**" = skin bronzing + diabetes + cirrhosis
- Labs: **High serum ferritin, high transferrin saturation (>45%)**; liver biopsy (hepatic iron index)
- Treatment: **Phlebotomy** (500 mL weekly until ferritin <50 μg/L); deferasirox/deferiprone (if phlebotomy not possible)

#### Autoimmune Hepatitis (AIH)
- Young women, associated with other autoimmune diseases
- **Type 1**: Anti-smooth muscle (ASMA), ANA positive
- **Type 2**: Anti-LKM1 (liver-kidney microsomal), anti-LC1
- High IgG, elevated transaminases, interface hepatitis (piecemeal necrosis) on biopsy
- Treatment: **Prednisolone + azathioprine**

#### Primary Biliary Cholangitis (PBC)
- Middle-aged women, fatigue, pruritus, jaundice (late)
- **AMA (Anti-Mitochondrial Antibody)** — specifically **anti-M2** — >95% sensitivity/specificity
- **High ALP**, mild transaminases, high IgM
- Liver biopsy: Florid duct lesion (granulomatous destruction of bile ducts)
- Treatment: **UDCA (ursodeoxycholic acid)**; obeticholic acid (second-line); transplant for end-stage

**PYQ HOTSPOT: PBC = middle-aged woman + pruritus + AMA positive + high IgM**

#### Primary Sclerosing Cholangitis (PSC)
- Young men, **associated with UC** (70-80% of PSC patients have UC)
- **p-ANCA positive** (60-80%)
- Multifocal strictures of intra and extrahepatic bile ducts
- MRCP: **Beaded appearance** of bile ducts (alternating strictures and dilatations)
- High ALP, high IgG4 (in IgG4-PSC — respond to steroids; classic PSC does not)
- Risk of **cholangiocarcinoma** (15% lifetime risk)
- No effective medical treatment; UDCA not proven to reduce mortality; liver transplant

### Pancreatitis

#### Causes — I GET SMASHED Mnemonic
**I**diopathic, **G**allstones (most common in India and worldwide), **E**thanol (most common in Western countries), **T**rauma, **S**teroids, **M**umps (viral), **A**utoimmune, **S**corpion sting, **H**yperlipidemia (TG >1000)/Hypercalcemia, **E**RCP, **D**rugs

**PYQ HOTSPOT: In India, gallstones = most common cause. In West, alcohol = most common**

#### Ranson's Criteria (Severity Assessment)
**At Admission** (5 criteria):
- Age >55
- WBC >16,000/mm³
- Blood glucose >200 mg/dL
- LDH >350 IU/L
- AST >250 IU/L

**At 48 Hours** (5 criteria):
- HCT fall >10%
- BUN rise >5 mg/dL
- Ca²⁺ <8 mg/dL
- PaO2 <60 mmHg
- Base deficit >4 mEq/L
- Fluid sequestration >6L

Score ≥3 = severe pancreatitis; Each criterion scores 1 point

#### Atlanta Classification (2012)
- **Interstitial edematous**: Mild, self-limiting
- **Necrotizing**: Necrosis of pancreas or peripancreatic tissue
- Severity: Mild (no organ failure, no local complications) → Moderate (transient organ failure <48h) → Severe (persistent organ failure >48h)
- Local complications: ANC (acute necrotic collection), WOPN (walled-off necrosis), APF (acute peripancreatic fluid collection), pseudocyst

#### Chronic Pancreatitis
- Causes: Alcohol (most common), tropical (nutritional), hereditary, autoimmune, idiopathic
- Features: Steatorrhea (fat malabsorption), diabetes (late), epigastric pain
- **Ductal stones (calcifications on plain X-ray)** — pathognomonic
- Treatment: **Pancreatic enzyme replacement** (lipase for steatorrhea), pain management, treat diabetes, endoscopic drainage

## NEUROLOGY

### Stroke

#### Ischemic vs Hemorrhagic

| Feature | Ischemic | Hemorrhagic (ICH/SAH) |
|---|---|---|
| Onset | Often on waking, gradual | **Sudden, maximal at onset** |
| Headache | Usually absent | **Severe "thunderclap" (SAH)** |
| CT | Hypodensity (after 24-48h) | **Immediate hyperdensity** |
| BP | Moderate elevation | Markedly elevated (ICH) |
| LP (SAH) | Normal | **Xanthochromia** (after 12h) |

#### tPA Criteria (Ischemic Stroke)
**Eligibility**: Age ≥18, ischemic stroke with measurable deficit, <3-4.5 hours from onset (Extended window to 4.5h)
**Absolute Contraindications**: ICH on CT, onset unknown, BP >185/110 (after treatment), active bleeding, recent intracranial surgery, INR >1.7, platelets <100,000
**Dose**: Alteplase 0.9 mg/kg (max 90 mg); 10% as bolus, remainder over 60 min

**Mechanical Thrombectomy**: Large vessel occlusion (M1, ICA), up to **24 hours** from onset (if penumbra on perfusion imaging), NIHSS-guided

#### NIHSS (NIH Stroke Scale)
Assesses: Consciousness, gaze, visual fields, facial palsy, motor arm/leg, ataxia, sensory, language, dysarthria, extinction/inattention
Score 0 = normal; **>25 = severe stroke**

#### Lacunar Infarcts
- Small vessel (perforating arteries) disease, deep structures
- **Pure motor hemiparesis**: Posterior limb of internal capsule or basis pontis
- **Pure sensory**: Thalamus (VPL nucleus)
- **Ataxic hemiparesis**: Basis pontis or internal capsule
- **Dysarthria-clumsy hand**: Basis pontis
- **No cortical signs** (no aphasia, no hemineglect)

#### TIA (Transient Ischemic Attack)
- Focal neurological symptoms, **<24 hours** (most last <1 hour), no infarct on MRI
- **ABCD2 Score**:
  - **A**ge ≥60 (1)
  - **B**P ≥140/90 (1)
  - **C**linical features: unilateral weakness (2), speech disturbance without weakness (1)
  - **D**uration: ≥60 min (2), 10-59 min (1)
  - **D**iabetes (1)
  - Score 4-5 = intermediate risk; ≥6 = high risk (2-day stroke risk ~8%)

### Headache

#### Migraine
- Unilateral, pulsating, moderate-severe, nausea/vomiting, photo/phonophobia
- **POUND Criteria** (≥4 = likely migraine): **P**ulsatile, duration **O**ne day (4-72h), **U**nilateral, **N**ausea, **D**isabling
- **Aura**: Homonymous visual (most common), sensory, motor, language; precedes headache by <1h, fully reversible
- Treatment acute: **Triptans** (sumatriptan — 5-HT1B/1D agonist), NSAIDs, antiemetics
- Prophylaxis: **Propranolol** (first-line), topiramate, amitriptyline, valproate, **CGRP antagonists** (erenumab, fremanezumab — new biologics)
- **Contraindications to triptans**: IHD, uncontrolled HTN, hemiplegic migraine, basilar migraine, stroke

#### Cluster Headache
- Strictly **unilateral periorbital** pain, excruciating, 15-180 minutes
- Autonomic features: **Ptosis, miosis (Horner's)**, lacrimation, rhinorrhea, facial flushing
- Male predominance, **nocturnal**, seasonal clusters
- Patient is **restless** (unlike migraine — prefers to lie still)
- Acute treatment: **100% O2** (first-line, 15L/min for 15 min), sumatriptan SC/intranasal
- Prophylaxis: **Verapamil** (drug of choice), lithium, topiramate

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Cluster = unilateral + Horner's + 100% O2. Migraine = bilateral throbbing + photophobia + triptans**

#### Thunderclap Headache
- Maximal pain at onset, "worst headache of life"
- **Subarachnoid hemorrhage** until proven otherwise
- CT head first (sensitive >95% in first 6h); if negative → **LP** (xanthochromia after 12h, spectrophotometry gold standard)
- Also: Cerebral venous thrombosis, reversible cerebral vasoconstriction syndrome (RCVS), hypertensive emergency

### Epilepsy

#### ILAE Classification

**Focal (Partial) Seizures**:
- Without impaired awareness (simple partial)
- With impaired awareness (complex partial)
- Focal to bilateral tonic-clonic

**Generalized Seizures**:
- Absence (typical — 3Hz spike-wave), myoclonic, clonic, tonic, tonic-clonic, atonic

#### Drug of Choice by Seizure Type

| Seizure Type | First-Line Drug |
|---|---|
| Generalized tonic-clonic (GTCS) | **Valproate** (sodium valproate); lamotrigine alternative |
| Absence | **Ethosuximide** (or valproate) |
| Myoclonic | **Valproate** |
| Focal seizures | **Carbamazepine**, lamotrigine, levetiracetam |
| Infantile spasms (West syndrome) | **ACTH**, vigabatrin |
| Lennox-Gastaut | Valproate, lamotrigine, clonazepam |
| Juvenile myoclonic epilepsy (JME) | Valproate (levetiracetam if pregnancy planned) |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Carbamazepine and phenytoin WORSEN absence and myoclonic seizures. Avoid in these**

**Drugs Contraindicated in Pregnancy**: Valproate (neural tube defects), carbamazepine, phenytoin. **Lamotrigine and levetiracetam** — safer.

#### Status Epilepticus Management
- Defined: Seizure >5 minutes OR ≥2 seizures without return to baseline
1. **0-5 min**: IV access, O2, glucose check
2. **5-20 min (Benzodiazepines)**: Lorazepam 4 mg IV (or diazepam 10 mg IV/rectal; midazolam buccal/IM)
3. **20-40 min (Second-line)**: Levetiracetam, phenytoin, valproate IV
4. **>40 min (Refractory)**: Intubation, general anesthesia (thiopentone/propofol/midazolam infusion)

### Parkinson's Disease

- **TRAP**: **T**remor (resting, pill-rolling, 4-6 Hz), **R**igidity (cogwheel/leadpipe), **A**kinesia/bradykinesia, **P**ostural instability
- **Lewy bodies** (alpha-synuclein aggregates) in substantia nigra pars compacta
- Loss of **dopaminergic neurons** in **substantia nigra** → dopamine depletion in striatum
- Asymmetric onset, tremor most visible feature
- Non-motor: anosmia (earliest), constipation, REM sleep behavior disorder, depression, dementia (late)

#### Treatment
- **Levodopa + Carbidopa** (gold standard, most effective): Carbidopa prevents peripheral conversion → more enters CNS; complications: wearing off, on-off fluctuations, dyskinesias
- **Dopamine agonists**: Pramipexole, ropinirole, rotigotine — first-line in young patients (delay levodopa, reduce dyskinesia risk)
- **MAO-B inhibitors**: Selegiline, rasagiline — mild symptoms, neuroprotective (controversial)
- **COMT inhibitors**: Entacapone, tolcapone — reduce "off" time (give with levodopa)
- **Anticholinergics**: Benztropine, trihexyphenidyl — **mainly for tremor**, not motor symptoms; **contraindicated in elderly** (confusion, urinary retention)
- **DBS (Deep Brain Stimulation)**: Subthalamic nucleus or globus pallidus interna — for advanced disease, motor fluctuations

**PYQ HOTSPOT: "Parkinson disease drug of choice = levodopa/carbidopa. Most common cause of failure = dopamine dysregulation. DBS target = subthalamic nucleus"**

### Multiple Sclerosis

- **McDonald Criteria (2017)**: Dissemination in space (DIS) + Dissemination in time (DIT)
  - DIS: ≥2 different CNS areas (periventricular, cortical/juxtacortical, infratentorial, spinal cord)
  - DIT: New T2/gadolinium lesion on follow-up MRI, or simultaneous enhancing + non-enhancing lesions
- **MRI**: Periventricular plaques ("Dawson's fingers"), corpus callosum lesions, ovoid perpendicular lesions
- **Lhermitte's sign**: Electric shock sensation down spine on neck flexion (cervical spinal cord involvement)
- **Optic neuritis**: Monocular vision loss, painful (retrobulbar neuritis), afferent pupillary defect (Marcus Gunn)
- **Uhthoff's phenomenon**: Worsening with heat
- **Oligoclonal bands** (IgG) in CSF — present in >90%
- Most common type: **Relapsing-Remitting MS (RRMS)**
- Evoked potentials: Visual EP (most commonly abnormal)

#### MS Treatment
- **Acute relapse**: IV methylprednisolone (does not alter long-term course)
- **Disease-modifying therapy (DMT)**:
  - First-line: **Interferon-beta-1a/1b**, glatiramer acetate, dimethyl fumarate, teriflunomide
  - High efficacy: **Natalizumab** (anti-VLA-4; risk of PML with JC virus), **Alemtuzumab** (anti-CD52), **Ocrelizumab** (anti-CD20)
- Symptom management: Baclofen (spasticity), amantadine/modafinil (fatigue), oxybutynin (bladder)

### Meningitis

#### CSF Analysis

| Feature | Normal | Bacterial | Viral | TB/Fungal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Appearance | Clear | Turbid/purulent | Clear | Opalescent/xanthochromic |
| Pressure | 80-180 mmH2O | Elevated | Normal/mildly elevated | Elevated |
| WBC | <5 (lymphocytes) | **>1000, PMN** | 10-500, **lymphocytes** | 100-500, **lymphocytes** |
| Protein | 15-45 mg/dL | **>100 (markedly elevated)** | Normal to mildly elevated | **High (100-500)** |
| Glucose | 60-80% serum | **<45 (low)** | Normal | **Low** |
| Other | — | Culture, Gram stain | PCR (HSV) | AFB, India ink (crypto), ADA elevated |

#### Organisms by Age
| Age | Common Organisms |

## ENDOCRINOLOGY

### Diabetes Mellitus

#### ADA Diagnostic Criteria (Any ONE):
1. **FPG ≥126 mg/dL** (fasting ≥8 hours)
2. **2h PG ≥200 mg/dL** during 75g OGTT
3. **HbA1c ≥6.5%**
4. **Random PG ≥200 mg/dL + symptoms** (polyuria, polydipsia, weight loss)

**Pre-diabetes**: FPG 100-125, 2h PG 140-199, HbA1c 5.7-6.4%

#### Type 1 vs Type 2

| Feature | Type 1 | Type 2 |
|---|---|---|
| Mechanism | Autoimmune beta-cell destruction | Insulin resistance + progressive beta-cell failure |
| Onset | Usually young (<30), acute | Usually older, insidious |
| BMI | Normal/thin | Obese |
| Antibodies | **Anti-GAD, anti-IA2, anti-islet cell, anti-insulin** | Usually negative |
| Ketosis | **Prone (DKA)** | Resistant (HHS more common) |
| C-peptide | **Low/absent** | Normal/elevated |
| Treatment | **Insulin** (mandatory) | Lifestyle + oral agents ± insulin |

#### DKA vs HHS

| Feature | DKA | HHS |
|---|---|---|
| Type | Usually T1DM | Usually T2DM |
| Blood glucose | 250-600 mg/dL | **>600 mg/dL** |
| Ketones | **Present (++++)** | Absent/trace |
| pH | **<7.3** | Normal/near-normal |
| Bicarbonate | **<18 mEq/L** | >15 mEq/L |
| Anion gap | **High** (>12) | Normal |
| Osmolality | Mildly elevated | **>320 mOsm/kg** |
| Consciousness | Variable | **Profound obtundation/coma** |
| Mortality | 1-5% | **5-15%** |

**DKA Management**:
1. **IV Normal Saline** (aggressive resuscitation — 1L in first hour)
2. **IV Regular Insulin** (0.1 unit/kg/h; start only after K⁺ >3.5 mEq/L)
3. **Potassium replacement** (add to IV fluids when K⁺ <5.5; aggressive if <3.5)
4. **Bicarbonate** only if pH <6.9
5. Switch to dextrose when glucose <200 mg/dL (to prevent hypoglycemia while continuing insulin)
6. Transition to SC insulin when anion gap closed and patient eating

**PYQ HOTSPOT: DKA = high anion gap metabolic acidosis + ketones. Insulin drives K+ into cells → must monitor K+ carefully**

#### Dawn vs Somogyi Phenomenon
- **Dawn**: Early morning hyperglycemia due to growth hormone surge (physiological); glucose rises from 3 AM
- **Somogyi**: Morning hyperglycemia after **nocturnal hypoglycemia** (rebound); counter-regulatory response; glucose drops at 3 AM then rises

#### Diabetic Complications
- **Nephropathy**: Stage 1 (hyperfiltration) → 2 (normoalbuminuria) → 3 (microalbuminuria 30-300 mg/day) → 4 (macroalbuminuria) → 5 (ESRD)
- **Retinopathy**: NPDR (microaneurysms, dot/blot hemorrhages, cotton wool spots) → PDR (neovascularization, risk of vitreous hemorrhage, detachment); **macular edema = most common cause of blindness**
- **Neuropathy**: Distal symmetric polyneuropathy (most common — stocking/glove), autonomic (gastroparesis, orthostatic hypotension, bladder, ED), mononeuropathy multiplex

### Thyroid Disorders

#### Hypothyroidism
- Primary (high TSH, low T4): Hashimoto's (anti-TPO, anti-thyroglobulin), iodine deficiency (most common worldwide), post-radioiodine/surgery
- Features: Fatigue, weight gain, cold intolerance, constipation, bradycardia, dry skin, coarse hair, periorbital puffiness, delayed relaxation of reflexes, **carpal tunnel syndrome**
- **Myxedema coma**: Severe hypothyroidism + precipitant (cold, infection, drugs); hypothermia, bradycardia, hypoventilation, altered consciousness
  - Treatment: **IV T3 (liothyronine)** or T4, IV hydrocortisone (adrenal insufficiency may coexist), warming

#### Hyperthyroidism — Graves' Disease
- **TSI (Thyroid-stimulating immunoglobulin) / TRAb** (TSH receptor antibody) — pathognomonic of Graves'
- Features: Weight loss, palpitations, heat intolerance, tremor, diarrhea, oligomenorrhea
- **Specific to Graves'**: **Pretibial myxedema** (infiltrative dermopathy — shin), **thyroid acropachy** (clubbing + periosteal new bone), **exophthalmos/proptosis**
- Treatment: **Carbimazole/propylthiouracil (PTU)**, beta-blockers (symptom control), radioiodine (definitive for most), thyroidectomy
- **PTU preferred in first trimester of pregnancy** (carbimazole — aplasia cutis); carbimazole preferred in second/third trimester (PTU hepatotoxicity)

#### Thyroid Storm
- **Burch-Wartofsky Score**: Temperature, HR, CNS effects, GI dysfunction, AF, precipitating illness → score >45 = thyroid storm
- Treatment: **PTU 600mg loading**, propranolol, hydrocortisone, lugol's iodine (given 1h after PTU — blocks thyroid release), treat precipitant, cooling

#### Thyroid Nodule Evaluation
- **Ultrasound first**: Assess for malignant features (hypoechoic, irregular margins, microcalcifications, taller-than-wide, vascularity)
- **FNAC (Fine Needle Aspiration Cytology)**: For nodules ≥1 cm (or smaller with suspicious features)
- **Bethesda System**: I (non-diagnostic) → VI (malignant)
- **TSH suppressed + hot nodule on scan**: Thyroid adenoma; FNAC less useful (malignancy rare in hot nodules)

### Adrenal Disorders

#### Addison's Disease (Primary Adrenal Insufficiency)
- Low cortisol + low aldosterone + **high ACTH** (feedback)
- Causes: Autoimmune (most common in developed countries), TB (most common worldwide)
- Features: **Hyperpigmentation** (high ACTH/MSH), weakness, fatigue, weight loss, salt craving, **hyponatremia, hyperkalemia, hypoglycemia**, hypotension
- **Waterhouse-Friderichsen syndrome**: Bilateral adrenal hemorrhage (N. meningitidis, DIC)
- Diagnosis: **Short Synacthen test** (0, 30, 60 min cortisol after synthetic ACTH); cortisol <500 nmol/L at 30 min = insufficient
- Treatment: **Hydrocortisone + Fludrocortisone** (primary); prednisolone alone (secondary — no aldosterone deficiency)
- **Addisonian crisis**: IV hydrocortisone 100mg bolus, then IV fluids (normal saline)

#### Cushing's Syndrome

**Causes**:
1. **Iatrogenic** (exogenous steroid use) — most common overall
2. **Pituitary adenoma (Cushing's DISEASE)** — most common endogenous cause; bilateral adrenal hyperplasia; **ACTH-secreting**
3. **Ectopic ACTH** (small cell lung cancer, carcinoid) — very high ACTH, rapid onset
4. **Adrenal adenoma/carcinoma** — low ACTH (autonomous cortisol production)

**Dexamethasone Suppression Test**:
| Test | Cushing's Disease | Ectopic ACTH | Adrenal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Low-dose (1mg overnight) | Does NOT suppress | Does NOT suppress | Does NOT suppress |
| High-dose (8mg overnight) | **Suppresses (>50% reduction)** | Does NOT suppress | Does NOT suppress |
| ACTH levels | Mildly elevated | **Very high** | **Low** |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: High-dose DST suppression = pituitary source (Cushing's disease). No suppression + very high ACTH = ectopic. Low ACTH = adrenal**

#### Conn's Syndrome (Primary Hyperaldosteronism)
- **Aldosterone-producing adenoma** (Conn's) or bilateral adrenal hyperplasia
- Features: **Hypertension + Hypokalemia** (no edema — escape phenomenon) + **Low renin**
- Metabolic alkalosis, weakness, polyuria
- Diagnosis: **Aldosterone:renin ratio >30** (screening); adrenal CT; adrenal vein sampling (lateralization)
- Treatment: Surgical (unilateral adrenalectomy for adenoma); spironolactone/eplerenone (bilateral hyperplasia or surgical contraindication)

#### Pheochromocytoma
**Rule of 10**:
- 10% bilateral, 10% malignant, 10% extra-adrenal (paraganglioma), 10% in children, 10% familial (MEN2, VHL, NF1, SDH mutations)

- Features: **Paroxysmal hypertension** (episodic), headache, sweating, palpitations (pheo triad)
- Diagnosis: **24h urine VMA/metanephrines** (most sensitive); plasma metanephrines (very sensitive); CT/MRI for localization; **MIBG scan** (functional imaging)
- Treatment: **Alpha-blockade FIRST** (phenoxybenzamine/phentolamine) → THEN beta-blockade (to prevent hypertensive crisis from unopposed alpha); **never start beta-blockers first**
- Surgical: Laparoscopic adrenalectomy

#### MEN Syndromes

| Syndrome | Components |
|---|---|
| **MEN 1 (Werner's)** | **3 P's**: Pituitary (prolactinoma most common), Parathyroid (most common manifestation), Pancreas (gastrinoma most common) |
| **MEN 2A (Sipple)** | Medullary thyroid cancer (MTC) + Pheochromocytoma + Parathyroid hyperplasia |
| **MEN 2B** | MTC + Pheochromocytoma + Marfanoid habitus + Mucosal neuromas (NO parathyroid) |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: MEN 1 gene = Menin (tumor suppressor, chromosome 11). MEN 2 = RET proto-oncogene mutation**
**MEN 2A/2B: Thyroidectomy performed prophylactically based on RET mutation type**

## RHEUMATOLOGY

### Rheumatoid Arthritis

#### ACR/EULAR 2010 Classification Criteria (score ≥6 = RA)

| Domain | Score |
|---|---|
| Joint involvement: 1 large joint | 0 |
| 2-10 large joints | 1 |
| 1-3 small joints | 2 |
| 4-10 small joints | 3 |
| >10 joints (at least 1 small) | 5 |
| Serology: Low positive RF/anti-CCP | 2 |
| High positive RF/anti-CCP | 3 |
| Acute-phase reactants: abnormal CRP or ESR | 1 |
| Duration: ≥6 weeks | 1 |

- **RF (Rheumatoid Factor)**: IgM against IgG Fc; positive in 80% RA; also in other diseases (SLE, Sjogren's, endocarditis)
- **Anti-CCP (Anti-cyclic citrullinated peptide)**: More specific for RA (specificity ~95%); precedes disease by years; associated with erosive disease

#### Steinbrocker Radiological Stages
- Stage I: Periarticular osteoporosis
- Stage II: Joint space narrowing + osteoporosis
- Stage III: Cartilage/bone erosions + deformities
- Stage IV: Fibrous/bony ankylosis

#### RA Features
- Symmetrical small joint (MCPs, PIPs) polyarthritis, morning stiffness >1h
- **Ulnar deviation, swan neck, boutonniere deformities**
- **Baker's cyst** (popliteal), **carpal tunnel syndrome**, atlantoaxial subluxation (C1-C2)
- Extraarticular: Rheumatoid nodules (extensor surfaces), vasculitis, pulmonary (UIP/NSIP pattern), Felty's syndrome (RA + splenomegaly + neutropenia), **Caplan's syndrome** (RA + pneumoconiosis)

#### RA Treatment
- **MTX (Methotrexate)**: First-line DMARD; folate supplementation required; monitor LFTs
- **Hydroxychloroquine**: Mild disease; ophthalmic monitoring (maculopathy)
- **Leflunomide**: Alternative to MTX
- **Sulfasalazine**: Used in combination (COBRA regimen)
- **Biologics** (if inadequate response to 2 DMARDs):
  - Anti-TNF: Etanercept, infliximab, adalimumab, certolizumab, golimumab
  - Anti-CD20: Rituximab
  - Anti-IL-6R: Tocilizumab
  - T-cell costimulation inhibitor: Abatacept
  - JAK inhibitors: Tofacitinib, baricitinib (oral targeted synthetic DMARDs)
- **DAS28 score**: <2.6 = remission; 2.6-3.2 = low activity; 3.2-5.1 = moderate; >5.1 = high activity

### Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE)

#### ACR 1997 Criteria (SOAP BRAIN MD Mnemonic) — ≥4/11 = SLE
- **S**erositis (pleuritis/pericarditis)
- **O**ral ulcers (painless)
- **A**rthritis (non-erosive, ≥2 joints)
- **P**hotosensitivity
- **B**lood disorders (hemolytic anemia, leukopenia, lymphopenia, thrombocytopenia)
- **R**enal (proteinuria >0.5g/day or cellular casts)
- **A**NA positive (most sensitive, ≥95%)
- **I**mmunologic: anti-dsDNA, anti-Sm, antiphospholipid Ab, false + VDRL
- **N**eurological (seizures, psychosis)
- **M**alar rash (butterfly rash — spares nasolabial folds)
- **D**iscoid rash

#### Antibodies in SLE
- **ANA**: Most sensitive (95-99%); also in many other conditions
- **Anti-dsDNA**: Specific for SLE (70%); correlates with **disease activity and nephritis**
- **Anti-Sm (anti-Smith)**: **Most specific** for SLE (25-30%); does NOT correlate with activity
- **Anti-Ro/SSA**: SLE, Sjogren's; associated with neonatal lupus, congenital heart block
- **Anti-La/SSB**: Sjogren's primarily
- **Anti-histone**: **Drug-induced lupus** (procainamide, hydralazine, isoniazid, quinidine)
- **Antiphospholipid antibodies**: APLS overlap

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Most specific antibody in SLE = anti-Sm. Most correlated with disease activity = anti-dsDNA**

#### Lupus Nephritis (ISN/RPS Classification)
| Class | Description |
|---|---|
| I | Minimal mesangial |
| II | Mesangial proliferative |
| III | Focal proliferative (<50% glomeruli) |
| **IV** | **Diffuse proliferative (>50% glomeruli)** — most severe/common, **"wire-loop" lesions** |
| V | Membranous |
| VI | Advanced sclerosing |

Class III/IV: Cyclophosphamide (Eurolupus regimen) or mycophenolate mofetil (MMF) + steroids

#### APLS (Antiphospholipid Syndrome)
- Recurrent thrombosis (arterial/venous) and/or pregnancy morbidity
- **Lab criteria**: Lupus anticoagulant, anticardiolipin IgG/IgM, anti-beta2-glycoprotein I — on **2 occasions ≥12 weeks apart**
- **Paradox**: aPTT prolonged in lab (LA effect) but THROMBOTIC in vivo
- Treatment: **Anticoagulation** (warfarin target INR 2-3; INR 3-4 if recurrent arterial)
- Obstetric APLS: Low-dose aspirin + LMWH

### Gout

- **Monosodium urate (MSU) crystals**: **Negatively birefringent** under polarized light (yellow when parallel to slow vibration axis)
- Hyperuricemia: Uric acid >6.8 mg/dL (above saturation threshold)
- Pseudogout: Calcium pyrophosphate crystals — **positively birefringent** (blue when parallel)

#### Phases
- Asymptomatic hyperuricemia → Acute gouty arthritis → Intercritical period → Chronic tophaceous gout
- **First MTP joint** (podagra) — most classic; also ankle, knee

#### Triggers
- Purine-rich foods (red meat, shellfish), alcohol, dehydration, diuretics, low-dose aspirin, cytotoxic therapy (tumor lysis)

#### Treatment
- **Acute attack**: **Colchicine** (within 36h, most effective early), **NSAIDs** (indomethacin), corticosteroids (if contraindications to above)
- **Do NOT start/stop urate-lowering therapy during acute attack** (may prolong/precipitate flare)
- **Prophylaxis/long-term ULT**:
  - **Allopurinol** (xanthine oxidase inhibitor) — first-line; target uric acid <6 mg/dL (<5 in tophi)
  - **Febuxostat** (selective XOI) — if allopurinol intolerant
  - **Probenecid** (uricosuric) — avoid if renal stones
  - Start ULT with colchicine cover (0.5mg twice daily for 6 months)

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Allopurinol + azathioprine = dangerous interaction (azathioprine toxicity — reduce azathioprine dose by 75%)**

### Spondyloarthropathies

#### Ankylosing Spondylitis (AS)
- **HLA-B27** (>90% of patients)
- Young men, inflammatory back pain (better with activity, worse with rest)
- Sacroiliitis (bilateral, symmetric), ascending spinal involvement
- **Bamboo spine** on X-ray (syndesmophytes — vertical ossification of annulus fibrosus)
- Enthesitis (inflammation at tendon/ligament insertion — Achilles tendon, plantar fascia)
- Extra-articular: **Anterior uveitis** (most common), aortitis, apical lung fibrosis, restrictive lung disease, conduction defects
- Modified New York Criteria: Clinical (back pain ≥3 months, limitation, reduced chest expansion) + radiological (sacroiliitis grade ≥2 bilateral)
- Treatment: **NSAIDs** (first-line), anti-TNF biologics (if NSAID failure), **NO role for DMARDs** for axial disease

#### Reiter's Syndrome (Reactive Arthritis)
- Mnemonic: "**Can't see, can't pee, can't climb a tree**" = **Conjunctivitis + Urethritis + Arthritis**
- Post-infectious: Chlamydia (genitourinary), Salmonella/Shigella/Yersinia/Campylobacter (enteric)
- Skin lesions: **Keratoderma blenorrhagica** (palms/soles), **Circinate balanitis**
- Oral ulcers, nail changes (similar to psoriasis)
- Self-limiting; NSAIDs + antibiotics (for Chlamydia if active infection)

**PYQ HOTSPOT: HLA-B27 positive in 70% of Reiter's. Genitourinary form more common in West, enteric more common in developing countries**

### Vasculitis

#### Giant Cell Arteritis (GCA) / Temporal Arteritis
- Elderly (>50 years), **temporal headache**, scalp tenderness, jaw claudication
- **Most feared complication**: **Anterior ischemic optic neuropathy → blindness** (irreversible)
- Elevated **ESR >50 mm/h** (sometimes >100)
- Diagnosis: **Temporal artery biopsy** (skip lesions — giant cell granulomas)
- 50% association with **Polymyalgia Rheumatica** (shoulder/hip girdle aching, high ESR, normal CK)
- Treatment: **High-dose prednisolone** (40-60 mg/day) — start **immediately** before biopsy if visual symptoms

#### Takayasu's Arteritis (Pulseless Disease)
- Young women, large vessel vasculitis (aorta and branches)
- **Absent pulses** in upper limbs, BP differential >10 mmHg between arms
- Features: Subclavian steal, aortic regurgitation, renal artery stenosis (secondary HTN)
- ESR elevated, angiography (gold standard for diagnosis), MRA/CTA
- Treatment: **Corticosteroids**; MTX or azathioprine as steroid-sparing; stenting/bypass for critical stenosis

#### Granulomatosis with Polyangiitis (GPA / Wegener's)
- **Triad**: Upper respiratory tract (saddle nose deformity, nasal septum perforation, sinusitis) + Lower respiratory (pulmonary nodules, cavitating lesions) + **Necrotizing glomerulonephritis** (crescentic GN)
- **c-ANCA (anti-PR3)** — highly specific
- Biopsy: Necrotizing granulomatous vasculitis
- Treatment: **Cyclophosphamide + prednisolone** (remission induction); rituximab (alternative); azathioprine/MTX (maintenance)

#### Polyarteritis Nodosa (PAN)
- Medium vessel vasculitis
- **p-ANCA negative** (distinguishes from microscopic polyangiitis)
- Renal involvement: Renovascular hypertension, renal infarcts (**NOT glomerulonephritis** — spares glomerular capillaries)
- Associated with **Hepatitis B** (30%)
- Features: Mononeuritis multiplex, testicular pain, livedo reticularis, mesenteric ischemia
- Diagnosis: Angiography (microaneurysms, beading), biopsy
- Treatment: Prednisolone ± cyclophosphamide; **treat HBV** if associated

**PYQ HOTSPOT: PAN spares the lungs and glomeruli (medium vessel). GPA involves both (small vessel + granulomatous)**
