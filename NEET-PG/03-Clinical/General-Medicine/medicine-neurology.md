## NEUROLOGY

### Stroke

#### Ischemic vs Hemorrhagic

| Feature | Ischemic | Hemorrhagic (ICH/SAH) |
|---|---|---|
| Onset | Often on waking, gradual | **Sudden, maximal at onset** |
| Headache | Usually absent | **Severe "thunderclap" (SAH)** |
| CT | Hypodensity (after 24-48h) | **Immediate hyperdensity** |
| BP | Moderate elevation | Markedly elevated (ICH) |
| LP (SAH) | Normal | **Xanthochromia** (after 12h) |

#### tPA Criteria (Ischemic Stroke)
**Eligibility**: Age ≥18, ischemic stroke with measurable deficit, <3-4.5 hours from onset (Extended window to 4.5h)
**Absolute Contraindications**: ICH on CT, onset unknown, BP >185/110 (after treatment), active bleeding, recent intracranial surgery, INR >1.7, platelets <100,000
**Dose**: Alteplase 0.9 mg/kg (max 90 mg); 10% as bolus, remainder over 60 min

**Mechanical Thrombectomy**: Large vessel occlusion (M1, ICA), up to **24 hours** from onset (if penumbra on perfusion imaging), NIHSS-guided

#### NIHSS (NIH Stroke Scale)
Assesses: Consciousness, gaze, visual fields, facial palsy, motor arm/leg, ataxia, sensory, language, dysarthria, extinction/inattention
Score 0 = normal; **>25 = severe stroke**

#### Lacunar Infarcts
- Small vessel (perforating arteries) disease, deep structures
- **Pure motor hemiparesis**: Posterior limb of internal capsule or basis pontis
- **Pure sensory**: Thalamus (VPL nucleus)
- **Ataxic hemiparesis**: Basis pontis or internal capsule
- **Dysarthria-clumsy hand**: Basis pontis
- **No cortical signs** (no aphasia, no hemineglect)

#### TIA (Transient Ischemic Attack)
- Focal neurological symptoms, **<24 hours** (most last <1 hour), no infarct on MRI
- **ABCD2 Score**:
  - **A**ge ≥60 (1)
  - **B**P ≥140/90 (1)
  - **C**linical features: unilateral weakness (2), speech disturbance without weakness (1)
  - **D**uration: ≥60 min (2), 10-59 min (1)
  - **D**iabetes (1)
  - Score 4-5 = intermediate risk; ≥6 = high risk (2-day stroke risk ~8%)

### Headache

#### Migraine
- Unilateral, pulsating, moderate-severe, nausea/vomiting, photo/phonophobia
- **POUND Criteria** (≥4 = likely migraine): **P**ulsatile, duration **O**ne day (4-72h), **U**nilateral, **N**ausea, **D**isabling
- **Aura**: Homonymous visual (most common), sensory, motor, language; precedes headache by <1h, fully reversible
- Treatment acute: **Triptans** (sumatriptan — 5-HT1B/1D agonist), NSAIDs, antiemetics
- Prophylaxis: **Propranolol** (first-line), topiramate, amitriptyline, valproate, **CGRP antagonists** (erenumab, fremanezumab — new biologics)
- **Contraindications to triptans**: IHD, uncontrolled HTN, hemiplegic migraine, basilar migraine, stroke

#### Cluster Headache
- Strictly **unilateral periorbital** pain, excruciating, 15-180 minutes
- Autonomic features: **Ptosis, miosis (Horner's)**, lacrimation, rhinorrhea, facial flushing
- Male predominance, **nocturnal**, seasonal clusters
- Patient is **restless** (unlike migraine — prefers to lie still)
- Acute treatment: **100% O2** (first-line, 15L/min for 15 min), sumatriptan SC/intranasal
- Prophylaxis: **Verapamil** (drug of choice), lithium, topiramate

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Cluster = unilateral + Horner's + 100% O2. Migraine = bilateral throbbing + photophobia + triptans**

#### Thunderclap Headache
- Maximal pain at onset, "worst headache of life"
- **Subarachnoid hemorrhage** until proven otherwise
- CT head first (sensitive >95% in first 6h); if negative → **LP** (xanthochromia after 12h, spectrophotometry gold standard)
- Also: Cerebral venous thrombosis, reversible cerebral vasoconstriction syndrome (RCVS), hypertensive emergency

### Epilepsy

#### ILAE Classification

**Focal (Partial) Seizures**:
- Without impaired awareness (simple partial)
- With impaired awareness (complex partial)
- Focal to bilateral tonic-clonic

**Generalized Seizures**:
- Absence (typical — 3Hz spike-wave), myoclonic, clonic, tonic, tonic-clonic, atonic

#### Drug of Choice by Seizure Type

| Seizure Type | First-Line Drug |
|---|---|
| Generalized tonic-clonic (GTCS) | **Valproate** (sodium valproate); lamotrigine alternative |
| Absence | **Ethosuximide** (or valproate) |
| Myoclonic | **Valproate** |
| Focal seizures | **Carbamazepine**, lamotrigine, levetiracetam |
| Infantile spasms (West syndrome) | **ACTH**, vigabatrin |
| Lennox-Gastaut | Valproate, lamotrigine, clonazepam |
| Juvenile myoclonic epilepsy (JME) | Valproate (levetiracetam if pregnancy planned) |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Carbamazepine and phenytoin WORSEN absence and myoclonic seizures. Avoid in these**

**Drugs Contraindicated in Pregnancy**: Valproate (neural tube defects), carbamazepine, phenytoin. **Lamotrigine and levetiracetam** — safer.

#### Status Epilepticus Management
- Defined: Seizure >5 minutes OR ≥2 seizures without return to baseline
1. **0-5 min**: IV access, O2, glucose check
2. **5-20 min (Benzodiazepines)**: Lorazepam 4 mg IV (or diazepam 10 mg IV/rectal; midazolam buccal/IM)
3. **20-40 min (Second-line)**: Levetiracetam, phenytoin, valproate IV
4. **>40 min (Refractory)**: Intubation, general anesthesia (thiopentone/propofol/midazolam infusion)

### Parkinson's Disease

- **TRAP**: **T**remor (resting, pill-rolling, 4-6 Hz), **R**igidity (cogwheel/leadpipe), **A**kinesia/bradykinesia, **P**ostural instability
- **Lewy bodies** (alpha-synuclein aggregates) in substantia nigra pars compacta
- Loss of **dopaminergic neurons** in **substantia nigra** → dopamine depletion in striatum
- Asymmetric onset, tremor most visible feature
- Non-motor: anosmia (earliest), constipation, REM sleep behavior disorder, depression, dementia (late)

#### Treatment
- **Levodopa + Carbidopa** (gold standard, most effective): Carbidopa prevents peripheral conversion → more enters CNS; complications: wearing off, on-off fluctuations, dyskinesias
- **Dopamine agonists**: Pramipexole, ropinirole, rotigotine — first-line in young patients (delay levodopa, reduce dyskinesia risk)
- **MAO-B inhibitors**: Selegiline, rasagiline — mild symptoms, neuroprotective (controversial)
- **COMT inhibitors**: Entacapone, tolcapone — reduce "off" time (give with levodopa)
- **Anticholinergics**: Benztropine, trihexyphenidyl — **mainly for tremor**, not motor symptoms; **contraindicated in elderly** (confusion, urinary retention)
- **DBS (Deep Brain Stimulation)**: Subthalamic nucleus or globus pallidus interna — for advanced disease, motor fluctuations

**PYQ HOTSPOT: "Parkinson disease drug of choice = levodopa/carbidopa. Most common cause of failure = dopamine dysregulation. DBS target = subthalamic nucleus"**

### Multiple Sclerosis

- **McDonald Criteria (2017)**: Dissemination in space (DIS) + Dissemination in time (DIT)
  - DIS: ≥2 different CNS areas (periventricular, cortical/juxtacortical, infratentorial, spinal cord)
  - DIT: New T2/gadolinium lesion on follow-up MRI, or simultaneous enhancing + non-enhancing lesions
- **MRI**: Periventricular plaques ("Dawson's fingers"), corpus callosum lesions, ovoid perpendicular lesions
- **Lhermitte's sign**: Electric shock sensation down spine on neck flexion (cervical spinal cord involvement)
- **Optic neuritis**: Monocular vision loss, painful (retrobulbar neuritis), afferent pupillary defect (Marcus Gunn)
- **Uhthoff's phenomenon**: Worsening with heat
- **Oligoclonal bands** (IgG) in CSF — present in >90%
- Most common type: **Relapsing-Remitting MS (RRMS)**
- Evoked potentials: Visual EP (most commonly abnormal)

#### MS Treatment
- **Acute relapse**: IV methylprednisolone (does not alter long-term course)
- **Disease-modifying therapy (DMT)**:
  - First-line: **Interferon-beta-1a/1b**, glatiramer acetate, dimethyl fumarate, teriflunomide
  - High efficacy: **Natalizumab** (anti-VLA-4; risk of PML with JC virus), **Alemtuzumab** (anti-CD52), **Ocrelizumab** (anti-CD20)
- Symptom management: Baclofen (spasticity), amantadine/modafinil (fatigue), oxybutynin (bladder)

### Meningitis

#### CSF Analysis

| Feature | Normal | Bacterial | Viral | TB/Fungal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Appearance | Clear | Turbid/purulent | Clear | Opalescent/xanthochromic |
| Pressure | 80-180 mmH2O | Elevated | Normal/mildly elevated | Elevated |
| WBC | <5 (lymphocytes) | **>1000, PMN** | 10-500, **lymphocytes** | 100-500, **lymphocytes** |
| Protein | 15-45 mg/dL | **>100 (markedly elevated)** | Normal to mildly elevated | **High (100-500)** |
| Glucose | 60-80% serum | **<45 (low)** | Normal | **Low** |
| Other | — | Culture, Gram stain | PCR (HSV) | AFB, India ink (crypto), ADA elevated |

#### Organisms by Age
| Age | Common Organisms |
|---|---|
| Neonates (<1 month) | **Group B Strep**, E. coli, Listeria |
| 1-3 months | Group B Strep, **S. pneumoniae**, **N. meningitidis** |
| >3 months to 5 years | N. meningitidis, S. pneumoniae |
| Adults | S. pneumoniae (most common), N. meningitidis |
| Elderly/immunocompromised | S. pneumoniae, Listeria monocytogenes, Gram-negatives |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: N. meningitidis = petechiae/purpura + meningism in young adults. DIC can occur (Waterhouse-Friderichsen syndrome)**

#### Bacterial Meningitis Treatment
- **Empiric**: Ceftriaxone 2g IV every 12h + Dexamethasone (0.15 mg/kg every 6h for 4 days, given before or with first antibiotic dose — reduces hearing loss in H. influenzae, neurological sequelae)
- Add **vancomycin** if MRSA/penicillin-resistant pneumococcus suspected
- Add **ampicillin** for Listeria (neonates, elderly, immunocompromised)
