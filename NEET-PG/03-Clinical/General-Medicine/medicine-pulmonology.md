## PULMONOLOGY

### COPD

- **Definition**: FEV1/FVC <0.70 (post-bronchodilator) — **fixed ratio**
- Cause: **Cigarette smoking** (most common), biomass fuels, air pollution
- Alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency: Panacinar emphysema, **PiZZ genotype**, affects lower lobes

#### GOLD Staging (based on FEV1 % predicted)
| Stage | FEV1 | Severity |
|---|---|---|
| GOLD 1 | ≥80% | Mild |
| GOLD 2 | 50-79% | Moderate |
| GOLD 3 | 30-49% | Severe |
| GOLD 4 | <30% | Very Severe |

#### Blue Bloater vs Pink Puffer

| Feature | Blue Bloater (Chronic Bronchitis) | Pink Puffer (Emphysema) |
|---|---|---|
| Definition | Cough + sputum ≥3 months/year for ≥2 years | Alveolar destruction, air trapping |
| Appearance | Cyanotic, edematous | Thin, barrel chest, pursed-lip breathing |
| PaO2 | Low | Near normal |
| PaCO2 | Elevated (CO2 retainer) | Normal or low |
| Polycythemia | Present | Absent |
| Cor pulmonale | Common | Less common |
| Hypoxic drive | **At risk — caution with high-flow O2** | Less risk |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Chronic bronchitis = clinical diagnosis (productive cough ≥3 months/year for ≥2 years). Emphysema = pathological diagnosis (alveolar wall destruction)**

#### Cor Pulmonale
- RV hypertrophy/failure due to pulmonary hypertension from lung disease
- ECG: Right axis deviation, P pulmonale (tall peaked P in II), RBBB, S1Q3T3 pattern
- Most common cause: **COPD**

#### COPD Exacerbation Management
- **Controlled O2**: Target SpO2 88-92% (hypoxic drive preservation)
- **Short-acting bronchodilators**: Salbutamol + ipratropium nebulization
- **Systemic corticosteroids**: Prednisolone 40mg for 5 days
- **Antibiotics**: If increased sputum/change in color (amoxicillin-clavulanate, doxycycline, or azithromycin)
- **NIV (BiPAP)**: For respiratory failure (pH <7.35, PaCO2 >6 kPa)

#### LTOT (Long-Term Oxygen Therapy) Criteria
- PaO2 ≤55 mmHg (or SpO2 ≤88%) on room air — 2 measurements 3 weeks apart
- PaO2 56-59 mmHg with cor pulmonale/polycythemia/nocturnal hypoxemia
- Use >15 hours/day; **improves survival**

### Asthma

#### GINA Classification

| Severity | SABA use | Nighttime symptoms | FEV1 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Intermittent | ≤2 days/week | ≤2/month | >80% |
| Mild persistent | >2 days/week, not daily | 3-4/month | >80% |
| Moderate persistent | Daily | >1/week | 60-80% |
| Severe persistent | Continuous | Frequent | <60% |

#### Reversibility Test
- **FEV1 improvement ≥12% AND ≥200 mL** after bronchodilator = significant reversibility (asthma)
- COPD = <12% or <200 mL improvement (fixed obstruction)

#### Status Asthmaticus
- Severe asthma not responding to initial bronchodilators
- Features of life-threatening asthma: SpO2 <92%, silent chest, cyanosis, bradycardia, confusion, PaCO2 rising (normal/high = impending respiratory failure — **normocapnia in asthma is alarming**)
- **Magnesium sulfate IV** (4g over 20 min) for severe/life-threatening
- IV/nebulized salbutamol, IV hydrocortisone, heliox
- **Intubation** if GCS falling, PaCO2 rising despite therapy

### Pneumonia

#### CURB-65 Score (Community-Acquired Pneumonia severity)
- **C**onfusion (new onset)
- **U**rea >7 mmol/L (BUN >19 mg/dL)
- **R**espiratory rate ≥30/min
- **B**lood pressure (SBP <90 or DBP ≤60)
- Age **65** or older

Score 0-1 = outpatient; 2 = hospital admission; 3-5 = consider ICU

**PYQ HOTSPOT: PSI (Pneumonia Severity Index) = more comprehensive; CURB-65 = simpler, bedside tool**

#### CAP Organisms
- **Most common overall**: Streptococcus pneumoniae
- Young adults: Mycoplasma pneumoniae (atypical)
- Elderly/aspiration: Gram-negatives, anaerobes
- Immunocompromised: PCP (Pneumocystis jirovecii), CMV, fungi
- Alcoholics: Klebsiella pneumoniae (currant jelly sputum, upper lobe)
- Bird exposure: Chlamydia psittaci (psittacosis)
- CAP empiric: Beta-lactam + macrolide OR respiratory fluoroquinolone

#### HAP (Hospital-Acquired Pneumonia)
- >48h after admission
- Organisms: **Pseudomonas aeruginosa, MRSA, Acinetobacter, Klebsiella**
- **VAP** (ventilator-associated): >48h after intubation
- Treatment: Broad-spectrum (piperacillin-tazobactam + vancomycin/linezolid for MRSA risk)

#### Atypical Pneumonia Features

| Organism | Key Feature | Diagnosis |
|---|---|---|
| **Mycoplasma pneumoniae** | **Cold agglutinins** (IgM anti-I), bullous myringitis, young adults | Cold agglutinin test, PCR |
| **Legionella pneumophila** | **Hyponatremia**, water coolers/AC, GI symptoms, confusion | **Urinary antigen** (most practical) |
| Chlamydia pneumoniae | Mild, older adults | Serology |
| Coxiella burnetii (Q fever) | Animal exposure (cattle/sheep), hepatitis | Serology (Phase II IgG) |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Legionella = hyponatremia + confusion + water cooler exposure + urinary antigen test**

### Tuberculosis

#### Primary vs Post-Primary TB

| Feature | Primary TB | Post-Primary (Reactivation) TB |
|---|---|---|
| Occurrence | First exposure, any age | Reactivation in adults |
| Location | Mid/lower zones, hilar nodes | **Upper lobe (apical/posterior segments)** |
| Complex | **Ghon complex** (Ghon focus + hilar nodes) | Fibrosis, cavitation |
| Simon's foci | — | **Seeded apical foci** during primary bacteremia |
| Cavitation | No | **Yes (pathognomonic)** |
| Spread | Lymphohematogenous | Contiguous/bronchogenic |

#### Miliary TB
- Hematogenous dissemination
- CXR: Millet seed-like uniform nodules (1-3mm) throughout lung fields
- Most common cause of TB meningitis
- Tuberculin test may be **negative** (anergy)
- Treatment: Same as standard but **longer duration** (9-12 months)

#### DOTS/RNTCP Regimen (India)
**Revised NTEP (National TB Elimination Programme) — Daily regimen**:
- **DS-TB**: 2HRZE/4HR (2 months intensive + 4 months continuation)
- **DR-TB**: Longer regimens based on resistance pattern (bedaquiline-based)

**Drug codes**: H = INH, R = Rifampicin, Z = Pyrazinamide, E = Ethambutol, S = Streptomycin

#### Adverse Effects of TB Drugs
| Drug | Key Side Effect |
|---|---|
| INH | Peripheral neuropathy (give **pyridoxine/Vit B6**), hepatitis, seizures |
| Rifampicin | **Orange discoloration** of fluids, hepatitis, drug interactions (CYP inducer) |
| Pyrazinamide | **Hyperuricemia**, hepatitis, arthralgia |
| Ethambutol | **Optic neuritis** (retrobulbar) — check visual acuity |
| Streptomycin | **Ototoxicity** (vestibular), nephrotoxicity |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Ethambutol = optic neuritis (color vision, then acuity). Streptomycin = NOT used in pregnancy (ototoxicity to fetus)**

#### IRIS (Immune Reconstitution Inflammatory Syndrome)
- Occurs after starting ART in TB-HIV patients (paradoxical worsening)
- Treatment: **NSAIDs**; severe cases — prednisolone

### Pleural Effusion

#### Light's Criteria (Exudate if ANY one met)
1. Pleural fluid protein/serum protein **>0.5**
2. Pleural fluid LDH/serum LDH **>0.6**
3. Pleural fluid LDH >**2/3 upper limit of normal** serum LDH

**Transudate** (<0.5 on all): CHF, cirrhosis, nephrotic syndrome, hypothyroidism

#### Causes by Fluid Type
| Transudate | Exudate |
|---|---|
| CCF (most common bilateral) | Pneumonia (parapneumonic) |
| Cirrhosis (hepatic hydrothorax) | TB (lymphocyte predominant) |
| Nephrotic syndrome | Malignancy |
| Hypothyroidism | Pulmonary embolism |
| Meig's syndrome (ovarian fibroma) | RA, SLE |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Glucose <60 mg/dL in pleural fluid = empyema, TB, RA, esophageal rupture. Amylase elevated = pancreatitis or esophageal rupture**

### Interstitial Lung Disease (ILD)

#### HRCT Patterns

| Pattern | Description | Associated Disease |
|---|---|---|
| **UIP (Usual IP)** | Honeycombing + traction bronchiectasis, basal predominance, subpleural | **IPF** |
| NSIP | Ground-glass + fine reticulation, no honeycombing | CTD-ILD (Scleroderma, PM/DM) |
| DIP | Diffuse ground glass, smokers | DIP |
| COP | Peribronchovascular consolidation, migratory | Organizing pneumonia |
| LIP | Diffuse ground glass, cysts | Sjogren's, HIV |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Honeycombing on HRCT = UIP pattern = IPF. Traction bronchiectasis within honeycombing**

#### IPF Treatment
- **Pirfenidone** (antifibrotic, TGF-β inhibitor) — slows progression
- **Nintedanib** (tyrosine kinase inhibitor) — slows progression
- Neither improves survival significantly; lung transplant is curative
- Steroids: **NOT effective** in IPF (may be harmful)

### Pulmonary Hypertension

#### WHO/Dana Point Classification

| Group | Cause |
|---|---|
| **Group 1 (PAH)** | Idiopathic, heritable, drug/toxin, CTD, portal HTN, HIV, congenital |
| Group 2 | Left heart disease |
| Group 3 | Lung disease/hypoxia |
| Group 4 | Chronic thromboembolic (CTEPH) |
| Group 5 | Multifactorial/unclear |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Group 4 (CTEPH) = only potentially curable with pulmonary thromboendarterectomy**

#### Eisenmenger's Syndrome
- **Left-to-right shunt** (VSD/ASD/PDA) → pulmonary HTN → reversal to right-to-left shunt → **cyanosis**
- Central cyanosis, clubbing, polycythemia
- **Iron deficiency common** (due to erythrocytosis and phlebotomy)
- Most common cause of reversed shunt: **VSD**
- **PDE-5 inhibitors (sildenafil), ERA (bosentan), prostacyclins** — palliation

#### Group 1 PAH Treatment
- CCB (only for vasoreactivity responders on acute testing)
- **PDE-5 inhibitors**: Sildenafil, tadalafil
- **Endothelin receptor antagonists**: Bosentan, ambrisentan
- **Prostacyclins**: Epoprostenol (IV), iloprost (inhaled), treprostinil
- Lung transplant: Definitive
