## CARDIOLOGY

### Heart Failure

#### HFrEF vs HFpEF

| Feature | HFrEF (Systolic) | HFpEF (Diastolic) |
|---|---|---|
| EF | <40% | ≥50% |
| Mechanism | Impaired contraction | Impaired relaxation |
| Common cause | IHD, DCM | Hypertension, DM, obesity |
| BNP | Elevated | Elevated |
| Treatment | ACEi/ARB/BB/diuretics/ARNI/SGLT2i | Diuretics, treat cause |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: HFrEF EF <40%, HFpEF EF ≥50%, HFmrEF 40-49%**

#### Frank-Starling Mechanism
- Increased preload → increased stroke volume (up to a point)
- In failing heart: curve is depressed and flattened
- **Sarcomere optimal length: 2.2 microns**

#### Compensatory Mechanisms in Heart Failure
1. **Frank-Starling mechanism** (acute)
2. **Neurohormonal activation**: RAAS, SNS activation
3. **Cardiac remodeling**: ventricular hypertrophy
4. **Tachycardia** (Bowditch/Treppe effect)

#### NYHA Classification

| Class | Symptoms |
|---|---|
| I | No limitation with ordinary activity |
| II | Slight limitation; comfortable at rest |
| III | Marked limitation; comfortable at rest |
| IV | Unable to carry on any activity without discomfort |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: NYHA Class III-IV → consider CRT/ICD implantation in eligible patients**

#### Treatment of Chronic HFrEF
- **ACEi/ARB**: Reduce afterload, prevent remodeling (ramipril, enalapril)
- **Beta-blockers**: Carvedilol, metoprolol succinate, bisoprolol (reduce mortality)
- **Diuretics**: Symptom relief (furosemide for loop, spironolactone for aldosterone antagonism)
- **ARNI (Sacubitril/Valsartan)**: Superior to ACEi (PARADIGM-HF trial); **cannot combine with ACEi** (36-hour washout)
- **SGLT2 inhibitors**: Empagliflozin/dapagliflozin — **reduce HF hospitalizations and CV death** (EMPEROR-Reduced, DAPA-HF trials)
- **Ivabradine**: HR >70 on max beta-blocker, sinus rhythm
- **Hydralazine + Nitrates**: ACEi/ARB intolerant, especially in **Black patients**

**PYQ HOTSPOT: The "Fantastic Four" in HFrEF = ACEi/ARB/ARNI + BB + MRA + SGLT2i**

#### Acute Pulmonary Edema Management
Mnemonic: **LMNOP**
- **L**asix (furosemide IV)
- **M**orphine (reduces preload, anxiolysis) — controversial
- **N**itrates (sublingual/IV — reduce preload)
- **O**xygen (CPAP/BiPAP if needed)
- **P**ositioning (sit upright, legs dependent)

Additional: Dobutamine (inotrope) if cardiogenic shock; Nesiritide (BNP analogue)

### Ischemic Heart Disease / ACS

#### ACS Spectrum

| Feature | STEMI | NSTEMI | Unstable Angina |
|---|---|---|---|
| ECG | ST elevation, new LBBB | ST depression/T-wave changes | Normal or ST depression |
| Troponin | Elevated | Elevated | **Normal** |
| Pain at rest | Yes | Yes | Yes (new or crescendo) |
| Culprit | Complete occlusion | Partial occlusion | Partial occlusion |
| Treatment | **Immediate reperfusion** | Anticoagulation + early PCI | Anticoagulation + PCI (risk-stratified) |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: UA = ACS with normal troponin. NSTEMI = ACS with elevated troponin, no ST elevation**

#### Troponin Timeline
- **Rises**: 3-6 hours post-MI
- **Peaks**: 12-24 hours (Troponin I/T)
- **Normalizes**: 5-14 days
- **CK-MB**: Rises 4-6h, peaks 12-24h, normalizes 48-72h (useful for **reinfarction detection**)

#### ECG Changes in STEMI
- **Hyperacute T waves** (earliest change)
- **ST elevation** (>1mm in 2 contiguous limb leads, >2mm in precordial)
- **Q waves** (irreversible necrosis, ≥0.04s duration, ≥25% R wave height)
- **T wave inversion** (reperfusion)

**PYQ HOTSPOT: New LBBB = STEMI equivalent; Right bundle branch block does NOT mimic STEMI**

#### Localization of MI
| Territory | Leads | Artery |
|---|---|---|
| Anterior | V1-V4 | LAD |
| Lateral | I, aVL, V5-V6 | LCx |
| Inferior | II, III, aVF | RCA |
| Posterior | Tall R in V1-V2 (mirror image) | RCA/LCx |
| RV infarct | V4R | RCA proximal |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: RV infarct = inferior STEMI + hypotension → fluid challenge (NOT nitrates)**

#### Prinzmetal (Variant) Angina
- **Coronary vasospasm** without fixed obstruction
- ST elevation during episode (unlike stable angina)
- Occurs at rest, often at **night/early morning**
- Treatment: **CCB (nifedipine/diltiazem), nitrates**; **beta-blockers CONTRAINDICATED** (can worsen spasm)

#### Dressler's Syndrome
- **Post-MI pericarditis** (2-10 weeks after MI)
- Fever, pleuritic chest pain, pericardial effusion
- **Autoimmune** mechanism (anti-heart antibodies)
- Treatment: **NSAIDs + colchicine**; steroids if refractory

#### Complications of MI — Killip Classification

| Class | Feature | Mortality |
|---|---|---|
| I | No signs of HF | ~6% |
| II | S3 gallop, mild pulmonary rales | ~17% |
| III | Acute pulmonary edema | ~38% |
| IV | Cardiogenic shock | ~81% |

#### Mechanical Complications Timeline

| Complication | Timing | Key Feature |
|---|---|---|
| Free wall rupture | 3-5 days | Sudden death, hemopericardium |
| VSD (interventricular) | 3-5 days | New holosystolic murmur, step-up in RV O2 |
| Papillary muscle rupture (MR) | 3-7 days | Acute severe MR, pulmonary edema |
| LV aneurysm | Weeks-months | Persistent ST elevation, thrombus, arrhythmia |
| LV pseudoaneurysm | Days-weeks | Contained rupture, high risk of rupture |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Free wall rupture = most common cause of death after initial 24h. Papillary muscle most commonly affected = posteromedial (single RCA supply)**

#### GRACE Score
Predicts in-hospital mortality in NSTEMI/UA; variables include: age, HR, BP, creatinine, Killip class, cardiac arrest, ST deviation, troponin elevation

#### Thrombolysis in STEMI
**Indications**: STEMI <12h, no PCI available within 120 minutes
**Contraindications**:
- Absolute: Prior hemorrhagic stroke, ischemic stroke <3 months, active bleeding, aortic dissection, severe uncontrolled HTN, intracranial neoplasm
- Relative: Recent surgery <3 weeks, active peptic ulcer, INR >2

**Agents**: tPA (alteplase), rPA (reteplase), TNK-tPA (tenecteplase)

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Streptokinase = non-fibrin specific, antigenic (cannot repeat within 6 months). tPA = fibrin specific, preferred**

#### PCI vs CABG
| Factor | PCI preferred | CABG preferred |
|---|---|---|
| Anatomy | Single/double vessel disease | Left main disease, 3-vessel disease |
| Diabetics | Less preferred | **Preferred** (FREEDOM trial) |
| EF | Normal | Reduced EF with viable myocardium |
| Urgency | Primary PCI for STEMI | Elective for stable CAD |

### Arrhythmias

#### Atrial Fibrillation

**CHADS-VASC Score** (for stroke risk, decide anticoagulation):
- **C**HF (1), **H**ypertension (1), **A**ge ≥75 (2), **D**iabetes (1), **S**troke/TIA (2), **V**ascular disease (1), **A**ge 65-74 (1), **S**ex female (1)
- Score ≥2 (men) or ≥3 (women) → anticoagulate
- **Score 0 → no anticoagulation; Score 1 (men) → consider**

**HAS-BLED Score**: bleeding risk with anticoagulation

**Rate vs Rhythm Control**:
- Rate control: beta-blockers, CCB (diltiazem/verapamil), digoxin; target HR <80/min at rest
- Rhythm control: cardioversion (electrical/chemical), ablation
- **AFFIRM trial**: Rate = rhythm control in terms of mortality
- **New-onset AF <48h**: Cardiovert directly; **>48h or unknown**: Anticoagulate 3 weeks before and 4 weeks after cardioversion (or TEE to exclude thrombus)

**PYQ HOTSPOT: WPW + AF = DO NOT give AV nodal blocking drugs (digoxin/verapamil/adenosine) → risk of VF via accessory pathway → use procainamide or cardiovert**

#### Ventricular Tachycardia / Ventricular Fibrillation
- **VT (monomorphic)**: Wide complex tachycardia, AV dissociation, capture/fusion beats
- **VF**: Irregular, chaotic → immediate defibrillation
- **Torsades de Pointes**: Polymorphic VT, associated with prolonged QT; treatment = **IV magnesium**; avoid drugs prolonging QT
- **ACLS for pulseless VT/VF**: Defibrillate → CPR → Epinephrine → Amiodarone (or lidocaine)
- **Stable VT**: Amiodarone IV, then cardioversion if no response

#### WPW Syndrome
- **Accessory pathway (Bundle of Kent)** bypasses AV node
- **Delta wave** (slurred upstroke of QRS), short PR interval, wide QRS
- **AVRT**: Orthodromic (narrow complex, most common) vs Antidromic (wide complex)
- **WPW + AF** = Life-threatening (as above)
- Definitive treatment: **Radiofrequency catheter ablation**

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Delta wave = hallmark of WPW. Accessory pathway = Bundle of Kent**

#### Heart Blocks

| Block | ECG Feature | Pacemaker? |
|---|---|---|
| 1st degree | Prolonged PR (>0.20s), all P conducted | No |
| 2nd degree Mobitz I (Wenckebach) | Progressive PR lengthening → dropped beat | Usually no (benign, AV node) |
| 2nd degree Mobitz II | Constant PR, sudden dropped beat | **Yes** (His-Purkinje, unpredictable) |
| 3rd degree (complete) | P and QRS dissociated, escape rhythm | **Yes** (always) |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Mobitz II and 3rd degree = always require pacemaker. Wenckebach = usually benign**

**Pacemaker Indications (other)**:
- Sick sinus syndrome with symptoms
- Bifascicular/trifascicular block with syncope
- Post-MI heart block

#### SVT (AVNRT — most common SVT)
- Narrow complex tachycardia, HR 150-250
- P wave in QRS or just after
- Treatment: **Vagal maneuvers → Adenosine (6mg IV rapid push, can repeat 12mg)**
- **Adenosine**: Half-life 10 seconds, causes transient AV block
- **Contraindicated in WPW (antidromic SVT) and asthma**
- Maintenance: beta-blockers, CCB, ablation

### Hypertension

#### Classification (ACC/AHA 2017 vs older JNC 7)

| Stage | Systolic | Diastolic |
|---|---|---|
| Normal | <120 | <80 |
| Elevated | 120-129 | <80 |
| Stage 1 | 130-139 | 80-89 |
| Stage 2 | ≥140 | ≥90 |

**India uses WHO/JNC 7**: HTN = BP ≥140/90 mmHg

#### Compelling Indications for Antihypertensives

| Condition | Preferred Drug |
|---|---|
| Heart failure (HFrEF) | ACEi/ARB + BB + diuretic |
| Post-MI | ACEi/ARB, BB |
| Diabetes | ACEi/ARB (nephroprotection) |
| CKD with proteinuria | **ACEi/ARB** (first-line) |
| Pregnancy | **Methyldopa** (safest), labetalol, nifedipine |
| Isolated systolic HTN (elderly) | CCB, thiazide |
| Benign prostatic hyperplasia | Alpha-1 blockers (doxazosin) |
| Pheochromocytoma | Alpha-blockers first, then beta |
| Migraine prophylaxis | BB, CCB |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: ACEi/ARB contraindicated in pregnancy (teratogenic - causes renal tubular dysplasia)**

#### Hypertensive Emergency vs Urgency

| Feature | Emergency | Urgency |
|---|---|---|
| BP | Usually >180/120 | >180/120 |
| End-organ damage | **Yes** (brain/heart/kidney) | **No** |
| Treatment | IV drugs (labetalol, nitroprusside, nicardipine) | Oral drugs (amlodipine, clonidine) |
| BP reduction | Lower by 25% in first hour | Gradually over 24-48h |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Hypertensive encephalopathy, aortic dissection, pulmonary edema, eclampsia = emergencies**
**Nitroprusside = cyanide toxicity with prolonged use → use sodium thiosulfate as antidote**

#### Secondary Hypertension

| Cause | Key Feature | Test |
|---|---|---|
| **Conn's syndrome** | Hypokalemia, no edema, low renin | Aldosterone:renin ratio |
| **Cushing's syndrome** | Central obesity, striae, buffalo hump | 24h urine cortisol, low-dose DST |
| **Pheochromocytoma** | Paroxysmal headache/sweating/palpitations | **24h urine VMA/metanephrines** |
| **Renovascular** | Young female (FMD) or atherosclerotic | Duplex ultrasound, MRA, captopril scan |
| **Coarctation of aorta** | Weak femoral pulses, rib notching on CXR | Echo, CT aortography |
| **OSA** | Obese, morning headache, snoring | Polysomnography |

### Valvular Heart Disease

#### Murmur Characteristics

| Valve Lesion | Timing | Location | Radiation | Key Features |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mitral stenosis | Mid-diastolic | Apex | None | **Opening snap** (OS), tapping apex, loud S1, best in left lateral position |
| Mitral regurgitation | Pansystolic | Apex | Axilla | Soft S1, S3, displaced apex |
| Aortic stenosis | Ejection systolic | Aortic area (2nd RICS) | Carotids | **Plateau pulse, slow rising, narrow pulse pressure** |
| Aortic regurgitation | Early diastolic | Left sternal edge | — | **Collapsing/water-hammer pulse, wide pulse pressure** |
| MVP | Mid-systolic click + late systolic murmur | Apex | — | Click moves earlier with standing/Valsalva |
| Tricuspid regurgitation | Pansystolic | Lower sternal border | — | Increases with inspiration (**Carvallo's sign**) |
| Pulmonary stenosis | Ejection systolic | Pulmonary area (2nd LICS) | — | Wide split S2, ejection click |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Opening snap = mitral stenosis. Earlier OS = more severe MS (higher LA pressure)**

#### Mitral Stenosis (Rheumatic)
- Rheumatic fever: Group A Streptococcus → cross-reactive antibodies
- **MVA <1 cm² = critical MS**
- Features: Malar flush, OS, mid-diastolic rumble, Graham Steell murmur (PR due to PAH)
- **S1 loud** (pliable leaflets) → becomes soft when calcified
- Treatment: Diuretics, beta-blockers; **PTMC (balloon valvuloplasty)** if MVA <1.5 cm² with symptoms; surgery for severe/calcified

#### Aortic Regurgitation Signs
- **Corrigan's (water-hammer) pulse**: Abrupt rise and collapse
- **de Musset's sign**: Head bobbing
- **Quincke's sign**: Nail bed pulsation
- **Duroziez's sign**: Systolic/diastolic bruit over femoral
- **Hill's sign**: Popliteal BP > brachial BP by >20 mmHg
- **Traube's sign**: Pistol shot sound over femoral

**PYQ HOTSPOT: AR = wide pulse pressure, diastolic murmur at left sternal edge, best heard sitting forward in expiration**

#### MVP (Most Common Valvular Lesion)
- **Most common** valvular heart disease in developed countries
- Mid-systolic click + late systolic murmur
- **Increases severity** with standing, Valsalva (less preload)
- **Decreases severity** with squatting, leg raise (more preload)
- Myxomatous degeneration of valve leaflets
- Complications: MR, infective endocarditis, arrhythmias, sudden death

### Pericardial Diseases

#### Acute Pericarditis
- **Pleuritic chest pain** (worse supine, better sitting forward)
- **Pericardial friction rub** (3 components: atrial systole, ventricular systole, early diastole)
- **ECG**: Saddle-shaped (concave up) ST elevation in ALL leads except aVR and V1 (which show ST depression); PR depression (**earliest ECG change**)
- Most common cause: **Viral (Coxsackievirus B, echovirus)**
- Treatment: **NSAIDs + Colchicine** (reduces recurrence); steroids only if refractory

**PYQ HOTSPOT: PR depression is the earliest and most specific ECG finding in pericarditis**

#### Cardiac Tamponade
- Compression of heart by pericardial fluid → decreased cardiac output
- **Beck's Triad**: Hypotension + JVD + Muffled heart sounds
- **Pulsus paradoxus**: >10 mmHg fall in SBP with inspiration
- **Kussmaul's sign**: NOT present in tamponade (JVP rises with inspiration in constriction, not tamponade)
- ECG: **Electrical alternans** (alternating QRS amplitude) — pathognomonic
- **Low voltage QRS** on ECG
- Echo: Diastolic collapse of RV (most sensitive) and RA
- Treatment: **Pericardiocentesis** (emergent), surgical window if recurrent

#### Constrictive Pericarditis
- Fibrous/calcified pericardium → impaired diastolic filling
- **Kussmaul's sign IS PRESENT** (JVP rises with inspiration)
- Friedreich's sign: Rapid y-descent (steep)
- Pericardial knock (replaces OS)
- **Pericardial calcification on CXR/CT** — eggshell calcification
- Square root sign (dip-and-plateau) on cardiac catheterization
- Treatment: **Pericardiectomy** (definitive)
- Common causes: TB (worldwide), viral, post-cardiac surgery, radiation

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Tamponade = no Kussmaul's, prominent x-descent; Constriction = Kussmaul's present, prominent y-descent**
