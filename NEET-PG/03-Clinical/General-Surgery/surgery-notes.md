## SHOCK

### Definition and Classification

**Shock = inadequate tissue perfusion relative to metabolic demands**

#### Weil-Shumaker Classification (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Type | Mechanism | Examples |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Hypovolemic** | Decreased preload (↓ circulating volume) | Hemorrhage, burns, dehydration, vomiting |
| **Cardiogenic** | Pump failure (↓ cardiac output) | MI, cardiomyopathy, arrhythmia |
| **Distributive** | Abnormal vasodilation (↓ SVR) | Septic, anaphylactic, neurogenic |
| **Obstructive** | Outflow obstruction | Tension pneumothorax, cardiac tamponade, massive PE |

#### Hemodynamic Profile in Shock

| Type | CO | SVR | PCWP | CVP |
|------|-----|-----|------|-----|
| Hypovolemic | ↓ | ↑ | ↓ | ↓ |
| Cardiogenic | ↓ | ↑ | ↑ | ↑ |
| Septic (early) | ↑ | ↓ | ↓ | ↓ |
| Obstructive | ↓ | ↑ | Variable | ↑ |

### Hemorrhagic Shock - ATLS Classification (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Class | Blood Loss (mL) | % Blood Vol | HR | BP | RR | Urine Output | Mental Status |
|-------|----------------|-------------|-----|-----|-----|--------------|---------------|
| **I** | <750 | <15% | <100 | Normal | 14-20 | >30 mL/hr | Normal/anxious |
| **II** | 750-1500 | 15-30% | 100-120 | Normal | 20-30 | 20-30 mL/hr | Anxious |
| **III** | 1500-2000 | 30-40% | 120-140 | Decreased | 30-40 | 5-20 mL/hr | Confused |
| **IV** | >2000 | >40% | >140 | Very low | >35 | <5 mL/hr | Lethargic/unconscious |

**Key Points:**
- **Class II**: BP still normal (compensated) - first sign is ↑HR and ↓pulse pressure
- **Class III**: First class with decreased BP (decompensated) - requires blood transfusion
- **Normal blood volume**: 70 mL/kg adult; 80 mL/kg child; 90 mL/kg neonate
- **Crystalloid resuscitation**: 3:1 rule (3L crystalloid per 1L blood loss)

### Septic Shock (Sepsis-3 Criteria)
- **Sepsis**: Life-threatening organ dysfunction due to dysregulated host response (SOFA score ≥2)
- **Septic shock**: Sepsis + vasopressor requirement + lactate >2 mmol/L despite fluid
- **SOFA score components**: Respiratory (PaO2/FiO2), Coagulation (platelets), Liver (bilirubin), CVS (MAP/vasopressors), CNS (GCS), Renal (creatinine/urine output)
- **First-line vasopressor**: **Norepinephrine**
- **Refractory septic shock**: Add vasopressin (0.03 units/min)

### Anaphylactic Shock
- IgE-mediated (Type I hypersensitivity)
- Treatment: **Adrenaline 0.5 mg IM (1:1000)** - first line
- Then: antihistamines, steroids, fluid resuscitation

## TRAUMA - ATLS

### Primary Survey: ABCDE (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Step | Action | Life-threatening conditions |
|------|--------|----------------------------|
| **A** - Airway + C-spine | Clear airway, jaw thrust | Obstruction |
| **B** - Breathing | Ventilation, oxygenation | Tension pneumothorax, open pneumothorax, flail chest, massive hemothorax |
| **C** - Circulation | Hemorrhage control, IV access | Massive hemorrhage, cardiac tamponade |
| **D** - Disability | GCS, pupils, lateralizing signs | Herniation syndromes |
| **E** - Exposure | Full exposure, temperature | Missed injuries |

### Immediately Life-Threatening Thoracic Injuries (The "Killer 6")
1. **Airway obstruction**
2. **Tension pneumothorax** - immediate needle decompression (2nd ICS MCL)
3. **Open pneumothorax** - 3-sided dressing, then chest tube
4. **Massive hemothorax** - chest tube (4th-5th ICS AAL); >1500mL initial or >200mL/hr = thoracotomy
5. **Flail chest** - PPV, pain control
6. **Cardiac tamponade** - pericardiocentesis (Beck's triad: hypotension/muffled sounds/JVD; Kussmaul sign)

### Secondary Survey
- Head-to-toe examination
- AMPLE history: **A**llergies, **M**edications, **P**ast history, **L**ast meal, **E**vents
- Adjuncts: X-rays (CXR, pelvis, C-spine), FAST ultrasound, labs

### FAST Examination (Focused Assessment with Sonography for Trauma)
- **4 windows**: Perihepatic (Morrison's pouch), Perisplenic, Pericardial, Pelvic (Pouch of Douglas)
- **Extended FAST (eFAST)**: + bilateral pleural spaces for pneumothorax
- **Positive FAST** in unstable patient = OR (not CT)

### Damage Control Surgery (DCS)
**Indications**: pH <7.2, temp <34°C, coagulopathy (lethal triad)
- **DCS-I**: Initial hemorrhage control (packing, clamping), laparostomy
- **DCS-II**: ICU resuscitation (correct physiology)
- **DCS-III**: Definitive repair (24-72 hours later)

### Massive Transfusion Protocol (MTP)
- **Trigger**: >10 units pRBC in 24h, or rapidly bleeding patient
- **Ratio**: **1:1:1** (pRBC: FFP: Platelets)
- **TXA (Tranexamic acid)**: Within 3 hours of injury, 1g over 10 min then 1g over 8 hours
- **Permissive hypotension**: Target SBP 80-90 mmHg until hemorrhage control (except TBI)

## SURGICAL INFECTIONS

### Cellulitis vs Necrotizing Fasciitis

| Feature | Cellulitis | Necrotizing Fasciitis |
|---------|------------|----------------------|
| Depth | Dermis/subcutaneous | Deep fascia |
| Gas on X-ray | No | **Yes (crepitus)** |
| Systemic toxicity | Mild | **Severe** |
| Skin appearance | Erythema, warm | Initially mild, then necrosis/bullae |
| Treatment | IV antibiotics | **Immediate surgical debridement** |

**NF Types:**
- **Type I** (polymicrobial): Fournier's gangrene (perineum), diabetics/immunocompromised
- **Type II** (monomicrobial): **Group A Streptococcus** - flesh-eating disease

### Gas Gangrene (Clostridial Myonecrosis)
- **Organism**: **Clostridium perfringens** (Type A, alpha toxin - lecithinase)
- **Features**: Crepitus, bronze/bronze-brown skin, sweet musty odor, gram-positive rods on smear
- **Treatment**: Wide surgical debridement + **Penicillin G** + hyperbaric oxygen (adjunct)
- **X-ray**: Gas in muscle planes

### Tetanus Prophylaxis (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Wound Type | Immunization History | TIG | Td/Tdap |
|-----------|---------------------|-----|---------|
| Clean minor | 3 doses, last <5 yrs | No | No |
| Clean minor | 3 doses, last 5-10 yrs | No | Yes |
| Clean minor | <3 doses or unknown | No | Yes |
| Dirty/tetanus-prone | 3 doses, last <5 yrs | No | No |
| Dirty/tetanus-prone | 3 doses, last >5 yrs | No | Yes |
| Dirty/tetanus-prone | <3 doses or unknown | **Yes** | Yes |

- **TIG dose**: 250 units IM (opposite arm from toxoid)
- **Tetanus-prone wounds**: >6 hours old, crush/burn/frostbite, presence of devitalized tissue, contaminated with soil/feces

## WOUND HEALING (PYQ HOTSPOT)

### Phases of Wound Healing

| Phase | Timing | Key Events | Dominant Cell |
|-------|--------|-----------|---------------|
| **Hemostasis** | 0-several hours | Platelet plug, fibrin clot, vasoconstriction then dilation | Platelets |
| **Inflammation** | Days 1-4 | Neutrophil (first 24-48h) → Macrophage (48h onwards) | Neutrophils → Macrophages |
| **Proliferation** | Days 4-21 | Fibroblast, collagen (Type III initially), angiogenesis, re-epithelialization | **Fibroblasts** |
| **Remodeling** | 21 days - 2 years | Type III → Type I collagen, wound contraction, tensile strength 80% max | Myofibroblasts |

**Key facts:**
- **Most important cell for wound healing**: Macrophage (orchestrates response)
- **Collagen synthesis peak**: 5-7 days post-injury
- **Type III → Type I collagen**: During remodeling (collagenase/MMPs)
- **Maximum tensile strength**: Never exceeds **80%** of original
- **Wound contraction**: Myofibroblasts (modified fibroblasts with smooth muscle actin)

### Types of Wound Closure
- **Primary intention**: Clean wound, closed immediately (minimal scarring)
- **Secondary intention**: Left open to heal by granulation/contraction (infected wounds, bites)
- **Tertiary/delayed primary**: Left open initially, closed at 4-5 days (contaminated wounds)

### Keloid vs Hypertrophic Scar (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Feature | Keloid | Hypertrophic Scar |
|---------|--------|------------------|
| Extends beyond wound | **Yes** | No |
| Regresses spontaneously | No | **Yes** |
| Time of appearance | Months to years | Weeks |
| Common sites | Chest, shoulder, ear lobe | Anywhere |
| Recurrence after excision | **High** | Low |
| Genetics | Family history, common in dark skin | Less genetic |
| Treatment | Steroids, compression, surgery + adjuvant | Conservative, excision |

### Factors Affecting Wound Healing

| Impairs Healing | Promotes Healing |
|----------------|-----------------|
| Malnutrition (esp. protein/Vit C/Zinc) | Adequate nutrition |
| Diabetes mellitus | Normal blood glucose |
| Steroids (anti-inflammatory) | Growth factors (EGF, TGF-β, PDGF) |
| Ischemia, infection | Good blood supply |
| Radiation, chemotherapy | Moist wound environment |
| Jaundice, uremia | Appropriate moisture |

## FLUIDS AND ELECTROLYTES

### Crystalloid Compositions (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Fluid | Na (mEq/L) | Cl (mEq/L) | K (mEq/L) | Glucose (g/L) | Osmolarity | pH |
|-------|-----------|-----------|-----------|--------------|-----------|-----|
| **Normal Saline (0.9% NaCl)** | 154 | 154 | 0 | 0 | 308 | 5.0 |
| **Ringer's Lactate (Hartmann's)** | 130 | 109 | 4 | 0 | 273 | 6.5 |
| **D5W** | 0 | 0 | 0 | 50 | 278 | ~4 |
| **0.45% NaCl** | 77 | 77 | 0 | 0 | 154 | - |

**Key Points:**
- **NS causes**: Hyperchloremic metabolic acidosis (↑Cl)
- **RL is closest to plasma**: Best resuscitation fluid
- **D5W**: Distributes throughout TBW (8% stays intravascular), used for free water replacement
- **Hyponatremia correction**: Not >8-10 mEq/L per day (risk: central pontine myelinolysis)
- **Hypernatremia correction**: Not >10-12 mEq/L per day (risk: cerebral edema)

### Third Spacing
- Fluid sequestration in non-functional extravascular space
- Causes: Pancreatitis, burns, major surgery, peritonitis, hypoalbuminemia
- **Not mobilized** until condition resolves (Day 3-5 post-op = "third space mobilization" → risk of fluid overload)

### Electrolyte Disturbances

| Disorder | ECG Changes | Treatment |
|---------|------------|-----------|
| **Hypokalemia** | Flattened T, U waves, prolonged QT | Oral/IV KCl (max 40 mEq/hr peripheral, 20 mEq/hr central) |
| **Hyperkalemia** | Peaked T, wide QRS, sine wave | Ca gluconate (membrane stabilization) → Insulin + glucose → Kayexalate/dialysis |
| **Hyponatremia (<120)** | Seizures, coma | Hypertonic saline (3% NaCl) + correct slowly |
| **Hypernatremia** | Lethargy, seizures | Free water (D5W or 0.45% NaCl) + correct slowly |
| **Hypocalcemia** | Prolonged QT, Trousseau/Chvostek | IV calcium gluconate |

## BREAST

### BIRADS Classification (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| BIRADS | Description | Malignancy Risk | Action |
|--------|------------|-----------------|--------|
| **0** | Incomplete assessment | - | Additional imaging |
| **1** | Negative | Essentially 0% | Routine screening |
| **2** | Benign | 0% | Routine screening |
| **3** | Probably benign | <2% | 6-month follow-up |
| **4** | Suspicious (4A/4B/4C) | 2-95% | Biopsy |
| **5** | Highly suspicious | >95% | Biopsy |
| **6** | Known malignancy | 100% | Treatment planning |

### Breast Cancer Types

| Type | Frequency | Key Features |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| **IDC (Infiltrating Ductal)** | **Most common (75%)** | Hard irregular mass, spiculated on mammography |
| **ILC (Infiltrating Lobular)** | 10-15% | Loss of E-cadherin, **Indian file pattern**, bilateral |
| **Medullary** | Rare | Pushing margins, lymphocytic infiltrate, BRCA1 association, **good prognosis** |
| **Mucinous (Colloid)** | Rare | Mucin pools, good prognosis, elderly women |
| **Inflammatory** | 1-3% | Skin edema/erythema, **peau d'orange**, dermal lymphatic invasion, **worst prognosis among non-metastatic** |
| **Paget's disease** | Rare | Eczematous nipple changes, Paget cells on biopsy |

**BRCA Genes:**
- **BRCA1**: Chr 17, breast + ovarian cancer; medullary subtype association
- **BRCA2**: Chr 13, breast (male and female) + ovarian + pancreatic cancer
- **Testing indication**: First-degree relative <50 years, bilateral, multiple primaries, male breast cancer

### TNM Staging (Simplified)

| Stage | Features | 5-Year Survival |
|-------|---------|----------------|
| 0 | DCIS/LCIS | ~100% |
| I | T1, N0, M0 (≤2cm) | ~99% |
| II | T2-3 N0-1 | 70-90% |
| III | Large/N2-3 (locally advanced) | 40-70% |
| IV | Any M1 | ~20% |

### Receptor Status and Treatment (PYQ HOTSPOT)
- **ER/PR positive**: Hormonal therapy (tamoxifen premenopausal; aromatase inhibitors postmenopausal)
- **HER2 positive**: Trastuzumab (Herceptin)
- **Triple negative (ER-/PR-/HER2-)**: Chemotherapy only; worst prognosis; common in young/BRCA1 patients

### Sentinel Lymph Node Biopsy (SLNB)
- **Indication**: Clinically node-negative axilla
- **Technique**: Blue dye (isosulfan/methylene) ± Tc-99m sulfur colloid
- **First draining node** = sentinel node; if negative, no axillary dissection needed
- **Skip metastasis**: Possible in ILC

### Surgical Options
- **Modified Radical Mastectomy**: Removal of breast + level I, II axillary nodes (preserve pectoralis major)
- **Lumpectomy (BCS)**: Wide local excision + RT; equivalent survival to mastectomy for stage I/II
- **Halsted radical mastectomy**: Historical, includes pectoralis major - rarely done now

## THYROID SURGERY

### Graves' Disease
- **Autoimmune** (TSH-R stimulating antibodies - TRAb/TSI)
- **Clinical triad**: Hyperthyroidism + exophthalmos + pretibial myxedema
- **Exophthalmos**: Due to glycosaminoglycan deposition in retro-orbital tissue (TSH-R on fibroblasts)
- **Pretibial myxedema**: Non-pitting edema on anterior shin (orange peel skin)
- **Treatment**: Antithyroid drugs (PTU/carbimazole) → radioiodine/surgery

### Thyroid Cancer Types (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Type | Frequency | Origin | Features | Prognosis |
|------|-----------|--------|---------|-----------|
| **Papillary** | **Most common (80%)** | Follicular cells | Psammoma bodies, ground glass (Orphan Annie) nuclei, nuclear grooves, RET/BRAF mutation, lymphatic spread | **Best** |
| **Follicular** | 10-15% | Follicular cells | Capsular + vascular invasion (not lymphocytic infiltrate), hematogenous spread, RAS mutation | Good |
| **Medullary** | 5-10% | **C cells (parafollicular)** | Calcitonin marker, amyloid stroma, MEN2A (RET mutation)/MEN2B, CEA marker | Intermediate |
| **Anaplastic** | <2% | Dedifferentiated | All ages, rapid growth, tracheal invasion, no iodine uptake | **Worst** |

**High-yield facts:**
- **Papillary**: Spreads to lymph nodes but still **best prognosis** ("benign thyroid cancer")
- **Follicular**: Cannot distinguish adenoma from carcinoma on FNAC (need capsule/vascular invasion on histology)
- **Medullary**: Screen family with calcitonin/RET mutation; prophylactic thyroidectomy in MEN2B infancy
- **Lateral aberrant thyroid**: Actually **metastatic papillary carcinoma** in lymph node
- **FNAC contraindicated in follicular lesion** (cannot distinguish adenoma from carcinoma)

### Thyroidectomy Complications (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Complication | Structure Injured | Presentation |
|-------------|------------------|-------------|
| **Recurrent laryngeal nerve** | During thyroidectomy | Unilateral: hoarseness; Bilateral: stridor, respiratory distress |
| **External branch of SLN** | During superior pole ligation | Loss of high-pitched voice, vocal fatigue |
| **Parathyroid glands** | Inadvertent removal/devascularization | Hypocalcemia (Chvostek/Trousseau signs, tetany) |
| **Thyroid storm** | Post-op thyrotoxic crisis | High fever, tachycardia, altered sensorium |
| **Reactionary hemorrhage** | Wound hematoma | Stridor (compresses trachea) - **open wound immediately** |

**Hypocalcemia post-thyroidectomy**: Earliest sign = **perioral numbness** (24-48h post-op); treat with IV calcium gluconate

## GI SURGERY

### ESOPHAGUS

#### Achalasia Cardia (PYQ HOTSPOT)
- **Pathology**: Loss of ganglion cells in Auerbach's plexus → failure of LES relaxation + aperistalsis
- **Gold standard diagnosis**: **Manometry** (incomplete LES relaxation, aperistalsis)
- **Barium**: Bird-beak/rat-tail appearance; dilated esophagus
- **Endoscopy**: Exclude malignancy (pseudo-achalasia)
- **Treatment**: 
  - Pharmacological: Ca channel blockers/nitrates (temporary)
  - **Pneumatic dilation** (best non-surgical)
  - **Heller cardiomyotomy** (surgical gold standard) ± fundoplication
  - Botox injection (elderly/poor surgical candidates)

#### Esophageal Carcinoma
- **Upper 2/3**: **Squamous cell carcinoma** (smoking, alcohol, hot food, Plummer-Vinson syndrome)
- **Lower 1/3**: **Adenocarcinoma** (Barrett's esophagus - metaplasia from GERD)
- **Barrett's esophagus**: Intestinal metaplasia; IM → dysplasia → adenocarcinoma sequence; **Diagnosis**: Endoscopy + biopsy (goblet cells)
- **Staging**: CT chest/abdomen/pelvis + EUS (best for T staging) + PET-CT
- **Surgery**: Ivor-Lewis esophagectomy (right thoracotomy + midline laparotomy)

### STOMACH

#### Gastric Cancer
- **Most common site**: Antrum (pyloric antrum)
- **Lauren Classification**:
  - **Intestinal type**: Gland formation, associated with H. pylori, better prognosis, common in males
  - **Diffuse type**: Signet ring cells, linitis plastica, younger patients, **worse prognosis**
- **Metastatic patterns** (PYQ HOTSPOT):
  - **Virchow's node**: Left supraclavicular lymph node (Troisier's sign)
  - **Krukenberg tumor**: Ovarian metastasis (signet ring cells)
  - **Sister Mary Joseph nodule**: Umbilical metastasis
  - **Blumer's shelf**: Rectal shelf (on PR exam = peritoneal deposits)
- **Diagnosis**: Endoscopy + biopsy; EUS for staging
- **Surgery**: Gastrectomy (D2 dissection preferred); **D2 > D1** for curative intent

#### GIST (Gastrointestinal Stromal Tumor)
- **Origin**: Interstitial cells of Cajal (pacemaker cells)
- **Marker**: **c-KIT (CD117)** - tyrosine kinase receptor; also CD34
- **Morphology**: Spindle cells (70%), epithelioid (20%)
- **Mutation**: **c-KIT exon 11** (most common); PDGFRA
- **Treatment**: Surgical resection; **Imatinib (Gleevec)** for unresectable/metastatic/adjuvant
- **Risk stratification**: Size + mitotic index (Fletcher criteria)

### SMALL BOWEL

#### Meckel's Diverticulum - Rule of 2s (PYQ HOTSPOT)
- **2%** of population
- **2 feet** (60 cm) from ileocecal valve (on antimesenteric border)
- **2 inches** (5 cm) long
- **2 years** most symptomatic (children)
- **2 types** of ectopic mucosa: Gastric (most common - cause bleeding) and pancreatic
- **True diverticulum** (all layers of bowel wall)
- **Most common presentation adult**: Intestinal obstruction; **Most common presentation child**: Painless rectal bleeding
- **Diagnosis**: **Meckel's scan** (Tc-99m pertechnetate - localizes to gastric mucosa)
- **Lithmann's diverticulum**: Persistent connection to umbilicus

#### Small Bowel Obstruction (SBO)
- **Causes**: Adhesions (most common) > hernias > intussusception > tumors
- **Closed loop obstruction**: **Most dangerous** - no proximal decompression, rapid vascular compromise
- **Signs**: Colicky pain, distension, vomiting (early = high obstruction), obstipation
- **X-ray**: Step-ladder pattern, air-fluid levels, valvulae conniventes (distinguishes from colon)
- **Treatment**: NBM + IV fluids + NG tube; Surgery if no improvement or closed loop/strangulation

### APPENDIX

#### Appendicitis (PYQ HOTSPOT)
- **McBurney's point**: Junction of lateral 1/3 and medial 2/3 of line from ASIS to umbilicus
- **Clinical Signs**:
  - **Rovsing's sign**: Pain at McBurney's on palpating LIF
  - **Psoas sign**: Pain on right hip extension (retrocecal appendix)
  - **Obturator sign**: Pain on internal rotation of flexed right hip (pelvic appendix)
  - **Dunphy's sign**: Increased pain with cough (peritoneal irritation)

**Alvarado Score (MANTRELS)**:

| Parameter | Score |
|-----------|-------|
| Migration of pain to RIF | 1 |
| Anorexia | 1 |
| Nausea/Vomiting | 1 |
| Tenderness RIF | 2 |
| Rebound tenderness | 1 |
| Elevated temperature | 1 |
| Leucocytosis | 2 |
| Shift to left | 1 |
| **Total** | **10** |

- Score ≥7: Highly likely appendicitis; Score 5-6: CT/observation; Score <4: Unlikely
- **Treatment**: Appendicectomy (open or laparoscopic)
- **Appendix mass**: Appendicular mass (5-10 days) = Ochsner-Sherren regimen (conservative) initially, interval appendicectomy after 6-8 weeks
- **Appendicular abscess**: Drain + antibiotics

#### Carcinoid Tumor
- **Most common tumor of appendix** (and most common GI carcinoid)
- **Site**: Tip of appendix (not at base)
- **Size <2 cm**: Appendicectomy; **>2 cm**: Right hemicolectomy
- **Carcinoid syndrome**: Only with liver metastasis (serotonin bypasses portal metabolism)
- **Marker**: **5-HIAA in urine** (5-hydroxyindoleacetic acid)
- **WDHA**: Watery diarrhea, hypokalemia, achlorhydria (VIPoma - different from carcinoid)

### COLON

#### Colorectal Cancer (PYQ HOTSPOT)

**Hereditary Syndromes:**

| Syndrome | Gene | Features |
|---------|------|---------|
| **FAP** | **APC gene (chr 5q)** | >100 polyps, 100% cancer risk by 40, autosomal dominant; Gardner (osteomas+desmoids), Turcot (brain tumors) variants |
| **Attenuated FAP** | APC (3' end) | 10-100 polyps, later onset |
| **HNPCC (Lynch)** | **MLH1, MSH2** (mismatch repair genes) | Amsterdam criteria, microsatellite instability (MSI-H), right-sided colon, early onset |
| **MAP** | MYH (MUTYH) | Autosomal recessive, 10-100 polyps |
| **PJS** | STK11 | Hamartomatous polyps, pigmentation |

**Amsterdam Criteria for Lynch (3-2-1-0):**
- **3** relatives with CRC/Lynch-related cancer
- **2** successive generations
- **1** relative <50 years
- **0** FAP excluded

**Left vs Right Colon Cancer:**

| Feature | Right (Ascending) | Left (Descending/Sigmoid) |
|---------|-----------------|--------------------------|
| Presentation | Iron deficiency anemia, mass | Change in bowel habit, obstruction |
| Blood | Occult | Frank rectal bleeding |
| Shape | Polypoid/fungating | Annular/"Apple core" |
| Surgery | Right hemicolectomy | Left hemi/sigmoid/anterior resection |

**Staging:**

| Dukes | Modified Astler-Coller | TNM | 5-year Survival |
|-------|----------------------|-----|----------------|
| A | A, B1 | T1-2 N0 M0 | ~90% |
| B | B2, B3 | T3-4 N0 M0 | ~65-75% |
| C | C1, C2, C3 | Any T N1-2 M0 | ~30-60% |
| D | D | Any M1 | <10% |

### RECTUM AND ANUS

#### Hemorrhoids (PYQ HOTSPOT)

**Internal Hemorrhoids Grading:**

| Grade | Description | Treatment |
|-------|------------|---------|
| I | Bleed but don't prolapse | Conservative, sclerotherapy |
| II | Prolapse on straining, reduce spontaneously | Banding |
| III | Prolapse, require manual reduction | Banding/surgical |
| IV | Permanently prolapsed, irreducible | **Surgical (hemorrhoidectomy)** |

- **Internal**: Above dentate line, covered by columnar epithelium, **painless** bleeding (superior rectal artery - 3,7,11 o'clock)
- **External**: Below dentate line, covered by squamous epithelium, **painful** thrombosis

#### Fistula-in-Ano

**Parks Classification:**
1. **Intersphincteric** (most common - 70%)
2. **Transsphincteric** (25%)
3. **Suprasphincteric**
4. **Extrasphincteric**

**Goodsall's Rule (PYQ HOTSPOT):**
- **Anterior fistulas** (anterior to transverse anal line): Open internally at nearest crypt
- **Posterior fistulas**: Open internally at posterior midline crypt (curved track)
- Exception: Anterior opening >3cm from anus follows Goodsall's posterior rule

#### Anal Fissure
- **Site**: **Posterior midline** (90%) - least blood supply (ischemic theory)
- Anterior fissure in women (obstetric trauma), Crohn's, syphilis
- **Internal anal sphincter spasm** → reduced blood flow → chronic fissure
- **Sentinel pile**: Skin tag at lower end of chronic fissure
- **Treatment stepladder**:
  1. Conservative (sitz bath, laxatives)
  2. 0.2% GTN ointment (headache side effect)
  3. Calcium channel blockers (diltiazem)
  4. Botox injection
  5. **Lateral internal sphincterotomy** (gold standard surgical)

## LIVER, BILIARY, AND PANCREAS

### GALLBLADDER

#### Cholelithiasis
**Types of Stones:**

| Type | Composition | Color | X-ray | Associations |
|------|------------|-------|-------|-------------|
| **Cholesterol** | Cholesterol (>50%) | Yellow/white | **Radiolucent** (90%) | 5Fs: Fat, Forty, Female, Fertile, Fair |
| **Pigment (Black)** | Bilirubin + calcium | Black | Radiopaque | Hemolysis (sickle cell), cirrhosis, ileal disease |
| **Pigment (Brown)** | Calcium bilirubinate | Brown | Radiolucent | Bacterial infection (Clonorchis), biliary stasis |
| **Mixed** | Mixed | - | Variable | Most common |

**Murphy's Sign**: Inspiratory arrest on palpation of RUQ (acute cholecystitis)
**Courvoisier's Law**: Palpable, non-tender gallbladder in jaundice = **not** gallstones (= periampullary tumor)

#### Biliary Infections (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Condition | Features |
|----------|---------|
| **Charcot's Triad** | RUQ pain + Fever + Jaundice = Cholangitis |
| **Reynolds' Pentad** | Charcot's triad + Hypotension + Mental changes = **Suppurative cholangitis** |

- **Management of cholangitis**: Antibiotics + **urgent ERCP** (biliary decompression)
- **Mirizzi syndrome**: External compression of CHD by stone in cystic duct/Hartmann's pouch

#### Gallbladder Carcinoma
- **Risk factors**: Gallstones (large >3 cm), porcelain gallbladder (**calcified GB - still risk factor**), typhoid carriers, anomalous pancreaticobiliary junction
- **Incidental finding** post-cholecystectomy common
- **Prognosis poor**: Usually advanced at diagnosis

### LIVER

#### Hepatocellular Carcinoma (HCC)
- **Risk factors**: **Cirrhosis** (any cause), HBV (without cirrhosis), HCV, aflatoxin B1, hemochromatosis, alcoholic cirrhosis
- **Marker**: **AFP (alpha-fetoprotein)** - >400 ng/mL highly suggestive
- **Diagnosis**: AFP + triphasic CT (arterial enhancement + washout)
- **Child-Pugh Score** for resectability:

| Parameter | 1 point | 2 points | 3 points |
|-----------|--------|---------|---------|
| Bilirubin (mg/dL) | <2 | 2-3 | >3 |
| Albumin (g/dL) | >3.5 | 2.8-3.5 | <2.8 |
| PT prolongation (sec) | <4 | 4-6 | >6 |
| Ascites | None | Mild | Severe |
| Encephalopathy | None | Grade 1-2 | Grade 3-4 |

- **Child A (5-6)**: Good; **Child B (7-9)**: Moderate; **Child C (10-15)**: Poor
- **MILAN criteria** for transplant: Single nodule ≤5 cm or ≤3 nodules all ≤3 cm, no vascular invasion/extrahepatic spread

#### Hydatid Cyst
- **Organism**: **Echinococcus granulosus** (tapeworm of dogs)
- **Structure**: Pericyst (host) + ectocyst + endocyst (germinal layer); daughter cysts; brood capsules; scolices; hydatid sand
- **Casoni test**: Intradermal test (wheal reaction) - **not used clinically now** (Weinberg complement fixation preferred)
- **X-ray**: Calcified cyst; **CT**: Daughter cysts pathognomonic
- **NEVER aspirate** (risk of anaphylaxis/seeding) - contraindicated
- **Treatment**: PAIR (Puncture-Aspiration-Injection-Reaspiration) with albendazole cover; or surgery

### PANCREAS

#### Acute Pancreatitis

**Ranson's Criteria:**

| At Admission | Within 48 Hours |
|-------------|----------------|
| Age >55 | Hematocrit fall >10% |
| WBC >16,000 | BUN rise >5 mg/dL |
| Blood glucose >200 | Calcium <8 mg/dL |
| LDH >350 | PaO2 <60 mmHg |
| AST >250 | Base deficit >4 mEq/L |
| | Fluid sequestration >6L |

- **Score ≥3**: Severe; **Mortality**: 0-2: <1%, 3-4: 15%, 5-6: 40%, >6: 100%

**Atlanta Classification (Revised):**
- **Mild**: No organ failure, no local complications
- **Moderately severe**: Transient organ failure (<48h) or local complications
- **Severe**: Persistent organ failure (>48h), single or multi-organ

**Complications:**
- **Pseudocyst**: Collection encased in fibrous wall, >4 weeks; treatment if symptomatic = EUS-guided drainage
- **Pancreatic necrosis**: CT - non-enhancing areas; infected necrosis = CT-guided aspiration or necrosectomy
- **Pancreatic abscess**: Well-defined collection of pus

**Etiology**: Gallstones (most common in women) > Alcohol (most common in men) > ERCP > Hypercalcemia > Hyperlipidemia > Trauma

#### Chronic Pancreatitis
- **Alcohol**: Most common cause
- **Cambridge classification** of ductal changes on ERCP
- **Duct of Wirsung**: Main pancreatic duct; calcifications in alcoholic CP
- **Puestow procedure**: Lateral pancreaticojejunostomy (for dilated duct >7mm); decompression surgery
- **Whipple operation**: Indicated if cannot exclude malignancy

#### Pancreatic Cancer
- **Head most common** (70%); body/tail detected late
- **Presentation**: Progressive obstructive jaundice (head), weight loss, new-onset diabetes, back pain
- **Double duct sign**: Simultaneous dilation of **CBD + pancreatic duct** on CT/MRCP (= periampullary/head tumor)
- **CA 19-9**: Tumor marker (Lewis antigen dependent; false negative in Lewis antigen negative)
- **Whipple's procedure (Pancreaticoduodenectomy)**: Head tumors; pylorus-preserving variant (PPPD)
- **Borderline resectable**: Criteria based on vascular involvement
- **Prognosis**: 5-year survival ~10% (overall); resected ~20-25%

## VASCULAR SURGERY

### Peripheral Arterial Disease (PAD)

**Fontaine Classification (PYQ HOTSPOT):**

| Stage | Symptoms |
|-------|---------|
| I | Asymptomatic |
| **IIa** | Claudication >200m |
| **IIb** | Claudication <200m |
| III | Rest pain |
| IV | Tissue loss (ulcer/gangrene) |

- **ABI (Ankle-Brachial Index)**: Normal >0.9; Claudication 0.4-0.9; Rest pain <0.4; Critical ischemia <0.3
- **Intermittent claudication**: Calf pain on walking, relieved by rest (5 minutes)
- **Critical limb ischemia**: Rest pain >2 weeks or tissue loss
- **Leriche syndrome**: Aortoiliac occlusion → bilateral claudication + impotence + absent femoral pulses

### Aortic Aneurysm (PYQ HOTSPOT)
- **Definition**: Dilation >1.5× normal diameter or **>3 cm** for infrarenal aorta
- **Repair threshold**: **5.5 cm** (men); 5.0 cm (women) or growth >1 cm/year
- **Infrarenal AAA**: Most common type; risk factor: smoking, hypertension, atherosclerosis
- **Endovascular repair (EVAR)**: Preferred if anatomy suitable; lower short-term mortality; requires follow-up
- **Open repair**: Required if unsuitable anatomy
- **Ruptured AAA**: Triad - hypotension + pulsatile abdominal mass + back/flank pain; **emergency surgery**

### DVT/PE

**Wells Score for DVT:**
- Active cancer (1); immobilization (1); bedridden >3 days or surgery <12 weeks (1); tenderness along deep vein (1); entire leg swollen (1); calf >3 cm than other (1); pitting edema (1); collateral veins (1); previous DVT (1); alternative diagnosis likely (-2)
- **≥2**: High probability → duplex USG; **<2**: Low probability → D-dimer

**Diagnosis:**
- **D-dimer**: High sensitivity, low specificity; use to rule out (negative predictive value)
- **Duplex ultrasound**: **Diagnostic investigation of choice** for DVT
- **CTPA**: Gold standard for PE
- **V/Q scan**: Alternative for PE if CTPA contraindicated (CKD, contrast allergy, pregnancy)

**Treatment:**
- **Anticoagulation**: LMWH/fondaparinux → warfarin (INR 2-3) or DOAC (rivaroxaban, apixaban)
- **Duration**: Provoked DVT: 3-6 months; Unprovoked/recurrent: 6-12 months; cancer: indefinite LMWH
- **Massive PE**: Thrombolysis (alteplase) or embolectomy if contraindicated
- **IVC filter**: If anticoagulation contraindicated or failure

### Varicose Veins
- **Great saphenous vein** territory most common
- **Saphena varix**: Dilation of saphenous vein at saphenofemoral junction; transmitted cough impulse; **bluish lump at groin**
- **Trendelenburg test**: Control at SFJ then release = SFJ incompetence (most common cause)
- **Trendelenburg tourniquet test**: For perforator incompetence
- **CEAP Classification**: **C**linical, **E**tiological, **A**natomical, **P**athophysiological

| CEAP C Class | Description |
|-------------|------------|
| C0 | No visible disease |
| C1 | Telangiectasias |
| C2 | Varicose veins |
| C3 | Edema |
| C4 | Skin changes |
| C5 | Healed venous ulcer |
| C6 | Active venous ulcer |

- **Venous ulcer**: Medial malleolus, sloping edges, painless (cf. arterial = punched out, painful, lateral malleolus)

## UROLOGY

### BPH (Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia)
- **Zone**: **Transition zone** enlargement
- **IPSS (International Prostate Symptom Score)**: 0-7 mild; 8-19 moderate; 20-35 severe
- **PSA**: Normal <4 ng/mL; 4-10 ng/mL = "gray zone"; >10 ng/mL = high suspicion
- **PSA density**: PSA/volume; >0.15 suspicious
- **Treatment**:
  - Medical: **Alpha-1 blockers** (tamsulosin - fast relief); **5-ARI** (finasteride - reduces prostate volume, long-term)
  - Surgical: **TURP** (gold standard); TUIP; open prostatectomy
- **TURP syndrome**: Dilutional hyponatremia from glycine irrigation absorption

### Prostate Cancer
- **Zone**: **Peripheral zone**
- **Gleason score**: 2-10; sum of two most prevalent grades (3+4=7 intermediate; 4+3=7 intermediate but worse prognosis)
- **PSA**: >10 ng/mL high suspicion; free:total PSA ratio
- **Staging**: Bone scan (M1b), CT (N staging)
- **Treatment**:
  - Localized: Radical prostatectomy or radiotherapy
  - Advanced: **Androgen deprivation therapy (LHRH agonists/antagonists + antiandrogens)**
  - Castration-resistant: Enzalutamide, docetaxel

### Renal Cell Carcinoma (RCC)
- **Most common type**: **Clear cell RCC** (75%) - **VHL gene** (chromosome 3p)
- **Classic triad (PYQ HOTSPOT)**: Flank pain + hematuria + flank mass (only 10% have all three = advanced)
- **Paraneoplastic**: Polycythemia (EPO), hypertension (renin), hypercalcemia (PTHrP), Stauffer syndrome (liver dysfunction without metastasis)
- **Staging**: CT with contrast; thrombus in IVC common
- **Treatment**: Radical nephrectomy; partial for small tumors; systemic therapy (sunitinib, pazopanib) for metastatic

### Transitional Cell Carcinoma (TCC)
- **Occupational risk**: **Aniline dyes** (beta-naphthylamine), rubber industry; smoking most common overall
- **Bilateral involvement**: If multifocal; upper tract TCC
- **Cystoscopy + biopsy**: Diagnostic gold standard
- **Treatment**: Superficial: TUR-BT + intravesical BCG/mitomycin; Invasive: Radical cystectomy + ileal conduit/neobladder

### Nephrolithiasis

| Stone Type | Frequency | X-ray | Urine pH | Treatment |
|-----------|-----------|-------|---------|---------|
| **Calcium oxalate** | 75-80% | **Radiopaque** | Acidic | Hydration, thiazides |
| **Struvite (triple phosphate)** | 10-15% | **Radiopaque (staghorn)** | Alkaline | PCNL, antibiotics |
| **Uric acid** | 5-10% | **Radiolucent** | Acidic | Alkalinize urine, allopurinol |
| **Cystine** | 1-3% | Faintly opaque | - | Hydration, alkalinize |

**Investigations**: Plain KUB → CT KUB (most sensitive) → IVU

**Treatment indications:**
- **ESWL**: <2 cm, accessible stone, no obstruction (except lower pole - poor results)
- **PCNL**: >2 cm, staghorn, failed ESWL, lower pole
- **URS**: Mid and lower ureter stones

## HERNIAS

### Classification and Comparison (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Feature | Indirect Inguinal | Direct Inguinal | Femoral |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|---------|
| Through | **Deep ring → inguinal canal → superficial ring** | **Hesselbach's triangle** | Femoral canal |
| Relation to inferior epigastric | **Lateral** | Medial | - |
| Hesselbach's triangle | No | Yes | No |
| Covered by internal oblique | Yes (complete) | No (partial/fundus only) | No |
| Sex | M >>> F | M > F | **F > M** (but inguinal still more common overall) |
| Reduces with pressure | Deep ring | Medial to ring | Femoral ring |
| Complications | Less strangulation | Less strangulation | **Most commonly strangulates** |
| Type | Congenital (patent processus vaginalis) | Acquired (muscle weakness) | Acquired |

**Hesselbach's Triangle**: Inguinal ligament (inferior) + lateral border of rectus (medial) + inferior epigastric vessels (lateral/superior)

### Hernia Complications
- **Reducible**: Can be pushed back
- **Irreducible (Incarcerated)**: Cannot reduce; no vascular compromise
- **Obstructed**: Irreducible + bowel obstruction
- **Strangulated**: Irreducible + vascular compromise → ischemia → gangrene
- **Richter's hernia**: Only one wall of bowel in sac (can strangulate without obstruction)
- **Littre's hernia**: Meckel's diverticulum in sac
- **Maydl's hernia**: Two loops in sac (W-shaped); middle loop in abdomen strangulates

### Surgical Repair Options

| Technique | Description |
|-----------|------------|
| **Bassini** | Approximates conjoint tendon to inguinal ligament (tension repair) |
| **Shouldice** | 4-layer repair of posterior wall; low recurrence; no mesh |
| **Lichtenstein** | Tension-free mesh repair; **gold standard** open repair |
| **TEP (Total Extraperitoneal)** | Laparoscopic; pre-peritoneal mesh; no peritoneal entry |
| **TAPP (TransAbdominal PrePeritoneal)** | Laparoscopic; enters peritoneum |

## BURNS

### Rule of Nines (PYQ HOTSPOT)

**Wallace Rule of Nines - Adult:**

| Region | BSA% |
|--------|------|
| Head + Neck | **9%** |
| Each Upper Limb | **9%** |
| Anterior Trunk | **18%** |
| Posterior Trunk | **18%** |
| Each Lower Limb | **18%** |
| Perineum | **1%** |
| **Total** | **100%** |

**Lund-Browder Chart (Children)**: Head = 18% (larger), legs = less; more accurate

**Burn depth:**
- **Superficial (1st degree)**: Epidermis only; erythema, pain; no blistering; heals without scarring
- **Partial thickness (2nd degree)**: Epidermis + dermis; **blistering**, painful; superficial partial heals 2 weeks, deep partial may need grafting
- **Full thickness (3rd degree)**: All skin layers; **leathery, painless**, eschar; requires grafting

### Fluid Resuscitation - Parkland Formula (PYQ HOTSPOT)
**Formula**: **4 × weight (kg) × % TBSA burned** = mL of **Ringer's Lactate** in first 24 hours

- **First 8 hours**: Half the calculated volume (from time of burn, not time of arrival)
- **Next 16 hours**: Remaining half
- **Goal urine output**: 0.5-1 mL/kg/hr (adults); 1 mL/kg/hr (children)

**Modified Brooke Formula**: 2 × weight × %TBSA in RL + colloid second 24h

### Escharotomy Indications
- Full-thickness circumferential burns of extremities (compartment syndrome)
- Circumferential chest burns (respiratory compromise)
- Technique: Longitudinal incisions through eschar to viable tissue

### Skin Grafting
- **Split-thickness skin graft (STSG)**: Epidermis + partial dermis; donor site heals; mesh graft for large areas
- **Full-thickness skin graft (FTSG)**: Better cosmesis; requires closure of donor site; face, hands
- **Autograft**: Own skin; best; permanent
- **Allograft (homograft)**: Cadaveric; temporary (rejected in 2-3 weeks)
- **Xenograft (heterograft)**: Pig skin; temporary biological dressing
