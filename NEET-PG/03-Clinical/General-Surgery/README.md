# General Surgery

General Surgery covers the surgical management of conditions across multiple organ systems — including GI, hepatobiliary, endocrine, vascular, and trauma surgery — contributing ~15 questions per NEET PG paper.

## Files in This Folder

| File | Description |
|------|-------------|
| `surgery-notes.md` | Condensed exam-focused notes organized by surgical system and topic |
| `surgery-lecture.md` | In-depth lecture notes covering operative principles, indications, and complications |
| `surgery-mcqs.md` | MCQ bank with high-yield questions on surgical scenarios, imaging, and management |
| `WEAK-TOPICS.md` | Personally flagged weak areas requiring extra revision attention |

## High-Yield Areas

- Hepatobiliary surgery: obstructive jaundice causes, Courvoisier's law, CBD stone management, carcinoma pancreas staging
- GI surgery: intestinal obstruction types, X-ray signs, colorectal carcinoma screening, and appendicitis management
- Hernias: inguinal (direct vs indirect anatomy), femoral hernia, classification of hernias, and operative repair options
- Fluids, electrolytes, and burns: IV fluid composition, electrolyte corrections, Burns formula (Parkland), and Lund-Browder chart
- Surgical oncology: breast carcinoma staging and management, thyroid carcinoma types, and sentinel lymph node biopsy

## NEET PG Weight

Avg 15 questions/year (2019–2024). Stable trend — radiological sign recognition (Rigler's sign, step-ladder pattern, bird-beak) and surgical anatomy are tested every year; GI and hepatobiliary chapters carry the highest question density.
