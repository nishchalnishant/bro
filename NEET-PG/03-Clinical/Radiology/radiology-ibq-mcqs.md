**The chest X-ray finding shown above is MOST consistent with:**
- A) Lobar pneumonia
- B) Pulmonary oedema
- C) Pulmonary embolism
- D) Tension pneumothorax

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct: B** — **Bat-wing (butterfly) sign**, seen in **acute pulmonary oedema**. Bilateral perihilar shadowing with relative peripheral sparing creates a bat-wing shape; caused by fluid redistribution to the perihilar zone due to raised pulmonary venous pressure. Other options: A) lobar pneumonia causes unilateral lobar consolidation; C) PE shows oligaemia (Westermark sign); D) tension pneumothorax shows absent lung markings with mediastinal shift away.

**The radiological finding shown is the Westermark sign. This is MOST associated with:**
- A) Tension pneumothorax
- B) Pulmonary embolism
- C) Lung abscess
- D) Lobar collapse

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

Other options: A) pneumothorax shows absent lung markings at the periphery with a visible pleural line; C) lung abscess shows a cavity with an air-fluid level; D) lobar collapse shows increased density and volume loss.

**Q4.**

**The abdominal X-ray sign shown is the "coffee bean sign." This finding is diagnostic of:**
- A) Intussusception
- B) Caecal volvulus
- C) Sigmoid volvulus
- D) Paralytic ileus

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct: C** — **Coffee bean sign (Omega/horseshoe sign)** on plain AXR is pathognomonic of **sigmoid volvulus**. The massively dilated sigmoid loop with its two walls fused centrally forms a coffee-bean shape pointing to the right upper quadrant. Other options: A) intussusception shows target sign on USS/CT; B) caecal volvulus produces a kidney-shaped loop in the left upper quadrant; D) paralytic ileus shows generalised gaseous distension without a dominant loop.

**Q5.**

![Rigler sign — pneumoperitoneum](../../images/radiology-rigler-sign.jpg)

**The finding of gas outlining both sides of the bowel wall (Rigler/double wall sign) on AXR indicates:**
- A) Intramural gas (pneumatosis intestinalis)
- B) Free intraperitoneal air (pneumoperitoneum)
- C) Retroperitoneal air
- D) Portal venous gas

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

Normally only the inner mucosal surface is visible (gas within lumen only). Other options: A) pneumatosis shows gas within the bowel wall itself; C) retroperitoneal air outlines the kidneys and psoas; D) portal venous gas is a branching lucency overlying the liver.

</details>

**The "string sign of Kantor" shown on barium follow-through is characteristic of:**
- A) Ulcerative colitis
- B) Crohn's disease of the terminal ileum
- C) Intestinal tuberculosis
- D) Carcinoid tumour

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Q8.**

![Silhouette sign — consolidation](../../images/radiology-silhouette-sign-cardiorespiratory.jpg)

**The "silhouette sign" on chest X-ray (loss of a normal border) localises pathology. Loss of the right heart border indicates consolidation in which lobe?**
- A) Right lower lobe
- B) Right upper lobe
- C) Right middle lobe
- D) Left lower lobe

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct: C** — The **silhouette sign**: an intrathoracic opacity erases the border of an adjacent structure when they share the same density. The right heart border is formed by the right middle lobe — consolidation here erases it. Right lower lobe consolidation erases the right hemidiaphragm; right upper lobe erases the right upper mediastinum. This is Felson's silhouette sign.

</details>

**Q9.**

![Air bronchogram sign](../../images/radiology-air-bronchogram.jpg)

**Air bronchograms (visible air-filled airways within opacification) are MOST characteristic of:**
- A) Pleural effusion
- B) Lobar collapse/atelectasis
- C) Airspace (alveolar) consolidation
- D) Pulmonary abscess

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct: C** — **Air bronchograms** occur when the alveoli surrounding airways fill with fluid/exudate while the airways remain air-filled, making them visible. This is characteristic of **airspace consolidation** (pneumonia, pulmonary oedema, haemorrhage). Other options: A) pleural effusion does not contain airways; B) collapse/atelectasis causes absorbed airways — air bronchograms are absent in obstructive collapse; D) abscess shows a cavity with an air-fluid level.

</details>

**Q10.**

**Kerley B lines on a chest X-ray represent:**
- A) Pleural plaques
- B) Dilated lymphatics / interstitial oedema at the costophrenic angles
- C) Pneumonia in the lower zones
- D) Rib metastases

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

</details>

**Q12.**

![Codman triangle — aggressive bone tumour](../../images/radiology-codman-triangle.jpg)

**The Codman triangle (periosteal elevation) is seen in this X-ray. This finding indicates:**
- A) A benign, slow-growing lesion
- B) An aggressive bone lesion (malignant or aggressive infection)
- C) Avascular necrosis
- D) Stress fracture

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct: B** — **Codman triangle** is a triangular area of periosteal new bone formed when a rapidly growing tumour (or aggressive infection) lifts the periosteum — the centre is too fast-growing for mineralisation. It signifies **aggressive bone pathology** (osteosarcoma, Ewing's, aggressive osteomyelitis). It is NOT a specific diagnosis but indicates malignancy or rapid growth. Other options: A) benign lesions form a complete periosteal shell; C) AVN shows crescent/double-line signs; D) stress fractures show a thin transverse lucency.

</details>

**The "crescent sign" shown subchondrally in the femoral head represents:**
- A) Stress fracture through the femoral head

**Q15.**

![Double-line sign AVN — MRI](../../images/radiology-double-line-sign-avascular-necrosis.jpg)

**The "double-line sign" on MRI of the femoral head (bright inner rim, dark outer rim on T2) is pathognomonic of:**
- A) Transient osteoporosis of the hip
- B) Avascular necrosis (osteonecrosis)
- C) Pigmented villonodular synovitis
- D) Stress fracture

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct: B** — **Double-line sign on MRI T2**: the inner bright line represents reactive granulation/hyperaemic tissue; the outer dark line represents sclerotic bone at the interface of viable and necrotic tissue. Other options: A) transient osteoporosis shows diffuse marrow oedema without a double-line; C) PVNS shows haemosiderin (dark on all sequences); D) stress fracture shows a band of low signal without a double-line.

</details>

**Q16.**

![Lemon sign — spina bifida](../../images/radiology-lemon-sign.jpg)

**The "lemon sign" on fetal ultrasound (flattening of the frontal bones giving a lemon shape) is associated with:**
- A) Anencephaly
- B) Open neural tube defect (spina bifida)
- C) Holoprosencephaly
- D) Dandy-Walker malformation

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct: B** — **Lemon sign** (seen at 16–24 weeks): scalloping of the frontal bones creates a lemon-shaped skull on axial USS, caused by downward herniation of the hindbrain through the foramen magnum (Arnold-Chiari type II) in **open spina bifida**. It is seen in ~99% of open NTDs at 16–24 weeks. Other options: A) anencephaly shows absent vault; C) holoprosencephaly shows fused thalami/monoventricle; D) Dandy-Walker shows enlarged posterior fossa with absent vermis.

</details>

**Q17.**

- A) Aqueductal stenosis with hydrocephalus

- D) Normal cerebellar shape variant

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct: B** — **Banana sign**: the cerebellum is curved/crescent-shaped (like a banana) rather than dumbbell-shaped because the posterior fossa is small and the cerebellum wraps around the brainstem, as the hindbrain herniates through the foramen magnum in **Arnold-Chiari type II malformation** associated with open spina bifida. **Q24.**

![Boot-shaped heart — Tetralogy of Fallot](../../images/cxr-tof-boot-shaped-heart.jpg)

**The "boot-shaped heart" (coeur en sabot) on CXR with oligaemic lung fields is the classic appearance of:**
- A) Transposition of the great arteries
- B) Total anomalous pulmonary venous connection
- C) Tetralogy of Fallot
- D) Ventricular septal defect

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct: C** — **Boot-shaped heart in TOF**: right ventricular hypertrophy elevates the cardiac apex, pulmonary infundibular stenosis causes a concave pulmonary bay, and decreased pulmonary blood flow causes oligaemic lung fields — creating the "coeur en sabot" (boot-shaped heart). TOF is the most common cyanotic CHD after 1 year. Other options: A) TGA shows egg-on-its-side appearance; B) TAPVC shows snowman/figure-of-eight appearance; D) VSD shows cardiomegaly with plethoric lung fields.

</details>

**Q25.**

![Egg-on-side — Transposition of great arteries](../../images/cxr-tga-egg-on-side.jpg)

**The "egg-on-its-side" (egg-on-a-string) appearance on CXR with a narrow superior mediastinum and plethoric lung fields is the classic sign of:**
- A) Tetralogy of Fallot
- B) Transposition of the great arteries (TGA)
- C) Truncus arteriosus
- D) Tricuspid atresia

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct: B** — **Egg-on-its-side sign in TGA**: the aorta arises anteriorly from the RV and the pulmonary artery from the LV — the parallel relationship of the great arteries creates a narrow vascular pedicle on frontal CXR, with an oval cardiac shadow (egg shape) and plethoric lung fields (increased pulmonary blood flow). TGA is the most common cyanotic CHD presenting in the **neonatal period**. Other options: A) TOF shows boot-shaped heart with oligaemia; C) truncus arteriosus shows a high right aortic arch; D) tricuspid atresia shows a flat right heart border.

</details>
