## Antenatal Care (ANC)

### Booking Visit
- **Gestational age by LNMP**: Naegele's rule — subtract 3 months, add 7 days, add 1 year
- **Gestational age by USG**: CRL (crown-rump length) most accurate <12 weeks; BPD + FL + AC used after 12 weeks
- **Earliest USG finding**: Gestational sac at 4.5 weeks; Fetal heartbeat at 6 weeks
- First booking visit ideally before 10 weeks

### ANC Schedule (India)
| Visit | Timing | Key Actions |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| 1st | <12 weeks | History, weight, BP, Hb, blood group, VDRL, HIV, urine R/E, USG dating |
| 2nd | 14-20 weeks | Anomaly USG at 18-20 weeks, quadruple screen |
| 3rd | 28-32 weeks | GDM screening (GCT/OGTT), repeat Hb, anti-D if Rh-negative |
| 4th | 36-40 weeks | Presentation, pelvimetry, birth planning |

### Fundal Height — McDonald's Rule
- **Fundal height in cm = Gestational age in weeks** (from pubic symphysis to uterine fundus)
- 12 weeks: Pubic symphysis
- 20 weeks: Umbilicus
- 36 weeks: Xiphisternum
- 40 weeks: Drops slightly (lightening)
- **±2 cm discrepancy warrants USG**

### Fetal Movement Counting
- **Cardiff Count-to-10**: Patient counts until 10 movements felt — should be within 12 hours
- **Alarm signal**: Fewer than 10 movements in 12 hours — requires NST/BPP
- Fetal movements begin: **18-20 weeks** (primigravida), **16-18 weeks** (multigravida)

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: McDonald's rule; Cardiff count definition; booking visit investigations

## Normal Labor

### Bishop Score (Cervical Assessment for Induction)

| Parameter | 0 | 1 | 2 | 3 |
|-----------|---|---|---|---|
| Dilatation (cm) | 0 | 1-2 | 3-4 | ≥5 |
| Effacement (%) | 0-30 | 40-50 | 60-70 | ≥80 |
| Station | -3 | -2 | -1/0 | +1/+2 |
| Consistency | Firm | Medium | Soft | — |
| Position | Posterior | Mid | Anterior | — |

- **Score ≥8**: Favorable cervix, induction likely to succeed
- **Score ≤6**: Unfavorable, consider cervical priming (PGE2/Foley catheter/misoprostol)

### Stages of Labor

| Stage | Definition | Primi | Multi |
|-------|-----------|-------|-------|
| Stage I (latent) | Onset → 4 cm dilatation | Up to 20h | Up to 14h |
| Stage I (active) | 4 cm → full dilatation | Up to 12h (Friedman's curve: 1.2 cm/h) | Up to 6h (1.5 cm/h) |
| Stage II | Full dilatation → delivery | Up to 2h (3h with epidural) | Up to 1h (2h with epidural) |
| Stage III | Delivery → placenta expulsion | 15-30 min | 15-30 min |

- Stage IV: First 2 hours postpartum (observation)

### Mechanism of Labor (Vertex — LOA most common)
1. **Engagement**: Biparietal diameter passes through pelvic inlet (head at 0 station)
2. **Descent**: Throughout labor
3. **Flexion**: Chin onto chest — suboccipitobregmatic diameter (9.5 cm) presents
4. **Internal rotation**: Occiput rotates to OA (pubic symphysis) — 90° rotation in LOA
5. **Crowning**: Largest diameter passes through introitus
6. **Extension**: Head delivers by extension under pubic symphysis
7. **External rotation (restitution)**: Head rotates to face mother's thigh (45°)
8. **Expulsion**: Anterior shoulder delivered first, then posterior

### Active Management of Third Stage (AMTSL)
- **Oxytocin 10 IU IM** (first-line) within 1 minute of delivery
- **Syntometrine** (ergometrine + oxytocin) — avoided in hypertension
- **Controlled cord traction (Brandt-Andrews)**: Suprapubic counterpressure + gentle traction on clamped cord
- **Uterine massage**: After placenta delivery, not before (may cause partial separation)
- Signs of placental separation: Lengthening of cord, gush of blood, uterus becomes globular and rises

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Bishop score cutoffs; LOA mechanism; AMTSL components

## Antepartum Hemorrhage (APH)

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: APH = bleeding after 24 weeks (or 28 weeks by some definitions); before = abortion/threatened abortion

### Differential Diagnosis

| Feature | Placenta Previa | Placental Abruption |
|---------|----------------|---------------------|
| Pain | **Painless** | **Painful** |
| Bleeding | Bright red, revealed | Dark red, may be concealed |
| Uterus | Soft, non-tender | Hard, hypertonic ("woody hard") |
| Fetal presentation | Malpresentation common | Normal |
| Fetal heart | Usually normal | May be absent (severe) |
| Shock | Proportionate to blood loss | Disproportionate (concealed) |
| USG | Placenta over os | Retroplacental clot |
| Onset | After painless bleed | Often after trauma/hypertension |

### Placenta Previa
**Classification:**
- **Type I (Low-lying)**: Placenta in lower segment, not reaching os
- **Type II (Marginal)**: Edge reaches margin of internal os
- **Type III (Partial)**: Partially covers internal os
- **Type IV (Complete/Central)**: Completely covers internal os

**Management by Gestational Age:**

| Gestation | Management |
|-----------|-----------|
| <34 weeks, stable | Hospitalize, corticosteroids, no PV exam |
| 34-36 weeks | Elective LSCS at 36-37 weeks |
| >37 weeks or active bleeding | Immediate LSCS |

- **Diagnosis**: Transvaginal USG (safe and accurate); transabdominal may have false positive due to bladder
- **Double setup examination**: Abandoned — replaced by USG
- **Placenta accreta spectrum**: Abnormal invasion — accreta (superficial), increta (into myometrium), percreta (through serosa) — risk with previous LSCS + anterior low-lying placenta

### Placental Abruption
- **Revealed**: Blood tracks down between membranes and uterine wall — external bleeding
- **Concealed**: Blood collects behind placenta — no external bleeding, worse prognosis
- **Mixed**: Both components
- **Couvelaire uterus (Uteroplacental apoplexy)**: Blood extravasates into myometrium — purple/blue uterus, uterus doesn't contract
- **Complications**: DIC (consumptive coagulopathy), acute renal failure, PPH, stillbirth
- **Kleihauer-Betke test**: Detects fetal RBCs in maternal blood — quantifies fetal-maternal hemorrhage
- **Management**: Stabilize, blood products, deliver (vaginal if stable + mild, LSCS if fetal distress/severe)

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Couvelaire uterus; Kleihauer-Betke test; Type IV placenta previa = complete

## Hypertensive Disorders of Pregnancy

### Classification
| Disorder | Criteria |
|---------|---------|
| Gestational hypertension | BP ≥140/90 after 20 weeks, NO proteinuria, resolves by 12 weeks postpartum |
| Pre-eclampsia | BP ≥140/90 after 20 weeks + proteinuria ≥300mg/24h OR protein:creatinine ratio ≥0.3 OR dipstick 2+ |
| Eclampsia | Pre-eclampsia + **seizures** (not attributed to other cause) |
| Chronic hypertension | Pre-existing or <20 weeks |
| Superimposed pre-eclampsia | Chronic hypertension + new-onset proteinuria/worsening |

### Severe Features of Pre-eclampsia (any one = severe)
- **BP ≥160/110 mmHg**
- Thrombocytopenia (<100,000/μL)
- Renal insufficiency (creatinine >1.1 mg/dL)
- Impaired liver function (AST/ALT >2× normal)
- Pulmonary edema
- New-onset headache unresponsive to medications
- Visual disturbances (scotomata, photophobia)
- **RUQ/epigastric pain** (hepatic subcapsular hematoma)

### HELLP Syndrome
- **H**: Hemolysis (microangiopathic, schistocytes)
- **EL**: Elevated Liver enzymes (AST/ALT elevated)
- **LP**: Low Platelets (<100,000/μL)
- Tennessee classification: Class I (PLT <50,000), Class II (50-100,000), Class III (100-150,000)
- Treatment: Deliver (regardless of gestational age if severe), MgSO4, corticosteroids (may improve platelet count)

### Treatment of Severe Hypertension (Acute)
| Drug | Dose | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| **Labetalol IV** | 20mg, repeat 40-80mg q10min, max 300mg | First-line |
| Hydralazine IV | 5mg bolus, repeat 5-10mg q20min | Older drug, tachycardia |
| Nifedipine oral | 10mg, repeat in 30 min | Avoid sublingual |

**Target BP**: 140-155/90-105 (avoid rapid reduction — uteroplacental insufficiency)

### MgSO4 for Eclampsia
- **Loading dose**: 4g IV over 15-20 min
- **Maintenance**: 1-2 g/hr IV OR 5g IM every 4 hours (Pritchard regimen)
- **Therapeutic level**: 4-7 mEq/L (2-3.5 mmol/L)
- **Toxicity signs** (in order): Loss of patellar reflexes (9 mEq/L) → Respiratory depression (12 mEq/L) → Cardiac arrest (15 mEq/L)
- **Antidote**: **Calcium gluconate 1g IV** (10 mL of 10% solution)
- Monitor: Urine output >25 mL/hr, respiratory rate >12/min, patellar reflex present

### Timing of Delivery
- Severe pre-eclampsia ≥34 weeks: Deliver
- Eclampsia: Stabilize first, then deliver (not emergency CS for eclampsia alone)
- Preterm severe PE <34 weeks: Individualize (steroids, MgSO4, antihypertensives, then deliver)

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: MgSO4 antidote = calcium gluconate; HELLP criteria; therapeutic MgSO4 level

## Preterm Labor

- **Definition**: Onset of labor before **37 completed weeks**
- **PPROM** (Preterm Premature Rupture of Membranes): Rupture before 37 weeks, before labor

### Diagnosis of PPROM
- **Pooling of liquor** in posterior fornix
- **Ferning**: Amniotic fluid dries in fern pattern on microscopy
- **Nitrazine test**: pH >7 turns paper blue (vaginal pH normally 4.5)
- **AmniSure/ROM Plus**: PAMG-1 or IGFBP-1 detection (more accurate)
- **GBS (Group B Streptococcus) prophylaxis**: Penicillin G if <37 weeks or GBS positive

### Tocolytics
| Drug | Class | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| **Nifedipine** | Ca channel blocker | First-line; maternal hypotension |
| Atosiban | Oxytocin receptor antagonist | Expensive; Europe preferred |
| Indomethacin | NSAID | <32 weeks; avoid >32 weeks (premature ductal closure) |
| Ritodrine/Terbutaline | Beta-2 agonist | Maternal tachycardia, pulmonary edema |
| MgSO4 | — | Neuroprotection <32 weeks; not primary tocolytic |

### Antenatal Corticosteroids
- **Betamethasone**: 12mg IM × 2 doses 24 hours apart OR
- **Dexamethasone**: 6mg IM × 4 doses 12 hours apart
- **Indication**: 24-34 weeks (consider 34-36 weeks for late preterm)
- **Effect**: Lung maturity (surfactant), reduces RDS, IVH, NEC by 50%
- **Full effect**: 24 hours after last dose, optimal by 48 hours, lasts 7 days

### Cervical Incompetence
- **Painless cervical dilation** in second trimester without contractions
- **Risk factors**: Previous cervical trauma (LEEP, D&C), congenital (DES exposure)
- **Diagnosis**: USG — cervical length <25 mm at <24 weeks
- **Treatment**: **Cervical cerclage** (McDonald's/Shirodkar suture — removed at 36-37 weeks)
- **Progesterone**: 17-OHPC or vaginal progesterone for short cervix

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Betamethasone dose; nitrazine test; cerclage indications

## Medical Disorders in Pregnancy

### Diabetes in Pregnancy

**Screening:**
- **GCT (Glucose Challenge Test)**: 50g oral glucose, 1-hour plasma glucose
  - ≥140 mg/dL = positive → proceed to OGTT
- **OGTT (100g, 3-hour)** (Carpenter-Coustan criteria — GDM if ≥2 values elevated):
  - Fasting: ≥95 mg/dL
  - 1h: ≥180 mg/dL
  - 2h: ≥155 mg/dL
  - 3h: ≥140 mg/dL
- **WHO criteria (75g OGTT)**: FBS ≥126 OR 2h ≥200 = overt DM; 2h 140-199 = GDM

**Risks of GDM:**
- Fetal: **Macrosomia**, polyhydramnios, shoulder dystocia, stillbirth, neonatal hypoglycemia
- Maternal: Pre-eclampsia, operative delivery, future T2DM (50% within 10 years)

**Management:**
- Diet control first (2 weeks); if fasting >95 or postprandial >120 → start insulin
- **Insulin**: Drug of choice in pregnancy (metformin/glibenclamide used in some guidelines)
- Target: FBS 70-95 mg/dL, 1h postprandial <140, 2h <120
- Pre-gestational DM: Optimize control preconception, folic acid 5mg, switch to insulin

### Cardiac Disease in Pregnancy
- **Commonest rheumatic valve disease**: **Mitral stenosis** — worsens in pregnancy (increased CO + HR → raised LAP → pulmonary edema)
- **Peripartum cardiomyopathy**: Last month to 5 months postpartum, ejection fraction <45%, diagnosis of exclusion
- **Congenital heart disease**: ASD/VSD/PDA — generally well tolerated; Eisenmenger's — **absolute contraindication to pregnancy** (50% maternal mortality)
- **Low-dose aspirin** (150mg/day from 12 weeks): For women at high risk of pre-eclampsia (previous PE, CKD, DM, autoimmune)
- **New York Heart Association (NYHA) Class III/IV**: High risk, pregnancy discouraged

### Thyroid in Pregnancy
- **Iodine requirements increase** (200 mcg/day recommended in pregnancy)
- Thyroid-binding globulin rises (estrogen effect) → total T3/T4 raised but free T4 normal
- **Hypothyroidism**: Levothyroxine (increase dose 30-50% in pregnancy); target TSH 0.1-2.5 mU/L in first trimester
- **Hyperthyroidism**:
  - **First trimester**: **Propylthiouracil (PTU)** (less teratogenic, but hepatotoxic)
  - **Second/Third trimester**: **Methimazole (Carbimazole)** (PTU hepatotoxicity risk in mother)
  - Both cross placenta — fetal hypothyroidism risk
  - Fetal thyrotoxicosis: TSH receptor antibodies (TRAB) cross placenta

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: PTU in 1st trimester; MS commonest rheumatic valve; GCT cutoff 140 mg/dL

## Abnormal Labor

### Dystocia — 3 P's
- **Passenger**: Fetal size, position, presentation (OP/OT position)
- **Passage**: Pelvic dimensions (android pelvis worst for delivery), soft tissue
- **Power**: Uterine contractions (hypotonic/hypertonic/incoordinate)

### Obstructed Labor
- **Definition**: Descent arrested despite strong uterine contractions
- **Signs**:
  - **Bandl's ring** (pathological retraction ring): Visible/palpable groove between upper and lower uterine segments — indicates impending rupture
  - Secondary arrest of dilatation/descent
  - **Caput succedaneum** + **Molding** (3+)
  - Maternal exhaustion, fetal distress
  - Edematous, non-reducible cervical lip
- **Management**: Immediate LSCS (if viable fetus), symphysiotomy (resource-limited)

### Shoulder Dystocia
- **Definition**: Head delivers but shoulders impacted above pubic symphysis (turtle sign)
- **Risk factors**: Macrosomia, DM, previous shoulder dystocia, prolonged second stage

**Management (HELPERR mnemonic):**
- **H**: Call for Help
- **E**: Evaluate for Episiotomy (won't relieve bony impaction but gives access)
- **L**: Legs (McRoberts maneuver) — hyperflexion of maternal thighs → flattens lumbar lordosis
- **P**: Suprapubic Pressure (Rubin I) — disimpacts anterior shoulder
- **E**: Enter (internal rotational maneuvers)
  - **Rubin II**: Pressure on posterior aspect of anterior shoulder
  - **Woods screw**: Pressure on anterior aspect of posterior shoulder
  - **Zavanelli maneuver**: Head restitution + emergency LSCS (last resort)
- **R**: Remove the posterior arm
- **R**: Roll the patient (all-fours — Gaskin maneuver)

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Bandl's ring = impending rupture; McRoberts = first maneuver in shoulder dystocia

## Operative Obstetrics

### Forceps Delivery
**Types:**
- **Wrigley's**: Outlet/low-cavity, short, used in CS
- **Neville-Barnes/Simpson's**: Low/mid-cavity
- **Kielland's**: Rotational forceps (OT/OP positions) — no pelvic curve
- **Piper's**: For after-coming head in breech

**Prerequisites (FORCEPS):**
- **F**ull dilatation
- **O**S intact membranes (ruptured)
- **R**upture of membranes
- **C**onsent and analgesia
- **E**ngagement (head at ≥0 station)
- **P**ositional certainty
- **S**kill of operator

**Contraindications**: Unengaged head, face presentation (except mentoanterior), CPD

### Vacuum (Ventouse)
- **Contraindications**: <34 weeks (scalp fragile), face/brow presentation, uncertain position
- Chignon (artificial caput) common, resolves in 24-48h
- **Cephalohematoma** risk higher than forceps

### Episiotomy
- **Types**: Mediolateral (preferred in India — less risk of 3rd/4th degree tear), median (US), J-shaped, lateral
- **Mediolateral at 45°** from midline at 5 o'clock (right) or 7 o'clock (left)
- Repair: 3-layer (vaginal mucosa → perineal muscle → skin) with absorbable suture

### LSCS (Lower Segment Caesarean Section)
- **Pfannenstiel incision**: 3 cm above pubic symphysis, transverse
- **Joel-Cohen incision**: Straight transverse, 3 cm below ASIS line (faster)
- **Lower uterine segment transverse**: Standard
- **Classical CS**: Vertical upper segment incision — used in: preterm <30 weeks, anterior placenta previa, carcinoma cervix, fibroids in lower segment

**Perimortem CS**: Delivery within **4 minutes** of maternal cardiac arrest to improve maternal resuscitation outcomes

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Kielland's = rotational; Piper's = breech; Mediolateral episiotomy preferred in India

## Postpartum Hemorrhage (PPH)

### Definition
- **Primary PPH**: Blood loss >500 mL after vaginal delivery or >1000 mL after CS, within 24 hours
- **Secondary PPH**: Excessive bleeding 24 hours to 6 weeks postpartum

### Causes — 4 T's
| Cause | Examples | Frequency |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| **Tone** (80%) | Uterine atony | Most common |
| **Trauma** | Lacerations, uterine rupture, inversion | |
| **Tissue** | Retained placenta, membranes | |
| **Thrombin** | Coagulopathy (DIC, HELLP, inherited) | |

### Management — Step-wise
1. **Bimanual compression + oxytocin 20 IU in 1L at 60 drops/min**
2. **Ergometrine 0.5 mg IM/IV** (avoid in hypertension)
3. **Carboprost (15-methyl PGF2α) 250 mcg IM** every 15 min, max 8 doses (avoid in asthma)
4. **Misoprostol 800 mcg sublingual/rectal**
5. **Tranexamic acid 1g IV** within 3 hours
6. **Balloon tamponade** (Bakri balloon — intrauterine)
7. Surgical: **B-Lynch compression suture**, uterine artery ligation (O'Leary suture), internal iliac artery ligation
8. **Interventional radiology**: Uterine artery embolization
9. **Hysterectomy**: Last resort

### Secondary PPH
- Common causes: **Retained products of conception (RPOC)**, endometritis, subinvolution
- Management: Antibiotics, surgical evacuation (ERPC), anti-fibrinolytics

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Carboprost contraindicated in asthma; B-Lynch suture; 4 T's mnemonic

## Puerperium

### Physiological Changes
- **Involution of uterus**: 1 cm/day; at umbilicus Day 0 → pelvic organ by Day 14
- **Lochia**:
  - Rubra: Days 1-4, red (blood)
  - Serosa: Days 4-9, pinkish-brown
  - Alba: Days 10-14+, white (leukocytes)
- **Breastfeeding**: Hormones — **prolactin** (milk secretion), **oxytocin** (milk ejection/"let-down" reflex)
- **Lactational amenorrhea**: Reliable contraception if exclusive breastfeeding + amenorrhea + <6 months postpartum (98% effective)

### Postnatal Contraception
- **Progesterone-only pill (POP)**: Can start Day 21 postpartum; safe in breastfeeding (does not affect milk supply)
- **Combined OCP**: Avoid <6 weeks postpartum in breastfeeding (estrogen reduces milk supply + VTE risk)
- **Cu-IUD**: Can insert immediately postpartum or after 6 weeks
- **LNG-IUS (Mirena)**: After 6 weeks

### Puerperal Fever
- **Definition**: Temperature ≥38°C on any 2 of first 10 days postpartum (excluding first 24 hours)
- **Causes by timing**:
  - Day 1-2: Atelectasis (Wind)
  - Day 3-4: Urinary tract infection (Water)
  - Day 4-5: **Wound infection** (Wound)
  - Day 5-7: **Endometritis** (Womb) — most important
  - Day 7-10: DVT (Walking)

### Puerperal Psychosis
- **Onset**: 10-14 days postpartum (earlier than depression)
- **Risk**: History of **bipolar disorder** (strongest risk factor), previous puerperal psychosis
- Presents with: Hallucinations, delusions, confusion, behavioral change
- **Treatment**: Antipsychotics (haloperidol/olanzapine), mood stabilizers, hospitalization (with baby)

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Lochia rubra/serosa/alba duration; puerperal psychosis 10-14 days, bipolar risk

## Menstrual Disorders

### Amenorrhea

**Primary amenorrhea**: No menarche by age 15 (with secondary sexual characteristics) or 13 (without)
**Secondary amenorrhea**: Cessation of menses for ≥3 months (previously regular) or ≥6 months (irregular)

**Workup Algorithm:**
1. Pregnancy test (first always)
2. FSH/LH, prolactin, TSH
3. Karyotype (if primary + no sexual characteristics)

| Level | Cause | FSH/LH | Estrogen | Notes |
|-------|-------|--------|----------|-------|
| Outflow/Uterine | Mullerian agenesis (MRKH), Asherman's, imperforate hymen | Normal | Normal | Progestogen challenge negative |
| Ovarian failure | Turner's (45X), POF | **↑↑ FSH** | Low | Hypergonadotropic hypogonadism |
| Pituitary | Adenoma, Sheehan's | **↓ FSH/LH** | Low | Prolactin elevated in adenoma |
| Hypothalamic | Anorexia, exercise, stress, Kallmann's | **↓ FSH/LH** | Low | GnRH deficiency |

- **Kallmann's syndrome**: Hypogonadotropic hypogonadism + **anosmia** (absent olfactory bulbs)
- **Sheehan's syndrome**: Postpartum pituitary necrosis (PPH → spasm → infarction); fails to lactate first sign

### AUB — PALM-COEIN Classification
**Structural (PALM):**
- **P**olyp
- **A**denomyosis
- **L**eiomyoma
- **M**alignancy/hyperplasia

**Non-structural (COEIN):**
- **C**oagulopathy (vWD most common inherited)
- **O**vulatory dysfunction (PCOS, hypothyroidism)
- **E**ndometrial
- **I**atrogenic (OCPs, anticoagulants)
- **N**ot yet classified

### Dysmenorrhea
- **Primary**: No organic pathology; excess prostaglandins (PGF2α) → uterine cramps; onset with first periods
  - Treatment: **NSAIDs** (mefenamic acid first-line), OCP
- **Secondary**: Organic cause (endometriosis, adenomyosis, fibroids, PID, IUD); onset after pain-free periods
  - Treat underlying cause

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: FSH elevated in ovarian failure; Kallmann = anosmia + hypogonadism; PALM-COEIN

## Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS)

### Rotterdam Criteria — 2 of 3:
1. **Oligoovulation or anovulation** (cycles >35 days or <8/year)
2. **Clinical/biochemical hyperandrogenism** (hirsutism, acne, elevated free testosterone)
3. **PCO morphology on USG**: ≥12 follicles 2-9mm per ovary OR ovarian volume >10 mL

- Exclude other causes: CAH (17-OHP elevated), Cushing's, androgen-secreting tumor

### Features
- LH:FSH ratio >2:1 (not in diagnostic criteria but classic)
- **Insulin resistance** in 70%; obesity in 50%
- Metabolic syndrome: HTN, dyslipidemia, T2DM risk
- Endometrial hyperplasia/carcinoma risk (unopposed estrogen)

### Management
| Goal | Treatment |
|------|-----------|
| Menstrual regulation | **Combined OCP** (Yasmin/Diane-35 with anti-androgen effect) |
| Hyperandrogenism | OCP, spironolactone |
| Insulin resistance/weight | **Metformin** (lifestyle modification first) |
| Ovulation induction | **Clomiphene citrate** (first-line), letrozole (better live birth rate), gonadotropins |
| Anovulatory infertility | Above + laparoscopic ovarian drilling (LOD) |

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Rotterdam criteria 2/3; LH:FSH >2:1; Letrozole better than clomiphene for PCOS fertility

## Contraception

### Combined Oral Contraceptive Pill (COCP)
- **Mechanism**: Inhibits ovulation (mainly), thickens cervical mucus, thins endometrium
- **Benefits**: Reduced dysmenorrhea, regulated cycles, reduced endometrial/ovarian cancer, PMS, acne
- **Absolute contraindications (WHO MEC 4):**
  - Age >35 + smoker (≥15/day)
  - Hypertension (uncontrolled)
  - Migraine with aura
  - Personal history of VTE/stroke/ischemic heart disease
  - Breastfeeding <6 weeks
  - Liver disease (active)
  - Breast cancer (current)

### Progestogen-Only Pill (POP/Mini-pill)
- Can be used in breastfeeding, cardiovascular disease, migraine with aura
- Must be taken within same 3-hour window daily (Cerazette: 12-hour window)

### DMPA (Depo-Provera)
- 150mg IM every 12 weeks; failure rate <1%
- Delayed return of fertility (average 10 months)

### Intrauterine Devices
| Feature | Cu-T 380A | LNG-IUS (Mirena) |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| Mechanism | Copper ions spermicidal + inflammatory | Levonorgestrel → cervical mucus thickening + thin endometrium |
| Duration | 10 years | 5 years |
| Effect on periods | May increase bleeding | Reduces/eliminates bleeding |
| Non-contraceptive benefit | Emergency contraception | Menorrhagia treatment, dysmenorrhea |
| Contraindications | Wilson's disease, copper allergy | Progestin-sensitive cancer, unexplained bleeding |

- **Emergency contraception**: Cu-IUD within 5 days (most effective, >99%); Levonorgestrel 1.5mg within 72h (preferred); Ulipristal acetate within 120h

### Permanent Contraception
- **Female sterilization (Tubectomy)**:
  - **Pomeroy method**: Ligation and division of tube — most common, used in postpartum (within 48h or after 6 weeks)
  - **Filshie clip**: Laparoscopic, reversible (partially), clips at isthmo-ampullary junction
  - **Uchida method**: Subserosal injection of saline, resect tube
  - **Fimbriectomy**: Removes fimbriae — non-reversible
- **Vasectomy**: More effective than tubectomy, simpler, local anesthesia; no-scalpel technique; semen analysis after 3 months (20 ejaculations)

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Cu-T = emergency contraception within 5 days; COCP absolute CI = migraine with aura

## Infertility

- **Definition**: Failure to conceive after **12 months** of regular (2-3×/week) unprotected intercourse (6 months if age >35)
- **Primary**: Never conceived; **Secondary**: Previous pregnancy

### Semen Analysis (WHO 2021 Reference Values)
| Parameter | Lower Reference Limit |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Volume | ≥1.4 mL |
| Total sperm count | ≥39 million |
| Concentration | ≥16 million/mL |
| Progressive motility | ≥30% |
| Total motility | ≥42% |
| Morphology (Kruger strict) | ≥4% normal forms |

### Female Factor Investigations
- **Ovulatory**: BBT chart, mid-luteal progesterone (>16 nmol/L confirms ovulation), LH surge kits, USG follicle tracking
- **Tubal**: **HSG** (first-line, hydrosalpinx, peritubal adhesions) + **Laparoscopy with chromopertubation** (gold standard)
- **Uterine**: Hysteroscopy (polyps, fibroids, Asherman's), SHG (saline infusion sonography)
- **Cervical**: Postcoital test (largely abandoned)

### Assisted Reproductive Technology
| ART | Indication |
|-----|-----------|
| **IUI** | Male factor (mild), unexplained, cervical factor |
| **IVF** | Tubal blockage, moderate-severe male factor, failed IUI |
| **ICSI** | Severe oligospermia, azoospermia (TESA/PESA) |
| **Donor egg/sperm** | Premature ovarian failure, severe male factor |

- **OHSS (Ovarian Hyperstimulation Syndrome)**: Complication of gonadotropin stimulation; enlarged ovaries, ascites, hemoconcentration, VTE risk; severe — hospitalize, cabergoline, GnRH antagonist cycle preferred

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: WHO 2021 morphology ≥4%; laparoscopy gold standard for tubal patency; ICSI for azoospermia

## Uterine Fibroids (Leiomyoma)

### Classification
| Type | Location | Predominant Symptom |
|------|---------|---------------------|
| **Submucous** | Bulge into cavity | **Menorrhagia** (most bleeding) |
| Intramural | Within myometrium | Bulk symptoms, menorrhagia |
| Subserosal | Outer surface | **Pressure symptoms** (urinary, bowel) |
| Pedunculated | On stalk | Torsion, pressure |
| Parasitic | Separate blood supply | — |

- **Commonest gynecological tumor** in women of reproductive age
- Estrogen-dependent (shrink post-menopause)

### Management
**Medical:**
- **GnRH agonists** (leuprolide): Pre-surgical shrinkage 3-6 months; temporary (rebound on stopping)
- Tranexamic acid: For bleeding
- OCP/progestins: Symptom control (not shrinkage)
- **Ulipristal acetate** (SPRM): Reduces uterine volume + bleeding

**Surgical:**
- **Myomectomy**: Preserves uterus — for fertility-desiring women; risk of recurrence
- **Hysterectomy**: Definitive cure
- **Hysteroscopic myomectomy**: Submucous fibroids
- **Laparoscopic myomectomy**: Subserosal/small intramural

**Interventional:**
- Uterine artery embolization (UAE): Fibroid shrinkage; not for fertility plans

- **Malignant transformation** (Leiomyosarcoma): <1% (not degeneration of fibroid — arises de novo)

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Submucous fibroid = most bleeding; leiomyosarcoma arises de novo not from fibroid

## Endometriosis & Adenomyosis

### Comparison Table

| Feature | Endometriosis | Adenomyosis |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| Definition | Ectopic endometrial glands + stroma **outside** uterus | Endometrial glands + stroma **within myometrium** |
| Age | Reproductive age | Perimenopausal (35-50) |
| Parity | Nulliparous/low parity | Multiparous |
| Uterus | Normal size | **Uniformly enlarged, boggy** |
| Pain | Dysmenorrhea, dyspareunia | Dysmenorrhea, menorrhagia |
| USG/MRI | Chocolate cysts, endometriomas | **Junctional zone >12 mm on MRI** |
| CA-125 | Elevated | May be elevated |
| Diagnosis | **Laparoscopy** (gold standard) | Clinical/MRI/hysterectomy specimen |
| Treatment | OCP/GnRH agonists/excision | OCP/LNG-IUS/hysterectomy |

### Endometriosis Staging (Revised ASRM)
- **Stage I**: Minimal (isolated superficial implants)
- **Stage II**: Mild (deep implants, <5cm total)
- **Stage III**: Moderate (endometriomas, significant adhesions)
- **Stage IV**: Severe (large endometriomas, frozen pelvis)

### Chocolate Cyst (Endometrioma)
- Ovarian endometriosis containing old "chocolate" (hemosiderin-laden) blood
- USG: Homogeneous low-level echoes ("ground glass")
- Treatment: Cystectomy (not drainage — recurrence)

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Laparoscopy gold standard for endometriosis; junctional zone >12mm = adenomyosis on MRI

## Cervical Cancer

### HPV Association
- **High-risk (oncogenic)**: Types **16, 18** (responsible for 70% cervical cancers); also 31, 33, 45
- **Low-risk**: Types **6, 11** (genital warts/condylomata acuminata)
- **Mechanism**: E6 protein → degrades p53; E7 → inactivates Rb (tumor suppressors)

### Pap Smear — Bethesda System
| Category | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| **NILM** | Negative for intraepithelial lesion or malignancy |
| **ASC-US** | Atypical squamous cells of undetermined significance → HPV reflex testing |
| **ASC-H** | Atypical squamous cells, cannot exclude HSIL → colposcopy |
| **LSIL** | Low-grade SIL = CIN 1 |
| **HSIL** | High-grade SIL = CIN 2/3 → colposcopy |
| **SCC** | Squamous cell carcinoma |
| **AGC** | Atypical glandular cells → endocervical sampling |

### Low-Resource Screening
- **VIA (Visual Inspection with Acetic Acid)**: Acetowhite areas = CIN; immediate treatment possible
- **VILI (Lugol's Iodine)**: CIN = mustard yellow, normal = mahogany brown

### CIN Management
- **CIN 1**: Observe (regresses spontaneously 60%)
- **CIN 2/3**: LEEP (Loop Electrosurgical Excision Procedure) or cold knife cone biopsy

### Invasive Carcinoma
- **Radical hysterectomy (Wertheim's)**: FIGO Stage IA2-IIA — removes uterus + parametrium + upper vagina + pelvic nodes
- **Concurrent chemoradiation** (cisplatin): Stage IIB+ (or unfit for surgery)
- Squamous cell carcinoma most common (80%); adenocarcinoma rising

### FIGO Staging (2018)
- **Stage I**: Confined to cervix
- **Stage II**: Beyond cervix but not pelvic wall or lower third vagina
- **Stage IIB**: Parametrial involvement
- **Stage III**: Pelvic wall or lower vagina or hydronephrosis
- **Stage IV**: Bladder/rectum or distant metastasis

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: HPV 16/18 = cervical cancer; HPV 6/11 = warts; Wertheim's = radical hysterectomy

### Classification and Tumor Markers

## Endometrial (Uterine) Cancer

### Type I vs Type II

| Feature | Type I | Type II |
|---------|--------|---------|
| Histology | **Endometrioid** (80%) | Serous, clear cell |
| Estrogen | Estrogen-related | Not estrogen-related |
| Mutation | **PTEN**, microsatellite instability (Lynch) | **p53**, HER2 |
| Prognosis | **Better** | Worse (aggressive) |
| Precursor | Atypical hyperplasia | Endometrial intraepithelial carcinoma |
| Risk factors | Obesity, DM, PCOS, unopposed estrogen | Older, thin women |

### Clinical Features
- **Postmenopausal bleeding (PMB)**: Must rule out endometrial carcinoma until proven otherwise
- Premenopausal: Intermenstrual/irregular bleeding

### Investigations
- Pipelle endometrial biopsy (outpatient, 90% sensitivity)
- **Transvaginal USG**: Endometrial thickness >4 mm in postmenopausal = biopsy
- Hysteroscopy + directed biopsy: Gold standard
- MRI: Myometrial invasion depth

### Treatment
- **Surgery (TAH + BSO + pelvic washings ± node dissection)**: Stage I/II
- Radiotherapy: Adjuvant for high-risk Stage I, Stage II
- Chemotherapy (carboplatin + paclitaxel): Stage III/IV
- Progestins: Inoperable/young women with fertility desire (Stage IA, Grade 1)

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: PMB = investigate for endometrial carcinoma; PTEN mutation = Type I; p53 = Type II

## Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID)

- **Causative organisms**: **Neisseria gonorrhoeae** and **Chlamydia trachomatis** most common (often polymicrobial)
- **Risk factors**: Multiple partners, IUD in first 3 weeks of insertion, previous PID, young age

### Clinical Features
- Lower abdominal pain, vaginal discharge, fever
- **Chandelier sign (CMT — cervical motion tenderness)**: Exquisite pain on cervical movement
- Bilateral adnexal tenderness

### Complications
- **Tubo-ovarian abscess (TOA)**: Fluctuant adnexal mass; IV antibiotics; drain if no response
- **Fitz-Hugh-Curtis syndrome**: Perihepatitis — RUQ pain; **"violin string" adhesions** between liver capsule and anterior abdominal wall (seen on laparoscopy)
- Infertility (tubal blockage), ectopic pregnancy, chronic pelvic pain

### Treatment
**Inpatient (IV):**
- Cefoxitin 2g IV q6h + Doxycycline 100mg PO q12h OR
- Clindamycin 900mg IV q8h + Gentamicin loading 2mg/kg then 1.5mg/kg q8h

**Outpatient (oral):**
- Ceftriaxone 500mg IM × 1 + Doxycycline 100mg BD × 14 days + Metronidazole 400mg BD × 14 days

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Chandelier sign; violin string adhesions = Fitz-Hugh-Curtis; TOA treatment

## Ectopic Pregnancy

### Risk Factors (mnemonic: PELT)
- **P**revious ectopic, **P**ID
- **E**ndometriosis
- **L**igated tubes
- **T**ubal surgery, **T**IUD, **T**rauma (pelvic surgery)

### Sites
| Site | Frequency | Note |
|------|-----------|------|
| **Ampulla** | **70%** | Most common site |
| Isthmus | 12% | **Ruptures earliest** (narrowest) |
| Fimbria | 11% | Latest rupture |
| Interstitial/Cornual | 2-4% | Most dangerous rupture (massive hemorrhage) |
| Ovarian, cervical, abdominal | Rare | |

### Triad
1. **Amenorrhea** (6-8 weeks)
2. **Pain** (unilateral iliac fossa/shoulder tip from diaphragmatic irritation)
3. **Vaginal bleeding** (dark, scanty)

### Investigations
- **β-hCG**: Should double every 48 hours (non-doubling or slow rise = ectopic/non-viable IUP)
- **USG (TVS)**: Empty uterus + adnexal mass + free fluid in pouch of Douglas
- **Discriminatory zone**: β-hCG >1500-2000 IU/L with no IUP on TVS = ectopic until proven otherwise
- Culdocentesis: Non-clotting blood (historical)

### Management

| Criteria | Treatment |
|---------|-----------|
| Methotrexate eligible: β-hCG <5000, unruptured, <3.5 cm, no FCA, compliant | **Methotrexate 50mg/m² IM** (monitor hCG days 4 and 7, >15% fall = success) |
| Surgical + no contralateral tube disease | **Salpingotomy** (incise tube, remove ectopic) |
| Contralateral tube healthy / recurrent / rupture | **Salpingectomy** |
| Hemodynamically unstable | Emergency salpingectomy |

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Ampulla most common site; isthmus ruptures earliest; MTX criteria: hCG <5000

## Menopause

- **Definition**: 12 consecutive months of amenorrhea following last menstrual period; average age **51 years** (India: 47-48 years)
- **Perimenopause**: 2-8 years before to 1 year after final period
- **POF/POI** (Premature Ovarian Insufficiency): Menopause <40 years

### Symptoms
- **Vasomotor**: Hot flushes, night sweats (most common; estrogen loss → reduced hypothalamic thermoregulatory threshold)
- **Urogenital atrophy**: Vaginal dryness, dyspareunia, recurrent UTI, stress incontinence
- **Psychological**: Mood changes, insomnia, cognitive changes
- **Long-term**: Cardiovascular disease (estrogen protective before menopause), **osteoporosis** (accelerated bone loss 2-3%/year in first 5-7 years)

### HRT (Hormone Replacement Therapy)
| Uterus Status | HRT Type | Why |
|--------------|----------|-----|
| **Hysterectomy** | **Estrogen only** | No uterus, no need for progestogen |
| **Uterus intact** | **Combined (estrogen + progestogen)** | Progestogen protects endometrium from hyperplasia |

**Benefits**: Vasomotor symptoms relief (most effective), urogenital atrophy, osteoporosis prevention, colon cancer risk reduction

**Risks (WHI trial):**
- Estrogen + progestogen: Small increased risk of **breast cancer** (after 5 years), VTE, stroke
- Estrogen alone: No increased breast cancer risk; reduced breast cancer in some studies

**Contraindications**: Breast cancer, endometrial cancer, unexplained vaginal bleeding, active VTE, liver disease

### SERMs
- **Raloxifene**: Estrogen agonist on bone (prevents osteoporosis) + antagonist on breast/uterus; does not help vasomotor symptoms
- **Ospemifene**: For vaginal atrophy/dyspareunia

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Combined HRT if uterus intact; raloxifene for osteoporosis + no uterine effect; menopause = 12 months amenorrhea
