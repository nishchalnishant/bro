## PREOPERATIVE ASSESSMENT

### ASA Physical Status Classification (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| ASA Class | Description | Examples |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| **ASA I** | Normal healthy patient | No disease, non-smoker |
| **ASA II** | Mild systemic disease | Well-controlled DM/HTN, obesity BMI 30-40, smoker |
| **ASA III** | Severe systemic disease (not life-threatening) | Poorly controlled DM/HTN, COPD, morbid obesity, active hepatitis |
| **ASA IV** | Severe systemic disease - constant threat to life | Recent MI (<3 months), CVA, decompensated CHF, sepsis, CRF |
| **ASA V** | Moribund, not expected to survive without operation | Ruptured AAA, massive trauma, intracranial bleed with herniation |
| **ASA VI** | Brain-dead, organ donation | - |
| **E suffix** | Emergency surgery (added to class) | ASA IIIE, IIIE, etc. |

### Preoperative Fasting Guidelines - 2-4-6-8 Rule (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Substance | Fasting Time |
|---------|------------|
| **Clear liquids** | **2 hours** |
| **Breast milk** | **4 hours** |
| **Light meal/formula** | **6 hours** |
| **Heavy/fatty meal** | **8 hours** |

**Key:** Applies to elective surgery in otherwise healthy patients

### Risk Stratification
- **Revised Cardiac Risk Index (RCRI/Lee Index)**: 6 factors - IHD, CCF, cerebrovascular disease, DM on insulin, CKD (Cr >2), high-risk surgery
- **METs (Metabolic Equivalents)**: <4 METs = poor functional capacity (unable to climb one flight stairs)
- **Preoperative investigations**: Not routine; based on clinical assessment and type of surgery

### Medication Management
- **Continue**: Antihypertensives (esp. beta-blockers - abrupt cessation = rebound), antiepileptics, steroids, inhalers, thyroid medications
- **Stop**: **ACE inhibitors/ARBs** (day of surgery - hypotension); **Metformin** (day of surgery + 48h post - lactic acidosis risk); **warfarin** (5 days pre-op); **clopidogrel** (7 days); **aspirin** (7 days for elective, continue for cardiac)
- **Insulin**: Continue basal; hold prandial; monitor glucose intraoperatively

## AIRWAY MANAGEMENT

### Mallampati Classification (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Class | Visible Structures | Intubation |
|-------|------------------|-----------|
| **I** | Soft palate, fauces, uvula, pillars | Easy |
| **II** | Soft palate, fauces, upper uvula | Slightly difficult |
| **III** | Soft palate, base of uvula only | Difficult |
| **IV** | Hard palate only (soft palate not visible) | Very difficult |

**Technique**: Patient seated, mouth wide open, tongue protruded, no phonation

### Difficult Airway Predictors

**LEMON Assessment:**
- **L** - **L**ook externally (facial trauma, beard, obesity)
- **E** - **E**valuate 3-3-2 rule (mouth opening ≥3 fingers; hyoid-mental distance ≥3 fingers; thyromental distance ≥2 fingers)
- **M** - **M**allampati class III/IV
- **O** - **O**bstruction (epiglottitis, abscess, foreign body)
- **N** - **N**eck mobility (cervical immobility/fusion)

**El-Ganzouri Risk Index (EGRI)**: Scoring system including mouth opening, thyromental distance, Mallampati, neck movement, mandibular protrusion, weight, history of difficult intubation

### Laryngoscopy and Intubation

**Cormack-Lehane Grading (Laryngoscopic View):**
- **Grade I**: Full view of vocal cords
- **Grade II**: Partial view of cords
- **Grade III**: Epiglottis only
- **Grade IV**: Nothing visible

**Rapid Sequence Induction (RSI):**
1. Pre-oxygenation (3-5 min, 100% O2)
2. Cricoid pressure (Sellick's maneuver - controversial)
3. **Thiopentone** (3-5 mg/kg) or propofol
4. **Succinylcholine** 1.5-2 mg/kg (fastest onset 60 sec, shortest duration)
5. Avoid PPV until intubated
6. Confirm: ETCO2 waveform + bilateral chest movement + fogging

**BURP Maneuver**: Backward, Upward, Rightward Pressure on thyroid cartilage to improve laryngoscopic view

### LMA (Laryngeal Mask Airway)
- **Classic LMA**: Reusable; does not protect from aspiration
- **ProSeal LMA**: Drainage tube for gastric tube; higher seal pressure; better for IPPV
- **LMA Supreme**: Disposable version of ProSeal
- **i-gel**: No inflatable cuff; good seal
- **Contraindications**: Full stomach, hiatal hernia, known GERD with risk of aspiration, oropharyngeal pathology

### Surgical Airway
- **Cricothyrotomy**: Emergency (CICO - cannot intubate cannot oxygenate); membrane between thyroid and cricoid cartilages
- **Tracheostomy**: Elective; between 2nd-3rd tracheal rings; percutaneous (Ciaglia technique with Seldinger) vs surgical
- **Complications of tracheostomy**: Hemorrhage (innominate artery), tracheomalacia, stenosis, tracheoesophageal fistula

## GENERAL ANESTHESIA

### Stages of Anesthesia - Guedel's Stages (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Stage | Name | Features |
|-------|------|---------|
| **Stage I** | Analgesia | Conscious, analgesic, amnesia may occur |
| **Stage II** | Excitement/Delirium | **Dangerous** - breath-holding, vomiting, laryngospasm; breath irregular; **avoid stimuli** |
| **Stage III** | Surgical anesthesia | **4 planes**; regular breathing; required for surgery; loss of reflexes |
| **Stage IV** | Medullary depression | Respiratory/cardiovascular arrest; overdose |

**Stage III Planes:**
- Plane 1: Roving → fixed eyes; regular breathing
- Plane 2: Loss of corneal/laryngeal reflex; regular breathing
- Plane 3: Intercostal paralysis begins; diaphragm breathing
- Plane 4: Complete intercostal paralysis; diaphragmatic breathing only

### Inhalational Anesthetic Agents (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Agent | MAC (%) | BP (°C) | Blood:Gas | Properties/Notes |
|-------|---------|---------|----------|----------------|
| **Nitrous oxide (N2O)** | **105%** | -88 | **0.47** (lowest - fastest onset) | No muscle relaxation; diffusion hypoxia; 2nd gas effect; contraindicated in closed air spaces |
| **Halothane** | **0.75%** | 50 | 2.4 | **Hepatotoxicity (halothane hepatitis)**; **malignant hyperthermia trigger**; sensitizes heart to catecholamines |
| **Isoflurane** | 1.15% | 49 | 1.4 | Coronary steal (theoretical); airway irritant |
| **Sevoflurane** | **2.0%** | 59 | **0.65** | **Most commonly used**; smooth induction; compound A (renal toxicity with low flow); safe for MH susceptible - **NO** (still triggers MH) |
| **Desflurane** | **6.0%** | 23.5 | **0.42** | **Most pungent - airway irritant**; cannot use for induction; fastest offset; sympathetic stimulation on rapid increase |
| **Enflurane** | 1.68% | 57 | 1.9 | Seizures on EEG; **nephrotoxic (metabolized to fluoride)** |

**MAC Concept:**
- **MAC**: Concentration at which 50% of patients don't move to surgical incision (in O2 at 1 atm)
- **MAC awake**: ~1/3 MAC (patient conscious but amnesia)
- **MAC BAR** (block adrenergic response): ~1.5 MAC
- **Factors ↓ MAC**: Age, hypothermia, anemia, hypoxia, opioids, pregnancy, acute alcohol
- **Factors ↑ MAC**: Hyperthermia, chronic alcohol, children (higher MAC), hyperthyroidism

**Diffusion Hypoxia (Fink Effect)**: After N2O discontinuation, N2O diffuses out of blood into alveoli → dilutes alveolar O2 → hypoxia; prevent by **100% O2 for 5-10 min after stopping N2O**

**N2O Contraindications**: Pneumothorax, bowel obstruction, middle ear surgery, pneumocephalus, intraocular gas (SF6), air embolism (expands air spaces - 34× more soluble in blood than N2)

### Intravenous Anesthetic Agents (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Agent | Onset | Duration | Properties | Uses |
|-------|-------|---------|----------|-----|
| **Propofol** | Fast | Short (context sensitive) | **Lipid emulsion (soybean)**; anti-emetic; ↓ICP; ↓IOP; pain on injection; **PRIS** (propofol infusion syndrome - acidosis, rhabdomyolysis, cardiac failure); **TIVA** | Induction, TIVA, sedation |
| **Ketamine** | Fast | Medium | **Dissociative anesthesia**; ↑ICP/IOP; **bronchodilator**; ↑HR/BP; **raises secretions (atropine premedication)**; emergence reactions (benzodiazepine reduces); analgesia | Emergency, pediatric, bronchospasm |
| **Thiopentone** | Very fast (30 sec) | Short (redistribution) | Highly lipid soluble; **anticonvulsant**; **↓ICP** (cerebral protection); **porphyria contraindicated**; necrosis if intra-arterial | RSI, neuroprotection |
| **Etomidate** | Fast | Short | **Minimal cardiovascular effects** - preferred in cardiac patients; **adrenal suppression** (single dose - not significant unless infusion); myoclonus; pain on injection | Cardiac patients, hemodynamically compromised |
| **Midazolam** | Moderate | Medium | Benzodiazepine; **anterograde amnesia**; **flumazenil reversal**; ↓ICP modestly | Premedication, sedation, induction in elderly |
| **Dexmedetomidine** | Moderate | Medium | **Alpha-2 agonist**; **preserves respiratory drive**; reduces opioid/anesthetic requirements; sedation without respiratory depression | ICU sedation, awake fiberoptic |

**Propofol Infusion Syndrome (PRIS)**: Metabolic acidosis + rhabdomyolysis + cardiac failure + renal failure; associated with high doses >4 mg/kg/hr for >48 hours or in children with viral infections

## REGIONAL ANESTHESIA

### Spinal Anesthesia (Subarachnoid Block) (PYQ HOTSPOT)
- **Space entered**: Subarachnoid (intrathecal) space
- **Common level**: **L3-L4 interspace** (below spinal cord termination at L1-L2)
- **Dural puncture**: CSF obtained; needle passes through epidural space + dura mater + arachnoid
- **Local anesthetic spreads**: By baricity relative to CSF

**Baricity:**
- **Hyperbaric** (heavy spinal - with glucose): Settles dependent; used in most spinal techniques; position patient appropriately
- **Isobaric**: Remains at injection level
- **Hypobaric**: Rises to uppermost area

**Spinal Anesthesia Complications:**
- **PDPH (Post-Dural Puncture Headache)**: Positional headache (worse upright, better supine); CSF leak; treatment = bed rest, analgesics, **epidural blood patch** (gold standard)
- **High/total spinal**: Spread above T1 → respiratory arrest, hypotension, loss of consciousness
- **Spinal shock**: Hypotension + bradycardia (motor block of sympathetics)
- **Urinary retention**: Common
- **Cauda equina syndrome**: Rare; with hyperbaric lidocaine (transient neurological symptoms)
- **Anterior spinal artery syndrome**: Rare; hypotension → cord ischemia

### Epidural Anesthesia
- **Space**: Epidural space (outside dura); fat and veins; **no CSF obtained**
- **Test dose**: 3mL of 1.5% lidocaine with 1:200,000 epinephrine; if IV injection → tachycardia >20 bpm; if intrathecal → spinal block
- **Onset**: Slower than spinal (15-20 min vs 5-10 min)
- **Duration**: Continuous via catheter; unlimited duration
- **Volumes**: Larger volumes needed; **segmental spread**

### Spinal vs Epidural Comparison (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Feature | Spinal | Epidural |
|---------|--------|---------|
| Space | Intrathecal (CSF) | Epidural space |
| Onset | **Fast (5-10 min)** | Slow (15-20 min) |
| Volume | Small (2-4 mL) | Large (15-30 mL) |
| CSF obtained | **Yes** | No |
| Block density | Dense, predictable | Variable, patchy possible |
| Duration | Fixed (fixed dose) | Continuous (catheter) |
| PDPH risk | **Higher** | Lower (if inadvertent dural puncture) |
| Catheter | No (usually) | **Yes** |
| Hypotension | More sudden | More gradual |
| Level | L3-L4 | Any |

### Local Anesthetic Agents (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Agent | Type | pKa | Protein Binding | Duration | Notes |
|-------|------|-----|----------------|---------|------|
| **Lidocaine** | Amide | 7.9 | 65% | 1-2h | Most versatile; topical use; anti-arrhythmic; **maximum dose 3 mg/kg (7 mg/kg with epinephrine)** |
| **Bupivacaine** | Amide | 8.1 | **95%** | 4-8h | **Cardiotoxic** (Na channel block); treat toxicity with **intralipid 20%**; not for IV regional (Bier's block) |
| **Ropivacaine** | Amide | 8.1 | 94% | 4-8h | Less cardiotoxic than bupivacaine; less motor block; good for labor analgesia |
| **Prilocaine** | Amide | 8.0 | 55% | 1-2h | **Methemoglobinemia**; EMLA cream component |
| **Cocaine** | Ester | 8.7 | 91% | 1h | Only LA with intrinsic vasoconstriction; topical only (nasal); **not injectable** |
| **Procaine** | Ester | 9.1 | 6% | Short | Metabolized to PABA (allergy); spinal |
| **Chloroprocaine** | Ester | 9.1 | 0% | Shortest | Fastest offset; obstetric spinal/epidural |

**LA Toxicity (PYQ HOTSPOT):**
- **CNS first**: Circumoral numbness → tinnitus → visual disturbance → seizures → coma
- **CVS next**: Bradycardia → hypotension → VF (bupivacaine most cardiotoxic - binds fast, dissociates slow: "fast in, slow out")
- **Treatment**: Stop LA; ABC; seizures - benzodiazepine; **Intralipid 20% (1.5 mL/kg bolus)** for cardiac toxicity
- **Vasoconstrictors (epinephrine)**: Prolongs duration; reduces systemic absorption; **NEVER in ring blocks** (fingers, toes, penis, nose, ear - end arteries)

### Nerve Blocks

**Brachial Plexus Approaches:**

| Approach | Structures Blocked | Uses | Risks |
|---------|------------------|-----|------|
| **Interscalene** | C5-C6-C7 (upper/middle trunk) | Shoulder surgery | Phrenic nerve (ipsilateral diaphragm paralysis - **contraindicated in contralateral pneumonectomy**); vertebral artery; **Horner's syndrome** |
| **Supraclavicular** | All trunks/divisions | Whole arm surgery | **Pneumothorax most common** complication |
| **Infraclavicular** | Cords | Arm/forearm | Less pneumothorax |
| **Axillary** | Terminal branches at axilla | Forearm/hand | Hematoma; musculocutaneous nerve often missed |

## NEUROMUSCULAR BLOCKING AGENTS (NMBAs)

### Depolarizing NMBAs - Succinylcholine (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Property | Detail |
|---------|-------|
| Mechanism | **ACh receptor agonist** → sustained depolarization (Phase I block) |
| Onset | **60 seconds** (fastest) |
| Duration | **10-15 minutes** (shortest) - due to pseudocholinesterase |
| Phase II block | With repeated/high doses; resembles non-depolarizing block; can reverse with neostigmine |
| Fasciculations | Present (myalgias post-op); prevent with small dose non-depolarizing agent |
| **Hyperkalemia** | **0.5-1 mEq/L rise** (normal); dangerous if pre-existing hyperkalemia, burns (>24h-2yr), denervation injury, prolonged immobility, crush injury, myopathies |
| Malignant hyperthermia | **Trigger** |
| Pseudocholinesterase deficiency | Prolonged apnea; treat with **fresh frozen plasma** or mivacurium wait |
| ICP/IOP | ↑Briefly |

**Contraindications to Succinylcholine:**
- Personal/family history of MH
- **Hyperkalemia** or risk of hyperkalemia (burns >24h, denervation, rhabdomyolysis, crush)
- **Myopathies** (Duchenne - fatal hyperkalemia + rhabdomyolysis)
- Pseudocholinesterase deficiency (relative)

### Non-Depolarizing NMBAs (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Agent | Duration | Metabolism/Elimination | Notes |
|-------|---------|----------------------|------|
| **Succinylcholine** (depolarizing) | Ultra-short | Pseudocholinesterase | Only depolarizing |
| **Mivacurium** | Short | Pseudocholinesterase | Short non-depolarizing; |
| **Rocuronium** | Intermediate | Hepatic/renal | **Fastest onset** (1-1.5 min at 1.2 mg/kg - RSI alternative); reversed by **sugammadex** |
| **Vecuronium** | Intermediate | Hepatic | No histamine; stable CVS; reversed by neostigmine/**sugammadex** |
| **Atracurium** | Intermediate | **Hofmann elimination** (spontaneous - pH/temp) | Safe in **liver + kidney failure**; releases histamine |
| **Cisatracurium** | Intermediate | **Hofmann elimination** | No histamine; better than atracurium; **preferred in organ failure** |
| **Pancuronium** | Long | Renal | ↑HR/BP (vagolytic); cheapest |

**Reversal of Non-Depolarizing NMBAs:**
- **Neostigmine** (+ glycopyrrolate to prevent bradycardia): Acetylcholinesterase inhibitor; wait for TOF ratio >0.1
- **Sugammadex**: Encapsulates rocuronium/vecuronium molecules; **immediate reversal regardless of block depth**; no atropine needed; only for aminosteroidal agents

**Train-of-Four (TOF)**: 4 supra-maximal stimuli at 2 Hz; TOF ratio (4th/1st) <0.9 = residual blockade; **<0.7 = clinically significant**

## MONITORING

### Standard ASA Monitoring (Mandatory)
- **Oxygenation**: Pulse oximetry (SpO2), inspired O2 fraction, FiO2
- **Ventilation**: **ETCO2** (most important monitor for ventilation + tube position), respiratory rate
- **Circulation**: ECG, blood pressure (NIBP every 5 min minimum), heart rate
- **Temperature**: Thermometer
- **Circulation**: Stethoscope (precordial/esophageal)

### Invasive Monitoring (PYQ HOTSPOT)

**Arterial Line:**
- **Uses**: Beat-to-beat BP, continuous monitoring, frequent ABG sampling
- **Common sites**: Radial (Allen's test first), femoral, axillary, dorsalis pedis
- **Normal arterial waveform**: Systolic peak → dicrotic notch (aortic valve closure) → diastolic trough
- **Complications**: Thrombosis, hematoma, infection, distal ischemia

**Central Venous Pressure (CVP):**
- **Normal**: **5-10 cmH2O** (2-8 mmHg)
- **↑CVP**: Fluid overload, right heart failure, tamponade, tension pneumothorax, PEEP
- **↓CVP**: Hypovolemia
- **CVP waveform**: a wave (atrial contraction) → c wave (tricuspid closure) → x descent → v wave (venous filling) → y descent
- **Cannon a waves**: Complete heart block, nodal rhythm (AV dissociation)

**Pulmonary Artery Catheter (Swan-Ganz):**
- **PCWP (pulmonary capillary wedge pressure)**: Reflects left atrial pressure/LVEDP; normal 6-12 mmHg
- **Fick's principle**: CO = O2 consumption / (arterial O2 content - venous O2 content)
- **Thermodilution**: Cold saline injected; temperature-time curve; area under curve inversely proportional to CO

### BIS (Bispectral Index) (PYQ HOTSPOT)
- **Monitors depth of anesthesia** using processed EEG
- **Range 0-100**: 100 = awake; **40-60 = adequate anesthesia** (surgical); 0 = EEG silence
- **Awareness under anesthesia**: Risk factors - light anesthesia, muscle relaxant use, previous awareness
- **BIS values**: 
  - >70: Light sedation/possible awareness
  - 40-60: General anesthesia (target for surgery)
  - <40: Deep anesthesia
  - 0: Isoelectric EEG

## PAIN MANAGEMENT

### WHO Analgesic Ladder (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Step | Drugs | Indication |
|------|-------|-----------|
| **Step 1** | Paracetamol + NSAIDs | Mild pain (NRS 1-3) |
| **Step 2** | Step 1 + weak opioids (codeine, tramadol) | Moderate pain (NRS 4-6) |
| **Step 3** | Step 1 + strong opioids (morphine, fentanyl, oxycodone) | Severe pain (NRS 7-10) |

**Adjuvants** (used at any step):
- **Gabapentin/Pregabalin**: Neuropathic pain, post-op pain, α2δ calcium channel subunit
- **Ketamine (low dose)**: NMDA antagonist; opioid-sparing; chronic pain
- **Tricyclic antidepressants**: Neuropathic pain
- **SNRIs (duloxetine)**: Neuropathic pain
- **Steroids**: Short-term, anti-inflammatory

### Opioid Pharmacology
- **Receptors**: Mu (μ) - analgesia/euphoria/respiratory depression/constipation; Kappa (κ) - spinal analgesia/sedation; Delta (δ) - mood/analgesia
- **Side effects**: Respiratory depression, constipation, nausea/vomiting, pruritus, urinary retention, sedation, tolerance, dependence
- **Reversal**: **Naloxone** (0.1-0.4 mg IV; short duration - may need infusion)
- **Morphine**: Gold standard; active metabolite M6G; accumulates in renal failure
- **Fentanyl**: 100× more potent; lipid-soluble; no histamine; transdermal patch
- **Tramadol**: Weak opioid + serotonin/noradrenaline reuptake inhibitor; lower abuse potential; serotonin syndrome risk

### Patient-Controlled Analgesia (PCA)
- Patient self-administers IV opioid on demand
- **Settings**: Demand dose (e.g., morphine 1 mg), lockout interval (5-10 min), background infusion (optional)
- **No background infusion** preferred (risk of respiratory depression)

### Chronic Pain Conditions (PYQ HOTSPOT)
- **CRPS (Complex Regional Pain Syndrome)**: Disproportionate burning pain, allodynia, autonomic changes, trophic changes; CRPS I (no nerve injury, reflex sympathetic dystrophy) vs CRPS II (with nerve injury, causalgia)
- **Neuropathic pain**: Burning/lancinating quality; allodynia/hyperalgesia; first line = gabapentinoids/TCAs/SNRIs
- **Phantom limb pain**: Pain in amputated limb; mirror therapy, pregabalin, NMDA antagonists
- **Fibromyalgia**: Widespread pain + fatigue; duloxetine, pregabalin, low-dose amitriptyline

## CPR / BLS / ACLS (PYQ HOTSPOT)

### Chain of Survival (AHA 2020)
1. Recognition and activation of EMS
2. **CPR immediately** (early CPR)
3. Rapid defibrillation
4. Advanced resuscitation
5. Post-cardiac arrest care
6. Recovery

### Adult BLS

| Parameter | Value |
|----------|------|
| Compression rate | **100-120/min** |
| Compression depth | **5-6 cm** |
| Compression:Ventilation ratio | **30:2** |
| Hand position | Lower half of sternum |
| Allow full recoil | Yes |
| Minimize interruptions | <10 seconds |

- **Untrained/alone bystander**: Compression-only CPR (hands-only) acceptable
- **Trained with 2 rescuers**: 30:2 until advanced airway, then continuous compressions + 1 breath every 6 seconds

### Shockable vs Non-Shockable Rhythms (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Category | Rhythms | Action |
|---------|---------|-------|
| **Shockable** | **VF, pulseless VT** | Defibrillation **200J biphasic** + CPR |
| **Non-shockable** | **Asystole, PEA** | CPR only + identify reversible causes |

**Adrenaline (Epinephrine):**
- **Non-shockable (Asystole/PEA)**: **1mg IV as soon as access obtained**, repeat every 3-5 min
- **Shockable (VF/pVT)**: **1mg IV after 3rd shock**, then every 3-5 min

**Amiodarone:**
- **Shockable rhythms** only (VF/pVT)
- **After 3rd shock**: 300 mg IV bolus
- **After 5th shock**: 150 mg IV bolus

**Reversible Causes - 4H's and 4T's:**
- **4H**: Hypoxia, Hypovolemia, Hypo/hyperkalemia, Hypothermia
- **4T**: Tension pneumothorax, Tamponade, Toxins, Thrombosis (PE/coronary)

### Defibrillation
- **Monophasic**: 360J all shocks
- **Biphasic**: 120-200J first shock; subsequent shocks ≥ first dose
- **AED**: Analyzes rhythm, delivers shock if shockable
- **Synchronized cardioversion**: For unstable SVT/AF/flutter (not VF); sync avoids T-wave shock

### Post-Resuscitation Care
- **Targeted Temperature Management (TTM)**: 32-36°C for 24 hours in comatose post-ROSC patients
- **Coronary angiography**: Urgent PCI if ST elevation post-ROSC
- **Avoid**: Hyperoxia (SpO2 94-98%), hypocapnia (PaCO2 35-45 mmHg), hypoglycemia

## ICU / CRITICAL CARE

### Mechanical Ventilation Modes

| Mode | Description | Settings | Use |
|------|------------|---------|-----|
| **AC (Assist-Control)** | Guaranteed breaths + patient triggers | TV, RR, FiO2, PEEP | Most common; risk of breath stacking |
| **SIMV** | Set RR + spontaneous breaths | TV, RR, PS for spontaneous | Weaning |
| **PSV (Pressure Support)** | Patient triggers; machine supports each breath | Pressure support level | Weaning; spontaneous breathing |
| **CPAP** | Continuous positive pressure; no mandatory breaths | CPAP level | Spontaneous breathing support |
| **PRVC** | Pressure-regulated volume control | TV target, pressure limit | Combines AC + pressure limiting |

### ARDS Management (PYQ HOTSPOT)

**Berlin Definition:**
| Severity | PaO2/FiO2 (P:F ratio) | PEEP |
|---------|----------------------|------|
| **Mild** | 200-300 | ≥5 cmH2O |
| **Moderate** | 100-200 | ≥5 cmH2O |
| **Severe** | <100 | ≥5 cmH2O |

**Protective Lung Ventilation (ARDSnet):**
- **Tidal volume**: **4-6 mL/kg** predicted body weight (not actual)
- **Plateau pressure**: <30 cmH2O
- **PEEP**: 5-15 cmH2O (higher in severe)
- **FiO2**: Minimize (<0.6 target when possible)
- **Prone positioning**: Indicated in P:F <150; improves V/Q matching; 16h/day minimum
- **Neuromuscular blockade**: Severe ARDS within 48h (cisatracurium infusion)

### Sepsis Management - Surviving Sepsis Campaign (PYQ HOTSPOT)

**Sepsis-3 Definition:**
- **Sepsis**: Life-threatening organ dysfunction (SOFA ≥2) from infection
- **Septic shock**: Sepsis + vasopressor need + lactate >2 mmol/L despite adequate fluids

**1-Hour Bundle:**
- Blood cultures **before** antibiotics
- **Broad-spectrum antibiotics** within 1 hour
- **30 mL/kg IV crystalloid** bolus (if hypotension or lactate ≥4 mmol/L)
- Vasopressors if hypotensive (target MAP ≥65 mmHg)
- Lactate measurement

**Vasopressor Choice:**
1. **Noradrenaline** (norepinephrine) - **First-line** vasopressor
2. **Vasopressin** 0.03 units/min - add to reduce noradrenaline dose; saves kidneys
3. **Adrenaline** (epinephrine) - Third line; can cause tachycardia
4. **Dopamine** - Not preferred (arrhythmias); consider in selected bradycardic patients

**Steroids**: Hydrocortisone 200mg/day IV if vasopressor-refractory (adjunct)

### Renal Replacement Therapy (RRT) - Indications (AEIOU)
- **A** - Acidosis (metabolic, unresponsive to treatment)
- **E** - Electrolyte disturbance (hyperkalemia refractory)
- **I** - Intoxication (dialyzable toxins)
- **O** - Overload (fluid overload unresponsive to diuretics)
- **U** - Uremia (symptomatic - encephalopathy, pericarditis, bleeding)

## MALIGNANT HYPERTHERMIA (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|-------|
| **Genetic basis** | **Ryanodine receptor (RYR1) mutation** (chromosome 19) |
| **Triggers** | **Succinylcholine + volatile inhalational agents** (halothane, sevoflurane, desflurane, isoflurane) |
| **Mechanism** | Uncontrolled Ca2+ release from sarcoplasmic reticulum → sustained muscle contraction |
| **First sign** | **↑ETCO2** (hypermetabolic state) |
| **Features** | Rigidity (masseter spasm after succinylcholine = early sign), hyperthermia (>38.5°C), acidosis, rhabdomyolysis, hyperkalemia, arrhythmias |
| **Diagnosis** | Clinical + **caffeine-halothane contracture test** (CHCT) - gold standard |
| **Treatment** | Stop trigger agents; **dantrolene 2.5 mg/kg IV** (repeat up to 10 mg/kg); cooling; treat hyperkalemia, acidosis; **100% O2; hyperventilate** |

**Dantrolene mechanism**: Inhibits RYR1 calcium release channel → reduces intracellular Ca2+

**Safe agents in MH-susceptible patients**: N2O, propofol, ketamine, opioids, non-depolarizing NMBAs, local anesthetics

## PEDIATRIC ANESTHESIA

### Airway Differences in Infants (PYQ HOTSPOT)
- **Obligate nasal breathers** (until 4-5 months)
- Larynx is **higher** (C3-C4 vs C5-C6 in adults)
- **Narrowest part**: **Subglottis** (cricoid ring) in children <8 years (vs glottis in adults)
- **Larger head** (occiput) → flexes neck when supine; shoulder roll to align
- **Tongue relatively larger**
- **Shorter trachea**: Right mainstem intubation risk

### Endotracheal Tube Size (PYQ HOTSPOT)
- **Uncuffed (< 8 years)**: **(Age/4) + 4** mm internal diameter
- **Cuffed (≥ 8 years)**: **(Age/4) + 3.5** mm
- **Depth (oral)**: (Age/2) + 12 cm or tube size × 3
- **Air leak**: Should have air leak at 15-20 cmH2O with uncuffed tube (ensures correct size)

### Pediatric Fluid Management
- **Maintenance (Holliday-Segar formula)**:
  - First 10 kg: **100 mL/kg/day** (4 mL/kg/hr)
  - Next 10 kg: **50 mL/kg/day** (2 mL/kg/hr)
  - Each kg after 20 kg: **20 mL/kg/day** (1 mL/kg/hr)
- **Blood volume**: Neonate 90 mL/kg; Infant 80 mL/kg; Child 70 mL/kg; Adult 70 mL/kg
- **MABL (Maximum Allowable Blood Loss)**: Weight × EBV × (starting Hct - minimum Hct) / starting Hct

### Neonatal Considerations
- **Fetal hemoglobin (HbF)**: High O2 affinity; left shift of oxyhemoglobin curve; low P50
- **Glucose homeostasis**: Prone to hypoglycemia; monitor; 10% dextrose infusion
- **Temperature regulation**: Poor thermoregulation; warm environment; warm IV fluids
- **Retinopathy of prematurity**: Limit FiO2 (SpO2 91-95% in preterm)
- **Neonatal emergency**: Congenital diaphragmatic hernia, tracheoesophageal fistula, omphalocele/gastroschisis

## HIGH-YIELD MNEMONICS

- **ASA classes**: Remember ASA V = moribund; VI = brain dead
- **MAC**: More potent = lower MAC; **N2O MAC = 105%** (need hyperbaric chamber for sole agent)
- **Induction agents order**: "Propofol, Ketamine, Thiopentone, Etomidate" - "Pretty Kittens Take Everything"
- **4H's and 4T's**: Reversible causes in cardiac arrest
- **LEMON**: Difficult airway assessment
- **Bupivacaine vs Ropivacaine**: Bupivacaine more cardiotoxic; Ropivacaine for labor (less motor block)
- **Sugammadex**: Only works on aminosteroidal agents (Rocuronium and Vecuronium)
- **MH treatment**: STOP triggers + Dantrolene 2.5 mg/kg + Cool + O2

## PYQ HOTSPOT QUICK REVISION

1. **ASA VI**: Brain-dead organ donor
2. **Fasting**: 2 hours clear liquids; 6 hours light meal; 8 hours heavy meal
3. **Mallampati IV**: Only hard palate visible; very difficult intubation
4. **Guedel Stage II**: Dangerous - avoid stimulation; irregular breathing
5. **MAC of N2O**: 105% (cannot be sole agent at 1 atm)
6. **Halothane**: Hepatotoxicity + malignant hyperthermia trigger
7. **Propofol**: PRIS; pain on injection; not in egg/soy allergy
8. **Ketamine**: Dissociative; raises ICP; bronchodilator; emergence reactions
9. **Etomidate**: Cardiac patients; adrenal suppression; myoclonus
10. **Succinylcholine**: Fastest onset (60 sec); contraindicated in burns (>24h), denervation, myopathies
11. **Sugammadex**: Reverses rocuronium/vecuronium immediately regardless of block depth
12. **Cisatracurium/Atracurium**: Hofmann elimination; safe in organ failure
13. **CVP normal**: 5-10 cmH2O
14. **BIS 40-60**: Adequate general anesthesia range
15. **CPR ratio**: 30:2; 100-120/min; 5-6 cm depth
16. **Shockable rhythms**: VF and pulseless VT → 200J biphasic
17. **Adrenaline in PEA/Asystole**: 1mg IV as soon as access; every 3-5 min
18. **Amiodarone**: 300mg after 3rd shock in VF/pVT
19. **ARDS protective ventilation**: 4-6 mL/kg TV; plateau <30 cmH2O; P:F ratio defines severity
20. **Malignant hyperthermia**: Ryanodine receptor; first sign = ↑ETCO2; treat with dantrolene 2.5 mg/kg
21. **PDPH treatment**: Epidural blood patch (gold standard)
22. **Bupivacaine toxicity**: Intralipid 20% treatment
23. **Pediatric ETT uncuffed**: (Age/4) + 4
24. **Noradrenaline**: First-line vasopressor in septic shock
