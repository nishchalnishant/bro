## PSYCHOTIC DISORDERS

### Schizophrenia

#### Schneider's First-Rank Symptoms (FRS)
Mnemonic: **ABCDE** (Auditory hallucinations — 3 types, Broadcasting of thought, Controlled/made experiences, Delusional perception, External agency)

1. **Auditory hallucinations**: Voices discussing patient in third person, voices commenting on patient's actions, **thought echo (Gedankenlautwerden)**
2. **Thought alienation**: Thought insertion, thought withdrawal, thought broadcasting
3. **Made experiences**: Made feelings, made impulses, made actions (passivity experiences)
4. **Delusional perception**: Normal perception + delusional interpretation (2-stage process)
5. **Somatic passivity**: Sensations made by external agency

**PYQ HOTSPOT: FRS are NOT pathognomonic of schizophrenia (can occur in bipolar, organic). FRS do NOT predict prognosis**

#### Positive vs Negative Symptoms

| Positive Symptoms | Negative Symptoms |
|---|---|
| Hallucinations | **Alogia** (poverty of speech) |
| Delusions | **Avolition** (lack of motivation) |
| Disorganized thought/speech | **Anhedonia** |
| Disorganized/catatonic behavior | **Affect flattening** |
| Agitation | **Asociality** |

**Negative symptoms** predict functional outcome; respond poorly to typical antipsychotics; **clozapine** most effective

#### Dopamine Hypothesis
- Excess dopaminergic activity in **mesolimbic pathway** → positive symptoms
- **Decreased** dopamine in **mesocortical pathway** → negative symptoms and cognitive dysfunction
- **PFC** dopamine deficiency → negative/cognitive symptoms

#### Subtypes of Psychotic Disorders (Duration Criteria)

| Disorder | Minimum Duration | Key Features |
|---|---|---|
| **Brief Psychotic Disorder** | **1 day to <1 month** | Sudden onset, often with stressor |
| **Schizophreniform Disorder** | **1-6 months** | Criteria for schizophrenia but <6 months |
| **Schizophrenia** | **≥6 months** | Includes ≥1 month active symptoms (A criteria) |
| **Schizoaffective Disorder** | Schizophrenia symptoms + mood episodes | Mood episode present for majority of total illness |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: 1 day-1 month = Brief psychotic; 1-6 months = Schizophreniform; >6 months = Schizophrenia**

#### Antipsychotic Treatment

**Typical (First-Generation) Antipsychotics**:
- D2 receptor blockers
- Chlorpromazine (low potency), haloperidol (high potency), trifluoperazine
- Side effects: **EPS** (acute dystonia, akathisia, Parkinsonism, tardive dyskinesia), **hyperprolactinemia** (amenorrhea, galactorrhea, sexual dysfunction), NMS

**Atypical (Second-Generation) Antipsychotics**:
- Block D2 + 5-HT2A
- Less EPS, more metabolic side effects
- **Clozapine** (most effective, treatment-resistant): Risk of **agranulocytosis** (1-2%) → weekly CBC for 6 months, then biweekly; also myocarditis, seizures (dose-related), excessive sedation, hypersalivation
- Olanzapine: Most metabolic side effects (weight gain, diabetes, dyslipidemia)
- Risperidone: High prolactin elevation (among atypicals)
- Quetiapine: Least EPS, used in Parkinson's psychosis
- Aripiprazole: Partial D2 agonist — minimal metabolic effects, least weight gain

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Clozapine = treatment-resistant schizophrenia + suicidal behavior in schizophrenia/schizoaffective**
**Agranulocytosis = most dangerous SE of clozapine. Monitor CBC**

#### Extrapyramidal Side Effects

| EPS | Timing | Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Acute dystonia | Hours-days | **Benztropine/procyclidine IV/IM** |
| Akathisia | Days-weeks | **Beta-blockers (propranolol)**, reduce dose, switch |
| Drug-induced Parkinsonism | Weeks-months | **Anticholinergics** (benztropine), reduce dose |
| **Tardive Dyskinesia** | Months-years | **Clonazepam, VMAT2 inhibitors (valbenazine, deutetrabenazine)**, switch to clozapine |

#### Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome (NMS)
- **Hyperthermia, Rigidity, Altered consciousness, Autonomic instability** (HRAO)
- **Elevated CPK** (markedly), leukocytosis, myoglobinuria
- Treatment: **Stop antipsychotic**, IV fluids, **dantrolene** (muscle relaxant), **bromocriptine** (dopamine agonist), **ECT** (may be needed)
- Mortality 5-20% if untreated

## MOOD DISORDERS

### Major Depressive Disorder (MDD)

#### DSM-5 Criteria — SIG E CAPS Mnemonic (≥5 symptoms for ≥2 weeks, including #1 or #2)
1. **S**leep disturbance (insomnia or hypersomnia)
2. **I**nterest loss (anhedonia) — **CORE SYMPTOM**
3. **G**uilt/worthlessness
4. **E**nergy loss/fatigue
5. **C**oncentration difficulty
6. **A**ppetite/weight change
7. **P**sychomotor changes (agitation or retardation)
8. **S**uicidal ideation
9. **Depressed mood** — **CORE SYMPTOM**

**PYQ HOTSPOT: 2 core symptoms = depressed mood + anhedonia. At least one must be present. Minimum 5/9 for 2 weeks**

#### Subtypes of Depression
- **Melancholic**: Early morning awakening, diurnal variation (worse AM), weight loss, anhedonia, psychomotor changes
- **Atypical**: Mood reactivity (brightens with positive events), reversed neurovegetative symptoms (hypersomnia, hyperphagia), leaden paralysis, rejection sensitivity
- **Psychotic depression**: Delusions/hallucinations (mood-congruent) — **ECT or antidepressant + antipsychotic**
- **Seasonal (SAD)**: Winter onset; **light therapy** (first-line), bupropion for prevention

### Bipolar Disorder

#### Bipolar I vs II

| Feature | Bipolar I | Bipolar II |
|---|---|---|
| Manic episode | **Yes (≥7 days or any duration if hospitalized)** | **No** (hypomania only) |
| Hypomanic episode | May occur | Yes (≥4 days) |
| Depressive episode | Usually present | Required |
| Hospitalization | May be required for mania | Does NOT require hospitalization (hypomania) |
| Psychosis | Can occur in mania | Does NOT occur in hypomania |
| Severity | More severe | Less severe overall |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Mania = ≥7 days (or any duration if hospitalized/psychotic). Hypomania = 4-6 days. Both = elevated mood + DIG FAST symptoms**

#### Manic Episode — DIG FAST
- **D**istractibility
- **I**mpulsivity/Indiscretion
- **G**randiosity
- **F**light of ideas
- **A**ctivity increased/Agitation
- **S**leep decreased (decreased NEED, not insomnia)
- **T**alkativeness (pressured speech)

#### Dysthymia and Cyclothymia
- **Dysthymia (Persistent Depressive Disorder)**: Depressed mood most days, ≥2 years, ≥2 depressive symptoms (not full MDD criteria)
- **Cyclothymia**: Numerous periods of hypomania + depression (not meeting full criteria) for ≥2 years; **does NOT meet criteria for BD I or II**

#### Lithium

**Therapeutic range**: **0.8-1.2 mEq/L** (acute mania); 0.6-0.8 mEq/L (maintenance)
**Toxic level**: >1.5 mEq/L (mild); >2.0 mEq/L (severe toxicity)

**Monitoring**: Lithium levels, **TFTs** (hypothyroidism risk), **renal function** (nephrogenic DI, chronic kidney disease), ECG (T-wave changes), pregnancy test (Ebstein's anomaly risk)

**Toxicity Features**:
- Mild (1.5-2): Tremor (coarse), nausea, diarrhea, lethargy
- Moderate (2-2.5): Confusion, ataxia, slurred speech, EEG changes
- Severe (>2.5): Seizures, coma, cardiovascular collapse
- Treatment: **Hydration, hemodialysis** (for severe toxicity)

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Lithium — narrow therapeutic window. Most common SE = fine tremor (treat with propranolol). Hypothyroidism in 20-40%. Teratogenic (Ebstein's anomaly)**

**Drug interactions**: NSAIDs, thiazides, ACE inhibitors → **increase lithium levels** (reduce renal clearance)

#### ECT (Electroconvulsive Therapy)

**Indications**:
- **Treatment-resistant depression** (most common indication)
- **Severe suicidality** (emergency)
- **Psychotic depression**
- **Mania** (treatment-resistant or severe)
- **Catatonia** (both depressive and schizophrenic)
- **NMS** (when pharmacotherapy fails)
- Pregnancy (depression requiring treatment — safer than drugs)
- **Acute suicidality** (fastest acting treatment)

**Contraindications**: No absolute contraindications; relative: raised ICP, recent MI (<3 months), aortic aneurysm, severe cardiorespiratory disease, pheochromocytoma

**Side Effects**: **Anterograde amnesia** (most common, usually transient), retrograde amnesia, post-ictal confusion, headache, myalgia, cardiovascular effects (brief hypertension/arrhythmias)
- Bilateral electrode placement → more amnesia vs unilateral (right non-dominant)

**PYQ HOTSPOT: ECT = fastest acting treatment for depression. Anterograde amnesia most common SE**

#### Mood Stabilizers Comparison

| Drug | Uses | Key SE |
|---|---|---|
| **Lithium** | Bipolar I (mania + maintenance), augmentation | Tremor, hypothyroidism, renal, Ebstein's anomaly |
| **Valproate (Sodium Valproate)** | Mania (rapid cycling), epilepsy | **Teratogenic (neural tube defects)**, hepatotoxicity, weight gain, PCOD |
| **Carbamazepine** | Mania, trigeminal neuralgia | SIADH, Stevens-Johnson, aplastic anemia, CYP inducer |
| **Lamotrigine** | **Bipolar depression** (best evidence) | **Stevens-Johnson syndrome** (especially with valproate), slow titration |
| **Quetiapine** | Bipolar depression, mania | Metabolic, sedation |

## ANXIETY DISORDERS

### GAD (Generalized Anxiety Disorder)
- **Excessive, uncontrollable worry** for **≥6 months** about multiple domains
- ≥3/6 symptoms: Restlessness, fatigue, concentration difficulty, irritability, muscle tension, sleep disturbance
- Treatment: **SSRIs/SNRIs** (first-line), buspirone (non-benzodiazepine, anxiolytic, no dependence), BZD (short-term only), **CBT**

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Duration criterion for GAD = 6 months. Buspirone acts on 5-HT1A receptors, not GABA**

### Panic Disorder
- **Recurrent unexpected panic attacks** + persistent concern about future attacks
- **≥4/13 symptoms** during attack (physical + cognitive):
  - **Physical**: Palpitations, sweating, trembling, dyspnea, choking, chest pain, nausea, dizziness, chills/hot flashes, paresthesias
  - **Cognitive**: Derealization/depersonalization, fear of losing control, fear of dying
- Peak at 10 minutes, resolve by 30 minutes
- **Agoraphobia**: Fear of situations where escape difficult (may accompany panic disorder)
- Treatment: **SSRIs** (first-line), **CBT + exposure therapy**, BZD (acute/short-term), TCAs (imipramine), alprazolam

### Social Anxiety Disorder (Social Phobia)
- Fear of social/performance situations (fear of embarrassment)
- **Performance subtype** (public speaking): **Beta-blockers** (propranolol) — situational use
- Treatment: **SSRIs/SNRIs, CBT**; phenelzine (MAOI) for severe/refractory

### Specific Phobia
- Marked fear of specific object/situation, out of proportion
- Subtypes: Animal, natural environment, blood-injection-injury (BII), situational, other
- **BII type**: Unique **vasovagal response** (fainting)
- Treatment: **Exposure therapy** (most effective)

## OCD

- **Obsessions**: Recurrent intrusive thoughts/images/impulses (ego-dystonic) → anxiety
- **Compulsions**: Repetitive behaviors/mental acts performed to reduce anxiety from obsessions
- Common themes: Contamination (washing), checking, ordering, intrusive thoughts (violence/religious/sexual)

#### Yale-Brown OC Scale (Y-BOCS)
- Gold standard for assessing OCD severity
- 10 items (5 obsession + 5 compulsion) rated 0-4 each; total 0-40
- 0-7: Subclinical; 8-15: Mild; 16-23: Moderate; 24-31: Severe; 32-40: Extreme

#### Treatment
- **First-line**: **Fluvoxamine** (highest evidence), **clomipramine** (TCA — most potent anti-OCD but SE profile), **ERP (Exposure and Response Prevention)** — therapy of choice
- Other SSRIs: Sertraline, fluoxetine, paroxetine (all effective, generally preferred over clomipramine due to SE)
- **Clomipramine vs SSRIs**: Clomipramine more effective but more SE (anticholinergic, cardiotoxic)
- Refractory OCD: Augmentation with **risperidone or aripiprazole** (atypical antipsychotics)
- **DBS or neurosurgery** (cingulotomy, anterior capsulotomy) for ultra-refractory cases

**PYQ HOTSPOT: OCD first-line = SSRI + ERP. Clomipramine most efficacious but second-line due to SE. OCD is ego-DYSTONIC (patient recognizes it is excessive)**

## PTSD AND STRESS DISORDERS

#### ASD vs PTSD

| Feature | Acute Stress Disorder (ASD) | PTSD |
|---|---|---|
| Duration | **3 days to 1 month** after trauma | **>1 month** after trauma |
| Core clusters | Intrusion, negative mood, dissociation, avoidance, arousal | Intrusion, avoidance, negative cognitions/mood, arousal/reactivity |
| Dissociation | **Required** (prominent feature) | Not required |
| Treatment | CBT, debriefing (NOT critical incident debriefing — harmful); monitor for PTSD | **Trauma-focused CBT, EMDR** (first-line); **SSRIs** (sertraline, paroxetine — FDA approved) |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: ASD = 3 days to 1 month. PTSD = >1 month. EMDR = Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing**

#### Adjustment Disorder
- Emotional/behavioral symptoms in response to **identifiable stressor**, within **3 months** of stressor
- Symptoms do NOT meet criteria for another disorder
- Duration: Resolves within **6 months** of stressor ending (or can persist if stressor is chronic)
- Treatment: **Psychotherapy** (first-line), short-term BZD if needed

## PERSONALITY DISORDERS

#### Cluster Classification

| Cluster | Type | Core Feature | Key Details |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A (Odd/Eccentric)** | **Paranoid** | Pervasive distrust and suspicion | Not delusional; "odd, suspicious" |
| A | **Schizoid** | Detachment from social relationships, restricted affect | Does NOT want social contact; anhedonia without depression |
| A | **Schizotypal** | Eccentric, magical thinking, ideas of reference | On schizophrenia spectrum; can decompensate |
| **B (Dramatic/Emotional)** | **Antisocial (ASPD)** | Disregard for others' rights, repeated violations | ≥18 years old, conduct disorder before 15; **psychopathy** |
| B | **Borderline (BPD)** | Instability in relationships, self-image, affect + impulsivity | **Parasuicidal behavior, splitting, identity disturbance, FDS** |
| B | **Histrionic** | Excessive emotionality and attention-seeking | Seductive, theatrical, easily influenced |
| B | **Narcissistic** | Grandiosity, need for admiration, lack of empathy | Fragile self-esteem, rage when criticized |
| **C (Anxious/Fearful)** | **Avoidant** | Social inhibition, inadequacy, hypersensitive to criticism | WANTS social contact but fears rejection |
| C | **Dependent** | Excessive need to be taken care of, submissive | Fears separation, clinging |
| C | **OCPD** | Preoccupation with orderliness, perfectionism, control | **Ego-syntonic** (unlike OCD which is ego-dystonic) |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: BPD = unstable relationships + identity + affect + impulsivity. OCPD = ego-syntonic (person is OK with traits, unlike OCD). Antisocial PD = requires conduct disorder before age 15**

#### BPD Treatment
- **DBT (Dialectical Behavior Therapy)** — first-line, specifically designed for BPD
- Key skills: **Mindfulness, distress tolerance, interpersonal effectiveness, emotion regulation**

## SUBSTANCE USE DISORDERS

### Alcohol Use Disorder

#### CAGE Questionnaire (≥2 = likely alcohol use disorder)
- **C**ut down — have you tried to cut down?
- **A**nnoyed — have others annoyed you by criticizing?
- **G**uilty — have you felt guilty about drinking?
- **E**ye-opener — do you drink first thing in the morning?

**AUDIT (Alcohol Use Disorders Identification Test)**: 10 questions, score ≥8 = hazardous use, ≥16 = dependent

#### Alcohol Withdrawal Syndrome
- **Timeline**:
  - 6-24h: Tremor, anxiety, nausea, sweating, tachycardia, hypertension
  - 24-48h: **Alcoholic hallucinosis** (auditory hallucinations, CLEAR sensorium)
  - 24-72h: **Withdrawal seizures** (tonic-clonic, typically brief, no focal features)
  - 72-96h (2-5 days): **Delirium Tremens** (delirium + autonomic instability + seizures)

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Alcoholic hallucinosis = hallucinations with CLEAR consciousness. DT = hallucinations + DELIRIUM + autonomic instability**

#### Delirium Tremens Treatment
- **Benzodiazepines** (first-line): IV lorazepam (CIWA-guided), chlordiazepoxide (oral, most studied)
- Symptom-triggered vs fixed-dose protocols
- IV fluids, thiamine BEFORE glucose, correct electrolytes
- **Prophylaxis for DT**: Carbamazepine or long-acting BZD (chlordiazepoxide)

#### Wernicke-Korsakoff Syndrome
- **Wernicke's encephalopathy**: Thiamine (Vit B1) deficiency
  - Triad: **Ophthalmoplegia + Ataxia + Confusion** (altered consciousness)
  - Treatment: **IV Thiamine 100mg** BEFORE glucose (glucose can precipitate Wernicke's)
- **Korsakoff's syndrome**: Chronic sequela of untreated Wernicke's
  - **Anterograde amnesia > retrograde amnesia**
  - **Confabulation** (characteristic — filling memory gaps with fabricated stories)
  - Mammillary body and dorsomedial thalamus damage
  - Often irreversible (only 20% recover fully)

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Give thiamine BEFORE dextrose in alcoholics. Korsakoff = anterograde amnesia + confabulation (consciousness intact)**

### Opioid Use Disorder

#### Opioid Withdrawal Timeline
- Short-acting opioids (heroin): Symptoms start **8-24h**, peak **36-72h**, resolve **5-7 days**
- Long-acting opioids (methadone): Start **24-48h**, peak **72-96h**, resolve up to **2 weeks**
- Withdrawal symptoms: **Rhinorrhea, lacrimation, yawning, piloerection, mydriasis, muscle cramps, diarrhea, insomnia, drug craving**
- **Rarely life-threatening** (contrast to alcohol/BZD withdrawal)

#### Opioid Intoxication
- Triad: **Miosis + Respiratory depression + Decreased consciousness**
- Treatment: **Naloxone** (opioid antagonist), short half-life → repeat doses may be needed

#### Maintenance Therapy
- **Methadone**: Full mu-opioid agonist, long-acting; dispensed daily; QTc prolongation
- **Buprenorphine/naloxone (Suboxone)**: Partial mu-agonist + naloxone; better safety profile; given sublingually; **preferred in outpatient settings**
- **Naltrexone**: Opioid antagonist; **not for acute withdrawal**; monthly injection (Vivitrol); requires detox first; motivational issues
- **LAAM**: No longer used (cardiac toxicity)

### Cannabis
- **THC** (tetrahydrocannabinol) — active ingredient, binds CB1/CB2 receptors
- Intoxication: Euphoria, relaxation, perceptual distortions, tachycardia, conjunctival injection, increased appetite
- **Cannabis use disorder**: Tolerance, withdrawal (irritability, anxiety, sleep disturbance, decreased appetite)
- **Amotivational syndrome**: Apathy, anergia, memory impairment
- **Cannabis hyperemesis syndrome**: Cyclic vomiting, compulsive hot bathing

### Stimulants (Amphetamines/Cocaine)
- Intoxication: Euphoria, hyperactivity, tachycardia, hypertension, **mydriasis**, hyperthermia, reduced appetite
- **Cocaine**: Blocks reuptake of DA/NE/5-HT; local anesthetic; nasal septum perforation (chronic snorting); **chest pain + MI in young adults**
- Withdrawal: **Crash** — fatigue, hypersomnia, hyperphagia, depression, drug craving; **no major physiological withdrawal**

### Hallucinogens
- LSD, psilocybin, mescaline — 5-HT2A agonists
- **HPPD (Hallucinogen Persisting Perception Disorder)**: Re-experiencing perceptual disturbances after cessation ("flashbacks")
- Treatment: Supportive; clonazepam for HPPD

## NEURODEVELOPMENTAL DISORDERS

### ADHD

#### DSM-5 Criteria
- ≥6 symptoms of **inattention** AND/OR **hyperactivity-impulsivity** (≥5 in adolescents ≥17 years)
- Present **before age 12**
- In ≥2 settings, functional impairment
- Subtypes: Predominantly inattentive, predominantly hyperactive-impulsive, combined

**Inattention**: Careless mistakes, poor sustained attention, poor listening, doesn't follow through, poor organization, avoids sustained mental effort, loses things, easily distracted, forgetful

**Hyperactivity-Impulsivity**: Fidgets, leaves seat, runs/climbs, can't play quietly, "on the go," talks excessively, blurts answers, trouble waiting, interrupts

#### Treatment
- **First-line**: **Methylphenidate** (Ritalin) — blocks reuptake of dopamine and norepinephrine; stimulant
- **Amphetamines** (Adderall): Alternative first-line in USA
- **Non-stimulants**: **Atomoxetine** (SNRi, PREFERRED in substance abuse history or tic disorders), guanfacine, clonidine
- **Behavioral interventions**: Parent training, school accommodations, CBT (for adults)

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Methylphenidate = first-line pharmacotherapy for ADHD. Atomoxetine = non-stimulant, good for comorbid anxiety or substance use disorder**

### Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)

#### DSM-5 Triad (now DYAD):
1. **Persistent deficits in social communication and interaction** across contexts
2. **Restricted, repetitive patterns** of behavior, interests, or activities

- Symptoms present in **early developmental period** (may not fully manifest until social demands exceed capacities)
- Severity levels 1-3 (based on support required)
- **Specific features**: Echolalia, pronoun reversal, insistence on sameness, sensory sensitivities, rigid routines, unusual interests, hand-flapping/rocking
- **Savant skills** (exceptional abilities in limited domains)
- Associated: Intellectual disability (30%), epilepsy (25-30%), ADHD
- **No curative treatment**; behavioral interventions (ABA therapy most evidence-based), speech/language therapy, special education

**PYQ HOTSPOT: ASD = social-communication deficit + restricted/repetitive behavior. NOT caused by MMR vaccine (Wakefield study retracted)**

### Intellectual Disability (ID)

| Severity | IQ Range | Adaptive Function |
|---|---|---|
| Mild | **50-70** | 6th grade academic level; can live independently |
| Moderate | **35-49** | 2nd grade level; supervised living |
| Severe | **20-34** | Requires extensive support |
| Profound | **<20** | Continuous intensive support |

- Most common genetic cause: **Down syndrome (Trisomy 21)**
- Most common preventable cause: **Fetal alcohol syndrome**
- Most common inherited cause: **Fragile X syndrome** (FMR1 gene, trinucleotide repeat, X-linked)

**PYQ HOTSPOT: IQ 70-80 = borderline. IQ 50-70 = mild ID (most common, 85% of all ID)**

## EATING DISORDERS

### Anorexia Nervosa

**DSM-5 Criteria**:
1. **Restriction** of energy intake → significantly low body weight
2. **Intense fear** of gaining weight
3. **Distorted body image** or denial of seriousness of low weight

- BMI criteria: **<17.5 kg/m²** (ICD) or significantly below expected (DSM-5)
- **Russell's sign**: Calluses on dorsal knuckles (from self-induced vomiting, more associated with bulimia but seen in purging subtype of AN)
- **Lanugo hair** (fine downy hair — metabolic adaptation)
- Physical complications: **Hypokalemia** (purging), hypophosphatemia, **QTc prolongation** (risk of fatal arrhythmia), bradycardia, hypotension, **refeeding syndrome** (dangerous during re-alimentation)
- **Highest mortality** of all psychiatric disorders (~5-10%)
- Treatment: **Re-alimentation** (CAREFUL to avoid refeeding syndrome — slow, start low), CBT, family therapy (Maudsley approach in adolescents); **olanzapine** for weight gain

**Refeeding Syndrome**: Hypophosphatemia, hypokalemia, hypomagnesemia after refeeding of malnourished patient → cardiac failure, seizures. Prevention: slow refeeding, phosphate supplementation, **thiamine before feeding**

### Bulimia Nervosa

**DSM-5 Criteria**:
1. Recurrent **binge eating** (large amount + lack of control) — at least **1/week for 3 months**
2. Recurrent **compensatory behaviors** (purging: vomiting, laxatives; non-purging: excessive exercise, fasting)
3. Self-evaluation unduly influenced by weight/shape

- **Normal weight** (BMI usually normal/low normal)
- **Parotid enlargement** (sialadenosis from vomiting), **dental erosion** (stomach acid), Russell's sign
- Metabolic: **Hypokalemia, metabolic alkalosis** (from vomiting)
- Treatment: **CBT** (first-line), **Fluoxetine 60mg** (only FDA-approved antidepressant for bulimia; higher dose than depression), interpersonal therapy

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Bulimia = CBT + fluoxetine 60mg. Bulimia patients are usually normal weight. AVOID bupropion (increases seizure risk in eating disorders)**

## SLEEP DISORDERS

### Insomnia
- Difficulty initiating/maintaining sleep or early morning awakening, ≥3 nights/week, ≥3 months
- Treatment: **CBT-I (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia)** — first-line
- Pharmacological: Z-drugs (zolpidem — short-term), melatonin, low-dose doxepin, trazodone; **BZD for short-term only**

### Narcolepsy (Hypersomnia)
- Tetrad: **EDS** (excessive daytime sleepiness) + **Cataplexy** (sudden muscle tone loss with emotion) + **Sleep paralysis** + **Hypnagogic/hypnopompic hallucinations**
- **Cataplexy is pathognomonic** for Type 1 narcolepsy
- **HLA-DQB1*0602** — strong genetic association
- **Orexin (hypocretin)** deficiency — loss of neurons in lateral hypothalamus
- PSG + MSLT: Mean sleep latency ≤8 min, ≥2 sleep-onset REM periods (SOREMPs)
- Treatment:
  - EDS: **Modafinil** (first-line), armodafinil, amphetamines
  - Cataplexy: **Sodium oxybate (GHB)** (most effective for cataplexy + EDS), **SSRIs**, venlafaxine, clomipramine

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Narcolepsy = EDS + cataplexy + hypocretin deficiency + HLA-DQB1. MSLT gold standard diagnostic**

### Parasomnias

| Parasomnia | Sleep Stage | Features |
|---|---|---|
| **Sleepwalking (somnambulism)** | **NREM (Stage N3 — slow wave)** | Complex behaviors, no memory, first 1/3 of night |
| **Night terrors** | **NREM (Stage N3)** | Screaming, fear, sweating, no memory, first 1/3 of night |
| **Nightmares** | **REM** | Frightening dreams, full awakening with recall, last 1/3 of night |
| **REM Sleep Behavior Disorder (RBD)** | **REM** | Acting out dreams (violence), loss of normal REM atonia; **associated with synucleinopathies (PD, DLB, MSA)** |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: RBD = REM stage, loss of atonia, predicts Parkinson's/DLB by years. Night terrors = NREM, no recall**

### Restless Legs Syndrome (RLS)
- Urge to move legs, worse at rest, worse in evening/night, relieved by movement
- Associated: Iron deficiency (most common secondary cause), pregnancy, renal failure, peripheral neuropathy
- Treatment: **Dopamine agonists** (pramipexole, ropinirole — first-line); iron supplementation if ferritin <50; gabapentin/pregabalin (alternative)

## GERIATRIC PSYCHIATRY

### Alzheimer's Disease (AD)
- Most common cause of dementia (60-70%)
- **Amyloid plaques (beta-amyloid, Abeta42)** + **Neurofibrillary tangles (tau protein)** — neuropathology
- **Gradual onset**, progressive memory impairment (short-term → long-term), language, visuospatial
- **Apolipoprotein E4 (APOE ε4)**: Biggest genetic risk factor for sporadic AD
- Early-onset AD (<65): **APP, PSEN1, PSEN2** gene mutations (autosomal dominant)
- MMSE: Max 30 points; <24 = likely dementia (not diagnostic); used for severity monitoring

**Treatment**: **Cholinesterase inhibitors** (donepezil, rivastigmine, galantamine) — for mild-moderate; donepezil also for severe; **Memantine** (NMDA antagonist) — moderate-severe or add to ChEI

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Cholinesterase inhibitors = first-line for mild-moderate AD. Memantine for moderate-severe. No disease-modifying treatment (yet — lecanemab, donanemab in trials)**

### Vascular Dementia
- Stepwise progression (not gradual), focal neurological signs
- **Hypertension** = most important risk factor
- Treatment: Vascular risk factor management; cholinesterase inhibitors have modest benefit

### Lewy Body Dementia (DLB)
- Triad: **Fluctuating cognition + Visual hallucinations (vivid, well-formed) + Parkinsonism**
- RBD often precedes
- **Extreme sensitivity to antipsychotics** (contraindicated — can cause severe NMS-like reaction)
- Cholinesterase inhibitors (rivastigmine — best evidence)

**PYQ HOTSPOT: DLB = avoid typical/atypical antipsychotics (haloperidol, olanzapine, risperidone). Quetiapine is relatively safer if antipsychotic needed**

### Delirium vs Dementia

| Feature | Delirium | Dementia |
|---|---|---|
| Onset | **Acute/subacute (hours-days)** | **Insidious (months-years)** |
| Course | **Fluctuating** | Progressive, stable (vascular = stepwise) |
| Consciousness | **Impaired** | **Preserved** until late |
| Attention | **Markedly impaired** | Relatively preserved early |
| Sleep-wake cycle | **Disturbed** | May be disturbed later |
| Hallucinations | Common (visual) | Less common early |
| Reversibility | **Often reversible** | Usually irreversible |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Key distinction = consciousness is IMPAIRED in delirium, PRESERVED in dementia (early)**

#### Delirium Treatment
- Identify and treat underlying cause (infection, metabolic, drugs, hypoxia, pain)
- Non-pharmacological: Reorientation, lighting, familiar faces, minimize drugs
- **Haloperidol** (low dose) for agitation, quetiapine (in PD/DLB); **BZD AVOID** (worsen confusion)
- Exception: BZD for alcohol/BZD withdrawal delirium

## CHILD PSYCHIATRY

### Separation Anxiety Disorder
- Excessive fear of separation from attachment figures; most common anxiety disorder in children
- **School refusal** (school phobia) — most common in 5-10 years; present with somatic complaints (abdominal pain, headache)
- Treatment: **CBT + family therapy + SSRIs** (if moderate-severe); **graduated return to school** (not prolonged absence)

### Conduct Disorder vs ODD

| Feature | Oppositional Defiant Disorder (ODD) | Conduct Disorder (CD) |
|---|---|---|
| Core behavior | Arguing, defiance, hostile behavior | **Violation of rules/rights of others** |
| Age of onset | Usually preschool-early school | <10 years (childhood onset) or ≥10 (adolescent onset) |
| Seriousness | Less severe | More severe (precursor to ASPD) |
| Physical aggression | No | **Yes** (physical cruelty, weapons) |

### Enuresis and Encopresis
- **Enuresis**: Involuntary voiding ≥2 times/week for ≥3 months, age ≥5 years; **nighttime (nocturnal) most common**
  - Treatment: **Moisture alarm (behavioral)** — most effective; **desmopressin** (ADH analogue — for nighttime); imipramine (TCA — third-line, anticholinergic)
- **Encopresis**: Voluntary or involuntary stool passage in inappropriate places, age ≥4 years
  - Most often with constipation and overflow incontinence
  - Treatment: Disimpaction, maintenance laxatives (polyethylene glycol), behavioral training

## TREATMENT

### Antidepressant Mechanisms

| Class | Examples | Mechanism | Key SE |
|---|---|---|---|
| **SSRIs** | Fluoxetine, sertraline, escitalopram, paroxetine, fluvoxamine | Block 5-HT reuptake | Sexual dysfunction, GI, serotonin syndrome; SAFE in overdose |
| **SNRIs** | Venlafaxine, duloxetine, desvenlafaxine | Block 5-HT + NE reuptake | HTN (venlafaxine), sweating |
| **TCAs** | Amitriptyline, imipramine, clomipramine, nortriptyline | Block 5-HT + NE + anticholinergic | Anticholinergic, cardiac (QRS widening in overdose), sedation, **LETHAL in overdose** |
| **MAOIs** | Phenelzine, tranylcypromine, selegiline | Block MAO-A/B (irreversible) | **Hypertensive crisis with tyramine-rich foods**, serotonin syndrome with 5-HT drugs; 2-week washout |
| **Bupropion** | — | NE + DA reuptake blocker | Seizures, NO sexual dysfunction, NO weight gain; good for smoking cessation; AVOID in eating disorders/seizure history |
| **Mirtazapine** | — | Alpha-2 antagonist, 5-HT2/5-HT3 blocker | **Weight gain, sedation** (antihistamine); GOOD for anxious/insomnia/anorexic depression |
| **Vortioxetine** | — | 5-HT reuptake blocker + 5-HT modulator | Nausea; cognitive benefits |

**PYQ HOTSPOT: TCA overdose = ECG changes (QRS widening >100ms = toxicity), treat with sodium bicarbonate. MAOIs + SSRIs = fatal serotonin syndrome (2-week washout required)**

### Serotonin Syndrome
- **Triad**: **Altered mental status** + **Autonomic instability** (hyperthermia, tachycardia, diaphoresis, hypertension) + **Neuromuscular abnormality** (clonus, hyperreflexia, tremor, myoclonus)
- **Causes**: SSRI + MAOI; SSRI + tramadol/fentanyl/linezolid; SSRI + triptan; lithium + SSRI overdose
- **Clonus** (especially inducible/spontaneous) — key distinguishing feature from NMS
- Onset within hours
- Treatment: **Discontinue offending drug**, cyproheptadine (5-HT2 antagonist), supportive care (cooling, BZD, IV fluids)

**NMS vs Serotonin Syndrome Comparison**:
| Feature | Serotonin Syndrome | NMS |
|---|---|---|
| Onset | **Hours** | Days-weeks |
| Rigidity | **Mild or absent** | **Severe (lead-pipe)** |
| Reflexes | **Hyperreflexia, clonus** | Normal/decreased |
| Cause | Excess serotonin | Dopamine blockade |
| Treatment | Cyproheptadine | Dantrolene, bromocriptine |

### Psychotherapy Types

| Therapy | Key Features | Indications |
|---|---|---|
| **CBT** | Identify and change negative thoughts/behaviors; structured, time-limited | Depression, anxiety, OCD, eating disorders |
| **DBT** | CBT + mindfulness + acceptance; skills training | **BPD** (specifically), self-harm, eating disorders |
| **Psychodynamic** | Exploration of unconscious, past experiences, therapeutic relationship | Personality disorders, chronic depression |
| **Interpersonal Therapy (IPT)** | Focus on interpersonal relationships; role transitions, grief | **Depression** (especially bulimia) |
| **EMDR** | Bilateral stimulation while recalling trauma | **PTSD** |
| **Motivational Interviewing (MI)** | Enhance intrinsic motivation to change; OARS (Open questions, Affirm, Reflect, Summarize) | Substance use, behavior change |
| **ERP** | Exposure without performing compulsion | **OCD** |

## LEGAL/FORENSIC PSYCHIATRY

### Mental Healthcare Act 2017 (India)
- Replaced Mental Health Act 1987
- **Rights** of mentally ill persons: Right to access mental healthcare, right to equality/non-discrimination, right to confidentiality, right to legal aid, **right to refuse ECT**
- **Voluntary admission**: Person with capacity can admit themselves and discharge with 24h notice
- **Independent admission**: For persons with less than full capacity; can be discharged by themselves or nominated representative
- **Supported admission**: For involuntary admission; requires two psychiatrists + mental health review board approval for extension beyond 30 days
- **Advance directive**: Mentally ill person can state preferences for treatment in advance

**PYQ HOTSPOT: Mental Healthcare Act 2017 = replaced 1987 Act. Right to refuse treatment (including ECT) established. Advance directives recognized**

### McNaughton Rules (Insanity Defense)
Established 1843, UK case (Daniel McNaughton)
Defense of insanity established if:
1. Accused did not know the **nature and quality** of the act, OR
2. If they did know it, they did not know that what they were doing was **wrong**

Due to **disease of mind** (mental disorder)

**PYQ HOTSPOT: McNaughton = cognitive test only (knowledge of wrongfulness). Does NOT include volitional control**

### Fitness to Stand Trial (Criminal Responsibility)
- Ability to understand nature of proceedings, instruct counsel, follow trial
- **Separate from criminal responsibility** (insanity defense is retrospective; fitness is current state)

### Testamentary Capacity
- Ability to make a valid will
- Must know: **Nature of act of making a will, extent of property, persons who have natural claims, no mental disorder affecting above decisions**

### Dangerous Offenders and Risk Assessment
- **MacArthur Risk Assessment Study**: Dynamic risk factors more predictive than static
- Static factors: Past violence, demographic
- Dynamic factors: Substance use, mental state, social support
