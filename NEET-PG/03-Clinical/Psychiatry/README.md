# Psychiatry — NEET PG
**NEET PG Weight:** 6 questions (~3%)
**Tier:** 3

## Files in This Folder
- `psychiatry-lecture.md` — First-principles lecture notes for deep understanding (read in Month 8)
- `psychiatry-notes.md` — Condensed exam-focused notes for revision (use from Month 9 onwards)

## Top NEET PG Topics
1. Schizophrenia — first-rank symptoms (Schneider), positive vs negative symptoms, antipsychotic drug choice and adverse effects
2. Mood disorders — diagnostic criteria for depression and mania (ICD-11/DSM-5), lithium toxicity and monitoring
3. Substance use disorders — dependence vs abuse, withdrawal features, management (delirium tremens, opioid withdrawal)

## Study Tips
- Rating scales (Hamilton, MMSE, BPRS, CAGE, AUDIT) are a reliable direct Q source — memorise which scale measures what.
- Drug adverse effects in Psychiatry are frequently tested: tardive dyskinesia, NMS, serotonin syndrome — know the clinical distinctions.
- A focused 3–4 day preparation is enough for Psychiatry; do not over-invest relative to the 6-question weight.
