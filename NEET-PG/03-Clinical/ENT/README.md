# ENT (Ear, Nose and Throat) — NEET PG
**NEET PG Weight:** 8 questions (~4%)
**Tier:** 3

## Files in This Folder
- `ent-lecture.md` — First-principles lecture notes for deep understanding (read in Month 7)
- `ent-notes.md` — Condensed exam-focused notes for revision (use from Month 9 onwards)

## Top NEET PG Topics
1. Otology — CSOM (tubotympanic vs atticoantral, cholesteatoma), audiogram patterns, tympanogram interpretation
2. Vertigo — Meniere's disease vs BPPV vs vestibular neuritis (clinical distinction, Dix-Hallpike, Epley manoeuvre)
3. Laryngology — carcinoma larynx (staging, treatment), vocal cord paralysis (unilateral vs bilateral), tracheostomy indications

## Study Tips
- Audiogram interpretation (conductive vs sensorineural vs mixed loss, patterns in specific conditions) is asked every year.
- ENT is anatomy-heavy — laryngeal framework, middle ear ossicles, nasal blood supply (Kiesselbach's plexus) are direct Q sources.
- Eponymous signs and operations (Schwartze, Killian, Caldwell-Luc, Trotter's triad) are compact, high-return revision items.
