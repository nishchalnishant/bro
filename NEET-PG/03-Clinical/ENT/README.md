# ENT (Ear, Nose and Throat)

ENT covers diseases of the ear, nose, paranasal sinuses, pharynx, larynx, and neck — contributing ~8 questions per NEET PG paper, with strong emphasis on audiological and anatomical knowledge.

## High-Yield Areas

- Otology: CSOM (tubotympanic vs atticoantral type, cholesteatoma features), audiogram patterns in conductive vs sensorineural loss, tympanogram types
- Vertigo: Meniere's disease vs BPPV vs vestibular neuritis — clinical distinction, Dix-Hallpike test, Epley manoeuvre
- Laryngology: carcinoma larynx (site-based staging, glottic vs supraglottic), vocal cord paralysis (unilateral vs bilateral features), tracheostomy indications
- Nasal conditions: epistaxis (Kiesselbach's plexus, Little's area), nasal polyps, and rhinosinusitis management
- Eponymous signs and operations: Schwartze sign, Killian's dehiscence, Caldwell-Luc, Trotter's triad, and Samter's triad
