# Orthopedics

Orthopedics covers the diagnosis and management of musculoskeletal conditions including fractures, dislocations, bone tumours, infections, and congenital deformities — contributing ~7 questions per NEET PG paper.

| `WEAK-TOPICS.md` | Personally flagged weak areas requiring extra revision attention |

## High-Yield Areas

- Fractures: Colles' fracture features, femoral neck fractures (Garden classification), common fracture complications (AVN, compartment syndrome, fat embolism)
- Bone tumours: osteosarcoma vs Ewing's sarcoma — age of peak incidence, X-ray patterns, histology, and treatment
- Infections: acute haematogenous osteomyelitis (most common organism by age, metaphyseal predilection), septic arthritis management
- Compartment syndrome: 6 Ps, pressure threshold for fasciotomy, compartments at risk (forearm, leg)
- Eponymous clinical tests: Thomas test (hip flexion contracture), McMurray's (meniscal tear), Lachman and anterior drawer (ACL), Trendelenburg (abductor weakness)
