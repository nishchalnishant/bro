## FRACTURE PRINCIPLES

### Definition and Classification

**Fracture**: Break in continuity of bone from force exceeding its strength

#### General Classifications
- **Open (compound)**: Skin breach; Gustilo-Anderson (I/II/IIIA/IIIB/IIIC)
- **Closed (simple)**: Intact skin
- **Complete vs Incomplete**: Greenstick (children - periosteum intact), torus/buckle
- **Comminuted**: Multiple fragments
- **Pathological**: Through abnormal bone (tumor, osteoporosis, Paget's)
- **Stress/fatigue**: Repeated submaximal force (metatarsal 2nd - march fracture)

### Salter-Harris Classification (PYQ HOTSPOT - Physeal Fractures in Children)

| Type | Description | Mnemonic | Prognosis |
|------|------------|----------|-----------|
| **I** | Through physis (transverse) | **S**traight across | Good |
| **II** | Physis + metaphysis | **A**bove (metaphysis) | **Most common (75%)** |
| **III** | Physis + epiphysis | **L**ower (epiphysis) | Worse (intra-articular) |
| **IV** | Metaphysis + physis + epiphysis | **E**verything (all three) | Worse |
| **V** | Crush of physis | **R**ammed/crushed | **Worst - growth arrest** |

**Mnemonic**: **SALTER** = Straight across / Above metaphysis / Lower epiphysis / Through Everything / Rammed

### Fracture Healing

#### Primary Bone Healing
- Cortex-to-cortex contact with rigid fixation (plate/screw)
- No visible callus
- **Contact healing** (direct osteonal remodeling) and **gap healing** (woven bone fills gap)

#### Secondary Bone Healing (Indirect - most common)
**Stages:**

| Stage | Timing | Events |
|-------|--------|--------|
| **Reactive/Hematoma** | Days 1-7 | Fracture hematoma, inflammatory response |
| **Soft callus** | Weeks 1-6 | Fibrocartilaginous callus (visible X-ray ~3 weeks) |
| **Hard callus** | Weeks 6-12 | Mineralization → woven bone |
| **Remodeling** | Months-years | Woven → lamellar bone; Wolff's law |

**Signs of union**: Painless weight bearing, cortical bridging, no movement at fracture site

### Fracture Complications (PYQ HOTSPOT)

#### Compartment Syndrome - 6 P's
1. **Pain** (out of proportion, on passive stretch - most sensitive early sign)
2. **Pressure** (tense compartment)
3. **Paresthesia**
4. **Paralysis** (late)
5. **Pallor**
6. **Pulselessness** (very late)

- **Compartment pressure**: Normal <10 mmHg; fasciotomy if >30 mmHg or within 30 mmHg of diastolic
- **Fasciotomy**: Emergency surgical decompression; all 4 compartments of leg
- **Most common site**: Leg (anterior compartment most vulnerable)

#### Volkmann's Ischemic Contracture
- Sequela of untreated compartment syndrome (usually forearm)
- **Pronation + wrist flexion + finger flexion** deformity
- Fibrosis replaces ischemic muscles
- **Prevents**: Early fasciotomy
- **Treatment**: Mild - physiotherapy; Severe - **muscle slide/Z-plasty/free muscle transfer**

#### Fat Embolism Syndrome (FES)
- **Classic triad**: Petechiae (skin, conjunctiva) + hypoxemia + neurological changes
- **Timing**: 24-72 hours post long bone/pelvic fracture
- **Gurd's criteria (major)**: Respiratory insufficiency, cerebral involvement, petechial rash
- **Treatment**: Supportive (O2); prophylactic early fixation of long bone fractures

#### Avascular Necrosis (AVN)
- **Common sites**: Femoral head (neck of femur fracture), scaphoid (proximal pole), talus body, humeral head
- **MRI**: Most sensitive early investigation
- **X-ray**: Crescent sign (subchondral collapse), sclerosis, flattening (late)
- **Ficat and Arlet staging** for femoral head AVN

#### Nonunion vs Delayed Union

| Feature | Delayed Union | Nonunion |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| Definition | Healing slower than expected | No healing at >twice expected time |
| X-ray | Callus present | No bridging callus, sclerotic ends |
| Treatment | Optimize nutrition/fixation | Surgery (bone grafting, exchange nailing) |

**Causes of nonunion**: Infection, poor blood supply, gap, motion, soft tissue interposition, systemic disease, smoking

## UPPER LIMB FRACTURES

### Clavicle Fracture
- **Most common fracture** in humans (most common site = **middle third**)
- **Mechanism**: Fall on outstretched hand or direct blow
- **Treatment**: Majority conservative (collar and cuff sling 6 weeks)
- **Surgical**: Displaced, shortening >20mm, neurovascular injury, open fractures
- **NV injury**: Brachial plexus, subclavian vessels

### Proximal Humerus Fracture - Neer Classification (PYQ HOTSPOT)
Based on **4 segments**: Head, greater tuberosity, lesser tuberosity, shaft
- **1-part**: Any displacement if not displaced/angulated; conservative
- **2-part**: One segment displaced; usually surgical for tuberosity
- **3-part**: Two segments; ORIF
- **4-part**: All 4 segments; **highest AVN risk**; hemi/total shoulder arthroplasty

### Humeral Shaft Fracture
- **Radial nerve injury** (spiral groove): **Wrist drop** (inability to extend wrist), diminished sensation first web space
- Also: High radial nerve = triceps weakness + wrist drop
- **Holstein-Lewis fracture**: Distal shaft spiral fracture - higher risk of **radial nerve** entrapment
- **Management**: Conservative (U-slab, hanging cast, functional brace); surgery for bilateral, floating elbow, open, failed conservative

### Supracondylar Fracture (PYQ HOTSPOT)
- **Most common elbow fracture in children** (5-10 years)
- **Type**: Extension type (95%) - posteriorly displaced; Gartland I-III
- **Nerve injury**: **Anterior interosseous nerve (AIN)** branch of median nerve → loss of pinch (flexor pollicis longus + FDP to index)
- **Vascular injury**: **Brachial artery** → check radial pulse; pulseless + pink hand = reduce first; pulseless + white = explore vessels
- **Cubitus varus** (gunstock deformity): Malunion complication
- **Volkmann's contracture**: Compartment syndrome complication
- **Treatment**: Gartland I = cast; Gartland II/III = closed reduction + percutaneous K-wires; open if neurovascular compromise

### Lateral Condyle Fracture
- **2nd most common elbow fracture in children**
- **Milch Classification** based on fracture line relative to capitellar ossification center
- **Milch II**: Fracture through lateral trochlear ridge; elbow unstable; **needs surgery**
- **Complications**: Cubitus valgus → **tardy ulnar nerve palsy** (delayed); AVN; nonunion; fishtail deformity

### Medial Epicondyle Fracture
- Associated with elbow dislocation (50%)
- **Ulnar nerve** at risk
- **Incarceration**: Fragment pulled into joint; indication for surgery

### Distal Radius Fractures (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Fracture | Deformity | Mechanism | Key Feature |
|---------|----------|-----------|------------|
| **Colles** | **Dinner fork** (dorsal displacement) | FOOSH (extension) | Most common; post-menopausal women |
| **Smith's** | **Garden spade/reverse dinner fork** (volar displacement) | Fall on flexed wrist | Less common |
| **Barton** | Intra-articular rim fracture + subluxation | Shearing force | Needs ORIF |

- **Colles features**: Dorsal displacement, radial shortening, radial tilt, dinner fork deformity; **Dinner fork = Colles**

### Scaphoid Fracture (PYQ HOTSPOT)
- **Most common carpal bone fracture**
- **Anatomical snuffbox tenderness** = scaphoid fracture until proven otherwise
- **Blood supply**: Distal to proximal (retrograde) → **proximal pole AVN risk** with waist/proximal fractures
- **X-ray often negative initially** (up to 20%); repeat at 10-14 days or MRI/bone scan
- **Most sensitive investigation**: **MRI** (bone scan also sensitive but less specific)
- **Treatment**: Non-displaced = cast; Displaced/proximal = ORIF; Nonunion = bone graft ± Herbert screw

## LOWER LIMB FRACTURES

### Neck of Femur Fracture - Garden Classification (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Grade | Description | Displacement | Management |
|-------|------------|-------------|-----------|
| **I** | Incomplete, impacted (valgus) | None | Internal fixation possible |
| **II** | Complete, undisplaced | None | Internal fixation |
| **III** | Complete, partial displacement | Partial | Hemi/total arthroplasty in elderly |
| **IV** | Complete, full displacement | Full | Hemi/total arthroplasty in elderly |

- **Garden I & II**: Undisplaced → **Internal fixation** (cannulated screws)
- **Garden III & IV**: Displaced → **Hemiarthroplasty** (elderly >65) or Total Hip Replacement; in young patients attempt fixation
- **Blood supply to femoral head**: Medial circumflex femoral artery (main); ascending cervical branches; **AVN risk highest in Garden IV**
- **Subcapital > transcervical > basicervical** for AVN risk

### Intertrochanteric vs Subtrochanteric

| Feature | Intertrochanteric | Subtrochanteric |
|---------|-----------------|----------------|
| Location | Between greater and lesser trochanter | Below lesser trochanter (5cm zone) |
| Blood supply | Well vascularized | Well vascularized |
| AVN risk | **Minimal** | Minimal |
| Treatment | **Dynamic Hip Screw (DHS)** | Intramedullary nail (PFNA/cephalomedullary nail) |
| Evans classification | Yes | Russell-Taylor |

### Femoral Shaft Fracture
- **Blood loss**: Approximately **1-1.5 liters** (thigh can accommodate) - risk of hypovolemic shock
- **Thomas splint**: Traction splint for temporary stabilization/transport
- **Definitive treatment**: **Intramedullary nail** (gold standard)
- **Children <6 years**: Spica cast; older children: elastic nails (TENS)

### Tibia Fracture
- **Most common long bone fracture** (especially tibial shaft)
- **Compartment syndrome**: Most common long bone complication; **anterior compartment** most vulnerable
- **Talar fractures**: Hawkins sign (subchondral resorption at dome of talus on X-ray 6-8 weeks) = **good sign** (AVN unlikely)

### Calcaneum Fracture (PYQ HOTSPOT)
- **Fall from height** mechanism; bilateral in 10%
- **Bohler's tuber-joint angle**: Normally **25-40°**; **decreased in calcaneal fracture** (flattened)
- **Associated injuries**: Vertebral fractures (axial load), contralateral calcaneum, distal radius
- **Diagnosis**: CT scan (Sanders classification)
- **Treatment**: Displaced intra-articular → ORIF; extra-articular → conservative

### Foot Injuries
- **Lisfranc injury**: Tarsometatarsal junction; subtle on X-ray (loss of alignment); missed frequently
- **Jones fracture**: 5th metatarsal base; avulsion vs Jones (tuberosity vs 1.5cm distal); Jones has poor healing (watershed zone)
- **Stress fracture of 2nd MT (March fracture)**: Military recruits, runners

## DISLOCATIONS

### Shoulder Dislocation (PYQ HOTSPOT)
- **Anterior dislocation**: Most common (95-98%)
  - **Mechanism**: Abduction + external rotation
  - **Nerve at risk**: **Axillary nerve** (deltoid weakness + patch of anesthesia over lateral deltoid)
  - **Hill-Sachs lesion**: Posterolateral humeral head impression fracture
  - **Bankart lesion**: Anterior labrum + capsule avulsion from glenoid
  - **Treatment**: Closed reduction (Hippocratic, Kocher, Stimson, Cunningham)
- **Posterior dislocation**: Associated with seizures, electrocution; **lightbulb sign** on AP X-ray
- **Inferior dislocation (Luxatio erecta)**: Arm fixed overhead; most traction injury; **axillary nerve + brachial plexus at risk**

### Hip Dislocation (PYQ HOTSPOT)
- **Posterior dislocation**: **Most common (90%)**
  - **Mechanism**: Dashboard injury (flexed adducted hip - knee hits dashboard)
  - **Position**: Hip flexed, adducted, internally rotated, shortened
  - **Nerve at risk**: **Sciatic nerve** (10-20% incidence)
  - **AVN risk**: Higher with delayed reduction; **reduce within 6 hours**
  - **Thompson-Epstein classification**
- **Anterior dislocation**: Hip extended, abducted, externally rotated; femoral artery/nerve at risk

### Patella Dislocation
- **Lateral dislocation** (most common)
- **Mechanism**: Twisting on extended knee
- **Predisposing**: Genu valgum, trochlear dysplasia, patella alta, weak VMO
- **Medial retinaculum tear** (stabilizing structure)
- **Treatment**: Closed reduction + immobilization; recurrent = medial patellofemoral ligament (MPFL) reconstruction

## SPINE

### Disc Prolapse (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Level | Nerve Root | Motor Deficit | Sensory | Reflex |
|-------|-----------|--------------|---------|--------|
| **L3-L4** | L4 | Knee extension weakness | Medial lower leg | **Knee jerk ↓** |
| **L4-L5** | L5 | **EHL (extensor hallucis longus) weakness** | Dorsum of foot, first web space | None |
| **L5-S1** | S1 | **Plantar flexion weakness** (gastrocnemius) | Lateral foot, sole | **Ankle jerk ↓** |

- **Most common level**: **L4-L5** (followed by L5-S1)
- **Most common nerve root affected**: L5
- **SLR (Straight Leg Raise)**: Positive if pain radiates below knee at <60° (sciatic nerve stretch)
- **Crossed SLR**: Raises opposite leg → pain on affected side = central/large disc herniation; **more specific**
- **Cauda equina syndrome**: Bilateral leg weakness + saddle anesthesia + bladder/bowel dysfunction → **emergency surgery**

### Cervical Disc Prolapse
- **Spurling test**: Axial compression with neck laterally flexed/rotated toward affected side → radicular pain
- **C5-C6**: C6 radiculopathy (biceps, brachioradialis reflex)
- **C6-C7**: **C7 most common**; triceps weakness, middle finger sensory loss

### Spondylolisthesis
**Meyerding Grading** (% slip):
- Grade I: 0-25%; Grade II: 25-50%; Grade III: 50-75%; Grade IV: 75-100%; Grade V: >100% (spondyloptosis)
- **Most common level**: L5-S1
- **Most common cause**: Isthmic (spondylolysis at pars interarticularis)
- **Scottie dog on oblique X-ray**: Collar = pars defect (spondylolysis)

### Scoliosis
- **Structural**: Does not correct on lateral bending; has rotation
- **Non-structural (functional)**: Corrects; no rotation; postural/antalgic
- **Cobb's Angle**: Angle between end vertebrae perpendicular lines
  - 10-20°: Observe
  - 20-40°: Bracing (Milwaukee/Boston)
  - **>40°**: **Surgical correction** (Harrington rod/pedicle screw system)
- **AIS (Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis)**: Most common type; right thoracic curve common

### Spinal Cord Injury (PYQ HOTSPOT)

**ASIA Classification:**
- **A**: Complete - no motor/sensory below injury level
- **B**: Incomplete - sensory preserved only
- **C**: Motor preserved, most key muscles <3
- **D**: Motor preserved, most key muscles ≥3
- **E**: Normal motor and sensory

**Spinal Shock**: Loss of all reflex activity below injury level (flaccid paralysis); resolves when bulbocavernosus reflex returns (24-48h)

**Neurogenic Shock**: Hypotension + bradycardia (T6 and above); loss of sympathetic tone; **treatment: vasopressors + fluids (avoid excessive fluids)**

**Incomplete Cord Syndromes:**

| Syndrome | Affected | Spared | Mechanism | Features |
|---------|---------|--------|-----------|---------|
| **Central cord** | Most common; central part | Peripheral tracts | Hyperextension in elderly, spondylosis | Arms > legs weakness; bladder dysfunction |
| **Anterior cord** | Corticospinal + spinothalamic | **Dorsal columns** | Flexion, vascular | Motor loss + pain/temp loss; **proprioception/vibration preserved** |
| **Brown-Séquard** | Ipsilateral hemicord | Contralateral | Stabbing injury | Ipsi motor + proprioception loss; Contra pain/temp loss |
| **Posterior cord** | Dorsal columns | Motor + spinothalamic | Rare | Loss of proprioception/vibration only |

## METABOLIC BONE DISEASE

### Osteoporosis (PYQ HOTSPOT)
- **DEXA scan T-score**:
  - Normal: ≥-1.0
  - **Osteopenia**: -1.0 to -2.5
  - **Osteoporosis**: ≤-2.5
  - Severe osteoporosis: ≤-2.5 + fragility fracture
- **Z-score**: Compared to age-matched controls
- **Fragility fractures**: Vertebral body, hip (neck of femur), distal radius, proximal humerus
- **Treatment**: Bisphosphonates (alendronate), calcium + Vit D, denosumab, teriparatide (severe)
- **Bisphosphonate side effects**: Esophagitis (take with full glass of water, remain upright), osteonecrosis of jaw

### Rickets (Children) and Osteomalacia (Adults)
- **Rickets**: Defective mineralization of **cartilage** and **osteoid** at growth plate
- **Clinical features of rickets**:
  - **Genu varum** (bow legs) most common
  - **Harrison's sulcus**: Horizontal groove along lower chest margin (diaphragm pull)
  - **Rachitic rosary**: Swelling at costochondral junctions
  - Widened metaphyses, cupped/frayed epiphyses on X-ray
  - Delayed fontanelle closure, craniotabes
- **Osteomalacia**: Adults; **Looser's zones (pseudofractures)**: Bands of radiolucency perpendicular to cortex (medial femoral neck, pubic rami, ribs)
- **Biochemistry**: ↓Ca, ↓Phosphate, ↑ALP, ↑PTH, ↓25(OH)Vit D

### Paget's Disease (Osteitis Deformans) (PYQ HOTSPOT)
- **Abnormal osteoclast activity** followed by reactive osteoblast activity → disorganized bone
- **Clinical features**:
  - Increased hat size (skull involvement)
  - Deafness (petrous involvement)
  - Bowing of legs (tibia, femur)
  - Warm skin over affected bone
  - Pain, fractures
  - **Sarcomatous transformation** (1%): Osteosarcoma risk (**most common complication to know**)
- **Biochemistry**: **Markedly elevated ALP** (osteoblastic); normal Ca and phosphate; ↑urinary hydroxyproline
- **X-ray features**:
  - **V-shaped lytic lesion** (leading edge - blade of grass sign in tibia)
  - **Cotton-wool skull** (sclerotic skull)
  - Osteoporosis circumscripta (lytic phase skull)
  - Enlarged, deformed bones with coarsened trabeculae
- **Treatment**: **Bisphosphonates** (alendronate/zoledronate) or calcitonin

## ORTHOPEDIC INFECTIONS

### Osteomyelitis (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Type | Age | Route | Organism | Site |
|-----|-----|-------|---------|-----|
| **Hematogenous** | Children | Bacteremia | **S. aureus most common** | **Metaphysis** (rich, sluggish blood flow) |
| Direct inoculation | Any | Trauma/surgery | Polymicrobial | Cortex |
| Contiguous spread | Any | Adjacent infection | Varies | - |

**Special organisms:**
- **Sickle cell disease**: Salmonella (equally common as Staph); Salmonella > Staph in some references
- **Neonates**: S. aureus, Group B Strep, gram negatives
- **Immunocompromised**: Fungi, gram negatives

**Radiological Progression:**
- Days 3-7: Deep soft tissue swelling, obliteration of fat planes
- Day 10-21: **Cortical destruction**, periosteal reaction
- Chronic: Sequestrum (dead bone), involucrum (new bone around), Cloaca (opening for pus)
- **Codman's triangle**: Periosteal reaction lifting (seen in both osteomyelitis and aggressive tumor)
- **Brodie's abscess**: Chronic form - walled-off cavity in metaphysis; **target sign/bull's eye on MRI**

**Treatment**: IV antibiotics (anti-staphylococcal) ± surgical debridement

### Septic Arthritis (PYQ HOTSPOT)
- **Emergency** - cartilage destroyed within hours (proteolytic enzymes)
- **Most common organism**: **S. aureus** (any age)
- **Sexually active young adults**: **N. gonorrhoeae** most common overall cause in this group
- **Children**: S. aureus, Streptococcus, H. influenzae
- **Synovial fluid**: WBC **>50,000/mm³** (PMN predominance), glucose <40 mg/dL, positive Gram stain/culture
- **Treatment**: **Emergency joint aspiration/lavage** + IV antibiotics; hip joint → open washout/arthrotomy (hip cannot be accessed well arthroscopically in children)

## BONE TUMORS

### Benign Tumors (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Tumor | Age | Site | Features | Treatment |
|-------|-----|-----|---------|---------|
| **Osteochondroma** | **Most common benign bone tumor** | Metaphysis (distal femur/proximal tibia) | Cortical + medullary continuity; hereditary multiple exostoses | Excision if symptomatic |
| **Enchondroma** | 20-40 | Short tubular bones (phalanges) | Ollier's disease (multiple); Maffucci's (+ soft tissue hemangiomas); malignant transformation risk | Conservative or curettage |
| **GCT (Giant Cell Tumor)** | 20-40 years | **Epiphysis** (distal femur, proximal tibia, distal radius) | **Soap bubble appearance**; locally aggressive (Grade III); **extends to subarticular bone** | Extended curettage + bone cement/graft; Denosumab for unresectable |
| **Osteoid osteoma** | 10-35 | Femoral neck, tibia (cortex) | **Nocturnal pain relieved by aspirin**; <2cm nidus; intense sclerosis | RFA or surgical excision |
| **Osteoblastoma** | Similar to OO | Posterior vertebrae, long bones | Similar to OO but >2cm; not aspirin responsive | Surgical |
| **ABC (Aneurysmal Bone Cyst)** | <20 | Metaphysis | Fluid-fluid levels on MRI; **eccentric expansile** | Curettage/embolization |

### Malignant Tumors (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Tumor | Age | Site | X-ray Features | Histology | Treatment |
|-------|-----|-----|--------------|---------|---------|
| **Osteosarcoma** | **10-25 years (2nd most common primary in adults: 40-60)** | **Distal femur > proximal tibia** | **Codman's triangle, sunburst pattern** | Osteoid produced by tumor cells | Neoadjuvant chemo + wide resection + adjuvant chemo |
| **Ewing's Sarcoma** | **5-20 years** | Diaphysis (femur, tibia, fibula) | **Onion peel periosteal reaction** | **Small round blue cells**; **t(11;22) translocation (EWSR1-FLI1)** | Chemo + RT ± surgery |
| **Chondrosarcoma** | >40 years | Pelvis, proximal femur | Rings and arcs calcification | Grade I-III (low to high grade) | Wide surgical resection (chemo-resistant) |
| **Multiple Myeloma** | >50 years | Skull, vertebrae, ribs, pelvis | **Punched-out (lytic) lesions**; raindrop skull; osteoporosis | Plasma cell tumor; monoclonal protein | Chemotherapy + bone protection (bisphosphonates) |

**CRAB criteria for Myeloma**: **C**alcium ↑, **R**enal failure, **A**nemia, **B**one lesions

## CONGENITAL CONDITIONS

### CTEV (Clubfoot - Congenital Talipes Equinovarus) (PYQ HOTSPOT)

**Components: CAVE mnemonic**
- **C** - **C**avus (high arch)
- **A** - Fore-foot **A**dductus (metatarsus adductus)
- **V** - Hind-foot **V**arus (inverted heel)
- **E** - Hind-foot **E**quinus (plantarflexion, tight Achilles)

- **Treatment (Ponseti method)**: Serial casting → Achilles tenotomy → foot abduction orthosis (Dennis-Brown splint); **most accepted method**
- **Surgical**: Posteromedial release if Ponseti fails (Cincinnati/McLay incision)
- **True CTEV**: All 4 components; resistant to passive correction
- **Positional/postural CTEV**: Fully correctable; no true structural deformity

### DDH (Developmental Dysplasia of Hip) (PYQ HOTSPOT)
**Risk factors**: Female, first-born, breech, family history, oligohydramnios

**Clinical Tests:**
- **Barlow test**: Provocation test - hip can be dislocated posteriorly (hip is located but dislocatable)
- **Ortolani test**: Reduction test - dislocated hip can be reduced (clunk on abduction)
- Both positive only in neonatal period
- **Galeazzi/Allis sign**: Unequal knee heights (in infants with hip flexed 90°)
- **Trendelenburg sign**: Pelvis drops on unaffected side (abductor weakness)

**Investigations by age:**
- <6 months: **Ultrasound** (Graf classification; hip not yet ossified)
- >6 months: **X-ray** (ossification center appears)

**Treatment:**
- 0-6 months: **Pavlik harness** (most widely used; success >90%)
- 6-18 months: Closed/open reduction + spica cast
- >18 months: Open reduction + femoral/pelvic osteotomy (Salter, Pemberton)

### Legg-Calve-Perthes Disease (PYQ HOTSPOT)
- **AVN of femoral head epiphysis in children**
- **Age**: **4-8 years** (2-12 years); male predominance (M:F = 5:1)
- **Presentation**: Painless limp, groin/thigh/knee pain, restricted abduction/internal rotation
- **Catterall Classification**: Based on extent of head involvement (I-IV)
- **Herring (lateral pillar) classification**: Prognosis based on lateral column height
- **X-ray stages**: 
  1. Sclerosis (avascular phase)
  2. Fragmentation (resorption phase)
  3. Re-ossification
  4. Remodeling/healed
- **Crescent sign**: Subchondral fracture (indicates collapse)
- **Treatment**: Containment (keep femoral head within acetabulum); Herring A/B with age <8 = conservative; Herring C = surgical

## PERIPHERAL NERVE INJURIES

### Upper Limb Nerve Injuries (PYQ HOTSPOT)

| Nerve | Level of Injury | Deformity | Motor Loss | Sensory Loss |
|-------|----------------|----------|-----------|-------------|
| **Radial** (spiral groove) | Humeral shaft | **Wrist drop** | Wrist/finger extensors intact triceps | 1st web space dorsum |
| **Radial** (elbow/PIN) | Lateral epicondyle | **Finger drop** (wrist intact) | Finger extensors (no wrist drop) | Minimal |
| **Ulnar** (medial epicondyle) | Medial epicondyle | **Claw hand** (ring + little) | Hypothenar, interossei, medial 2 lumbricals | Medial 1.5 fingers |
| **Median** (wrist/CTD) | Carpal tunnel | **Ape hand** (thenar wasting) | LOAF: Lateral 2 lumbricals, Opponens pollicis, Abductor pollicis brevis, Flexor pollicis brevis | Lateral 3.5 fingers |
| **Median** (AIN) | Forearm | **Pinch sign** (no OK sign) | FPL + FDP (index/middle) | None |
| **Median** (elbow/above) | Supracondylar fx | **Hand of benediction** (on attempted fist) | All median muscles + FDP index/middle | Lateral 3.5 fingers |

**Claw hand in ulnar nerve**: Ring + little fingers (intrinsics = lumbricals 3,4 + interossei); **worse at wrist** (Paradox of ulnar claw)

**Ulnar Paradox**: More proximal lesion (elbow) = less claw (FDP preserved); more distal (wrist) = more claw (FDP intact, no intrinsic correction)

**Carpal Tunnel Syndrome:**
- **Phalen's test**: Flexed wrist for 1 min → paraesthesia
- **Tinel's sign**: Percussion over carpal tunnel → tingling (less sensitive)
- **NCS/EMG**: Confirmatory; reduced motor/sensory conduction velocity
- **Treatment**: Splinting, steroid injection; **carpal tunnel release** (surgical)

### Lower Limb Nerve Injuries

| Nerve | Level | Deformity | Motor Loss |
|-------|-------|---------|-----------|
| **Common peroneal** | Fibular neck | **Foot drop** (high stepping gait) | Dorsiflexion + eversion |
| **Sciatic** | Hip dislocation/gluteal | Foot drop + loss of knee flexion | Hamstrings + all below knee |
| **Femoral** | Pelvic fracture/femoral triangle | Weakness of knee extension | Quadriceps |
| **Obturator** | Pelvic fracture | Weakness of adduction | Adductors |

**Common peroneal nerve injury at fibular neck**: Most common nerve injured in lower limb; **fibular neck fracture** most common cause

## KEY SCORING SYSTEMS AND CLASSIFICATIONS

| System | Use | Details |
|--------|-----|---------|
| **Gustilo-Anderson** | Open fracture | I (<1cm clean), II (1-10cm), IIIA (>10cm adequate soft tissue), IIIB (bone exposure, periosteal stripping), IIIC (vascular injury) |
| **Garden** | NOF fracture | I-IV (undisplaced → completely displaced) |
| **Salter-Harris** | Physeal fractures | I-V (transphyseal → crush) |
| **Neer** | Proximal humerus | 1-4 part based on displacement |
