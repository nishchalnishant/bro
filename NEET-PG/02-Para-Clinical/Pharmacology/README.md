# Pharmacology

Pharmacology covers the mechanisms, clinical uses, adverse effects, and pharmacokinetics of drugs across all body systems — contributing ~16 questions per NEET PG paper, making it one of the highest-weighted para-clinical subjects.

## High-Yield Areas

- Autonomic pharmacology: adrenergic/cholinergic agonists and antagonists, receptor selectivity, and clinical applications
- Antimicrobials: mechanism of action, spectrum, resistance mechanisms, and selection for specific infections
- CNS pharmacology: antiepileptic drug choices by seizure type, antipsychotic side effects, antidepressant classes
- Adverse effects and drug interactions: aminoglycoside toxicity, valproate in pregnancy, QT prolongation drugs
- Pharmacokinetics: zero-order vs first-order kinetics, volume of distribution, half-life, protein binding, and bioavailability
