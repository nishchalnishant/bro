# Pharmacology — NEET PG
**NEET PG Weight:** 16–17 questions (~8%)
**Tier:** 1

## Files in This Folder
- `pharmacology-lecture.md` — First-principles lecture notes for deep understanding (read in Month 3–4)
- `pharmacology-notes.md` — Condensed exam-focused notes for revision (use from Month 9 onwards)

## Top NEET PG Topics
1. Autonomic pharmacology — adrenergic and cholinergic agonists/antagonists, clinical uses, reversal agents
2. Antimicrobials — mechanism of action, spectrum, resistance mechanisms, clinical indications
3. CNS pharmacology — antiepileptics, antipsychotics, antidepressants, anxiolytics (adverse effects drive Qs)

## Study Tips
- For every drug: mechanism → adverse effect → contraindication — that is the standard question template.
- Drug interactions and adverse effects (e.g., aminoglycoside nephrotoxicity, valproate in pregnancy) are extremely high-yield.
- Pharmacokinetics (zero-order vs first-order, half-life, volume of distribution) is tested in general pharmacology Qs every year.
