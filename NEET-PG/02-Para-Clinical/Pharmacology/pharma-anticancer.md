## 7. ANTICANCER DRUGS

### Alkylating Agents

| Drug | Key SE | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Cyclophosphamide** | **Hemorrhagic cystitis** (acrolein metabolite — prevent with MESNA and hydration), myelosuppression, alopecia, SIADH | DOC for SLE nephritis, RA, NHL, breast cancer |
| **Ifosfamide** | **Hemorrhagic cystitis** (worse than cyclophosphamide), encephalopathy, nephrotoxicity | Always give MESNA |
| **Busulfan** | **Pulmonary fibrosis**, hyperpigmentation, Addison-like syndrome | Conditioning for BMT in CML |
| **Chlorambucil** | Myelosuppression, infertility | CLL |
| **Carmustine/Lomustine (nitrosoureas)** | **Delayed myelosuppression** (6 weeks!), pulmonary fibrosis | CNS tumors (BBB penetration) |
| **Cisplatin** | **Nephrotoxicity** (most common dose-limiting, prevent with hydration + amifostine + mannitol), **ototoxicity** (high-frequency hearing loss), peripheral neuropathy, N/V, hypomagnesemia | Testicular, ovarian, lung, bladder cancers |
| **Carboplatin** | **Myelosuppression** (unlike cisplatin — less nephro/neuro/ototoxicity) | Better tolerated, same cancers |
| **Oxaliplatin** | **Peripheral neuropathy** (cold-induced, acute; chronic cumulative) | Colorectal cancer |

### Antimetabolites

| Drug | Target | Mechanism | Key SE |
|------|--------|-----------|--------|
| **Methotrexate (MTX)** | **DHFR** (dihydrofolate reductase) | ↓ tetrahydrofolate → ↓ thymidylate synthesis → ↓ DNA synthesis | Myelosuppression, **mucositis**, hepatotoxicity (cirrhosis with chronic use), **pulmonary toxicity** (pneumonitis), nephrotoxicity (high dose); **RESCUE: Leucovorin (folinic acid)** |
| **5-Fluorouracil (5-FU)** | Thymidylate synthase | ↓ dTMP → ↓ DNA synthesis | Myelosuppression, mucositis, **hand-foot syndrome** (palmar-plantar erythrodysesthesia), diarrhea, **cerebellar ataxia** |
| **Capecitabine** | Same as 5-FU (oral prodrug) | Same | More hand-foot syndrome |
| **6-Mercaptopurine (6-MP)** | Purine synthesis | ↓ purine synthesis | Myelosuppression, hepatotoxicity; **allopurinol inhibits xanthine oxidase → ↓ 6-MP metabolism → TOXIC INTERACTION (reduce 6-MP dose to 25%)** |
| **Cytarabine (Ara-C)** | DNA polymerase | Chain termination | **Cerebellar toxicity** (high dose), myelosuppression, **Ara-C syndrome** (fever, rash) |
| **Gemcitabine** | DNA polymerase | Chain termination | Myelosuppression, pulmonary toxicity, **hemolytic uremic syndrome** |
| **Hydroxyurea** | Ribonucleotide reductase | ↓ DNA synthesis | Myelosuppression; USED for sickle cell (↑ HbF) |
| **Pemetrexed** | Multiple folate enzymes | ↓ purine + thymidylate synthesis | Myelosuppression, mucositis; **supplement folic acid + B12** |

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Leucovorin (folinic acid) rescues from MTX toxicity — given 24 hrs after MTX; 6-MP + allopurinol = toxicity (reduce 6-MP dose to 25%)

### Vinca Alkaloids and Taxanes

| Drug | Mechanism | Key SE |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Vincristine** | Binds tubulin → inhibit microtubule polymerization (mitotic spindle arrest) | **Peripheral neuropathy** (dose-limiting), minimal myelosuppression, constipation, SIADH, alopecia |
| **Vinblastine** | Same | **Myelosuppression** (dose-limiting, unlike vincristine), peripheral neuropathy less |
| **Vinorelbine** | Same | Myelosuppression, peripheral neuropathy |
| **Paclitaxel (Taxol)** | Stabilizes polymerized microtubules → prevents depolymerization | **Myelosuppression**, **peripheral neuropathy**, hypersensitivity (vehicle Cremophor EL), alopecia, bradycardia |
| **Docetaxel** | Same as paclitaxel | **Fluid retention** (edema, pleural effusion), myelosuppression, hypersensitivity |
| **Cabazitaxel** | Same | Myelosuppression; used in prostate cancer (docetaxel-resistant) |

### Topoisomerase Inhibitors

| Drug | Target | Key SE |
|------|--------|--------|
| **Doxorubicin (Adriamycin)** | Topoisomerase II + free radical generation | **Dilated cardiomyopathy** (cumulative dose-dependent, irreversible — limit total dose to 550 mg/m²; dexrazoxane (iron chelator) for cardioprotection), myelosuppression, alopecia, mucositis, **red urine** |
| **Epirubicin** | Same | Less cardiotoxicity than doxorubicin |
| **Idarubicin, Daunorubicin** | Same | Cardiotoxicity |
| **Etoposide (VP-16)** | Topoisomerase II | Myelosuppression, **secondary AML/leukemia**, alopecia, hypotension (rapid infusion) |
| **Irinotecan (CPT-11)** | Topoisomerase I | **Diarrhea** (early: cholinergic — treat with atropine; late: secretory — treat with loperamide), myelosuppression |
| **Topotecan** | Topoisomerase I | Myelosuppression |

### Other Important Anticancer Drugs

| Drug | Mechanism | Key SE |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Bleomycin** | DNA strand breaks (free radicals) | **Pulmonary fibrosis/toxicity** (most serious, cumulative dose), minimal myelosuppression, **hypersensitivity**, cutaneous effects (Raynaud's, nail changes) |
| **L-Asparaginase** | Depletes asparagine (ALL blast cells cannot synthesize) | **Pancreatitis**, coagulopathy (↓ clotting factors), allergic reactions/anaphylaxis, hepatotoxicity |

> **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Bleomycin — pulmonary fibrosis (avoid in >70 years or pre-existing lung disease; avoid high O2 during surgery — worsens fibrosis); doxorubicin — dose-dependent cardiomyopathy; cisplatin — nephrotoxicity/ototoxicity
