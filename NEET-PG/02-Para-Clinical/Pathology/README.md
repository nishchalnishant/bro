# Pathology

Pathology covers the structural and functional abnormalities underlying disease — from general pathological processes to systemic organ pathology — and is the highest-weighted pre/para-clinical subject at ~18 questions per NEET PG paper.

## Files in This Folder

| File | Description |
|------|-------------|

| `pathology-lecture.md` | In-depth lecture notes covering mechanisms, histological features, and gross findings |
| `pathology-mcqs.md` | MCQ bank with high-yield questions and annotated explanations |
| `WEAK-TOPICS.md` | Personally flagged weak areas requiring extra revision attention |

## High-Yield Areas

- Haematology: anaemia classification (peripheral smear findings, MCV, indices), leukaemias, lymphomas, and myeloproliferative disorders
- Neoplasia: tumour markers, oncogene mechanisms, carcinogenesis, and grading vs staging principles
- Cell injury and inflammation: types of necrosis, granuloma-forming conditions, apoptosis mechanisms, and wound healing
- Systemic pathology: renal (nephrotic vs nephritic), hepatic (cirrhosis, hepatitis patterns), and pulmonary pathology
- Haemodynamics: thrombosis (Virchow's triad), embolism types, infarction patterns, and shock pathophysiology
