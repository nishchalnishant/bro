## FORENSIC PATHOLOGY

### Death - Classification

| Type | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Somatic (Clinical) Death** | Cessation of vital functions: heartbeat, respiration, brain function |
| **Cellular (Molecular) Death** | Individual cells/tissues die after somatic death (brain: 4-6 min; liver: 10-15 min; skin/muscle: 2-3 hrs) |
| **Suspended Animation** | Apparent death; vital signs barely detectable (drowning, electrocution, neonates in cold); resuscitation possible |
| **Brain Death** | Irreversible cessation of all brain functions including brainstem |

#### Brain Death Criteria (India - Transplantation of Human Organs Act 1994)
- **Permanent unresponsive coma**
- Absence of brainstem reflexes (pupillary, corneal, oculocephalic, gag, cough reflex)
- **Apnea test** (PCO₂ rises to >60 mmHg with no respiratory effort)
- Must be certified by **panel of 4 doctors**: treating doctor, neurologist/neurosurgeon, anesthetist + hospital in-charge
- **TWO confirmatory tests** with **6-hour interval**
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Brain death certification required for organ donation; certified under THOA 1994; EEG/confirmatory tests not mandatory in India if clinical criteria met

#### Lazarus Phenomenon
- **Spontaneous return of circulation** after unsuccessful resuscitation
- Occurs within minutes of stopping CPR
- Rare; documented in literature

### Postmortem Changes

#### Rigor Mortis (Death Stiffening)

| Stage | Timing |
|-------|--------|
| Onset | **2-6 hours** after death |
| Complete (full rigidity) | **12 hours** (all muscles stiff) |
| Passes off | **36-48 hours** (due to putrefaction) |

- **Cause**: ATP depletion → actin-myosin cross-linking → stiffness
- **Nysten's law**: Starts from **masseter → face → neck → trunk → upper limb → lower limb**; passes in same order
- **Cadaveric spasm (instantaneous rigor)**: Rigor appearing at moment of death without primary flaccidity; seen in violent struggle/extreme emotion; **drowning, epilepsy, gunshot**; cannot be reproduced artificially → proves death was instantaneous
- **Heat stiffening (instantaneous rigidity)**: Due to heat causing protein coagulation; cannot be broken (unlike rigor mortis which can)
- **Cold stiffening**: Due to cold freezing tissue fat; thaws on warming
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Cadaveric spasm ≠ rigor mortis; cadaveric spasm has medico-legal importance (grasped objects retained)

#### Livor Mortis (Hypostasis / Cadaveric Lividity)

| Stage | Timing | Features |
|-------|--------|---------|
| **Onset** | **1-2 hours** after death | Faint, early, bluish-pink |
| **Confluent** | **3-6 hours** | Patches join |
| **Fixed (non-shifting)** | **6-12 hours** | Cannot be moved by pressure/repositioning |
| Complete | **12 hours** | Full development |

- **Mechanism**: Blood pools in dependent parts due to gravity
- **Color**: Bluish-purple (usual); **Cherry-red in CO poisoning, cyanide, cold exposure**; **Brown in methemoglobin (nitrites, aniline)**
- **Absent** in: Severe anemia, extensive burns
- **Medicolegal importance**: Position of body after death; time since death; evidence of body being moved
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Cherry-red livor = CO poisoning (classic MCQ); Fixed after 6-12 hours

#### Decomposition (Putrefaction)
| Stage | Timing | Features |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Fresh | 0-12 hrs | Livor, rigor |
| Early decomposition | 1-3 days | **Greenish discoloration** (right iliac fossa - cecum) |
| Bloating | 3-7 days | Gas production; swelling |
| Active decay | 7-21 days | Skin slippage, liquefaction |

- **Bloating** due to bacterial gas (H₂S, CH₄, NH₃)
- **Skin slippage**: Separation of epidermis from dermis in water immersion
- **Marbling**: Branching pattern on skin due to hemolysis in superficial vessels

#### Mummification
- **Desiccation** of body with preservation (not decomposition)
- Conditions: **Dry, hot, low humidity environment** (desert; hot attics)
- Takes **3 months to develop**
- Body becomes leathery; wrinkled; brown; weight reduced
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Mummification = preservation; adipocere = saponification

#### Adipocere Formation
- Conversion of body fat to **fatty acids (saponification)** → soap-like substance
- Conditions: **Warm, humid, wet environment** (buried in moist soil, submerged in water)
- Takes **3-12 months**
- Grayish-white, rancid, greasy material
- **Preserves body features** (can allow later identification)
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Adipocere = saponification of fat; wet conditions; whitish greasy material

#### Maceration
- Seen in **stillborn fetus retained in utero**
- Soft tissue softening, skin peeling, reddish/brownish color
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Maceration = fetal maceration in utero; NOT same as decomposition in adults

### Estimation of Time Since Death

| Method | Key Points |
|--------|-----------|
| **Rigor mortis** | Onset 2-6h; complete 12h; resolves 36-48h |
| **Livor mortis** | Fixed 6-12h; cherry-red = CO |
| **Stomach contents** | Empty after 4-6 hours (normal gastric emptying) |
| **Vitreous humor K⁺** | **Best/most reliable method** for PMI estimation |
| **Ocular changes** | Corneal cloudiness: 2h (open eye), 6h (closed eye); tache noire (dark line on sclera if eye open) |
| **Algor mortis** | Body cools ~1°C per hour; rectal temperature |
| **Fly activity (entomology)** | Blowflies (Calliphora) appear within hours; forensic entomology |

- **Henssge nomogram**: Used for temperature-based time of death estimation
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: **Vitreous potassium = gold standard** for time since death estimation

### Autopsy

| Type | Purpose | Consent |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Medicolegal (Forensic)** | Legal purposes; criminal investigation | NOT required (ordered by magistrate/police) |
| **Clinical (Pathological)** | Academic/teaching; cause of death | **Consent required** from next of kin |

- **Coroner's autopsy**: For unusual/suspicious deaths
- **Retention of organs**: Requires separate consent
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Medicolegal autopsy does NOT require consent of relatives

## WOUNDS

### Classification and Features

| Feature | **Incised Wound** | **Lacerated Wound** | **Stab Wound** |
|---------|------------------|-------------------|----------------|
| Cause | Sharp-edged weapon (knife) | Blunt force | Pointed weapon |
| Margins | **Smooth, clean** | Ragged, irregular | Depends on weapon |
| Edges | Well-defined | **Irregular, bruised** | May be clean (sharp) or irregular (blunt tip) |
| Wound base | Clean, no tissue bridging | **Tissue bridges** present | Deep |
| Length vs depth | Length > depth | Variable | **Depth > length** |
| Surrounding bruising | Absent | **Present** | Absent |
| Hemorrhage | Severe (major vessels cut) | Less severe | Severe if organ/vessel involved |

- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Tissue bridges = laceration (not incised wound); most important distinguishing feature
- **Tailing/fishtail appearance**: In incised wounds; due to tangential nature of termination

### Contusions (Bruises)
- **Vital reaction**: Only occurs in living; hemorrhage, inflammation
- **Color changes** (timeline):
  - **Fresh (0-2 days)**: Red/bluish-red (oxyhemoglobin)
  - **2-5 days**: Blue/purple (deoxyhemoglobin)
  - **5-7 days**: **Green** (biliverdin)
  - **7-12 days**: **Yellow** (bilirubin, hemosiderin)
  - **>2 weeks**: Brown → disappears
- Mnemonic: **RBGY** = Red → Blue → Green → Yellow
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Green bruise = approximately 5-7 days old; Yellow = 7-12 days

#### Special Bruises
- **Tardieu's spots**: Petechiae on serous surfaces (pleura, pericardium) in asphyxia
- **Patterned bruises**: Reflect the weapon/object used (tram-line bruising from rod)
- **Intradermal bruise**: In dermis; harder to see; may become apparent later

### Defense Wounds
- Sustained while defending from attack
- Location: **Palm, dorsum of hand, forearm (extensor surface)**
- Types: incised wounds (grabbing blade), bruises, fractured forearm (parrying)

### Hesitation Cuts (Tentative Wounds)
- Multiple small, superficial, parallel incised wounds
- Seen in **suicidal cut throat, wrist cuts**
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Hesitation cuts = suicidal; deep, single cut = homicidal (usually)

### Firearm Wounds

#### Entry vs Exit Wound

| Feature | **Entry Wound** | **Exit Wound** |
|---------|----------------|---------------|
| Size | Smaller | **Larger** |
| Edges | Inverted (depressed) | **Everted** |
| Abrasion collar | **Present** | Absent |
| Burning/tattooing | Present (if close range) | Absent |
| Shape | Circular/oval | Irregular, stellate |
| Hemorrhage | Less externally | More externally |

- **Abrasion collar (graze/dirt collar)**: Present at entry; due to bullet spinning and wiping skin
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Everted exit wound; abrasion collar at entry

#### Range of Fire

| Range | Features |
|-------|---------|
| **Contact (hard contact)** | Stellate laceration; **muzzle imprint**; inverted entry; heavy blackening; extensive burning; **internal tattooing of bones** |
| **Close (<15 cm)** | Burning, blackening, tattooing/stippling all present |
| **Intermediate (15-60 cm)** | Tattooing present; blackening absent (soot washed by blast) |
| **Distant (>60 cm)** | Clean entry wound; **only abrasion collar**; no burning/tattooing |

- **Tattooing (stippling)**: Unburnt/partially burnt powder grains embedded in skin; **cannot be wiped off** (differentiates from blackening)
- **Blackening (fouling)**: Soot deposited on skin; CAN be wiped off

#### Rifled vs Smooth Bore

| Feature | Rifled (Revolver/Rifle) | Smooth Bore (Shotgun) |
|---------|------------------------|----------------------|
| Multiple pellets | No (single bullet) | **Yes** |
| Entry wound | Single | Single (close) or multiple (distant) |
| Tattooing pattern | Stellate/circular | Scattered |
| Wad | No | **Yes** (plastic/cardboard wad) |

- **GSR (Gunshot Residue)**: Lead, barium, antimony; detected by SEM-EDX; collected from hands within 4-6 hours

### Chop Wounds
- Produced by **heavy, sharp, edged weapons** (axe, sword)
- Features of BOTH incised (clean margins) and lacerated wounds
- Deep; may involve bone; bone shows **cutting marks**

## ASPHYXIA

### Definition
- **Condition due to interference with oxygenation of blood** resulting in hypoxia and hypercapnia
- Types: Mechanical, Chemical, Environmental, Pathological

### Types of Mechanical Asphyxia

#### Hanging
- Suspension of body by ligature around neck with **body weight as constricting force**
- **Judicial hanging**: Fracture-dislocation of C2-C3 (hangman's fracture); instantaneous death
- **Suicidal/homicidal hanging**: Asphyxia + cerebral anemia

#### Ligature Mark in Hanging vs Strangulation

| Feature | **Hanging** | **Strangulation** |
|---------|------------|------------------|
| Direction | **Oblique (upward)** toward point of suspension | **Horizontal** (transverse) |
| Location | Above thyroid cartilage | At/below thyroid cartilage |
| Width | Varies (width of ligature) | Width of ligature |
| Mark continuity | **NOT complete** (gap at suspension point) | **Complete** (all around neck) |
| Hyoid fracture | Less common | **Common** |
| Bruising around mark | Less | **More** |

- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Hanging ligature mark = oblique, incomplete; Strangulation = horizontal, complete

#### Throttling (Manual Strangulation)
- Compression by **hands**
- **Fingernail marks (crescentic abrasions)**
- Hyoid fracture common; thyroid cartilage fracture
- **Finger-tip bruises** on neck

#### Smothering
- Closure of nose and mouth (e.g., pillow, hand)
- Petechiae around mouth, lips, eyes
- Bite marks (if victim resists)

### Features of Asphyxial Death
1. **Cyanosis** (blue discoloration of lips, face)
2. **Congestion** (face, sclera)
3. **Petechial hemorrhages (Tardieu spots)**: In conjunctiva, pleura, pericardium
4. **Fluidity of blood** (↑ fibrinolysin; blood doesn't clot)
5. **Visceral congestion**
6. **Cerebral edema**

- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Petechiae in conjunctiva + asphyxia = classic MCQ

## DROWNING

### Types

| Type | Mechanism |
|------|----------|
| **Wet drowning (90%)** | Water enters lungs |
| **Dry drowning (10%)** | Laryngospasm; little/no water enters lungs |
| **Secondary (delayed) drowning** | Pulmonary edema hours after rescue |
| **Immersion syndrome** | Sudden death from cardiac arrhythmia due to cold water |

### Diagnosis of Drowning
- **Diatom test (Riddell's test)**: **Gold standard** for confirming drowning
  - Diatoms found in **bone marrow, brain, liver, kidney** (NOT lungs only) = drowning in diatom-rich water
  - Diatoms in bone marrow especially = **ante-mortem drowning** (had to be alive for diatoms to be transported systemically)
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Diatom test = best confirmatory test; bone marrow is best site
- **Paltauf's hemorrhage**: Patchy hemorrhages at lung bases; due to alveolar wall rupture in drowning
- **Cadaveric spasm in drowning**: Hand tightly grasping weeds/material = victim was alive on entering water (not post-mortem immersion)
- **Cutis anserina (goose flesh)**: Due to erector pili muscle contraction; not specific to drowning

## BURNS

### Degrees of Burns

| Degree | Depth | Features |
|--------|-------|---------|
| **1st degree (superficial)** | Epidermis only | Redness, pain; no blisters |
| **2nd degree (partial thickness)** | Epidermis + partial dermis | **Blisters**; very painful |
| **3rd degree (full thickness)** | All skin layers | Painless (nerve destruction); leathery; no blisters |
| **4th degree** | Into underlying tissue (fat, muscle, bone) | Charring; serious |

- **Rule of 9 (Wallace)**: Head+neck = 9%, each arm = 9%, chest = 9%, abdomen = 9%, each thigh = 9%, each leg = 9%, genitalia = 1%
  - **Children**: Use **Lund-Browder chart** (more accurate for children)
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Blisters = 2nd degree (NOT 1st degree)

### Vital vs Postmortem Burns

| Feature | **Vital (Ante-mortem)** | **Postmortem** |
|---------|------------------------|----------------|
| Blisters | Contain **protein-rich fluid** (>100 mg/dL protein) | Contain clear fluid or air |
| Redness around burn | **Present (vital reaction)** | Absent |
| Carbon monoxide in blood | **HbCO elevated** | Normal |
| Carbon in airway | **Present** (inhaled) | Absent |
| Vital reaction in histology | **Present** | Absent |

- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Carbon in airway + elevated HbCO = vital, alive during fire

### Pugilistic Attitude
- **Boxing posture** assumed by body in fire
- Due to **heat coagulation of muscles** (NOT a sign of defense)
- Arms flexed, legs flexed; resembles fighting stance
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Pugilistic attitude = heat rigor; NOT evidence of violence before death

## SEXUAL OFFENSES

### Rape (Section 375-376, IPC)
- **Legal definition**: Penetration of penis/object into vagina, urethra, anus, or mouth without consent; or with consent obtained by force/fraud
- Age of consent: **18 years** for women; consensual sex below 18 = statutory rape
- **IPC 376**: Punishment for rape (minimum 7 years; can be life imprisonment)

### Medical Examination of Rape Victim
- Examine with **female attendant present**; with **consent of victim**
- Document: injuries (bruises, bite marks, abrasions), hymen status, signs of sexual activity
- Collect: vaginal swab (sperm, semen), nail scrapings, pubic hair, clothing, blood for DNA
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Examination only with consent; if unconscious/minor, legal guardian consent

### Hymen
- **Intact hymen does NOT exclude rape** (elastic hymen may remain intact)
- **Types**: Annular, cribriform, septate, imperforate, fimbriated, crescentic
- **Virginity cannot be determined by hymen examination**
- Old tears: Notch with smooth edges; Old rape: healed lacerations with fibrosis

### Age Determination for Sexual Offenses
- **Age of consent = 18 years**
- Medical age determination: X-ray (ossification centers), dental examination, secondary sexual characteristics
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: No definitive medical method to determine exact age; can give range

## MEDICAL JURISPRUDENCE

### Consent in Medicine

| Type | Features |
|------|---------|
| **Informed consent** | Patient understands nature, risks, alternatives, consequences of refusing |
| **Implied consent** | From actions (extending arm for injection); no verbal/written consent |
| **Proxy consent** | Guardian for minors, mentally incompetent; legal guardian |
| **Emergency consent** | Can proceed without consent to save life |

- **Age of consent for medical treatment**: **18 years** in India
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: In emergency with imminent threat to life, treatment can proceed without consent

### Medical Negligence

| Type | Key Differences |
|------|----------------|
| **Criminal negligence** | Gross and reckless disregard for patient safety; punishable by IPC 304A |
| **Civil negligence** | Failure to exercise reasonable care; compensation/damages |
| **Contributory negligence** | Patient contributed to injury by own negligence |
| **Vicarious liability** | Employer responsible for employee's negligence |

- **Bolam Test**: Standard of care = standard of a reasonably competent doctor in that specialty
- **Res ipsa loquitur**: "The thing speaks for itself"; negligence obvious from facts (wrong-site surgery)
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: IPC 304A = causing death by negligence (2 year imprisonment)

### Important Legal Acts

| Act | Key Provisions |
|-----|---------------|
| **MTP Act 1971** (Amended 2021) | Termination of pregnancy; 1 doctor up to 20 wks; 2 doctors 20-24 wks |
| **POCSO Act 2012** | Sexual offenses against children <18; mandatory reporting |

| **Mental Health Act 2017** | Rights of mentally ill; NIMHANS as apex body; replaces Mental Health Act 1987 |
| **THOA 1994** (Amended 2011) | Transplantation of Human Organs; brain death definition |

- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: **NMC replaced MCI in 2020**; Consumer Protection Act covers medical services

### Dying Declaration
- **Statement made by a person who believes they are about to die** regarding circumstances/cause of impending death
- **Admissibility in court**: VALID even without oath; can be sole basis for conviction
- Does NOT need to be made before magistrate (can be to police officer, doctor, or any person)
- **Doctor's role**: Certify that declarant was in fit mental state to make declaration
- **Types**: Verbal, written, gestural (head nods), by pointing
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Declarant NEED NOT die for declaration to be valid; person may survive and still declaration can be used

### Inquest
| Type | Authority | When |
|------|----------|------|
| **Police inquest** (Section 174 CrPC) | Police officer (Sub-Inspector) | Sudden/unnatural deaths |
| **Magistrate inquest** (Section 176 CrPC) | Executive/Judicial Magistrate | When police inquest inadequate; dowry deaths, rape, custody deaths |
| **Coroner's inquest** | Coroner | Mumbai (remnant of British system) |

- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Magistrate inquest mandatory for custodial deaths, dowry deaths, rape-related deaths

### Age Determination

#### Dental Age Estimation
| Teeth | Age |
|-------|-----|
| First primary tooth (lower central incisor) | 6-8 months |
| All 20 deciduous teeth complete | 2-2.5 years |
| First permanent molar + central incisors | **6-7 years** |
| All permanent teeth except wisdom | 14-15 years |
| Wisdom teeth (third molar) | **17-25 years** |

- **Gustafson's method**: Dental attrition, periodontosis, dentin deposition, cementum apposition, root resorption, root transparency → adult age estimation

#### Ossification Centers (Key for NEET)

| Bone/Epiphysis | Appears | Fuses |
|----------------|---------|-------|
| Femur head | Birth | 16-18 yrs (females), 17-19 (males) |
| Iliac crest | 14-15 yrs | **21-25 yrs** |
| **Medial clavicle** | 17-20 yrs | **25-31 yrs (LAST to fuse)** |
| Capitulum (elbow) | 1-2 yrs | 13-15 yrs |
| Radius distal | 1-2 yrs | 17-18 yrs |
| **Lower end radius** | 1 yr | 17-18 yrs |
| Femur lower end | Birth (6 months of pregnancy) | 17-18 yrs |

- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: **Medial clavicle = LAST ossification center to fuse (25-31 years)**; used to distinguish 25+ age

## TOXICOLOGY

### General Principles

#### Routes of Absorption
1. Oral (ingestion) - most common
2. Inhalation
3. Transdermal
4. Injection (IV fastest onset)
5. Rectal, vaginal

#### Forensic Analysis - Viscera Preservation

| Suspected Poison | Preservative for Viscera |
|-----------------|--------------------------|
| Most organic poisons | **Saturated NaCl (rectified spirit/alcohol NOT used)** |
| **Arsenic, mercury, alcohol, CO** | **No preservative (plain viscera)** |
| **Alcohol** | **Sodium/potassium fluoride** (prevents fermentation) |
| Phosphorus, volatile poisons | NO preservative; store in cold |

- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: For alcohol analysis = use sodium fluoride; No preservative for arsenic/mercury
- **Viscera sent for analysis**: Stomach with contents, liver, kidney, spleen, intestines, brain

### Corrosive Poisons

#### Strong Acids
| Acid | Key Features |
|------|-------------|
| **H₂SO₄ (Sulfuric acid)** | **Char/blacken tissues (carbonization)**; "chain of burns" (lips → esophagus → stomach); hematemesis |
| **HCl (Hydrochloric acid)** | Fumes; whitish-grey corrosion |
| **HNO₃ (Nitric acid)** | **Xanthoproteic reaction** (yellow staining of tissues/skin) |
| **Oxalic acid** | Hypocalcemia; **tetany**; calcium oxalate crystals in urine |
| **Carbolic acid (Phenol)** | White corrosion; odor of carbolic; **characteristic smell**; liver/kidney damage |

- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: H₂SO₄ = blackens/chars; HNO₃ = yellow (xanthoproteic); oxalic acid = hypocalcemia + tetany

#### Strong Alkalis
| Alkali | Key Features |
|--------|-------------|
| **NaOH/KOH (Lye)** | **Saponification** (soap-like liquefaction); soft, greasy corrosion; soapy feeling |
| Lime | Caustic burns; whitish |
| Ammonia | Fumes; respiratory damage; alkaline burns |

### Metallic Poisons

#### Arsenic
- **Chronic arsenic poisoning**: Mees' lines (transverse white bands on nails), **raindrop pigmentation** (alternating hypo/hyperpigmentation), peripheral neuropathy, Mees' lines
- **Acute poisoning**: Garlicky odor of breath/vomit; gastroenteritis; rice water stools
- **Garlic odor** (arsenic = garlic; phosphorus = garlic too)
- **Tests**: **Marsh test** (gold standard; arsenic mirror); **Reinsch test** (arsenic + heavy metals)
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Mees' lines = arsenic (also seen in thallium); garlic odor; Marsh test = gold standard

#### Lead (Plumbism/Saturnism)
- **Sources**: Lead paint, old pipes, industries (battery, paint)
- **Features**:
  - **Burton's line**: Blue-black line on gingival margin (lead sulfide deposits)
  - **Basophilic stippling** of RBCs
  - **Wrist drop** (radial nerve involvement = extensor muscles)
  - Foot drop, anemia (hypochromic), colic, encephalopathy
  - Radiological: **Dense metaphyseal lines** in children (lead lines)
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Burton's line + wrist drop + basophilic stippling = Lead poisoning; Saturnism = chronic lead poisoning

#### Mercury
- **Inorganic**: Mercurialism; **Pink disease (acrodynia)** in children; erethism (psychosis), tremors, stomatitis
- **Organic (Methyl mercury)**: **Minamata disease** (Japan); neurological damage, birth defects
- **Mad Hatter's disease**: Felt hat workers exposed to mercury; tremors, personality changes
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Minamata disease = organic mercury (fish); Pink disease = inorganic mercury in children

### Organic Poisons

#### Organophosphate Poisoning
- **Mechanism**: **Irreversible inhibition of acetylcholinesterase (AChE)**
- **Accumulation of ACh → cholinergic crisis**
- **DUMBELS mnemonic** (Muscarinic effects):
  - **D**efecation/Diarrhea
  - **U**rination
  - **M**iosis
  - **B**radycardia/Bronchospasm/Bronchorrhea
  - **E**mesis (vomiting)
  - **L**acrimation
  - **S**alivation/Sweating
- **Nicotinic effects**: Muscle fasciculations, weakness, paralysis, tachycardia, hypertension
- **CNS effects**: Anxiety, seizures, coma
- **Treatment**: **Atropine** (blocks muscarinic; no effect on nicotinic) + **Pralidoxime (2-PAM)** (reactivates AChE if given EARLY, before "aging")
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Miosis = OP poisoning (not just opioids); Atropine = treatment (large doses); Pralidoxime must be given EARLY

#### Dhatura (Anticholinergic/Atropine-like Poisoning)
- **Plant**: Datura stramonium; all parts toxic; tropane alkaloids
- **BLIND BAT mnemonic**:
  - **B**ladder distension (urinary retention)
  - **L**oss of coordination
  - **I**ncreased heart rate (tachycardia)
  - **N**arrow pupil... NO - **Dilated pupils (mydriasis)**
  - **D**ry skin, dry mouth
  - **B**right light intolerance (photophobia)
  - **A**gitiation/confusion/hallucinations
  - **T**achycardia
- Key features: **BLIND HOT HARE DRY** = "Blind as a bat (mydriasis), Hot as a hare (fever), Dry as a bone, Mad as a hatter"
- **Treatment**: **Physostigmine** (AChE inhibitor that crosses BBB)
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Dhatura = anticholinergic (dilated pupils, dry skin, urinary retention, hallucinations); Organophosphate = cholinergic (miosis, secretions)

#### Opium/Morphine/Opioids
- **Classic triad**: **Miosis (pinpoint pupils), respiratory depression, CNS depression (coma)**
- Mechanism: μ-receptor agonism
- **Naloxone** (Narcan): Specific antidote; reverses all opioid effects; short half-life
- **Methadone**: Longer acting opioid used in maintenance therapy
- **Heroin (diacetylmorphine)**: Injected; HIV transmission risk; track marks
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Miosis = opioid hallmark; Naloxone = antidote; 3 signs = miosis + respiratory depression + coma

#### Cocaine
- **Mechanism**: Blocks reuptake of catecholamines (DA, NE, 5HT) → sympathomimetic effects
- **Features**: Euphoria, tachycardia, hypertension, dilated pupils (mydriasis), hyperthermia
- **Chronic**: **Nasal septum perforation** (snorting); **cocaine bugs (Magnan's symptom)** = formication (sensation of insects crawling under skin)
- **Crack cocaine**: Smokable form; fastest onset
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Nasal septum perforation = cocaine; cocaine bugs = Magnan's symptom

### Insecticides

| Type | AChE inhibition | Key Feature |
|------|----------------|-------------|
| **Organophosphates** | Irreversible | Pralidoxime can reverse if given early |
| **Carbamates** | Reversible | Better prognosis; spontaneously reverses; Pralidoxime NOT needed |
| **Organochlorines (DDT)** | No AChE effect | CNS stimulation; long environmental half-life; persistent |

- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Carbamates = reversible AChE inhibitor; pralidoxime NOT required (can worsen)
- **DDT**: Environmental persistence; bioaccumulation; **banned for agricultural use**

### Alcohol

#### Ethanol Metabolism
- **Ethanol → Acetaldehyde** (by alcohol dehydrogenase, ADH)
- **Acetaldehyde → Acetate** (by aldehyde dehydrogenase, ALDH)
- **Fixed rate of metabolism**: ~10-15 mL pure ethanol/hour
- **Blood alcohol levels (BAC)**:

| BAC (mg/100mL) | Effects |
|----------------|---------|
| **50** | Mild effects; legal limit in India = 30 mg/100mL |
| **80** | Legally drunk in most countries |
| **100** | Ataxia, incoordination |
| **150** | Dysarthria, confusion |
| **300** | Stupor |
| **400+** | Coma; potentially fatal |
| **500** | **Usually fatal (LD₅₀)** |

- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: India legal driving limit = **30 mg/100 mL blood** (Motor Vehicles Act)
- **Acute alcohol poisoning**: Smell of alcohol, vomiting, coma, hypoglycemia, hypothermia
- **Chronic alcoholism**:
  - **Wernicke's encephalopathy**: Ataxia, ophthalmoplegia, confusion (thiamine deficiency)
  - **Korsakoff's psychosis**: Anterograde amnesia, confabulation (permanent)
  - Liver disease (fatty liver → hepatitis → cirrhosis)

#### Methanol (Wood Alcohol) Poisoning
- Found in: Illicit liquor, antifreeze, solvent
- Metabolized to **formic acid** (toxic)
- **Features**: Latent period (6-12 hours); **visual disturbance → blindness** (optic nerve damage from formic acid)
- **Treatment**:
  - **Ethanol** (competitive inhibitor of ADH; prevents methanol metabolism)
  - **Fomepizole** (ADH inhibitor; preferred)
  - **Dialysis** (removes methanol and formate)
  - **Folinic acid** (enhances formate metabolism)
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Methanol = blindness (formic acid damages optic nerve); treat with ethanol/fomepizole

### Snake Venom

| Type | Venom | Snakes | Features | Treatment |
|------|-------|--------|----------|-----------|
| **Neurotoxic** | Post-synaptic neurotoxin (bungarotoxin) | **Cobra (Naja naja)** | Descending paralysis; ptosis, respiratory failure; **NO local swelling** | Antivenom + Neostigmine (cobra; NOT krait) |
| **Neurotoxic (pre-synaptic)** | Pre-synaptic neurotoxin (β-bungarotoxin) | **Krait (Bungarus)** | Ascending paralysis; **NO neostigmine benefit** | Antivenom only |
| **Hemotoxic/Cytotoxic** | Proteases, phospholipases | **Viper (Russell's viper, pit viper)** | Local swelling, necrosis, DIC, hemolysis, renal failure | Antivenom + supportive; **Heparin CONTRAINDICATED** |
| **Mixed** | Neurotoxic + cytotoxic | King cobra | Both features | Antivenom |

- **PYQ HOTSPOT**:
  - Cobra neurotoxin = post-synaptic; responsive to **Neostigmine**
  - Krait = pre-synaptic; Neostigmine NOT helpful
  - Viper = DIC; **heparin contraindicated** (antivenom is treatment)
  - **Fang marks**: 2 puncture wounds = viper; may be absent in cobra

## FORENSIC SEROLOGY AND IDENTIFICATION

### Blood Groups in Forensic
- **ABO system**: Antigens on RBC; antibodies in serum
- **Secretors (80%)**: People who secrete blood group antigens in body fluids (saliva, semen, vaginal secretion)
- **Non-secretors (20%)**: Do not secrete antigens
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: Semen analysis can determine blood group only if person is secretor

### DNA Fingerprinting
- **PCR-STR (Short Tandem Repeats)**: Most common method in forensics
  - STR = variable number of tandem repeats; highly variable between individuals
  - Unique to each individual (except identical twins)
  - Can be done on tiny samples (blood, hair root, semen)
- **RFLP**: Older method; requires larger sample
- **mtDNA**: Maternal inheritance; for skeletal remains, hair without root
- **PYQ HOTSPOT**: DNA fingerprinting was developed by **Alec Jeffreys** (1984)

### Forensic Odontology
- **Bite mark analysis**: Compare bite marks to suspect's dental impressions
- **Rugoscopy (palatoscopy)**: Unique palatal rugae patterns for identification
- **Cheiloscopy**: Lip print patterns for identification
- **Age determination**: Gustafson's method; dental radiographs

## KEY MNEMONICS

### Postmortem Changes
- **"Rigor starts at jaw"** = Masseter first; Nysten's law proceeds downward
- **RBGY** = Bruise color: Red → Blue → Green → Yellow (days 1→2→5→7)

### Asphyxia
- Hanging ligature = **OBLIQUE** (like a noose going UP)
- Strangulation ligature = **HORIZONTAL** (like a tight collar)

### Toxicology
- **DUMBELS** = Organophosphate muscarinic effects (Defecation, Urination, Miosis, Bradycardia, Emesis, Lacrimation, Salivation)
- **"Blind as a bat, Hot as a hare, Dry as a bone, Mad as a hatter"** = Anticholinergic (Dhatura)
- **"Garlic odor + Mees' lines + Raindrop pigmentation"** = Arsenic
- **"Burton's line + Wrist drop + Basophilic stippling"** = Lead

### Age Determination
- **Medial clavicle = LAST to fuse (25-31 years)** = to prove person is ≥25 years

### Wounds
- **Entry wound = INVERTED (goes in)**
- **Exit wound = EVERTED (comes out)**
