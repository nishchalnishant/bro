## Upper Limb

### Brachial Plexus

**Roots:** C5, C6, C7, C8, T1 (remember: "5 roots, like 5 fingers")

**Mnemonic for formation:** "Real Teenagers Drink Cold Beer"
- Roots → Trunks → Divisions → Cords → Branches

#### Trunks
| Trunk | Root Contributions |
|---|---|
| Upper | C5, C6 |
| Middle | C7 |
| Lower | C8, T1 |

#### Cords (named by relation to axillary artery)
| Cord | Formation |
|---|---|
| Lateral | Anterior divisions of upper + middle trunks |
| Medial | Anterior division of lower trunk |
| Posterior | Posterior divisions of all three trunks |

#### Terminal Branches (Mnemonic: "My Aunt Really Makes Raspberry Jam")
- **Musculocutaneous** — Lateral cord (C5,C6,C7)
- **Axillary** — Posterior cord (C5,C6)
- **Radial** — Posterior cord (C5-T1)
- **Median** — Lateral + Medial cords (C6-T1)
- **Radial** (already listed)
- **Medial cutaneous nerves** — Medial cord
- **Ulnar** — Medial cord (C8,T1)

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Median nerve = both lateral and medial cords** — only terminal branch with dual cord origin

#### Injury Patterns
| Injury | Nerve Roots | Deformity | Cause |
|---|---|---|---|
| Erb's palsy | C5,C6 | Waiter's tip (arm adducted, medially rotated, extended, forearm pronated) | Forceps delivery, fall on shoulder |
| Klumpke's palsy | C8,T1 | Claw hand | Forceful abduction of arm |
| Saturday night palsy | Radial nerve | Wrist drop | Compression in spiral groove |
| Cubital tunnel | Ulnar nerve | Ring + little finger clawing | Medial epicondyle fracture |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Radial nerve winds around spiral groove of humerus** — injured in mid-shaft humeral fractures

### Rotator Cuff Muscles (SITS)
| Muscle | Nerve | Action | PYQ Point |
|---|---|---|---|
| **S**upraspinatus | Suprascapular (C5,C6) | Initiates abduction (0–15°) | **Most commonly torn** |
| **I**nfraspinatus | Suprascapular (C5,C6) | Lateral rotation | — |
| **T**eres minor | Axillary (C5,C6) | Lateral rotation | — |
| **S**ubscapularis | Upper + Lower subscapular (C5,C6) | Medial rotation | Tested by Gerber lift-off test |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Supraspinatus is most commonly torn** — tested by empty can test; abduction 15–90° by deltoid (axillary nerve C5)

### Carpal Tunnel

**Contents (Mnemonic: "4 flexors digitorum profundus + 4 superficialis + 1 FPL + 1 median nerve")**
- 4 tendons FDS
- 4 tendons FDP
- 1 tendon FPL
- **Median nerve** (most medially placed)

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Palmaris longus and ulnar nerve are NOT in carpal tunnel** — ulnar nerve passes through Guyon's canal

**Roof:** Flexor retinaculum (transverse carpal ligament)
**Floor:** Carpal bones

**Median nerve compression → Carpal Tunnel Syndrome:**
- Wasting of thenar eminence (LOAF muscles: Lumbricals 1&2, Opponens pollicis, Abductor pollicis brevis, Flexor pollicis brevis)
- Tinel's sign, Phalen's test positive

### Anatomical Snuffbox

**Boundaries:**
- Medial (ulnar): EPL (extensor pollicis longus)
- Lateral (radial): APL (abductor pollicis longus) + EPB (extensor pollicis brevis)
- Floor: Scaphoid + trapezium + styloid process of radius

**Contents:**
- Radial artery (crosses floor)
- Cephalic vein (roof)
- Superficial branch of radial nerve

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Tenderness in anatomical snuffbox = scaphoid fracture** — most common carpal bone fracture; risk of avascular necrosis (proximal pole has no direct blood supply)

### Cubital Fossa

**Mnemonic: "Really Need Beer To Be At My Best"** — from lateral to medial:
- **R**adial nerve
- **B**iceps tendon
- **B**rachial artery
- **M**edian nerve

**Boundaries:**
- Lateral: Brachioradialis
- Medial: Pronator teres
- Superior (base): Line joining epicondyles
- Roof: Deep fascia + bicipital aponeurosis + skin
- Floor: Brachialis + supinator

## Lower Limb

### Femoral Triangle

**Boundaries:**
- Superior (base): Inguinal ligament
- Lateral: Sartorius
- Medial: Adductor longus
- Roof: Fascia lata
- Floor: Iliopsoas + pectineus + adductor longus

**Contents (Mnemonic: NAVY — from lateral to medial):**
| Structure | Note |
|---|---|
| **N** — Femoral nerve | Lateral and outside femoral sheath |
| **A** — Femoral artery | In femoral sheath |
| **V** — Femoral vein | In femoral sheath |
| **Y** — Y-shaped lymphatics (deep inguinal) | Most medially placed; Cloquet's node |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Femoral nerve is NOT inside the femoral sheath** — femoral sheath contains only artery, vein, and lymphatics (3 compartments)

**Femoral Sheath compartments (lateral → medial):** Artery, Vein, Lymphatics (femoral canal)
**Femoral canal:** Contains lymph node of Cloquet (Rosenmüller); femoral hernia passes through here

### Sciatic Nerve

- Largest nerve in body (L4, L5, S1, S2, S3)
- Exits pelvis through greater sciatic foramen **below piriformis**
- Divides into **common peroneal (fibular)** and **tibial** nerves at apex of popliteal fossa

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Piriformis syndrome** — sciatic nerve exits above piriformis in 15% → compression

| Division | Injury Result |
|---|---|
| Common peroneal nerve | Foot drop (L4,L5) — injury at neck of fibula |
| Tibial nerve | Calcaneovalgus deformity — loss of plantar flexion + inversion |

### Lumbar Plexus (L1–L4)

| Nerve | Root | Motor | Sensory |
|---|---|---|---|
| Iliohypogastric | L1 | Internal oblique | Skin above inguinal ligament |
| Ilioinguinal | L1 | — | Medial thigh, scrotum/labia |
| Genitofemoral | L1,L2 | Cremaster (genital branch) | Femoral triangle (femoral branch) |
| Lateral femoral cutaneous | L2,L3 | None (pure sensory) | Lateral thigh |
| Femoral | L2,L3,L4 | Quads + iliopsoas + sartorius | Anterior + medial thigh |
| Obturator | L2,L3,L4 | Adductors | Medial thigh |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Meralgia paresthetica** = entrapment of lateral femoral cutaneous nerve (L2,L3) at ASIS — numbness/tingling outer thigh

### Sacral Plexus (L4–S4)

- Sciatic nerve (L4–S3), pudendal nerve (S2–S4), superior gluteal (L4,L5,S1), inferior gluteal (L5,S1,S2)

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Superior gluteal nerve** = abducts hip via gluteus medius/minimus; injury → Trendelenburg gait (pelvis drops to opposite side)

### Arches of Foot

| Arch | Bones | Key Supporting Structure |
|---|---|---|
| Medial longitudinal | Calcaneum → talus → navicular → 3 cuneiforms → 1st–3rd metatarsals | **Spring ligament** (plantar calcaneonavicular ligament) + tibialis posterior |
| Lateral longitudinal | Calcaneum → cuboid → 4th–5th metatarsals | Long plantar ligament |
| Transverse | Bases of metatarsals at Lisfranc joint | **Peroneus longus** (main support) |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Keystone of medial longitudinal arch = talus** (head); flat foot = pes planus (fallen medial arch); pes cavus = high arch

## Thorax

### Mediastinum

#### Superior Mediastinum Contents (Mnemonic: "Brave Thieves Are Very Good Plunderers")
- Brachiocephalic veins (L and R)
- Thoracic duct (upper part)
- Aortic arch + branches
- Vagus nerves + phrenic nerves
- Great vessels (SVC, aortic arch)
- Phrenic nerves (C3,C4,C5)

#### Divisions of Mediastinum
| Division | Anterior | Middle | Posterior |
|---|---|---|---|
| Contents | Thymus, lymph nodes | Heart/pericardium, great vessels, trachea bifurcation, main bronchi, phrenic nerves | Descending aorta, esophagus, thoracic duct, azygos system, sympathetic chain, vagus nerves |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Azygos vein** drains into SVC at T4; arch of aorta begins and ends at sternal angle (T4/T5 intervertebral disc)

### Heart Anatomy and Coronary Arteries

#### Coronary Artery Territories

| Artery | Territory | ECG Leads |
|---|---|---|
| **LAD** (Left Anterior Descending) | Anterior wall LV, interventricular septum (anterior 2/3), apex, anterior papillary muscle | V1–V4 |
| **LCx** (Left Circumflex) | Lateral and posterior LV wall, left atrium | I, aVL, V5, V6 |
| **RCA** (Right Coronary Artery) | RV, posterior LV wall, SA node (60%), AV node (80–90%) | II, III, aVF |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **SA node blood supply = RCA in 60%, LCx in 40%** | **AV node = RCA in 80–90%** | **Right dominant circulation in 85%** (RCA gives posterior interventricular artery)

#### Surface Anatomy of Heart
| Border | Structure |
|---|---|
| Right border | Right atrium |
| Left border | Left ventricle (mainly) + left auricle |
| Inferior border | Right ventricle (mainly) + small part LV |
| Superior border | Great vessels (aorta, pulmonary trunk, SVC) |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Apex of heart = left 5th intercostal space, midclavicular line** — formed by LV

#### Heart Valves Surface Anatomy
| Valve | Auscultation Area | Anatomical Position |
|---|---|---|
| Mitral | Apex (5th ICS, MCL) | Left 4th costal cartilage |
| Aortic | 2nd ICS, right sternal border | Behind sternum at 3rd ICS |
| Pulmonary | 2nd ICS, left sternal border | Left 3rd costal cartilage |
| Tricuspid | Lower left sternal border (4th ICS) | Behind sternum at 4th–5th ICS |

### Pleura

- **Parietal pleura** lines the chest wall, diaphragm, mediastinum (has pain fibers)
- **Visceral pleura** covers lung surface (no pain fibers — pain = referred)

**Costophrenic recess:** Lowest point of pleural cavity — fluid accumulates here first on erect X-ray

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Pleural tap (thoracocentesis)** — needle inserted above upper border of rib (to avoid neurovascular bundle which runs in costal groove below each rib: VAN = vein, artery, nerve from top to bottom)

## Abdomen

### Inguinal Canal (Mnemonic: MALT)

**Contents — Males:**
- **M**uscles: internal oblique forms cremasteric muscle
- **A**rtery: cremasteric artery + artery of vas deferens
- **L**ymphs: no lymphatics (drain to para-aortic nodes, NOT inguinal)
- **T**ube: vas deferens + ilioinguinal nerve

**Contents — Females:**
- Round ligament of uterus + ilioinguinal nerve

**Boundaries:**
| Wall | Structure |
|---|---|
| Anterior | External oblique aponeurosis (whole length) + internal oblique (lateral 1/3) |
| Posterior | Transversalis fascia (whole length) + conjoint tendon (medial 1/3) |
| Roof | Internal oblique + transversus abdominis arching fibers |
| Floor | Inguinal ligament + lacunar ligament (medially) |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Deep inguinal ring** = opening in transversalis fascia, lateral to inferior epigastric vessels | **Superficial inguinal ring** = opening in external oblique aponeurosis, above pubic tubercle

**Hernia types:**
| Type | Relation to inferior epigastric vessels |
|---|---|
| Indirect inguinal | Lateral (goes through deep ring) |
| Direct inguinal | Medial (goes through Hesselbach's triangle) |
| Femoral | Below and lateral to pubic tubercle |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Femoral hernia more common in females** (wider pelvis); **indirect inguinal hernia most common overall**

### Portal Circulation

**Portal vein (L1 level)** formed by splenic vein + superior mesenteric vein (behind neck of pancreas)

**Portosystemic anastomoses:**
| Site | Portal tributary | Systemic tributary | Clinical |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lower esophagus | Left gastric vein | Azygos vein | Esophageal varices |
| Umbilicus | Paraumbilical veins | Epigastric veins | Caput medusae |
| Rectum | Superior rectal vein | Middle + inferior rectal veins | Internal hemorrhoids |
| Bare area of liver | Hepatic veins | Diaphragmatic veins | — |
| Retroperitoneal | Colic/mesenteric veins | Renal/suprarenal/gonadal veins | — |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Caput medusae** = dilated paraumbilical veins radiating from umbilicus in portal hypertension

### McBurney's Point

- Junction of lateral 1/3 and medial 2/3 of line joining ASIS to umbilicus
- **Surface marking of appendix base** (McBurney's point)
- **Lanz point** = junction of lateral 1/3 and middle 1/3 of inter-ASIS line (appendix incision)

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Rovsing's sign** = pressing LIF → pain in RIF (appendicitis); **Psoas sign** = flexing hip against resistance worsens pain (retrocaecal appendix)

### Surface Anatomy of Abdominal Organs

| Organ | Level |
|---|---|
| Liver (right lobe) | Right 5th ICS to right costal margin |
| Spleen | Left 9th–11th ribs, posterior axillary line |
| Kidneys | T12–L3 (right slightly lower due to liver) |
| Aortic bifurcation | L4 (level of umbilicus) |
| Portal vein formation | L1/L2 (behind neck of pancreas) |
| Transpyloric plane (L1) | Pylorus, duodenojejunal flexure, neck of pancreas, hilum of kidneys, fundus of gallbladder |

## Head & Neck

### Cavernous Sinus

**Location:** Beside sella turcica (body of sphenoid), T3–T4 vertebral level equivalent

**Contents (Mnemonic: "O TOM CAT")**
- **O**culomotor nerve (CN III)
- **T**rochlear nerve (CN IV)
- **O**phthalmic division of trigeminal (V1)
- **M**axillary division of trigeminal (V2)
- **C**arotid artery (internal) + sympathetic plexus
- **A**bducens nerve (CN VI) — most medially placed, directly contacts ICA
- **T**rochlear (already counted)

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **CN VI most commonly affected in cavernous sinus thrombosis** (most medially placed, closest to ICA) → lateral gaze palsy

**Connections of cavernous sinus:**
- Receives: Superior ophthalmic vein (→ danger triangle of face), inferior ophthalmic vein, sphenoparietal sinus, superficial middle cerebral vein
- Drains into: Superior and inferior petrosal sinuses → sigmoid sinus/IJV

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Danger triangle of face** (nose-upper lip area) → infection spreads via facial vein → superior ophthalmic vein → cavernous sinus

### Pterygopalatine Fossa

**Openings:**
| Opening | Communicates with |
|---|---|
| Pterygomaxillary fissure | Infratemporal fossa |
| Sphenopalatine foramen | Nasal cavity |
| Inferior orbital fissure | Orbit |
| Palatine canals | Hard palate |
| Pharyngeal canal | Nasopharynx |
| Foramen rotundum | Middle cranial fossa (V2 enters) |
| Pterygoid canal (Vidian) | Foramen lacerum |

**Contents:** Maxillary nerve (V2), pterygopalatine ganglion, maxillary artery (3rd part)

### Cranial Nerve Exits from Skull

| CN | Exit Foramen |
|---|---|
| I Olfactory | Cribriform plate |
| II Optic | Optic canal |
| III, IV, V1, VI | Superior orbital fissure |
| V2 | Foramen rotundum |
| V3 | Foramen ovale |
| VI (also via) | Superior orbital fissure |
| VII, VIII | Internal acoustic meatus |
| IX, X, XI (cranial) | Jugular foramen |
| XI (spinal) | Foramen magnum then jugular |
| XII | Hypoglossal canal |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Foramen ovale** = V3 + accessory meningeal artery + lesser petrosal nerve | **Foramen spinosum** = middle meningeal artery (extradural hematoma if ruptured)

### Thyroid Gland

**Blood supply:**
- Superior thyroid artery (1st branch of external carotid) + external laryngeal nerve (cricothyroid)
- Inferior thyroid artery (thyrocervical trunk of subclavian) + recurrent laryngeal nerve

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **RLN (recurrent laryngeal nerve) runs in tracheoesophageal groove** — at risk during thyroid surgery; **left RLN hooks under aortic arch** (longer course → more vulnerable)

**Parathyroid glands (4):**
- Superior parathyroids: Develop from 4th pharyngeal pouch
- Inferior parathyroids: Develop from 3rd pharyngeal pouch (migrate further)

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Superior parathyroids from 4th pouch, Inferior from 3rd pouch** (counterintuitive — inferior comes from higher-numbered pouch that migrates more)

### Parotid Gland

**Relations:**
- Facial nerve (CN VII) runs through gland, divides into temporal, zygomatic, buccal, marginal mandibular, cervical branches (Mnemonic: "To Zanzibar By Motor Car")
- Retromandibular vein and external carotid artery within gland

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Frey's syndrome** (auriculotemporal nerve injury during parotidectomy) = gustatory sweating — misdirected regeneration of parasympathetic fibers to sweat glands

**Parotid duct (Stensen's duct):** Opens opposite upper 2nd molar; pierces buccinator

## Neuroanatomy

### Internal Capsule

**Genu:** Corticobulbar fibers (facial, tongue, swallowing)
**Anterior limb:** Frontopontine + thalamofrontal fibers
**Posterior limb:** Corticospinal fibers (leg > arm > face — somatotopic, anterior to posterior)

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Blood supply of internal capsule** = lenticulostriate arteries (branches of MCA) — "arteries of stroke/cerebral hemorrhage"; rupture → contralateral hemiplegia

**Lesion effects:**
- Complete internal capsule lesion → contralateral hemiplegia + hemi-sensory loss + hemianopia (if posterior limb involved)
- Pure motor stroke → posterior limb (corticospinal)

### Thalamic Nuclei

| Nucleus | Input | Output | Function |
|---|---|---|---|
| VPL (Ventral posterolateral) | Spinothalamic + dorsal columns | Sensory cortex | Body sensation |
| VPM (Ventral posteromedial) | Trigeminal | Sensory cortex | Face sensation |
| VL (Ventral lateral) | Cerebellum + basal ganglia | Motor cortex | Motor coordination |
| LGN (Lateral geniculate) | Optic tract | Visual cortex (calcarine) | Vision |
| MGN (Medial geniculate) | Inferior colliculus | Auditory cortex | Hearing |
| Pulvinar | Multiple | Association cortex | Attention |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Thalamic syndrome (Dejerine-Roussy)** = contralateral hemibody pain/hyperesthesia after thalamic infarct (VPL nucleus)

### Basal Ganglia Circuit

**Direct pathway (facilitatory):** Cortex → Striatum → GPi/SNr → Thalamus (VL) → Cortex
- GABA inhibits GPi → less inhibition of thalamus → increased cortical activity

**Indirect pathway (inhibitory):** Cortex → Striatum → GPe → STN → GPi/SNr → Thalamus → Cortex
- Net effect: More inhibition of thalamus → decreased cortical activity

**Dopamine (from SNc):** Excites direct pathway (D1), inhibits indirect pathway (D2) → net facilitation of movement

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Parkinson's = loss of dopamine from SNc** → indirect pathway overactive → movement suppressed (bradykinesia, rigidity, tremor); **Huntington's = loss of striatal neurons** (especially indirect pathway GABA neurons first) → choreiform movements (early loss of indirect pathway → less GPi activity → thalamus disinhibited → excessive movement)

**Hemiballismus:** Lesion of subthalamic nucleus (STN) → contralateral violent flinging movements

### Cerebellum

| Structure | Function | Lesion Result |
|---|---|---|
| Vermis | Trunk/axial coordination, gait | Truncal ataxia, gait ataxia |
| Intermediate zone (paravermal) | Limb coordination | Limb ataxia |
| Lateral hemisphere | Planning, timing of movement | Intention tremor, dysmetria |
| Flocculonodular lobe | Vestibular function, balance | Nystagmus, falling to side of lesion |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Midline (vermis) cerebellar lesion** (e.g., medulloblastoma in children) = truncal ataxia, wide-based gait, NO limb ataxia; **Hemisphere lesion** = ipsilateral limb ataxia (IPSILATERAL, because double-decussation)

**DIPS mnemonic for cerebellar signs:** Dysmetria, Intention tremor, Past-pointing (dysdiadochokinesia), Scanning dysarthria (also nystagmus, ataxia, hypotonia)

### Brainstem

#### Cranial Nerve Nuclei Locations
| Level | Cranial Nerves |
|---|---|
| Midbrain | III (oculomotor), IV (trochlear) |
| Pons | V (trigeminal), VI (abducens), VII (facial), VIII (vestibulocochlear) |
| Medulla | IX (glossopharyngeal), X (vagus), XI (accessory — cranial part), XII (hypoglossal) |

**Crossed syndromes (hallmark of brainstem lesions):**
- Ipsilateral CN palsy + contralateral hemiplegia = **brainstem lesion**

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Weber's syndrome** (midbrain) = ipsilateral CN III palsy + contralateral hemiplegia | **Wallenberg's syndrome** (lateral medulla/PICA) = ipsilateral face loss + contralateral body loss of pain/temp + dysphagia + nystagmus + Horner's

## Histology

### Epithelia

| Type | Location | Special Features |
|---|---|---|
| Simple squamous | Alveoli, Bowman's capsule, mesothelium, endothelium | Diffusion |
| Simple cuboidal | Thyroid follicles, PCT of kidney, collecting duct | Secretion/absorption |
| Simple columnar | Stomach, small intestine, large intestine, gallbladder | Microvilli; goblet cells |
| Pseudostratified columnar ciliated | Respiratory tract (trachea, bronchi) | Goblet cells; cilia |
| Stratified squamous (non-keratinized) | Esophagus, vagina, oral cavity | Lubrication |
| Stratified squamous (keratinized) | Skin | Protection |
| Transitional (urothelium) | Urinary bladder, ureter, renal pelvis | Distensible; umbrella cells |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Transitional epithelium** has characteristic umbrella/dome cells on surface when relaxed

### Glands

| Type | Mechanism | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Merocrine (eccrine) | Exocytosis | Most glands (salivary, pancreatic acini, eccrine sweat) |
| Apocrine | Apical cytoplasm pinched off | Mammary gland, axillary sweat glands |
| Holocrine | Cell disintegrates | Sebaceous glands |

### Connective Tissue

**Loose (areolar):** Most common CT; fibroblasts, collagen, elastin, ground substance
**Dense regular:** Tendons, ligaments (parallel collagen bundles)
**Dense irregular:** Dermis, organ capsules (random collagen bundles)

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Collagen type I** = bone, tendon, skin, dentin (most abundant); **Type II** = cartilage; **Type III** = reticular fibers (liver, spleen, lymph nodes); **Type IV** = basement membranes

### Cartilage Types

| Type | Fibers | Location | Perichondrium |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hyaline | Type II collagen (not visible) | Articular surfaces, trachea rings, costal cartilage, fetal skeleton | Yes (except articular) |
| Fibrocartilage | Type I collagen (visible) | Intervertebral discs, menisci, pubic symphysis, TMJ | **No** |
| Elastic | Elastic fibers | Epiglottis, auricle, Eustachian tube | Yes |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Articular cartilage = hyaline, NO perichondrium, NO blood vessels, NO nerves** — heals poorly

### Bone — Haversian System

**Osteon (Haversian system)** = structural unit of compact bone
- **Haversian canal** = central canal containing blood vessels and nerves, surrounded by concentric lamellae
- **Volkmann's canals** = transverse canals connecting Haversian canals to periosteum/endosteum
- **Lacunae** = spaces containing osteocytes
- **Canaliculi** = tiny channels connecting lacunae

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Osteoclasts** = multinucleated (mononuclear phagocyte system, NOT osteoprogenitor origin) | **Osteoblasts** = single nucleus, make osteoid | **Ruffled border of osteoclast** = site of bone resorption (creates Howship's lacuna)

### Muscle Types

| Feature | Skeletal | Cardiac | Smooth |
|---|---|---|---|
| Striations | Yes | Yes | No |
| Nuclei | Peripheral, multiple | Central, 1–2 | Central, 1 |
| T-tubules | At A-I junction | At Z-line | Absent/rudimentary |
| Intercalated discs | No | Yes | No |
| Control | Voluntary | Involuntary | Involuntary |
| SR development | Well developed | Moderate | Poorly developed |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Cardiac muscle** — intercalated discs contain: fascia adherens (attachment), desmosomes (strength), gap junctions (electrical coupling) | Longest muscle = sartorius; largest = gluteus maximus; smallest = stapedius

## Embryology

### Derivatives of Germ Layers

#### Ectoderm
- **Surface ectoderm:** Epidermis, hair, nails, lens, cornea, enamel of teeth, anterior pituitary (Rathke's pouch), parotid/lacrimal/mammary glands, external ear
- **Neuroectoderm (neural tube):** CNS (brain + spinal cord), retina, posterior pituitary, pineal gland
- **Neural crest cells:** PNS, Schwann cells, melanocytes, chromaffin cells (adrenal medulla), C-cells of thyroid, aorticopulmonary septum, facial bones/cartilage, teeth (dentin — odontoblasts)

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Neural crest cells** = "4th germ layer" — extremely high yield; adrenal medulla (chromaffin cells), C-cells of thyroid, melanocytes, aorticopulmonary septum

#### Mesoderm
- Muscle (all types), bone, cartilage, connective tissue
- Cardiovascular system (heart, blood vessels except neural crest contribution)
- Urogenital system (kidneys, gonads, genital ducts)
- Spleen (from dorsal mesogastrium)
- Adrenal cortex (mesoderm), dermis

#### Endoderm
- GI tract lining (stomach to rectum)
- Respiratory epithelium
- Thyroid, parathyroid (3rd and 4th pouches), thymus (3rd pouch)
- Liver, gallbladder, pancreas (from foregut)
- Urinary bladder (from urogenital sinus)
- Middle ear, Eustachian tube (from 1st pharyngeal pouch)

### Pharyngeal Arches, Pouches, and Clefts

#### Pharyngeal Arches — Derivatives

| Arch | Cartilage | Muscles | Nerve |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1st (Mandibular) | Meckel's cartilage → malleus, incus, mandible | Muscles of mastication, mylohyoid, ant. belly digastric, tensor tympani, tensor veli palatini | V3 (mandibular) |
| 2nd (Hyoid) | Reichert's cartilage → stapes, styloid process, stylohyoid ligament, hyoid (lesser horn + upper body) | Muscles of facial expression, stapedius, stylohyoid, post. belly digastric | VII (facial) |
| 3rd | Hyoid (greater horn + lower body) | Stylopharyngeus | IX (glossopharyngeal) |
| 4th | Thyroid cartilage, laryngeal cartilages | Cricothyroid, pharyngeal constrictors, soft palate muscles (except tensor) | X (vagus — superior laryngeal branch) |
| 6th | Cricoid, arytenoid, corniculate, cuneiform cartilages | Intrinsic laryngeal muscles (except cricothyroid) | X (recurrent laryngeal branch) |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **No 5th arch** in humans (vestigial/absent) | **Tensor veli palatini = V3** (1st arch), all other palate muscles = vagus (X)

### Neural Tube Defects

| Defect | Description | Associated Finding |
|---|---|---|
| Anencephaly | Failure of anterior neuropore to close (day 25) | Elevated AFP in amniotic fluid + maternal serum; polyhydramnios |
| Spina bifida occulta | Failure of vertebral arch fusion; no neural tissue exposed | Tuft of hair, dimple at lumbosacral area |
| Meningocele | Meninges herniate through vertebral defect | CSF-filled sac, no neural tissue |
| Meningomyelocele | Meninges + spinal cord herniate | Neurological deficits; most common form of spina bifida cystica |
| Encephalocele | Brain tissue through skull defect | — |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Neural tube closes by day 28** (anterior neuropore day 25, posterior day 27) | **Folic acid** prevents neural tube defects | **Elevated AFP** in anencephaly and open spina bifida

### Heart Development

**Cardiac looping:** Heart tube loops to right (D-loop) — failure → dextrocardia or situs inversus

**Septation:**
- Atrial septum: Septum primum grows down → foramen primum → ostium secundum forms → septum secundum grows to cover it → patent foramen ovale (functional closure at birth)
- **ASD** most common: **Ostium secundum type** (defect in fossa ovalis region)
- **VSD** most common congenital heart defect overall: **Membranous type** (aorticopulmonary septum, neural crest cells)

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Most common CHD = VSD** | **Most common CHD in Down syndrome = AVSD (atrioventricular septal defect)** | **Most common CHD in Turner syndrome = bicuspid aortic valve/coarctation of aorta**

**Aorticopulmonary (AP) septum:** Neural crest cells → divides truncus arteriosus into aorta and pulmonary trunk; failure → persistent truncus arteriosus or transposition of great arteries

### Congenital Anomalies

| Anomaly | Mechanism | Key Feature |
|---|---|---|
| Tracheoesophageal fistula (TEF) | Failure of tracheoesophageal septum | Most common type: esophageal atresia + distal TEF (Type C); polyhydramnios |
| Meckel's diverticulum | Persistence of vitellointestinal duct | "Rule of 2s": 2 inches long, 2 feet from ileocecal valve, 2% population, 2 types of ectopic tissue (gastric + pancreatic) |

## Genetics

### Mitosis vs Meiosis

| Feature | Mitosis | Meiosis |
|---|---|---|
| Cell type | Somatic | Germ cells (gonads) |
| Divisions | 1 | 2 (meiosis I + II) |
| Daughter cells | 2 diploid (2n) | 4 haploid (n) |
| Crossing over | No | Yes (meiosis I, prophase I) |
| Chiasmata | No | Yes |
| Duration | Hours | Days/years (oocytes arrested in prophase I until puberty) |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Oocyte arrested in prophase I (diplotene stage)** from fetal life until puberty — then completes meiosis I at ovulation; meiosis II completed only at fertilization | **Nondisjunction in meiosis I** → both homologs go to same cell (more severe) | **Meiosis II nondisjunction** → sister chromatids fail to separate

### Chromosomal Disorders

| Disorder | Karyotype | Key Features |
|---|---|---|
| **Down syndrome (Trisomy 21)** | 47,XX/XY +21 | Most common chromosomal disorder; flat face, epicanthal folds, single palmar crease, brushfield spots, AVSD (most common CHD), duodenal atresia, Alzheimer's at 40s; **Robertsonian translocation** (familial form, usually chr 14;21) |
| **Edwards syndrome (Trisomy 18)** | 47,XX/XY +18 | 2nd most common trisomy (viability); rocker-bottom feet, clenched fists (index over 3rd, 5th over 4th), micrognathia, VSD; **poor prognosis** (median survival weeks) |
| **Patau syndrome (Trisomy 13)** | 47,XX/XY +13 | Holoprosencephaly, cleft lip/palate, polydactyly, microphthalmia, cyclopia; **worst prognosis** |
| **Turner syndrome** | 45,X0 | Female phenotype; short stature, webbed neck, shield chest, widely spaced nipples, coarctation of aorta, bicuspid aortic valve, streak gonads, primary amenorrhea, **no Barr body** |
| **Klinefelter syndrome** | 47,XXY | Male phenotype; tall, long limbs, small testes (hyalinization), gynecomastia, infertility, learning disability; **1 Barr body** (N-1 rule: Barr bodies = no. of X chromosomes minus 1) |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Barr body count = (no. of X chromosomes – 1)**: 46XX = 1 Barr body; 47XXY = 1; 45X0 = 0; 47XXX = 2

**Maternal age effect:** Increases risk of trisomies (meiosis I error in oocytes) — especially trisomy 21, 18, 13 and XXX, XXY

**Robertsonian translocation (Down syndrome):**
- Translocation of chr 21 onto chr 14 (or 13, 15, 22)
- Carrier parent: 45 chromosomes phenotypically normal
- Risk of Down syndrome offspring: 1/3 theoretical (1/10 in practice)
- **Recurrence risk 100x higher than standard trisomy 21**

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Fragile X syndrome** = most common inherited cause of intellectual disability (trinucleotide CGG repeat on FMR1 gene, X-linked dominant with reduced penetrance); **Down syndrome = most common chromosomal cause** of intellectual disability
