## BLOOD (Q1–Q5)

**Q1. A 30-year-old man is administered heparin after a deep vein thrombosis. Heparin exerts its anticoagulant effect primarily by:**
- A) Inhibiting vitamin K-dependent clotting factor synthesis
- B) Activating antithrombin III, which inhibits thrombin and factor Xa
- C) Directly inhibiting fibrinogen polymerization
- D) Inhibiting platelet aggregation by blocking glycoprotein IIb/IIIa

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct: B**

Heparin potentiates antithrombin III (AT III), which then inactivates thrombin (IIa) and factor Xa — this is the primary mechanism of heparin's anticoagulant effect. A) Warfarin inhibits vitamin K-dependent factor synthesis (II, VII, IX, X, Protein C and S). C) No anticoagulant directly inhibits fibrinogen polymerization in clinical use. D) Abciximab blocks GP IIb/IIIa; heparin does not act this way.

</details>

**Q3. A mountaineer ascends to high altitude. After 2 weeks, his oxygen-hemoglobin dissociation curve shifts to the right. The MOST important cause of this shift at high altitude is:**
- A) Decreased PCO2
- B) Increased 2,3-diphosphoglycerate (2,3-DPG)
- C) Alkalosis from hyperventilation
- D) Decreased body temperature

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct: B**

At high altitude, increased erythropoiesis and hypoxia stimulate production of 2,3-DPG in RBCs, which reduces hemoglobin's oxygen affinity, shifting the curve rightward to facilitate O2 unloading to tissues. A) Decreased PCO2 (from hyperventilation) shifts the curve LEFT (Bohr effect). C) Alkalosis also shifts the curve left. D) Decreased temperature shifts the curve left (increased oxygen affinity).

</details>

**Q4. Westergren ESR is measured as the rate of RBC sedimentation in 1 hour. ESR is INCREASED in all of the following EXCEPT:**
- A) Pregnancy
- B) Multiple myeloma
- C) Sickle cell anaemia
- D) Rheumatoid arthritis

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct: C**

ESR is decreased in sickle cell anaemia because the sickled, distorted RBCs cannot stack into rouleaux (which is the prerequisite for sedimentation). A) Pregnancy increases ESR due to raised fibrinogen. B) Multiple myeloma increases ESR due to elevated immunoglobulins (paraproteins). D) Rheumatoid arthritis raises ESR via acute-phase proteins (fibrinogen, CRP).

</details>

## CARDIOVASCULAR (Q6–Q10)

**Q6. During auscultation of the heart, the first heart sound (S1) is heard. S1 is produced by:**
- A) Opening of the semilunar valves
- B) Closure of the mitral and tricuspid valves at the onset of ventricular systole
- C) Rapid ventricular filling in early diastole
- D) Closure of the aortic and pulmonary valves

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct: B**

S1 is produced by closure of the atrioventricular valves (mitral and tricuspid) at the beginning of ventricular systole (isovolumetric contraction phase). A) Opening of semilunar valves is normally silent. C) Rapid ventricular filling produces S3 (pathological in adults > 40 years — indicates heart failure). D) Closure of aortic and pulmonary valves produces S2.

</details>

**Q7. A patient in the ICU has a Swan-Ganz catheter. The pulmonary capillary wedge pressure (PCWP) is 25 mmHg (normal < 12 mmHg). This is most consistent with:**
- A) Right heart failure
- B) Hypovolemia
- C) Left heart failure
- D) Pulmonary arterial hypertension

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct: C**

PCWP reflects left atrial pressure (and hence left ventricular end-diastolic pressure). A raised PCWP indicates elevated left-sided filling pressures, consistent with left heart failure (LV cannot eject adequately, backs up into LA and pulmonary circulation). A) Right heart failure raises JVP/CVP, not PCWP. B) Hypovolemia lowers PCWP. D) PAH raises pulmonary artery pressure but PCWP is normal (< 15 mmHg).

</details>

**Q8. Frank-Starling's law of the heart states that:**
- A) Heart rate increases with increased venous return
- B) The force of cardiac contraction increases with increased end-diastolic volume (preload)
- C) Cardiac output decreases when afterload increases
- D) Stroke volume is inversely proportional to end-diastolic volume

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct: B**

Starling's law: within physiological limits, the force of cardiac contraction (and hence stroke volume) increases with increasing end-diastolic volume (preload), due to optimal overlap of actin and myosin filaments. A) Heart rate changes with autonomic tone (Bainbridge reflex), not Starling's law. C) Increased afterload reduces stroke volume, not directly Starling's. D) Stroke volume is directly (not inversely) proportional to EDV within limits.

</details>

**Q9. Which wave of the jugular venous pulse (JVP) corresponds to atrial contraction?**
- A) 'a' wave

- D) 'x' descent

<summary>Answer</summary>

B) 'c' wave is produced by tricuspid valve closure/bulging into the atrium at onset of systole. C) 'v' wave is produced by venous filling of the right atrium when the tricuspid is closed (systolic venous filling). D) 'x' descent represents atrial relaxation.

</details>

**Q10. Cardiac output is 5 L/min and heart rate is 72 beats/min. The stroke volume is approximately:**
- A) 50 mL
- B) 70 mL

<details>

**Q12. In the lungs, the V/Q ratio is highest at the:**
- A) Base of the lung
- B) Apex of the lung

- D) Same throughout all zones

<details>

A) The base has a LOW V/Q ratio (≈0.6) because perfusion exceeds ventilation here. C) Middle zone has a V/Q ratio of approximately 1 (balanced). D) V/Q varies significantly from apex to base due to gravity.

**Q13. Carbon dioxide is transported in the blood PREDOMINANTLY as:**
- A) Dissolved CO2 in plasma
- B) Carbaminohemoglobin
- C) Bicarbonate ions in plasma
- D) Carbonic acid

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct: C**

Approximately 70% of CO2 is transported as bicarbonate (HCO3−) in plasma, formed by the action of carbonic anhydrase in RBCs. About 23% is carried as carbaminohemoglobin (bound to Hb protein chains, not the heme), and only ~7% is dissolved in plasma. A) Dissolved CO2 is only 7%. B) Carbaminohemoglobin carries 23%. D) Carbonic acid is an intermediate, not a transport form.

</details>

**Q30. A patient is in a dark room and a bright light is suddenly shone in one eye. The direct and consensual pupillary light reflexes are tested. If the afferent limb (optic nerve) of the right eye is damaged:**
- A) Direct reflex absent in right eye, consensual reflex absent in left eye
- B) Both direct and consensual reflexes absent when right eye is tested; both present when left eye is tested
- C) Direct reflex present in right eye, consensual absent in left eye
- D) Both eyes fail to constrict regardless of which eye is stimulated

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**Correct: B**

If the right optic nerve (afferent) is damaged: stimulating the right eye sends no signal to the pretectal nucleus, so neither the right (direct) nor left (consensual) pupil constricts. But stimulating the LEFT eye (intact afferent) sends the signal normally → BOTH pupils constrict (direct in left, consensual in right). This pattern (swinging flashlight test) defines a relative afferent pupillary defect (RAPD/Marcus Gunn pupil). A) Incorrect description. C) This would suggest an efferent lesion. D) If both eyes fail regardless of stimulation, both efferent (oculomotor) nerves are damaged.

</details>
