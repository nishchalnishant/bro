## Blood

### ABO Blood Groups

| Blood Group | Antigens on RBC | Antibodies in Plasma | Can Donate To | Can Receive From |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| A | A antigen | Anti-B | A, AB | A, O |
| B | B antigen | Anti-A | B, AB | B, O |
| AB | A + B antigens | **None** | AB only | **All (universal recipient)** |
| O | **None** | Anti-A + Anti-B | **All (universal donor)** | O only |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **O negative = universal donor** for packed RBCs | **AB positive = universal recipient** | Antibodies in ABO system are **naturally occurring IgM** (no prior sensitization needed)

### Rh Blood Group

- **D antigen** is most immunogenic Rh antigen
- **Rh-negative** individuals have no anti-D antibodies unless sensitized
- **Hemolytic disease of newborn (HDN):** Rh-negative mother × Rh-positive fetus — 1st pregnancy usually unaffected; 2nd pregnancy: maternal IgG anti-D crosses placenta → hemolysis
- **Prevention:** Anti-D immunoglobulin (Rhogam) at 28 weeks and within 72 hours of delivery

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **ABO incompatibility can affect 1st pregnancy** (IgG naturally present) | **Rh HDN spares 1st pregnancy** (sensitization occurs at delivery)

### Coagulation Cascade

#### Intrinsic Pathway (PTT measures this)
**XII → XI → IX → (+ VIII + Ca2+ + PF3) → X**
Mnemonic: "12, 11, 9, 8" — activated by contact with collagen/exposed subendothelium

#### Extrinsic Pathway (PT/INR measures this)
**Tissue factor (III) + VII + Ca2+ → X**
Activated by tissue damage

#### Common Pathway
**Xa + Va + Ca2+ + PF3 → Thrombin → Fibrin**
- Factor X → Xa (prothrombinase complex)
- Prothrombin (II) → Thrombin (IIa)
- Fibrinogen (I) → Fibrin → cross-linked fibrin (factor XIII)

| Test | Pathway | Factors Measured |
|---|---|---|
| PT (INR) | Extrinsic + common | I, II, V, VII, X |
| aPTT | Intrinsic + common | I, II, V, VIII, IX, X, XI, XII |
| TT (Thrombin time) | Common | Fibrinogen |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Factor VII has shortest half-life** (6 hours) → PT prolonged first in liver disease/warfarin | **Factor XIII (fibrin stabilizer)** not measured by standard coagulation tests | **Warfarin** blocks factors II, VII, IX, X (vitamin K-dependent) and protein C, S | **Heparin** activates antithrombin III → inhibits thrombin (IIa) and Xa

**Vitamin K-dependent factors: II, VII, IX, X** (+ Protein C, S, Z)
Mnemonic: "1972" (1, 9, 7, 2 → factors X, IX, VII, II → also proteins C and S)

### Platelets

- Normal count: **150,000–400,000/μL**
- Life span: **7–10 days**
- Derived from megakaryocytes (thrombopoietin regulation)
- No nucleus; contain dense granules (ADP, serotonin, Ca2+) and alpha granules (vWF, fibrinogen, factor V, fibronectin, platelet factor 4)

**Platelet plug formation:**
1. Adhesion: vWF bridges platelet GPIb receptor to exposed subendothelial collagen
2. Activation: ADP, thromboxane A2 (TXA2) → shape change, granule release
3. Aggregation: Fibrinogen bridges GPIIb/IIIa receptors between platelets

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Bernard-Soulier** = GPIb deficiency (adhesion defect) | **Glanzmann's thrombasthenia** = GPIIb/IIIa deficiency (aggregation defect) | **vWF disease** = most common inherited bleeding disorder (vWF deficiency → adhesion failure + factor VIII instability)

### Hemoglobin

| Type | Chains | % in Adults | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **HbA** | α2β2 | **97%** | Normal adult Hb |
| **HbA2** | α2δ2 | **2.5%** | Increased in beta-thalassemia trait |
| **HbF** | α2γ2 | **< 1%** in adults; predominant in fetus | Higher O2 affinity; doesn't interact with 2,3-DPG |
| **HbS** | α2βS2 | — | Sickle cell; sickling on deoxygenation; **no β-globin charge = electrophoresis** |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **HbF has higher O2 affinity than HbA** (left-shifted curve) — because γ-chains don't bind 2,3-DPG as well as β-chains | **In sickle cell disease = HbS**, HbA is absent; **sickle cell trait = HbAS** (protective against Plasmodium falciparum)

### O2–Hb Dissociation Curve

**Normal P50 = 26.6 mmHg** (PO2 at which Hb is 50% saturated)

#### RIGHT Shift (↓ O2 affinity = more O2 released to tissues)
Mnemonic: "CADET face right" — CO2↑, Acid↑ (pH↓), 2,3-DPG↑, Exercise, Temperature↑
- Also: altitude (chronic adaptation), anemia, thyroid hormones, growth hormone

#### LEFT Shift (↑ O2 affinity = O2 held, less released)
- Alkalosis (pH↑), CO↓ (CO poisoning shifts left), 2,3-DPG↓ (stored blood), HbF, hypothermia, HbCO (carboxyhemoglobin), methemoglobin

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Bohr effect** = ↑CO2 or ↓pH → right shift → more O2 delivery to tissues | **2,3-DPG** (produced by RBCs via Rapoport-Luebering shunt) binds β-chains of deoxyHb → stabilizes T (tense) form → right shift | **CO poisoning** = SpO2 falsely normal (CO-oximetry needed to detect HbCO)

### ESR (Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate)

- **Normal:** Males < 15 mm/hr; Females < 20 mm/hr (Westergren method)
- **Principle:** RBC rouleaux formation — influenced by fibrinogen and globulins
- **Increased:** Infection, inflammation, malignancy, anemia, pregnancy, myeloma
- **Decreased:** Polycythemia, sickle cell disease, spherocytosis, hypofibrinogenemia

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **ESR is NOT raised in** sickle cell disease (abnormal RBC shape prevents rouleaux) | **CRP rises and falls faster than ESR** (better acute marker) | **Myeloma = very high ESR** (massive paraprotein production)

## Cardiovascular System

### Cardiac Cycle

**Heart rate 75/min → cycle duration = 0.8 seconds**

| Phase | Events | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Isovolumetric contraction | All valves closed; pressure ↑ in LV | 0.05 sec |
| Rapid ejection | Aortic valve opens; LV pressure > aortic; SV ejected | 0.09 sec |
| Reduced ejection | LV pressure < aortic; aortic valve still open | 0.13 sec |
| Isovolumetric relaxation | All valves closed; LV pressure drops | 0.08 sec |
| Rapid filling | Mitral opens; blood rushes into LV (E wave on echo) | 0.11 sec |
| Slow filling (diastasis) | Passive filling continues | 0.19 sec |
| Atrial systole (A wave) | Atrial contraction contributes last 20–30% of LV filling | 0.11 sec |

**Normal pressures:**
- LV systolic: 120 mmHg | LV diastolic: 0–8 mmHg
- Aortic: 120/80 mmHg | Pulmonary artery: 25/8 mmHg
- LVEDV (end-diastolic volume): 120–130 mL | LVESV: 50–60 mL | SV: 70 mL | EF: ~60%

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **During isovolumetric phases = ALL 4 valves closed** | **Aortic valve opens at end of isovolumetric contraction** | **Mitral valve closes at start of systole (S1)** | **Aortic valve closes at start of isovolumetric relaxation (S2)**

### Heart Sounds

| Sound | Mechanism | Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| S1 | Closure of MV + TV (beginning of systole) | Loud in mitral stenosis; soft in MR |
| S2 | Closure of AV + PV (beginning of diastole) | Physiological split on inspiration (A2 before P2) |
| S3 | Rapid ventricular filling (early diastole) | Normal in children/athletes; **pathological = LV failure, MR** |
| S4 | Atrial contraction into stiff ventricle (presystolic) | **Hypertension, hypertrophic cardiomyopathy, HOCM** |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **S3 gallop = heart failure** (volume overload) | **S4 gallop = pressure overload/stiff ventricle** | **Pericardial knock** sounds like S3 but in constrictive pericarditis | Opening snap (mitral stenosis) comes after S2

### Starling's Law (Frank-Starling Mechanism)

**"The heart pumps what it receives"**
- ↑ venous return → ↑ EDV (preload) → sarcomere stretch → ↑ force of contraction → ↑ SV
- Maximum force at optimal sarcomere length = **2.2 μm**
- Descending limb: > 2.6 μm stretch → cross-bridge overlap decreases → reduced force (theoretical, not significant in normal heart)

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **In heart failure** — the heart operates on a lower, depressed Starling curve; same preload gives less output | **Positive inotropes** = shift curve upward (digitalis, catecholamines, Ca2+) | **Negative inotropes** = shift curve downward (beta-blockers, calcium channel blockers, heart failure)

### JVP Waveforms

| Wave | Event | Abnormality |
|---|---|---|
| **a wave** | Atrial contraction | Giant a = TR (cannon a wave); absent in AF; large cannon in complete heart block |
| **c wave** | Tricuspid valve closure/bulge into atrium | Often not visible |
| **x descent** | Atrial relaxation + downward displacement of TV ring during systole | Exaggerated in cardiac tamponade |
| **v wave** | Venous filling of atrium against closed TV | Giant v = tricuspid regurgitation |
| **y descent** | Tricuspid opens, atrium empties into RV | Absent/slow in cardiac tamponade; steep in constrictive pericarditis |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Cardiac tamponade** = raised JVP + exaggerated x descent + absent y descent (Beck's triad: hypotension, raised JVP, muffled heart sounds) | **Kussmaul's sign** (JVP rises on inspiration) = constrictive pericarditis, RV failure, tricuspid stenosis

### Cardiac Output

**CO = HR × SV** (normal: 5 L/min)
**Cardiac Index = CO/BSA** (normal: 2.8–3.2 L/min/m2)

**Fick's Principle:**
CO = O2 consumption / (arteriovenous O2 difference)
CO = VO2 / (CaO2 – CvO2)

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Fick's principle** = gold standard for CO measurement | **Thermodilution** (Swan-Ganz catheter) = clinical gold standard | **O2 consumption = 250 mL/min**; arterial O2 content = 20 mL/dL; venous = 15 mL/dL → CO = 250/(5 mL/dL × 10 dL/L) = 5 L/min

### Blood Pressure Regulation

#### Baroreceptors
- Location: Carotid sinus (CN IX — Hering's nerve) and aortic arch (CN X)
- ↑ BP → stretch → ↑ baroreceptor firing → via NTS (nucleus tractus solitarius) → ↓ sympathetic + ↑ parasympathetic → ↓ HR, ↓ contractility, vasodilation → ↓ BP

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Carotid sinus massage** → slows HR (vagal reflex) — used to terminate SVT | **Bezold-Jarisch reflex** = ventricular receptors activated (e.g., MI, hypovolemia) → bradycardia + hypotension + apnea

#### RAAS (Renin-Angiotensin-Aldosterone System)
↓ BP / ↓ Na+ / ↓ renal perfusion → **Renin** (JGA, macula densa) → Angiotensinogen → **Angiotensin I** → **ACE (lungs)** → **Angiotensin II** → 
- Aldosterone secretion (adrenal zona glomerulosa) → Na+ retention
- Vasoconstriction (AT1 receptors)
- ADH release
- Thirst
- Sympathetic activation

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **ACE inhibitors** block AT I → AT II conversion → ↓ aldosterone; side effect = dry cough (bradykinin accumulation) | **ARBs** block AT1 receptors — no cough

## Respiratory System

### Lung Volumes and Capacities

| Volume/Capacity | Definition | Normal Value |
|---|---|---|
| Tidal Volume (TV) | Resting breath | **500 mL** |
| Inspiratory Reserve Volume (IRV) | Max additional inspiration after TV | 3000 mL |
| Expiratory Reserve Volume (ERV) | Max additional expiration after TV | 1100 mL |
| Residual Volume (RV) | Air remaining after max expiration | **1200 mL** (**cannot be measured by spirometry**) |
| Total Lung Capacity (TLC) | Total air in lungs at max inspiration | **6000 mL** (TV+IRV+ERV+RV) |
| Vital Capacity (VC) | Max air expired after max inspiration | 4800 mL (TLC – RV) |
| Inspiratory Capacity (IC) | Max air inspired from resting level | 3500 mL (TV+IRV) |
| Functional Residual Capacity (FRC) | Air in lungs at end of normal expiration | **2300 mL** (ERV+RV) — **cannot be measured by spirometry**; measured by helium dilution/body plethysmography |
| FEV1 | Forced expiration in 1st second | **≥ 80% of FVC** |
| FEV1/FVC ratio | — | **≥ 0.7 (70%)** |
| Dead space (anatomical) | Conducting airways | **150 mL** (Fowler's method — N2 washout) |

### Spirometry Patterns

| Pattern | FEV1 | FVC | FEV1/FVC | TLC | RV | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Obstructive | ↓ | Normal or ↓ | **< 0.7 (decreased)** | ↑ or N | ↑ | Asthma, COPD, bronchiectasis |
| Restrictive | ↓ | ↓ | **> 0.7 (normal or increased)** | ↓ | ↓ | Pulmonary fibrosis, obesity, neuromuscular |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **In COPD** — both FEV1 and FVC reduced but FEV1/FVC < 0.7; air trapping → ↑RV → ↑TLC | **In restrictive disease** = all volumes reduced proportionally; FEV1/FVC ratio preserved or increased

**Severity of obstruction (GOLD criteria for COPD):**
- Mild: FEV1 ≥ 80% predicted
- Moderate: 50–79%
- Severe: 30–49%
- Very severe: < 30%

### Hering-Breuer Reflex

- **Stretch receptors** in airway smooth muscle (slowly adapting)
- Activated by lung inflation → inhibit inspiration (prevent over-inflation)
- Afferent via **vagus nerve** (CN X)
- Active at tidal volumes > 1.5 L (not during normal quiet breathing in adults; important in infants)

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Hering-Breuer inflation reflex** = prevents over-inflation | **Deflation reflex** = prevents over-deflation (atelectasis prevention) | Both carried by vagus

### Hypoxia Types

| Type | PaO2 | O2 content | SaO2 | Cause |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Hypoxic (hypoxemic) | ↓ | ↓ | ↓ | Altitude, hypoventilation, V/Q mismatch, diffusion defect, R→L shunt |
| Anemic | Normal | ↓ | Normal | Anemia, CO poisoning, methemoglobinemia |
| Stagnant (ischemic) | Normal | Normal | Normal | Heart failure, shock, arterial occlusion |
| Histotoxic | Normal | Normal | Normal | Cyanide poisoning (blocks cytochrome c oxidase) |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **CO poisoning = anemic hypoxia** (Hb bound by CO can't carry O2, also left-shifts curve) | **Cyanide = histotoxic hypoxia** (tissues can't use O2; venous blood is bright red, not blue) | **PaO2 normal but SaO2 low** → suspect methemoglobinemia

### CO2 Transport

| Form | Percentage |
|---|---|
| Dissolved in plasma | 7–10% |
| **As bicarbonate (HCO3−)** | **70%** (main form) |
| Carbaminohemoglobin | 20–23% |

**CO2 + H2O ⇌ H2CO3 ⇌ H+ + HCO3−** (catalyzed by carbonic anhydrase in RBCs)

**Chloride shift (Hamburger phenomenon):** HCO3− moves out of RBC → Cl− moves in to maintain electroneutrality

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Haldane effect** = deoxygenation of Hb increases CO2 carrying capacity (opposite of Bohr effect) | Carbaminocompounds bind to N-terminus of globin chains (NOT heme) | **Carbonic anhydrase inhibitors (acetazolamide)** → less HCO3− formation → metabolic acidosis → respiratory stimulation (used for altitude sickness)

## Renal System

### GFR and Clearance

**Normal GFR = 125 mL/min** (180 L/day filtered; 1.8 L excreted)

**Clearance formula:** C = (U × V) / P
- U = urinary concentration of substance
- V = urine flow rate (mL/min)
- P = plasma concentration

| Substance | Clearance | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| **Inulin** | = GFR (125 mL/min) | Gold standard (freely filtered, neither secreted nor reabsorbed) |
| **Creatinine** | ~125–130 mL/min | Slight tubular secretion → slightly overestimates GFR; **clinical standard** |
| **PAH (para-aminohippuric acid)** | ~600–700 mL/min | Measures **effective renal plasma flow (ERPF)** — filtered + completely secreted |
| **Glucose** | 0 mL/min (normally) | Completely reabsorbed; appears in urine when plasma > Tm (180 mg/dL) |
| **Urea** | ~70 mL/min | Partially reabsorbed; clearance < GFR |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Clearance > GFR** = tubular secretion occurs | **Clearance < GFR** = tubular reabsorption occurs | **Clearance = GFR** = freely filtered only (inulin, creatinine approximately)

### Tubular Handling

| Segment | Na+ | K+ | Glucose | HCO3− | Other |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **PCT** | 65–70% reabsorbed (Na-K-ATPase) | Reabsorbed | **100% reabsorbed (Tm limited, 180 mg/dL)** | 85–90% reabsorbed | PO4, amino acids, uric acid |
| **Loop of Henle (thick ascending)** | 25% reabsorbed (NKCC2 co-transporter) | Reabsorbed (NKCC2) | — | — | Ca2+ (PTH independent here), Mg2+ |
| **Distal convoluted tubule** | 5% (NCC co-transporter; thiazide-sensitive) | K+ excreted | — | — | Ca2+ (PTH-dependent reabsorption) |
| **Collecting duct** | Na+ reabsorbed (ENaC; aldosterone-dependent) | **K+ secreted (aldosterone)** | — | H+ or HCO3− (pH regulation) | Water (ADH-dependent, aquaporin-2) |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Frusemide (loop diuretic)** = blocks NKCC2 (thick ascending limb) → ↓ Na+, K+, Cl−, Mg2+ reabsorption; also Ca2+ (increases calciuria) | **Thiazides** = block NCC (DCT) → ↓ Na+, Cl−; **increase Ca2+ reabsorption** (used in hypercalciuria/renal stones) | **Spironolactone** = aldosterone antagonist → ↓ Na+ reabsorption + ↓ K+ secretion → K+ sparing

### Countercurrent Mechanism

**Purpose:** Create hyperosmotic medullary interstitium (up to 1200 mOsm/kg at papilla) to concentrate urine

**Countercurrent multiplication (Loop of Henle):**
- Descending limb: Permeable to water (not solutes) → water leaves → tubular fluid becomes concentrated
- Ascending limb (thick): Impermeable to water; actively pumps NaCl out (NKCC2) → dilutes tubular fluid → concentrates interstitium

**Countercurrent exchange (vasa recta):** Maintains medullary gradient without washing it away

**ADH (Vasopressin):**
- Released from posterior pituitary (synthesized in supraoptic nucleus)
- Acts on V2 receptors → cAMP → inserts aquaporin-2 into collecting duct → water reabsorption → concentrated urine
- Released by ↑plasma osmolality (primary stimulus), ↓blood volume, angiotensin II, pain

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Diabetes insipidus (DI):** Central DI = ↓ADH release; Nephrogenic DI = renal resistance to ADH | **SIADH** = excess ADH → hyponatremia (dilutional), concentrated urine, low plasma osmolality | **Desmopressin (DDAVP)** treats central DI

**Aldosterone:**
- Zona glomerulosa (adrenal cortex)
- Increases ENaC expression → ↑ Na+ reabsorption → ↑ K+ secretion → ↑ H+ secretion
- Net: ↑ BP, hypokalemia, metabolic alkalosis
- Stimulated by: Angiotensin II, ↑K+, ACTH (minor)

### Acid-Base Disorders

| Disorder | pH | Primary Change | Compensation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Metabolic acidosis | ↓ | ↓HCO3− | ↓PCO2 (Kussmaul breathing); Winter's formula: PCO2 = 1.5 × HCO3 + 8 ± 2 |
| Metabolic alkalosis | ↑ | ↑HCO3− | ↑PCO2 (hypoventilation) |
| Respiratory acidosis | ↓ | ↑PCO2 | ↑HCO3− (renal; delayed) |
| Respiratory alkalosis | ↑ | ↓PCO2 | ↓HCO3− (renal) |

**Anion gap = Na+ – (Cl− + HCO3−)** Normal: **12 ± 2 mEq/L**

| High AG metabolic acidosis (MUDPILES) | Normal AG (hyperchloremic) metabolic acidosis |
|---|---|
| Methanol, Uremia, DKA, Propylene glycol, Isoniazid/Iron, Lactic acidosis, Ethylene glycol, Salicylates | Diarrhea, renal tubular acidosis (RTA), Addison's disease, carbonic anhydrase inhibitors, ureteral diversions |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **RTA Type 1 (distal)** = can't secrete H+ → urine pH > 5.5 in acidosis; hypokalemia; nephrocalcinosis | **RTA Type 2 (proximal)** = can't reabsorb HCO3−; hypokalemia | **RTA Type 4** = hypoaldosteronism → hyperkalemia (unique — only type with hyperkalemia)

## Endocrine System

### Hypothalamo-Pituitary Axis

| Hypothalamic Hormone | Effect on Pituitary | Pituitary Hormone |
|---|---|---|
| TRH | + | TSH, also prolactin |
| CRH | + | ACTH |
| GnRH (pulsatile) | + | LH, FSH |
| GHRH | + | GH |
| Somatostatin | − | GH, TSH |
| Dopamine | − | **Prolactin** (only anterior pituitary hormone under tonic inhibition) |
| PIH (dopamine) | Inhibits | Prolactin |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Dopamine = main prolactin inhibitor** → any dopamine blocker (metoclopramide, antipsychotics) → hyperprolactinemia | **Prolactin** is the only anterior pituitary hormone under predominant inhibitory control | **GnRH must be pulsatile** for effect; continuous → downregulation (principle behind GnRH analogs in prostate cancer)

**Posterior pituitary hormones (synthesized in hypothalamus, stored in posterior pituitary):**
- **ADH (Vasopressin):** Supraoptic nucleus → V1 (vasoconstriction), V2 (water reabsorption)
- **Oxytocin:** Paraventricular nucleus → uterine contraction, milk ejection (not production), bonding

### Thyroid Hormone Synthesis

1. Iodide uptake (NIS — sodium-iodide symporter) — blocked by **perchlorate, thiocyanate**
2. Oxidation of I− → I2 by **thyroid peroxidase (TPO)** — blocked by **PTU, methimazole, carbimazole**
3. Organification: I2 + tyrosine → MIT (monoiodotyrosine) + DIT (diiodotyrosine)
4. Coupling: DIT + DIT → **T4** (thyroxine, 80%); MIT + DIT → **T3** (20%)
5. Proteolysis of thyroglobulin → T4 and T3 released
6. T4 → T3 peripherally (deiodinase) — T3 is active form (3× more potent)

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **T3 is more potent** (3–4× T4); T4 is prohormone | **PTU** blocks both TPO AND peripheral T4 → T3 conversion (preferred in thyroid storm) | **Methimazole** blocks only TPO | **Wolff-Chaikoff effect** = excess iodide → temporary inhibition of thyroid hormone synthesis | **Jod-Basedow** = iodide load → hyperthyroidism in pre-existing nodular goiter

**Thyroid hormone actions (nuclear receptor, T3 > T4):**
- ↑ BMR, O2 consumption, heat production
- ↑ Na-K-ATPase activity
- ↑ β-adrenergic receptors → ↑ HR, CO
- ↑ protein synthesis
- Bone: ↑ bone turnover
- GH synergy for growth

### Adrenal Cortex Zones and Hormones

| Zone | Hormone | Regulation | Function |
|---|---|---|---|
| Zona glomerulosa (outer) | **Aldosterone** (mineralocorticoid) | Angiotensin II, ↑K+, ACTH (minor) | Na+ retention, K+ excretion |
| Zona fasciculata (middle, largest) | **Cortisol** (glucocorticoid) | ACTH | Glucose ↑, anti-inflammatory, immunosuppression, stress response |
| Zona reticularis (inner) | **DHEA, androgens** | ACTH | Adrenal androgens; precursor to sex steroids |

**Mnemonic:** "GFR = Salt (glomerulosa), Sugar (fasciculata), Sex (reticularis)" — from outside in

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Aldosterone** = ONLY mineralocorticoid released by adrenal cortex; not controlled by ACTH primarily | **Cortisol** = 24-hr cycle with peak at 8 AM (circadian), nadir at midnight | **Addison's disease** = all zones fail → hyponatremia, hyperkalemia, hypotension, hyperpigmentation (↑ACTH/MSH, as CRH/ACTH still elevated)

**Cortisol effects:**
- ↑ gluconeogenesis, ↑ glycogenolysis, ↑ lipolysis
- ↑ protein catabolism → wasting, skin thinning, poor wound healing
- Anti-inflammatory: ↓ phospholipase A2, ↓ COX-2, ↓ NF-κB
- ↑ neutrophils in blood (demargination) but ↓ lymphocytes, eosinophils
- ↑ BP (↑ vascular sensitivity to catecholamines)

### Insulin

**Mechanism:** Glucose → GLUT2 (β-cell) → ↑ATP/ADP → K+ channels close → depolarization → voltage-gated Ca2+ channels open → insulin exocytosis

**Insulin receptor:** Tyrosine kinase (α2β2 tetramer) — when insulin binds → autophosphorylation → IRS → PI3K/Akt pathway

**Actions of Insulin (anabolic):**
| Tissue | Effect |
|---|---|
| Liver | ↑ glycogen synthesis, ↑ glycolysis, ↑ lipogenesis; ↓ gluconeogenesis, ↓ ketogenesis |
| Muscle | ↑ glucose uptake (GLUT4), ↑ glycogen synthesis, ↑ protein synthesis |
| Adipose | ↑ glucose uptake (GLUT4), ↑ lipogenesis; ↓ lipolysis (inhibits HSL) |
| All cells | ↑ K+ uptake (↑ Na-K-ATPase) → hypokalemia |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **GLUT4 = insulin-dependent** (muscle, fat); **GLUT2 = insulin-independent**, high Km (liver, β-cells, small intestine, kidney); **GLUT1 = basal glucose uptake** (brain uses GLUT3 — high affinity, low Km) | **Brain uses glucose even during fasting** (GLUT3); switches to ketones during prolonged starvation

**Counter-regulatory hormones (↑blood glucose):** Glucagon, cortisol, epinephrine, GH (CAGE mnemonic: Cortisol, Adrenaline, Glucagon, Growth Hormone)

### Growth Hormone

- Secreted by somatotrophs (anterior pituitary)
- **Pulsatile, nocturnal peak** (during slow-wave sleep)
- Stimulated by: GHRH, hypoglycemia, exercise, stress, amino acids, deep sleep
- Inhibited by: Somatostatin, IGF-1 (negative feedback), hyperglycemia, obesity

**Direct effects (anti-insulin/diabetogenic):** ↑ lipolysis, ↑ gluconeogenesis, ↑ blood glucose

**Indirect effects (via IGF-1, somatomedin C from liver):** ↑ linear growth, ↑ protein synthesis, ↑ muscle mass, ↑ organ size

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **GH deficiency in children** = proportional short stature | **Acromegaly** (adults, excess GH after epiphyseal fusion) = coarse features, large hands/feet, prognathism, glucose intolerance | **Gigantism** (children) = tall stature | **Best screening test for acromegaly** = IGF-1 | **Confirmation** = oral glucose tolerance test (GH should suppress; in acromegaly it doesn't)

## Neurophysiology

### Resting Membrane Potential

- **−70 mV** (inside negative relative to outside)
- Mainly due to **K+ gradient** (K+ tries to leave → makes inside negative)
- Na-K-ATPase maintains gradient: pumps 3 Na+ out, 2 K+ in (electrogenic — contributes −4 mV)
- At rest: K+ channels open, Na+ channels mostly closed
- **Nernst equation for K+:** EK = −90 mV (actual RMP = −70 mV due to small Na+ leak)
- **Goldman equation** accounts for all ions (K+, Na+, Cl−)

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **K+ most important determinant of RMP** | **Hyperkalemia** → RMP closer to 0 → depolarization → cardiac arrhythmias | **Hypokalemia** → more negative RMP → hyperpolarization → muscle weakness/paralysis

### Action Potential Phases (Neuron)

| Phase | Ion Movement | Channels |
|---|---|---|
| Resting | K+ out ≈ Na+ in (small) | Leaky K+ channels open |
| Threshold (−55 mV) | — | Some Na+ channels open; point of no return |
| Rising/Depolarization | Na+ in rapidly | **Voltage-gated Na+ channels open** |
| Overshoot (+30 mV) | Peak | Na+ channels at maximum |
| Repolarization | K+ out | Na+ channels inactivate; **K+ channels open** |
| Undershoot (AHP) | K+ out continues | K+ channels slow to close |
| Return to RMP | Stabilization | K+ channels close; Na-K-ATPase restores |

**Absolute refractory period:** Na+ channels inactivated — NO action potential possible (during depolarization + early repolarization)
**Relative refractory period:** Some K+ channels still open (hyperpolarized) — suprathreshold stimulus needed

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **TTX (tetrodotoxin)** blocks voltage-gated Na+ channels → blocks action potential | **TEA (tetraethylammonium)** blocks K+ channels | **Local anesthetics** (lidocaine) block Na+ channels preferentially in active neurons (use-dependent block)

### Neuromuscular Junction

1. AP reaches motor nerve terminal
2. **Voltage-gated Ca2+ channels** open → Ca2+ influx
3. Acetylcholine vesicles fuse (SNARE proteins) → ACh released into synaptic cleft
4. ACh binds **nicotinic AChR** (Na+/K+ channel) → EPSP → muscle AP
5. ACh broken down by **acetylcholinesterase** (AChE) → choline recycled

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Botulinum toxin** = cleaves SNARE proteins (SNAP-25, synaptobrevin) → blocks ACh release → flaccid paralysis | **Organophosphates** = irreversibly inhibit AChE → ACh accumulates → SLUDGE (Salivation, Lacrimation, Urination, Defecation, GI distress, Emesis) + miosis, bradycardia | **Myasthenia gravis** = autoantibodies to nicotinic AChR → fatigable weakness | **Lambert-Eaton** = antibodies to presynaptic voltage-gated Ca2+ channels → proximal weakness, improves with repetitive stimulation

**Mnemonic SLUDGE for cholinergic excess:** Salivation, Lacrimation, Urination, Defecation, GI cramping, Emesis

### Synaptic Transmission

| Neurotransmitter | Type | Receptor (Ionotropic) | Receptor (Metabotropic) |
|---|---|---|---|
| ACh | Both | Nicotinic (NMJ, CNS, autonomic ganglia) | Muscarinic M1–M5 |
| Glutamate | Excitatory | AMPA, NMDA, Kainate | mGluR |
| GABA | Inhibitory | **GABAA** (Cl− channel) | GABAB (K+ channel) |
| Glycine | Inhibitory (spinal cord) | Glycine receptor (Cl−) | — |
| Dopamine | Both | — | D1–D5 |
| Serotonin | Mostly excitatory | 5-HT3 | 5-HT1,2,4–7 |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **NMDA receptor** = ligand-gated AND voltage-gated (Mg2+ block removed by depolarization); requires both glutamate AND glycine as co-agonist; key in LTP (long-term potentiation/memory) | **GABA-A receptor** = site of action of benzodiazepines (↑frequency of Cl− channel opening) and barbiturates (↑duration of Cl− channel opening)

### Reflexes

| Reflex | Type | Arc | Receptor |
|---|---|---|---|
| Knee jerk (patellar) | Monosynaptic stretch reflex | L2,L3,L4 | Muscle spindle (Ia afferents) |

### EEG Patterns and Sleep Stages

| Stage/Condition | Wave | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Alert/awake (eyes open) | **Beta (β)** | **> 13 Hz** |
| Relaxed/awake (eyes closed) | **Alpha (α)** | **8–13 Hz** |
| Stage N1 (drowsy) | **Theta (θ)** | 4–7 Hz |
| Stage N2 | Theta + **K-complexes + sleep spindles** | 4–7 Hz |
| Stage N3 (deep/slow-wave sleep) | **Delta (δ)** | **< 4 Hz** |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Delta waves** = deep sleep (N3/SWS) — **bedwetting, sleepwalking, night terrors** occur here | **REM sleep** = dreaming, penile erection, ↑BP + HR, parasomnias (REM behavior disorder) | **Sleep starts with NREM and first REM at 90 minutes** | **REM% increases** toward morning | **Benzodiazepines ↓ slow-wave sleep (N3) and REM**

## Gastrointestinal System

### Gastric Acid Secretion

**Parietal cells** (oxyntic cells) secrete HCl and intrinsic factor

**Stimulants of acid secretion:**
- **Gastrin** (G-cells, antrum) → via CCK-B receptor
- **Histamine** (ECL cells) → via H2 receptors on parietal cells → cAMP → H+/K+ ATPase
- **ACh** (vagus) → via M3 receptors → IP3/DAG → H+/K+ ATPase

**Inhibitors:**
- Somatostatin, secretin, GIP, prostaglandins (PGE2)
- Low pH (< 3) → inhibits gastrin release

#### Phases of Gastric Secretion

| Phase | Stimulus | Mechanism | % of Total Acid |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cephalic** (30%) | Sight/smell/thought of food | Vagus → ACh → parietal + G cells | 30% |
| **Gastric** (60%) | Food in stomach (distension + protein) | Local reflexes + gastrin | 60% |
| **Intestinal** (10%) | Chyme enters duodenum | Small intestinal gastrin (initially stimulatory) | 10% |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Sham feeding** (mock feeding without swallowing) → tests cephalic phase — only vagal | **Zollinger-Ellison syndrome** = gastrinoma → excess gastrin → ↑acid → recurrent peptic ulcers in unusual locations (jejunum) | **Intrinsic factor** (parietal cells) = necessary for B12 absorption; absent in pernicious anemia (autoimmune parietal cell destruction)

### GI Hormones

| Hormone | Secreted From | Stimulus | Actions |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Gastrin** | G-cells (antrum, duodenum) | Protein, vagal stimulation | ↑ acid, ↑ pepsinogen, ↑ gastric motility, ↑ intrinsic factor, trophic to gastric mucosa |
| **Secretin** | S-cells (duodenum) | **Acid** (pH < 4) in duodenum | ↑ pancreatic HCO3− (bicarbonate juice), ↓ gastrin, ↑ bile HCO3−, ↓ gastric acid |
| **CCK** (Cholecystokinin) | I-cells (duodenum, jejunum) | **Fat + protein** in duodenum | ↑ pancreatic enzymes, ↑ gallbladder contraction, ↓ gastric emptying, ↑ Oddi sphincter relaxation, satiety |
| **GIP** (Gastric Inhibitory Peptide / Glucose-dependent Insulinotropic Peptide) | K-cells (duodenum) | **Fat + glucose** | ↓ gastric acid, ↑ insulin release (incretin effect) |
| **Motilin** | M-cells (duodenum/jejunum) | Fasting | Initiates **MMC (migrating motor complex)** — housekeeping contractions between meals |
| **VIP** | Neurons (gut) | ACh | ↑ intestinal secretion, ↓ smooth muscle, ↓ gastric acid |
| **Somatostatin** | D-cells | Acid, fat, glucose | Inhibits all GI hormones and acid, ↓ pancreatic secretion |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **MMC (migrating motor complex)** = organized contractions during fasting, driven by motilin — sweeps residual food and bacteria aborally ("gastric housekeeper") | **Somatostatin = universal inhibitor** of GI functions | **GIP = incretin** — oral glucose causes more insulin release than IV glucose (incretin effect)

### Absorption Sites

| Nutrient | Primary Absorption Site |
|---|---|
| Iron (non-heme) | **Duodenum** (as Fe2+; reduced by vitamin C) |
| Calcium | **Duodenum** (active, vitamin D-dependent) |
| Folic acid | **Jejunum** |
| B12 (cobalamin) | **Terminal ileum** (requires intrinsic factor; also bile salts) |
| Bile salts | **Terminal ileum** (enterohepatic circulation) |
| Fat-soluble vitamins (A,D,E,K) | **Jejunum** (with fat, micelles) |
| Water-soluble vitamins (B,C) | **Jejunum** |
| Glucose, amino acids | **Jejunum** (Na-dependent cotransport — SGLT1) |
| Fructose | **Jejunum** (GLUT5, Na-independent) |
| Water | Colon (60%) + small intestine |
| Na+, Cl−, K+ | Colon (predominantly aldosterone-regulated here) |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Terminal ileum specific:** B12 + bile salts — both are impaired after ileal resection | **Vitamin K** absorbed in jejunum; also synthesized by gut bacteria | **Fat malabsorption** → fat-soluble vitamin deficiency (A, D, E, K) → night blindness, rickets, neurological issues, bleeding

### Motility Patterns

- **Peristalsis:** Progressive contraction + relaxation aborally; myenteric plexus (Auerbach's)
- **Segmentation (mixing):** Small intestine — rhythmic constrictions without net progression
- **MMC:** Fasting motor complex; every 90 minutes; Phase 3 = intense peristalsis
- **Gastroileal reflex:** Gastric filling → propulsion of ileal contents into colon

**Sphincters:**
| Sphincter | Control | Tone |
|---|---|---|
| Lower esophageal sphincter (LES) | Tonic contraction (myogenic); CCK relaxes, gastrin/ACh contracts | **Relaxed by:** VIP, NO, CCK, secretin, fat, alcohol, smoking |
| Ileocecal valve | Sympathetic (contraction), distension proximal (relaxes) | — |
| Internal anal sphincter | Involuntary (smooth muscle) | **Relaxes** with rectal distension (rectoanal inhibitory reflex — absent in Hirschsprung's) |
| External anal sphincter | Voluntary (skeletal muscle, pudendal nerve) | — |

## Special Senses

### Visual Pathway and Field Defects

**Pathway:** Retina → Optic nerve → Optic chiasma → Optic tract → **Lateral geniculate nucleus (LGN, thalamus)** → Optic radiation → Visual cortex (calcarine fissure, occipital lobe)

**At optic chiasma:** Nasal fibers (from nasal retina = temporal visual field) **decussate**; temporal fibers (from temporal retina = nasal visual field) do NOT cross

| Lesion Site | Visual Field Defect |
|---|---|
| Optic nerve (monocular) | Ipsilateral blindness |
| **Optic chiasma (midline, e.g., pituitary adenoma)** | **Bitemporal hemianopia** |
| Optic tract | Contralateral homonymous hemianopia |
| Optic radiation — temporal lobe (Meyer's loop) | Contralateral superior quadrantanopia ("pie in the sky") |
| Optic radiation — parietal lobe | Contralateral inferior quadrantanopia ("pie on the floor") |
| Visual cortex (occipital) | Contralateral homonymous hemianopia with **macular sparing** |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Pituitary tumor compresses optic chiasma from below** → bitemporal hemianopia (temporal visual field loss first = nasal retinal fibers decussate here) | **Macular sparing in occipital lesions** = dual blood supply to macular area (MCA + PCA) | **RAPD (relative afferent pupillary defect)** = optic nerve lesion → swinging flashlight test abnormal

### Pupillary Light Reflex Pathway

**Afferent:** Retina → Optic nerve → Optic tract → **Pretectal nucleus (midbrain)** → bilateral Edinger-Westphal nucleus (CN III parasympathetic nucleus)

**Efferent:** Edinger-Westphal → CN III → Ciliary ganglion → Short ciliary nerves → Sphincter pupillae → **Constriction (miosis)**

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Direct light reflex:** Light in one eye → that eye constricts | **Consensual reflex:** Light in one eye → other eye also constricts (due to bilateral pretectal connections) | **RAPD (Marcus Gunn pupil)** = afferent defect; direct reflex weak in affected eye, but consensual from other eye present | **Adie's (tonic) pupil** = ciliary ganglion damage; sluggish constriction; near-light dissociation

**Accommodation reflex (near vision):**
Cortex → pretectal nucleus → Edinger-Westphal → CN III → ciliary ganglion → ciliary muscle (accommodation) + sphincter pupillae (miosis) + medial recti (convergence)

**Argyll Robertson pupil:** "Prostitute's pupil" — accommodates but doesn't react (to light); midbrain dorsal lesion (neurosyphilis, DM); small irregular pupils

### Cochlea

- **Organ of Corti** on basilar membrane
- **Tonotopy:** High frequency (16,000 Hz) at base; Low frequency (20 Hz) at apex
- Hair cells: **Inner hair cells** (sensory, 95% of auditory nerve fibers) + outer hair cells (amplification via prestin)
- **Endolymph** (in scala media/cochlear duct): High K+, low Na+ (like intracellular fluid) — produced by stria vascularis
- **Perilymph** (scala vestibuli + tympani): Like ECF (high Na+)
- **Endocochlear potential:** +80 mV (maintained by stria vascularis)

**Deafness types:**
| Type | Rinne Test | Weber Test | Cause |
|---|---|---|---|
| Conductive | AC < BC (Rinne negative) | Lateralizes to AFFECTED ear | Middle ear (otitis media, otosclerosis) |
| Sensorineural | AC > BC (Rinne positive, but reduced) | Lateralizes to NORMAL ear | Inner ear/cochlear nerve (noise, aging, Meniere's) |

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **Normal Rinne** = AC > BC (positive); **Conductive hearing loss** = BC > AC (negative Rinne) | **Weber lateralizes to good ear** in SNHL; to bad ear in conductive loss

### Vestibular System

- **Semicircular canals** → detect angular acceleration (rotation)
- **Utricle + saccule (otolith organs)** → detect linear acceleration + gravity
- **Macula** of utricle (horizontal) + saccule (vertical) → hair cells with otoliths (calcium carbonate crystals)

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **BPPV (benign paroxysmal positional vertigo)** = otoconia (loose otoliths) in posterior semicircular canal → positional vertigo; treated by **Epley maneuver** | **Caloric test** (COWS): Cold Opposite, Warm Same — cold water → nystagmus to opposite side; warm water → nystagmus to same side

**Vestibulo-ocular reflex (VOR):** Head rotation → compensatory eye movement in opposite direction → maintains stable gaze; afferent via CN VIII, efferent via CN III/IV/VI (MLF connects)

> PYQ HOTSPOT: **MLF (medial longitudinal fasciculus) lesion** (MS) → internuclear ophthalmoplegia (INO): ipsilateral adduction failure on lateral gaze + contralateral abducting nystagmus
