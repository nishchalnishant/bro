## Year 2019

**Exam Date:** January 20, 2019
**Pattern:** Traditional recall-heavy paper; last year before major structural overhaul

### Theme of the Year
Pure recall and fact-based. Textbook definitions, specific numbers, and named syndromes dominated. Clinical vignettes were short (2–3 lines). High emphasis on pre-clinical subjects (Anatomy, Physiology, Biochemistry collectively ~35% of paper).

### Subject Distribution (Approximate)

| Subject | Questions |
|---|---|
| Medicine + Psychiatry + Dermatology | 55–60 |
| Surgery + Orthopedics + Anesthesia | 35–40 |
| OBG + Pediatrics | 35–40 |
| Pre-clinical (Anat + Physio + Biochem) | 30–35 |
| Para-clinical (Path + Pharma + Micro) | 40–45 |
| SPM/Community | 15–18 |
| ENT + Ophthalmology + Radiology + Forensic | 25–30 |

### High-Yield Topics That Appeared (2019)

1. **Brachial plexus injuries** — Erb's palsy vs Klumpke's, which nerve roots, which muscles affected
2. **Oxygen dissociation curve** — Bohr effect, 2,3-DPG, shifts in both directions with clinical correlation
3. **Vitamin B12 metabolism** — methylmalonyl CoA → succinyl CoA pathway; deficiency causing subacute combined degeneration
4. **Reed-Sternberg cells** in Hodgkin lymphoma — CD markers, subtypes
5. **Drug receptor pharmacology** — agonist vs antagonist, partial agonist efficacy concept
6. **HBV serology window period** — which markers present, which absent
7. **Antenatal care schedule** — number of visits, investigations at each visit per national guidelines
8. **Developmental milestones** — social smile, pincer grasp, stranger anxiety at specific ages
9. **Preeclampsia management** — MgSO4 dosing (Pritchard vs Zuspan regimen)
10. **Glaucoma drugs** — mechanism of prostaglandin analogues, timolol contraindications
11. **Time of death estimation** — rigor mortis sequence, algor mortis formula
12. **Biostatistics** — sensitivity/specificity definitions, PPV relationship with prevalence
13. **COPD spirometry** — FEV1/FVC < 0.7, GOLD staging criteria
14. **Fracture complications** — fat embolism syndrome after long bone fracture

### Pattern Observations
- Image-based questions: ~5–8% (mostly X-rays, a few histology slides)
- Very few questions required multi-step reasoning
- "Which of the following is NOT..." format used frequently
- Last year with this recall-dominant format

## Year 2020

**Exam Date:** January 5, 2020
**Pattern:** First paper conducted by NBE under new structure; slight increase in clinical vignettes; some image-based questions added

### Theme of the Year
Bridging year — retained recall base but introduced more case-based framing. SPM questions increased notably. Pharmacology had higher clinical relevance (drug of choice emphasis). Forensic medicine was relatively straightforward.

### Subject Distribution Changes vs 2019
- **SPM increased** by ~3–4 questions
- **Pharmacology** shifted toward "drug of choice" and clinical scenario type
- **Anatomy** reduced slightly
- Clinical subjects (Medicine, Surgery) maintained quota

### High-Yield Topics That Appeared (2020)

1. **ECG interpretation** — STEMI vs NSTEMI criteria, Wellens pattern (biphasic T in V2/V3 = critical LAD stenosis)
2. **Autoimmune hepatitis** — anti-smooth muscle antibody, anti-LKM, immunosuppression
3. **Wilson's disease** — Kayser-Fleischer rings, ceruloplasmin levels, hepatic copper
4. **Drug-induced lupus** — which drugs (hydralazine, procainamide, isoniazid), anti-histone antibodies
5. **Cholesteatoma** — bone erosion, most common CN affected (facial nerve/VII), surgical management
6. **Malignant hyperthermia** — trigger agents, RYR1 mutation, dantrolene treatment
7. **Placenta previa types** — grading system, clinical presentation, management
8. **Kawasaki disease** — diagnostic criteria, IVIG regimen, coronary artery complication
9. **Organophosphate poisoning** — SLUDGE features, cholinergic crisis, pralidoxime window (< 24 hours)
10. **Herd immunity** — concept, threshold calculation, which diseases most affected
11. **Vitamin D metabolism** — 25-hydroxylation in liver, 1-alpha hydroxylation in kidney, active form
12. **Retinoblastoma** — RB1 gene, Knudson's two-hit hypothesis, bilateral vs unilateral
13. **Compartment syndrome** — 5 P's, fasciotomy pressure threshold (> 30 mmHg or within 30 mmHg of diastolic)
14. **Cervical cancer screening** — Pap smear schedule, Bethesda classification

### Pattern Observations
- Negative marking began to influence test-taking behavior (more visible 2020 onward)
- Drug of choice questions replaced "mechanism of action" questions in some instances
- Tables and decision-making algorithms reflected in question format
- **Image percentage rose** to ~10–12%

## Year 2021

**Exam Date:** Two sessions — January 10, 2021 and April 18, 2021 (COVID disruption)
**Key Change:** Exam was conducted twice; January paper was pre-COVID-wave and April was after second wave. April paper had subtly different flavor.

### Theme of the Year
**COVID-19 integration** — questions on COVID virology, respiratory management, and public health measures appeared for the first time. Clinical reasoning questions increased substantially. Memory-only questions decreased by estimate 15–20% compared to 2019.

### Subject Distribution Changes
- **Respiratory medicine** questions increased (ARDS, ventilator management, COVID complications)
- **Microbiology** had novel virology questions (RNA viruses, vaccine mechanisms)
- **Community medicine** had COVID-specific public health questions

### High-Yield Topics That Appeared (2021)

1. **ARDS** — Berlin definition criteria (P/F ratio < 300), prone positioning, lung-protective ventilation
2. **COVID-19** — SARS-CoV-2 mechanism, spike protein, ACE2 receptor, vaccine types (mRNA concept)
3. **Sepsis-3 criteria** — qSOFA, SOFA score, organ dysfunction definition
4. **Anticoagulation** — DOACs (rivaroxaban, apixaban) mechanism, monitoring, reversal agents
5. **SLE** — SLICC 2012 criteria, anti-dsDNA, anti-Smith, complement consumption
6. **Tumor markers** — PSA, AFP, CEA, CA-125, CA 19-9 — cancer associations and screening use
7. **Neonatal jaundice** — phototherapy thresholds by gestational age, exchange transfusion criteria
8. **Intussusception** — age group, presentation, sonographic target sign, hydrostatic vs pneumatic reduction
9. **Ewing sarcoma vs osteosarcoma** — comparative features, imaging, chromosomal translocations
10. **ECT (Electroconvulsive therapy)** — indications, absolute vs relative contraindications, cognitive effects
11. **Hereditary spherocytosis** — ankyrin/spectrin defects, osmotic fragility test, splenomegaly
12. **Normal pressure hydrocephalus** — wet, wobbly, wacky triad; communicating hydrocephalus; LP as treatment
13. **Preterm labor tocolysis** — nifedipine, atosiban, betamethasone timing for lung maturity
14. **Food poisoning** — Staphylococcal (rapid onset, preformed toxin), Salmonella (12–24 hours), Clostridium
15. **Regional anesthesia** — spinal vs epidural differences, advantages, specific drugs used

### Pattern Observations
- **First appearance of COVID-related questions** — 3–5 questions directly COVID themed
- Clinical vignettes now averaged 4–5 lines (longer than 2019)
- More questions required elimination of distractors rather than positive recall
- **January 2021 paper** slightly more recall-based; **April 2021** more clinical reasoning oriented

## Year 2022

**Exam Date:** March 12, 2022
**Key Change:** First exam after NEET PG reform discussions; significant increase in image-based questions; NMC guidelines reflected

### Theme of the Year
**Clinical reasoning and image interpretation.** The paper moved decisively away from isolated recall. Images (X-rays, CT, histology, clinical photographs) constituted ~18–22% of questions. Integrated clinical scenarios became the norm — a single patient presentation spanning medicine, pharmacology, and pathology.

### Notable Shifts
- **Pre-clinical subjects** (Anatomy, Physiology, Biochemistry) dropped to ~20–22% combined
- **Clinical subjects** rose proportionally
- **Image questions** nearly doubled vs 2019
- **SPM** maintained high weightage (~10%)

### High-Yield Topics That Appeared (2022)

1. **CT chest findings** — COVID-19 ground glass vs organizing pneumonia pattern; PE finding (Hampton's hump)
2. **Diabetic ketoacidosis** — diagnostic criteria, anion gap calculation, treatment protocol (insulin, fluid)
3. **Thyroid storm** — Burch-Wartofsky score, treatment priorities (propranolol, PTU, iodine sequence)
4. **Acute pancreatitis** — Ranson's criteria (early vs 48 hours), BISAP score, management
5. **Renal cell carcinoma** — clear cell histology, VHL mutation, paraneoplastic syndromes, targeted therapy
6. **Postpartum hemorrhage** — causes (4 T's: Tone, Tissue, Trauma, Thrombin), management algorithm
7. **Meniere's disease** — triad, endolymphatic hydrops, dietary restriction, Epley vs Semont
8. **Leprosy reactions** — Type 1 reversal reaction vs Type 2 ENL, treatment differences
9. **Clozapine adverse effects** — agranulocytosis monitoring, metabolic syndrome, seizure threshold lowering
10. **SGLT2 inhibitors** — cardiorenal protective mechanism (not just glucose lowering), heart failure benefit
11. **Antiphospholipid syndrome** — lupus anticoagulant paradox (↑aPTT in vitro but thrombosis in vivo), pregnancy loss
12. **Acoustic neuroma** — CPA angle tumor, audiogram pattern, MRI with gadolinium
13. **Burns management** — Parkland formula (4 mL/kg/% BSA), first 8 hours vs next 16 hours
14. **Fragile X syndrome** — trinucleotide repeat, most common heritable intellectual disability, FMR1 gene

### Pattern Observations
- **Image integration was mandatory** to solve ~20% of questions
- "Theme question" clusters appeared — same patient revisited in 2–3 questions (mini-case format)
- Pharmacology questions shifted strongly toward adverse effects and drug interactions
- Community medicine had several questions on national programs and their updates

## Year 2023

**Exam Date:** March 5, 2023
**Key Change:** NMC's competency-based curriculum explicitly reflected; highest clinical integration seen; Radiology questions increased

### Theme of the Year
**Competency-based integrated clinical approach.** Questions were framed as real clinical decisions — "next step in management," "most appropriate investigation," "which drug would you choose given this profile." Pure recall questions were now clearly a minority. Ethics and medicolegal questions increased.

### Notable Shifts
- **"Next best step" format** dominated — probably 40–45% of questions in this format
- **Radiology integration** — imaging incorporated into clinical questions, not standalone image Q
- **Recent drug approvals** — biologics, targeted therapies tested
- **Ethics** — 3–5 questions on informed consent, patient autonomy, research ethics

### High-Yield Topics That Appeared (2023)

1. **Heart failure management** — HFrEF quadruple therapy (ACEi/ARB/ARNI, beta-blocker, MRA, SGLT2i) — evidence-based combinations
2. **Lung cancer** — EGFR, ALK, PD-L1 testing; targeted therapy vs immunotherapy selection
3. **Stroke management** — thrombolysis window (4.5 hours), thrombectomy indications, contraindications
4. **Hypothyroidism in pregnancy** — TSH targets, levothyroxine adjustment, effect on fetal brain
5. **Microbiome and gut dysbiosis** — C. difficile colitis, fecal microbiota transplantation
6. **Hemophilia A vs B** — factor VIII vs IX, X-linked recessive, specific treatments (recombinant factors, emicizumab)
7. **Ovarian cancer** — BRCA1/2 testing, PARP inhibitors (olaparib), FIGO staging
8. **Juvenile idiopathic arthritis** — types, uveitis association (oligoarticular), ANA positivity
9. **Sickle cell disease** — hydroxyurea mechanism, fetal hemoglobin induction, vaso-occlusive crisis management
10. **Celiac disease** — anti-tTG IgA antibodies, HLA-DQ2/DQ8, duodenal biopsy findings, GFD
11. **PCOS** — Rotterdam criteria (2 of 3), insulin resistance, metformin vs letrozole for ovulation
12. **Autism spectrum disorder** — DSM-5 criteria, early screening (M-CHAT), no curative pharmacotherapy
13. **Deep vein thrombosis** — Wells score interpretation, D-dimer utility, DOAC choice and duration
14. **Inborn errors** — MSUD (maple syrup urine disease), propionic acidemia — newborn screening integration
15. **Infective endocarditis** — modified Duke criteria, echocardiographic major criteria, antibiotic combinations

### Pattern Observations
- **Least recall-heavy paper** in the 2019–2024 series
- Drug questions required knowing indication + mechanism + monitoring simultaneously
- Ethics questions appeared for the first time as a recognizable category
- **Pediatric immunization** had IAP 2023 schedule questions (new vaccine additions)
- Difficulty was perceived as highest in this series by most candidates

## Year 2024

**Exam Date:** March 3, 2024
**Key Change:** Paper reverted slightly toward balanced recall + clinical; some subjects rebounded; post-NMC reform stabilization

### Theme of the Year
**Balanced hybrid approach.** After 2023's steep clinical reasoning demand, 2024 found middle ground. Core concepts tested via clinical scenarios (not pure recall), but specific numerical values, diagnostic thresholds, and named signs/criteria remained important. Image-based questions stabilized at ~18–20%.

### Notable Shifts
- **Pre-clinical subjects rebounded** slightly (~25% combined)
- **Community medicine** reached highest weight ever (~11–12%)
- **Pharmacology** had strong adverse effects and new drug theme
- Difficulty perceived as moderate — easier than 2023

### High-Yield Topics That Appeared (2024)

1. **Metabolic syndrome** — IDF criteria, ATP III criteria, waist circumference cutoffs (Asian population)
2. **Anti-VEGF therapy** — intravitreal injections, agents for diabetic macular edema and wet ARMD (faricimab)
3. **Immune checkpoint inhibitors** — irAE (immune-related adverse events): colitis, pneumonitis, thyroiditis
4. **GLP-1 receptor agonists** — semaglutide weight loss mechanism, CV outcome trials (LEADER, SUSTAIN)
5. **Hemochromatosis** — HFE gene (C282Y/H63D), transferrin saturation, serum ferritin, liver biopsy hepatic iron index
6. **Primary hyperaldosteronism** (Conn's) — hypertension + hypokalemia + metabolic alkalosis; adrenal CT, AVS
7. **Severe acute maternal morbidity** (near miss) — WHO definition, hospital-based audit
8. **Pediatric sepsis** — Sepsis-3 criteria applied to children, antibiotic de-escalation
9. **Bipolar disorder** — lithium monitoring (renal, thyroid), teratogenicity (Ebstein's anomaly), toxicity signs
10. **Collagen disorders** — Ehlers-Danlos types, Marfan syndrome FBN1 gene, aortic involvement
11. **Pancreatic adenocarcinoma** — KRAS mutation (90%), CA 19-9 marker, double duct sign on CT
12. **OSA** (Obstructive sleep apnea) — AHI classification, CPAP indications, cardiovascular risk
13. **Vitamin D toxicity** — hypercalcemia, calcium deposition, differentiation from deficiency
14. **Spirometry interpretation** — mixed obstructive-restrictive pattern, bronchodilator reversibility

### Pattern Observations
- **Post-COVID questions** normalized — not explicitly COVID but long COVID manifestations in general medicine
- New drug approvals (2022–2023) tested in several subjects
- National program updates (immunization, cancer screening policy) reflected in community medicine
- **Genomics/molecular diagnostics** appeared in pathology, oncology, and pediatrics

## Prediction for Upcoming Exam (Based on 2019–2024 Trends)

### Format Predictions
- **Image-based questions** will maintain ~18–22% — invest in X-ray, CT, histology recognition
- **Clinical vignette length** will remain 5–7 lines — practice case-based reading speed
- **"Next best step" management questions** will form 35–45% of the paper
- **Pure recall** will be < 30% — but numerical thresholds and specific criteria still tested

### Subject-Wise Predictions

**Likely to INCREASE:**
- Community Medicine — national health programs update yearly; always has new content
- Radiology — integration with clinical subjects growing; AI and new modalities appearing
- Pharmacology — new drugs (biologics, targeted therapy, gene therapy) continue entering syllabus
- Oncology (across Medicine, Surgery, Gynecology) — molecular markers, targeted therapy

**Likely to MAINTAIN:**
- OBG and Pediatrics — core competencies stable; always 10–12% each
- Medicine — largest subject block; stable at 10–12%
- Pathology — consistently hard; maintaining weight

**Likely to STABILIZE (not increase further):**
- Anatomy — floor reached around 2022; unlikely to drop further
- Forensic Medicine — stable low-yield; 4–6% consistently

### High-Probability Topics for Next Exam

1. **GLP-1 receptor agonists** — mechanism, CV and renal benefits, weight loss, comparison of agents
2. **Immune checkpoint inhibitors** — PD-1/PD-L1 drugs, adverse effects (irAEs), which cancers
3. **SGLT2 inhibitors** — non-diabetic CKD, heart failure across EF spectrum
4. **AI in diagnostics** — radiology AI, diabetic retinopathy screening — conceptual questions
5. **Faricimab** (bispecific antibody) for ARMD/DME — new drug, high probability
6. **Post-COVID complications** — long COVID cardiac, neurological, pulmonary sequelae
7. **Gene therapy** — specific diseases (SMA, LCA, hemophilia) — mechanism + disease
8. **FIGO 2023 staging** updates for gynecological cancers — if not yet tested in your year
9. **Microbiome** — FMT in C. difficile, dysbiosis in IBD, clinical application questions
10. **Updated national immunization schedule** — any new vaccine additions made in the past year
11. **Epigenetics clinical applications** — methylation biomarkers in cancer
12. **Monoclonal antibodies in dermatology** — dupilumab, lebrikizumab for atopic dermatitis
13. **Sepsis in special populations** — neonatal sepsis, obstetric sepsis, immunocompromised
14. **Mitochondrial diseases** — MELAS, MERRF, Leigh syndrome — maternal inheritance pattern
15. **DSM-5-TR changes** — prolonged grief disorder, new specifiers in common disorders

### Strategic Recommendations Based on Trend Analysis

1. **Do not ignore SPM** — it has steadily grown and rewards structured preparation
2. **Master ECG** — appears every single year in some form; 3–5 guaranteed questions
3. **Learn FIGO/WHO/criteria-based diagnostics** — SLE, preeclampsia, pancreatitis severity scoring appear repeatedly
4. **Image recognition** is no longer optional — dedicate 15–20% of study time to this
5. **Recent drug approvals** (2022–2024) from each specialty will likely be tested
6. **Integration is key** — a question on RA will test pathology (erosive arthritis) + pharmacology (DMARDs, biologics) + medicine (criteria)
