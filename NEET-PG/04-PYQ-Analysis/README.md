# 04 — PYQ Analysis

This folder contains past year question (PYQ) analysis for NEET PG. It breaks down question distribution by subject and year, identifies high-yield topics based on repeat frequency, and provides trend data to guide where to invest study time. Use this data to prioritize subjects with the highest question counts and to spot topics that appear consistently across multiple years.

## Files

| File | Description |
|------|-------------|
| `pyq-analysis-overview.md` | Top-level summary of PYQ trends, overall paper distribution, and key strategic takeaways |
| `Subject-Wise/subject-wise-pyq.md` | Question counts and percentage share broken down by subject across recent NEET PG papers |
| `Year-Wise/year-wise-pyq.md` | Year-by-year question breakdown showing how paper composition has shifted over time |
| `High-Yield-Topics/high-yield-topics.md` | Curated list of topics with the highest repeat frequency — the must-know list before exam day |

## How to Use

- Start with `pyq-analysis-overview.md` to get a macro view of where questions come from, then drill into subject-wise or year-wise files for detail.
- Use `Subject-Wise/subject-wise-pyq.md` to rank subjects by question count and allocate study hours proportionally — subjects contributing 20+ questions per paper deserve the most revision time.
- Use `Year-Wise/year-wise-pyq.md` to detect rising or falling trends; if a subject has gained 3–5 questions over the last two cycles, weight it higher in your final-month plan.
- Cross-reference `High-Yield-Topics/high-yield-topics.md` with your weak-topics trackers in `../02-Subject-Notes/*/WEAK-TOPICS.md` to ensure your highest-gap areas align with the highest-yield PYQ topics.

## Key Insights

- Medicine leads all subjects at an average of ~23 questions per paper and has shown a consistent upward trend over the last three exam cycles.
- Clinical subjects (Medicine, Surgery, OBG, Paediatrics, Orthopaedics, PSM) collectively account for approximately 55% of the total paper, making clinical reasoning the single most important skill to develop.
- Surgery and OBG each contribute ~15–18 questions; together they represent nearly 15–17% of the paper and are too large to treat as secondary subjects.
- Pathology and Pharmacology are the highest-yield pre-clinical subjects (~10–12 questions each) and have the greatest overlap with clinical question stems — strong foundation in both multiplies returns across the whole paper.
- Repeat topics (questions on the same theme in 3+ consecutive years) cluster in specific areas: cardiac pharmacology, hepatology, obstetric emergencies, growth & development, and community medicine calculations — mastering these repeaters alone can secure 15–20 marks.
