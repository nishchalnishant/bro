## 3. Weekly Performance Graph Template

### Manual Plotting Instructions

Draw a line graph on graph paper or in a notebook with:
- **X-axis**: GT number (GT1, GT2, GT3... in chronological order)
- **Y-axis**: Score out of 800 (range: 200–800, intervals of 50)
- Plot two lines: one for total score, one for accuracy percentage (scale accuracy × 8 to map to 800 scale)
- Draw a horizontal target line at your goal score

### Digital Tracking (Recommended)
Use a spreadsheet (Google Sheets / Excel):
1. Column A: GT number
2. Column B: Date
3. Column C: Total score
4. Column D: Accuracy %
5. Column E: Percentile
6. Insert > Chart > Line chart — select columns A, C, D, E

**What to look for in the graph**:
- Upward trend in score = preparation is working
- Flat line for 3+ consecutive GTs = plateau — change revision method
- Score improves but accuracy drops = attempting more questions recklessly — slow down
- High variance (zigzag) = inconsistency, likely syllabus gaps causing subject-dependent scores

### Trend Analysis Instructions

After 5+ GTs, do this analysis monthly:

1. **Moving average**: Calculate average score of last 3 GTs. Compare to average of GTs 4–6 before that. Is it higher? Lower? Same?
2. **Accuracy vs attempt rate correlation**: As attempt rate increased, did accuracy drop? This reveals overconfidence patterns
3. **Best GT conditions**: Which GTs had your best scores? What was different (day of week, time, sleep, prep the day before)?
4. **Worst GT conditions**: Any patterns in your worst GTs?
5. **Subject oscillation**: Are any subjects consistently your best across GTs? Are any consistently your worst? Stable patterns indicate structural gaps — not bad luck

Platform percentiles are useful only in context. Here is what to aim for at each stage:

| Time to Exam | Minimum Percentile | Target Percentile | Context |
|--------------|-------------------|--------------------|---------|
| 6 months out | Not applicable — start tracking, not ranking | 50th+ | Establish baseline |
| 5 months out | 55th | 70th | Pre-clinical completion expected |
| 4 months out | 65th | 78th | Para-clinical completion expected |
| 3 months out | 72nd | 85th | All subjects attempted at least once |
| 2 months out | 80th | 90th | Revision cycle 2 in progress |
| 1 month out | 87th | 94th | Full revision nearly complete |
| 2 weeks out | 90th | 96th | Final revision, no new topics |

### Platform Percentile Inflation Adjustment

Different platforms have different test-taker pools:
- **PrepLadder/Marrow GTs**: Large pool (50,000+ test takers), competitive. Percentile here is reliable
- **Smaller platforms**: Smaller pool = percentile can be inflated. A 90th percentile on a 500-person test means nothing
- **Rule**: Only compare percentiles from the same platform. Cross-platform score comparison (not percentile) is more reliable

### Percentile Interpretation by Pool Size

| Platform Pool Size | 90th percentile reliability | Recommendation |
|-------------------|-----------------------------|----------------|
| >30,000 test takers | High | Trust percentile |
| 10,000–30,000 | Moderate | Use as direction |
| <10,000 | Low | Ignore percentile, focus on raw accuracy |

## 8. Final Month Performance Protocol

The last month is not for learning new things. It is for using your performance data to deploy what you already know more effectively.

### Step 1: Sort Your Subjects (Week 1 of Final Month)

Using the Subject Classification Matrix, divide all 19 subjects into:
- **Protect** (Strong): Maintain with 1–2 quick revision sessions. Do not over-invest
- **Convert** (Average): These are your rank-moving subjects. Targeted revision can push accuracy from 65% to 75% with 1 week of focus
- **Stabilize** (Weak): Aim to reach 60% accuracy. High-yield topics only — no deep dives
- **Triage** (Critical): Accept the floor. Focus on the most predictable/high-yield 30% of these subjects only

### Step 2: Build Final Month GT Schedule

| Week | GT Frequency | Focus Between GTs |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| Week 1 | 2 GTs | Identify top 3 weak subjects. Targeted revision |
| Week 2 | 2 GTs | Revise all "Convert" subjects. Review error notebook |
| Week 3 | 3 GTs | Full simulation mode. Revise Never Again list daily |
| Week 4 (final) | 2 GTs (stop 3 days before exam) | Light revision only. Sleep, routine, mindset |

### Step 3: Final Week Decision Framework

Use this framework to decide what to revise in the final 7 days:

| Decision | Criteria |
|---------|---------|
| Revise heavily | Subject accuracy below 65% AND subject has 10+ questions in exam |
| Revise moderately | Subject accuracy 65–74% OR subject has 5–9 questions in exam |
| Revise lightly | Subject accuracy 75%+ AND consistent across GTs |
| Do not revise | Subject accuracy >80% AND stable trend — risk of overthinking |

### Step 4: 72-Hour Pre-Exam Protocol

- **3 days before**: Last GT (if planned). Review error notebook. Review Never Again list
- **2 days before**: Only high-yield one-liners, mnemonics, and Never Again list. No new questions
- **1 day before**: Rest. Light review of formulas, normal values, classifications only. Stop by 6 PM
- **Exam morning**: Wake up at your usual time. Eat normally. Review Never Again list (max 30 min). No new topics
