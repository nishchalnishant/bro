# 07 — Grand Tests

This folder contains resources for full-length mock tests that simulate actual NEET PG exam conditions — 200 MCQs, timed to 210 minutes, with negative marking. Grand tests are the primary tool for building exam stamina, identifying weak subject clusters under pressure, and calibrating time management before the real paper. Use this folder to plan, execute, and review mock attempts in a structured way.

## Files

| File | Description |
|------|-------------|
| `Mock-Tests/mock-test-guide.md` | Instructions for setting up and running a full-length mock test, including sourcing question banks and maintaining exam-like conditions |
| `Performance-Tracker/performance-tracker.md` | Template and running log to record scores, subject-wise accuracy, and time-per-section across multiple mock attempts |
| `Error-Notebook/error-notebook-template.md` | Template for logging every question answered incorrectly — captures the question, your wrong answer, the correct answer, and the reason for error |

## Test Strategy

- Simulate real conditions every time: sit for the full 210 minutes, no breaks, phone off, and mark answers as you would on the actual paper — anything less than full simulation gives misleading data.
- After each mock, spend at least as long reviewing as you spent attempting — every wrong answer gets logged in `Error-Notebook/error-notebook-template.md` before you move on.
- Use `Performance-Tracker/performance-tracker.md` to track subject-wise accuracy across attempts; a subject with accuracy below 50% across two consecutive mocks is a confirmed weak area.
- Feed weak-subject findings back into the subject-specific `WEAK-TOPICS.md` files in `../02-Subject-Notes/*/WEAK-TOPICS.md` — the grand test review and the subject notes system must stay in sync.
- Space grand tests strategically: one per week in the final 6 weeks, stepping up to one every 3–4 days in the final 2 weeks; do not attempt a grand test within 48 hours of the real exam.

## NEET PG Exam Format

200 MCQs | +4 per correct answer | −1 per wrong answer | 210 minutes total
