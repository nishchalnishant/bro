# Comparison Tables — NEET PG High-Yield

Frequently tested comparison tables. Each table covers distinguishing features between conditions that are commonly confused in MCQs.

---

## 1. DKA vs HHS (Hyperosmolar Hyperglycemic State)

| Feature | DKA | HHS |
|---|---|---|
| Patient type | Type 1 DM (mainly) | Type 2 DM (mainly elderly) |
| Precipitant | Infection, missed insulin, new DM | Infection, dehydration, medications |
| Blood glucose | >250 mg/dL (usually 250–500) | >600 mg/dL (often 800–1200) |
| Ketones | +++  (β-hydroxybutyrate elevated) | Absent or minimal (trace) |
| pH | <7.3 (acidosis) | >7.3 (normal or mildly reduced) |
| Bicarbonate | <18 mEq/L | >15 mEq/L (usually normal) |
| Anion gap | Elevated (>12) | Normal or mildly elevated |
| Osmolality | <320 mOsm/kg | >320 mOsm/kg (often 350+) |
| Consciousness | Usually conscious (may be obtunded) | Often severely altered (coma common) |
| Mortality | 1–5% | 10–20% |
| Onset | Hours to 1 day | Days to weeks |
| Kussmaul breathing | Yes (deep, rapid — blow off CO₂) | No |
| Fluid deficit | ~4–6 L | ~8–12 L (larger) |
| Treatment | IV fluids + IV insulin infusion + K⁺ | IV fluids (main) + low-dose insulin; avoid hypoglycemia |
| Insulin sensitivity | Lower (requires more insulin) | More sensitive to insulin |

## 2. Crohn's Disease vs Ulcerative Colitis

| Feature | Crohn's Disease | Ulcerative Colitis |
|---|---|---|
| Part of GI tract | Any part (mouth to anus) — "skip lesions" | Colon only; starts at rectum, continuous |
| Rectum involvement | Spared (perianal disease may occur) | Almost always involved |
| Distribution | Discontinuous (skip areas) | Continuous (from rectum upward) |
| Layer involvement | Full thickness (transmural) | Mucosa + submucosa only |
| Histology | Non-caseating granulomas, fissures | Crypt abscesses, pseudopolyps |
| Blood in stool | Less common | Very common (bloody diarrhea) |
| Fistulae | Common (entero-enteric, enterocutaneous, rectovaginal) | Rare |
| Strictures | Common (fibrosis — transmural) | Less common |
| Perianal disease | Common (fissures, fistulae, skin tags) | Rare |
| Cobblestone mucosa | Yes (on colonoscopy) | No |
| String sign on barium | Yes (terminal ileum — "string sign of Kantor") | No |
| Lead pipe appearance | No | Yes (loss of haustra, rigid colon) |
| Cancer risk | Slight increase | Significant increase (proportional to extent and duration) |
| Smoking | Worsens Crohn's | Protects (ex-smokers at risk) |
| NSAIDs | May worsen | May worsen |
| Surgery | Not curative (recurs) | Curative (proctocolectomy) |
| Serology | Anti-Saccharomyces cerevisiae (ASCA+) | p-ANCA positive |
| Extra-intestinal manifestations | More common (joints, skin, eye, liver) | Also present |

## 3. Nephrotic vs Nephritic Syndrome

| Feature | Nephrotic Syndrome | Nephritic Syndrome |
|---|---|---|
| Proteinuria | Massive (>3.5 g/day in adults) | Mild to moderate (<3.5 g/day) |
| Hematuria | Absent or mild | Prominent (RBC casts — hallmark) |
| Hypertension | Mild or absent | Common and significant |
| Edema | Severe (generalized — periorbital, anasarca) | Mild to moderate |
| GFR | Normal initially | Reduced (azotemia common) |
| Lipiduria | Yes (fatty casts, oval fat bodies) | No |
| Serum albumin | Markedly reduced (<3 g/dL) | Normal or mildly reduced |
| Serum cholesterol | Elevated (hyperlipidemia) | Normal |
| Mechanism | Increased permeability (protein leak) | Inflammation + immune complex |
| Classic examples | Minimal change, membranous, FSGS, diabetic nephropathy | Post-strep GN, IgA nephropathy, Goodpasture, MPGN |
| Urinalysis | Protein +++, fat bodies | RBCs, RBC casts, protein+ |
| Complications | Hypercoagulable state (loss of protein C, S, antithrombin), hypercholesterolemia, infection risk | Acute kidney injury, hypertensive complications |
| Thrombosis risk | High (renal vein thrombosis in membranous) | Low |

## 4. Type 1 vs Type 2 DM

| Feature | Type 1 DM | Type 2 DM |
|---|---|---|
| Age of onset | Typically <30 years (can occur at any age) | Typically >40 years (but rising in young) |
| Body habitus | Lean, normal weight | Obese (80–90% of cases) |
| Pathogenesis | Autoimmune destruction of β-cells | Insulin resistance + progressive β-cell failure |
| Insulin levels | Very low / absent (absolute deficiency) | Normal to high initially; reduces with time |
| C-peptide | Low or undetectable | Normal or elevated |
| Autoantibodies | Anti-GAD, anti-islet (ICA), anti-insulin, anti-ZnT8 | Absent |
| HLA association | HLA-DR3, DR4 | No specific HLA |
| Ketoacidosis | Common (DKA) | Rare (HHS more common) |
| Insulin requirement | Mandatory lifelong | Initially diet/oral drugs; may need insulin later |
| Genetic concordance (twins) | 30–50% | 70–90% (stronger genetic component) |
| Family history | Less strong | Strong |
| Onset | Acute, often symptomatic | Insidious (years of undetected hyperglycemia) |
| First-line pharmacotherapy | Insulin (basal-bolus) | Metformin |
| Islet histology | Lymphocytic infiltration (insulitis) | Amyloid deposition (islet amyloid polypeptide — IAPP) |

## 5. RA vs OA vs Ankylosing Spondylitis

| Feature | Rheumatoid Arthritis | Osteoarthritis | Ankylosing Spondylitis |
|---|---|---|---|
| Age/Gender | Middle-aged women | Elderly (>50), both sexes | Young men (20–30s) |
| Joints | Small joints (MCP, PIP, wrist) | Weight-bearing (knee, hip), DIP | Sacroiliac joints, spine |
| Pattern | Symmetric, polyarthritis | Asymmetric, oligoarthritis | Axial predominant |
| Morning stiffness | >1 hour | <30 minutes | >1 hour; improves with activity |
| Pain character | Worse in morning, improves with use | Worse with activity, improves with rest | Worse at night/rest, improves with exercise |
| Nodules | Rheumatoid nodules (extensor surfaces) | Heberden's (DIP) and Bouchard's (PIP) nodes | No |
| X-ray | Joint space narrowing, erosions, osteopenia | Osteophytes, joint space narrowing, subchondral sclerosis | Sacroiliitis, bamboo spine, squaring of vertebrae |
| Lab findings | RF+, Anti-CCP+, elevated ESR/CRP | Normal labs | HLA-B27 positive (90%), elevated ESR |
| Systemic features | Yes (lungs, heart, eyes, skin) | No | Yes (uveitis, aortitis, IBD) |
| Extra-articular | Interstitial lung disease, vasculitis | None | Uveitis (anterior), plantar fasciitis |
| Treatment | DMARDs (methotrexate first line); biologics (anti-TNF, anti-IL6) | NSAIDs, physiotherapy, joint replacement | NSAIDs (first line); anti-TNF; no DMARDs |

## 6. Benign vs Malignant Tumors (General Features)

| Feature | Benign | Malignant |
|---|---|---|
| Growth rate | Slow | Fast, uncontrolled |
| Encapsulation | Usually encapsulated | Non-encapsulated; infiltrative |
| Borders | Well-defined | Ill-defined, irregular |
| Differentiation | Well-differentiated (resembles parent tissue) | Poorly differentiated (anaplastic in high grade) |
| Mitoses | Rare, normal | Frequent, atypical (tripolar, bizarre) |
| Necrosis | Absent | Common |
| Recurrence after removal | Rare | Common |
| Metastasis | Never | Hallmark of malignancy |
| Invasion | No | Yes (local, lymphatic, hematogenous) |
| Systemic effects | Local compression only | Cachexia, paraneoplastic syndromes |
| Nuclear features | Normal (N:C ratio normal) | Hyperchromatic, pleomorphic, high N:C ratio |
| Prognosis | Excellent | Depends on type, stage, grade |
| Examples | Lipoma, fibroadenoma, leiomyoma | Liposarcoma, carcinoma, leiomyosarcoma |

## 7. Hodgkin's vs Non-Hodgkin's Lymphoma

| Feature | Hodgkin's Lymphoma (HL) | Non-Hodgkin's Lymphoma (NHL) |
|---|---|---|
| Peak incidence | Bimodal: 15–35 + >50 | Increasing with age (usually >50) |
| Reed-Sternberg cells | Pathognomonic (CD15+, CD30+) | Absent |
| Presentation | Cervical lymphadenopathy | Extranodal disease common |
| Spread | Contiguous (node to adjacent node) | Non-contiguous, random |
| B symptoms | Common (fever, night sweats, weight loss) | Less predictable |
| Mediastinal involvement | Common (nodular sclerosis subtype) | Less common |
| Extranodal disease | Rare | Common (bone marrow, liver, GI) |
| Bone marrow involvement | Late | Early (especially low-grade NHL) |
| EBV association | Present (especially mixed cellularity) | Some (Burkitt's t(8;14)) |
| Subtypes | NLPHL, Nodular Sclerosis, Mixed Cellularity, Lymphocyte-rich, Lymphocyte-depleted | Diffuse large B-cell (DLBCL), Follicular, Burkitt's, Mantle cell, etc. |
| Most common subtype (HL) | Nodular Sclerosis (Western countries) | DLBCL (most common NHL worldwide) |
| Treatment | ABVD (doxorubicin, bleomycin, vinblastine, dacarbazine) | CHOP ± rituximab (R-CHOP) |
| Curability | Highly curable (>90% cure in early stage) | Varies by type (DLBCL 60–70% cure; follicular indolent) |
| Prognosis marker | IPS (International Prognostic Score) | IPI (International Prognostic Index) |

## 8. AML vs ALL vs CML vs CLL

| Feature | AML | ALL | CML | CLL |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cell type | Myeloid blast | Lymphoid blast | Myeloid (all stages) | Mature B-lymphocyte |
| Age | Any age; peak 15–40 | Children (peak 2–5 years) | Middle-aged adults (40–60) | Elderly (>60) |
| WBC | Variable | Very high | Very high (>100k possible) | Very high (lymphocytes) |
| Blast cells in blood | Yes (>20% required for AML diagnosis) | Yes | <10% in chronic phase | No blasts (mature small lymphocytes) |
| Auer rods | Present (pathognomonic for AML) | Absent | Absent | Absent |
| Philadelphia chromosome | 20–25% of AML (poor prognosis) | 25% of adult ALL | 95%+ of CML (t(9;22)) | Absent |
| Smudge cells | Absent | Absent | Absent | Classic finding (CLL) |
| Basophilia | Absent | Absent | Characteristic | Absent |
| Splenomegaly | Moderate | Moderate | Massive (prominent feature) | Moderate |
| TdT (terminal deoxynucleotidyl transferase) | Negative | Positive (marker of lymphoid precursor) | Negative | Negative |
| Leukemoid reaction (LAP score) | — | — | Low (distinguishes from leukemoid) | — |
| Specific immunophenotype | CD13, CD33, MPO | CD10 (CALLA), CD19 (B-ALL); CD3 (T-ALL) | Not used for diagnosis | CD19, CD23, CD5 co-expression (characteristic) |
| Best prognosis | Depending on mutation | Childhood B-ALL (90% cure) | Excellent on imatinib/TKI | Indolent; variable |
| Targeted therapy | Imatinib (if BCR-ABL+) | Imatinib (if Ph+) | Imatinib, dasatinib (TKIs) | Ibrutinib, venetoclax |
| Treatment of choice | Cytarabine + anthracycline (7+3) | Induction: vincristine + prednisone + L-asparaginase | Imatinib (1st line TKI) | Watch-and-wait; ibrutinib |
| APL subtype | AML-M3 (t(15;17)); ATRA responsive | — | — | — |

## 9. Upper Motor Neuron (UMN) vs Lower Motor Neuron (LMN) Lesion

| Feature | UMN Lesion | LMN Lesion |
|---|---|---|
| Tone | Increased (spasticity — velocity-dependent) | Decreased (flaccidity) |
| Power/Weakness | Weakness (groups of muscles) | Weakness (individual muscles) |
| Reflexes (DTRs) | Exaggerated (hyperreflexia) | Diminished or absent (hyporeflexia) |
| Babinski sign | Positive (extensor plantar response) | Negative (flexor or absent) |
| Clonus | Present (>3 beats) | Absent |
| Fasciculations | Absent | Present (spontaneous muscle twitching) |
| Wasting/Atrophy | Minimal (disuse atrophy) | Significant (denervation atrophy) |
| Distribution | Pyramidal pattern (extensors arm, flexors leg in hemiplegia) | Individual muscle or nerve/root distribution |
| Location of lesion | Cortex, internal capsule, brainstem, spinal cord (above anterior horn) | Anterior horn cells, nerve roots, peripheral nerves, NMJ, muscle |
| Examples | Stroke, MS, cord compression, ALS (UMN component) | Polio, GBS, peripheral neuropathy, motor neuron disease (LMN component) |

## 10. Spinal Cord Syndromes

| Feature | Brown-Sequard Syndrome | Anterior Cord Syndrome | Central Cord Syndrome | Posterior Cord Syndrome |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cause | Hemisection (penetrating injury, MS, compression) | Anterior spinal artery occlusion; flexion injury | Hyperextension injury in elderly (cervical spondylosis) | Posterior spinal artery, tabes dorsalis |
| Motor | Ipsilateral spastic weakness below lesion | Bilateral flaccid paralysis at level + spastic below | Weakness — upper limbs > lower limbs (hand/arm most affected) | Normal motor |
| Pain and Temperature | Contralateral loss (2–3 levels below — spinothalamic tract) | Bilateral loss | Bilateral loss (bilateral spinothalamic) | Normal |
| Proprioception/Vibration | Ipsilateral loss below (dorsal column) | Intact | Often preserved | Bilateral loss (dorsal column) |
| Bladder function | Ipsilateral UMN bladder dysfunction | Urinary retention | Urinary retention (very common) | Impaired |
| Touch | Ipsilateral loss (dorsal column) | Bilateral loss | Variable | Bilateral loss |
| Prognosis | Best prognosis (most recovery) | Poor | Moderate (better than anterior) | Good for motor; sensory persists |
| Clinical scenario | Stab wound to spine | Cardiac surgery, aortic aneurysm repair | Whiplash/hyperextension injury, elderly fall | Syphilis (tabes), B12 deficiency |

## 12. Shock Types — Hemodynamic Parameters

| Parameter | Hypovolemic | Cardiogenic | Distributive (Septic) | Obstructive |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cause | Hemorrhage, dehydration, burns | MI, arrhythmia, cardiac tamponade | Sepsis, anaphylaxis, neurogenic | PE, tension pneumothorax, cardiac tamponade |
| HR | ↑ | ↑ | ↑ (or normal early) | ↑ |
| BP | ↓ | ↓ | ↓ | ↓ |
| Cardiac Output (CO) | ↓ | ↓↓ | ↑ (early sepsis) or ↓ (late) | ↓ |
| SVR (systemic vascular resistance) | ↑ | ↑ | ↓ (warm shock early) or ↑ (cold shock late) | ↑ |
| PCWP (preload) | ↓ | ↑ | ↓ | Variable (↑ in tamponade) |
| CVP | ↓ | ↑ | ↓ | Variable |
| Skin | Cold, pale, clammy | Cold, pale, clammy | Warm, flushed (early); cold (late) | Cold, pale |
| JVD | Absent | Present (raised JVP) | Absent | Present (in tamponade/PE) |
| Beck's triad | No | No | No | Yes (tamponade): JVD + muffled heart sounds + hypotension |
| Kussmaul's sign | No | No | No | Yes (tamponade/constrictive pericarditis) |
| Treatment | IV fluids | Inotropes (dobutamine) ± IABP | IV fluids + vasopressors (norepinephrine) + antibiotics | Treat cause (chest drain for tension PTX; pericardiocentesis for tamponade) |

## 13. Obstructive vs Restrictive Lung Disease

| Feature | Obstructive | Restrictive |
|---|---|---|
| Pathology | Airway obstruction; air trapping | Reduced lung compliance or chest wall restriction |
| FEV1 | ↓ | ↓ |
| FVC | Normal or ↓ | ↓↓ |
| FEV1/FVC ratio | ↓ (<70%) — DIAGNOSTIC criterion | Normal or ↑ (>70–80%) |
| TLC | Increased (hyperinflation — COPD, emphysema) | Decreased |
| RV | Increased (air trapping) | Decreased |
| FRC | Increased | Decreased |
| DLCO (diffusion capacity) | Decreased in emphysema; normal in asthma/bronchitis | Decreased (interstitial disease); normal (chest wall) |
| Flow-volume loop shape | Concave expiratory limb (scooped out) | Normal shape but reduced volumes |
| Examples | COPD, asthma, bronchiectasis, cystic fibrosis | IPF, sarcoidosis, kyphoscoliosis, obesity, pleural disease, neuromuscular |
| Reversibility | Partial (asthma) or fixed (COPD) | Fixed |
| Bronchodilator response | Significant in asthma (≥12% and ≥200 mL increase in FEV1) | Absent |

## 14. APH: Placenta Previa vs Abruptio Placentae

| Feature | Placenta Previa | Abruptio Placentae |
|---|---|---|
| Bleeding type | Revealed (external) | Concealed (internal) or revealed |
| Pain | Painless ("painless antepartum hemorrhage") | Painful (sudden onset, constant) |
| Uterus | Soft, non-tender, relaxed | Tense, rigid ("woody hard"), tender |
| Uterine contractions | Absent | Often present (uterine irritability) |
| Fetal presentation | Malpresentation common (floating head, transverse lie) | Normal presentation |
| Fetal heart sounds | Usually audible | May be absent (fetal distress/death) |
| Coagulopathy (DIC) | Rare | Common (especially with concealed hemorrhage) |
| Retroplacental clot | Absent | Present (dark clot behind placenta) |
| Diagnosis | USG (transvaginal or transabdominal) | Clinical; USG may be normal |
| Rectal examination | NEVER (risk of torrential hemorrhage) | Not contraindicated but rarely done |
| Maternal mortality | Lower | Higher (DIC, concealed hemorrhage) |
| Fetal prognosis | Good (if delivered timely) | Worse (hypoxia, preterm delivery) |
| Risk factors | Previous CS/uterine surgery, multiparity, advanced age | Hypertension, trauma, cocaine, smoking, PROM |
| Treatment | Conservative (remote from term, stable) or CS | Depends on severity; delivery often expedited |

## 15. Pemphigus Vulgaris vs Bullous Pemphigoid

| Feature | Pemphigus Vulgaris | Bullous Pemphigoid |
|---|---|---|
| Age | Middle-aged (40–60 years) | Elderly (>60 years) |
| Blister type | Flaccid, thin-roofed, easily rupture | Tense, thick-roofed, do not rupture easily |
| Oral involvement | Very common (often the presenting site) | Less common and less severe |
| Pruritus | Usually absent | Marked pruritus |
| Nikolsky sign | Positive | Negative |
| Level of blister | Intraepidermal (suprabasal — above basal layer) | Subepidermal (below basement membrane) |
| Acantholysis | Present (Tzanck cells/acantholytic cells) | Absent |
| Antibody | Anti-desmoglein 3 (mucous membranes); anti-desmoglein 1 (skin) | Anti-BP180 (BPAG2) and Anti-BP230 (BPAG1) |
| DIF pattern | Intercellular IgG + C3 (fish net / chicken wire) | Linear IgG + C3 along BMZ |
| Eosinophilia | Absent | Often present (blood and tissue eosinophilia) |
| Prognosis | Worse (fatal without treatment) | Better (may remit spontaneously) |
| Treatment | High-dose steroids; rituximab (anti-CD20) for severe cases | Topical or systemic steroids; doxycycline + niacinamide |

## 16. Hanging vs Strangulation (Forensic Features)

| Feature | Hanging | Strangulation |
|---|---|---|
| Manner of death | Usually suicidal (judicial: always) | Usually homicidal |
| Ligature mark direction | Oblique, runs upward toward knot; V-shaped at back of neck | Horizontal (transverse), circular, low on neck |
| Ligature mark completeness | Incomplete (break at knot site in suicidal hanging) | Complete (encircles neck fully) |
| Level of ligature mark | High (above thyroid cartilage) | Low (at or below thyroid cartilage) |
| Face color | Pale (typical hanging); congested if incomplete suspension | Congested, cyanosed, plethoric |
| Petechiae (Tardieu spots) | Uncommon in typical hanging | Common (conjunctival, facial, scleral) |
| Hyoid bone fracture | Less common | More common (especially manual strangulation) |
| Thyroid cartilage fracture | Rare | More common |
| Dribbling of saliva | Present (from angle of mouth toward dependent side) | Absent |
| Carotid intima tears | Can occur | Less common |
| Postmortem lividity | Lower limbs and forearms if body suspended | Face, neck, upper chest (congested) |
| Cyanosis | Absent (typically) in typical hanging | Present |
| Brain changes | Congestion/edema in partial suspension | Venous congestion |
| Ligature material | Rope, wire, cloth | Hands (manual), ligature |

## 17. Primary vs Secondary Amenorrhea

| Feature | Primary Amenorrhea | Secondary Amenorrhea |
|---|---|---|
| Definition | No menarche by age 15 (with secondary sexual characteristics) or age 13 (without any secondary sexual characteristics) | Cessation of previously normal menses for >3 months (or >6 months if irregular) |
| Most common cause | Gonadal dysgenesis (Turner's syndrome 45,XO) | Pregnancy (always exclude first) |
| FSH/LH elevated causes | Gonadal failure (Turner's, premature ovarian insufficiency) | Premature ovarian insufficiency, POI |
| FSH/LH low/normal causes | Hypothalamic (constitutional delay, Kallmann's) | Hypothalamic (functional — stress, exercise, weight loss) |
| Uterus absent | Mullerian agenesis (MRKH syndrome; 46,XX), AIS (46,XY — complete androgen insensitivity) | — |
| Associated features | Depends on cause (webbed neck in Turner's, anosmia in Kallmann's) | Galactorrhea (prolactinoma), hot flashes (premature menopause), virilization |
| Investigations | Karyotype (if Turner's suspected), FSH, LH, prolactin, imaging | Pregnancy test first; then prolactin, TSH, FSH, estradiol |
| Treatment | Depends on cause; HRT for gonadal dysgenesis | Treat underlying cause |

## 18. Thyroid Function Tests in Different Thyroid Conditions

| Condition | TSH | Free T4 | Free T3 | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Primary hypothyroidism | ↑↑ | ↓ | ↓ | Most common: Hashimoto's thyroiditis |
| Secondary hypothyroidism (pituitary) | ↓ or normal | ↓ | ↓ | Pituitary failure (pan-hypopituitarism) |
| Subclinical hypothyroidism | ↑ (usually <10) | Normal | Normal | Treat if TSH >10 or symptomatic |
| Primary hyperthyroidism | ↓ (suppressed) | ↑ | ↑ | Graves', toxic MNG, toxic adenoma |

| Subclinical hyperthyroidism | ↓ (suppressed) | Normal | Normal | Treat if symptomatic; higher AF risk |

## 19. Glaucoma Types Comparison

| Feature | Primary Open-Angle Glaucoma (POAG) | Primary Angle-Closure Glaucoma (PACG) — Acute | Normal Tension Glaucoma | Secondary Glaucoma |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Angle | Open | Closed (trabecular meshwork obstructed by iris) | Open | Open or closed |
| IOP | Elevated (>21 mmHg) | Very high (often >40 mmHg) | Normal (<21 mmHg) | Elevated |
| Presentation | Insidious, painless, gradual visual field loss | Acute, painful red eye, halos around lights | Insidious (like POAG) | Depends on cause |
| Cornea | Clear | Steamy (epithelial edema) | Clear | Variable |
| Pupil | Normal | Mid-dilated, fixed (irreactive) | Normal | Variable |
| Disc | Cupped (C/D ratio >0.6) | May be cupped | Cupped | May be cupped |
| Visual field | Peripheral loss (arcuate scotoma, nasal step) | Acute loss | Central vision affected (early) | Depends on cause |
| Systemic associations | Cardiovascular risk factors | Hypermetropia (shallow AC), Asian origin | Vascular disease (hypotension, migraine) | Diabetes, steroids, trauma, uveitis |
| First-line treatment | Prostaglandin analogues (latanoprost — once daily) | Emergency: IV mannitol + pilocarpine + acetazolamide → laser iridotomy | Prostaglandin analogues | Treat underlying cause |
| Definitive surgery | Trabeculectomy | Laser peripheral iridotomy | Trabeculectomy | Varies |

## 20. Hearing Loss: CHL vs SNHL

| Feature | Conductive Hearing Loss (CHL) | Sensorineural Hearing Loss (SNHL) |
|---|---|---|
| Pathology | Problem in outer or middle ear (sound conduction) | Cochlea or auditory nerve (perception) |
| Causes | Otosclerosis, otitis media, wax, perforation, ossicular chain disruption | Presbycusis, noise-induced, Meniere's, ototoxic drugs, acoustic neuroma |
| Air conduction | Reduced | Reduced |
| Bone conduction | Normal (or relatively better) | Reduced (same as air conduction) |
| Air-Bone gap | Present (AC > BC gap) | Absent (AC and BC both reduced equally) |
| Rinne test | Negative (BC > AC) | Positive (AC > BC — same as normal; false negative if profound SNHL) |
| Weber test | Lateralizes to affected (worse) ear | Lateralizes to unaffected (better) ear |
| Schwabach test | Increased (BC prolonged compared to examiner) | Decreased |
| Speech discrimination | Good (words sound quieter but not distorted) | Poor (words distorted even when loud enough) |
| Audiogram | Air-bone gap with normal bone conduction curve | Both curves depressed equally; no air-bone gap |
| Recruitment | Absent | Present (in cochlear SNHL — disproportionate loudness sensitivity) |
| Tympanogram | Type B (flat, no peak) in middle ear effusion; Type As (reduced compliance) in otosclerosis | Type A (normal) — middle ear not affected |
| Treatment | Medical (remove wax, treat OME) or surgical (stapedectomy for otosclerosis, tympanoplasty) | Hearing aids; cochlear implant for severe/profound bilateral SNHL |
| Cochlear implant candidacy | Not indicated (surgery more appropriate) | Severe-profound bilateral SNHL not benefiting from hearing aids |
