## Anatomy

**#1 Concept: Dermatomes & nerve injuries — the single highest-yield anatomy topic**

### Must-Know One-Liners
- **Saturday night palsy** = radial nerve injury → wrist drop; **sparing of triceps** if lesion distal to spiral groove
- **Axillary nerve** injury in surgical neck of humerus fracture → loss of deltoid + teres minor + regimental badge area loss
- **Ulnar nerve** at medial epicondyle → claw hand (ring + little finger); **median nerve** at wrist → ape thumb deformity
- Femoral nerve injury → loss of knee extension (quadriceps); **obturator nerve** → loss of adduction
- **Common peroneal nerve** injury → foot drop (dorsiflexion + eversion lost)
- Portal vein formed by **superior mesenteric vein + splenic vein** behind neck of pancreas
- Meckel's diverticulum: **2 feet from ileocecal valve, 2 inches long, in 2% population, at age 2, 2:1 male:female**

### Critical Numbers
- **Femoral canal** length: 1.25 cm
- **Inguinal ligament** = ASIS to pubic tubercle
- Left recurrent laryngeal nerve loops around **arch of aorta**; right loops around **subclavian artery**
- **McBurney's point**: 1/3 from ASIS on line to umbilicus

### Classic Differentials
- **Erb's palsy** (C5,C6 — waiter's tip) vs **Klumpke's** (C8,T1 — claw hand + Horner's if T1 sympathetics involved)
- **Carpal tunnel** (median nerve at wrist — thenar wasting, Tinel's positive) vs **cubital tunnel** (ulnar at elbow — hypothenar wasting)

### #1 Mnemonic
**"2s of Meckel's"**: 2%, 2 feet, 2 inches, 2 types of mucosa (gastric/pancreatic), 2:1 male, presents in 1st 2 years

## Physiology

**#1 Concept: Cardiac Action Potential phases — basis for antiarrhythmic drug mechanism questions**

### Must-Know One-Liners
- **Phase 0** = rapid Na+ influx; **Phase 2** (plateau) = Ca2+ influx = target of CCBs
- **Starling's law**: Increased end-diastolic volume → increased stroke volume (preload dependence)
- **Dead space**: Anatomical ~150 mL; physiological = anatomical + alveolar dead space
- **2,3-DPG** increases → right shift of O2 dissociation curve → decreased O2 affinity (more O2 release)
- **Cushing reflex**: hypertension + bradycardia + irregular breathing = raised ICP (brainstem compression)
- **GFR** controlled by afferent (dilate → ↑GFR) and efferent (constrict → ↑GFR) arterioles
- **Renin** secreted by JG cells of afferent arteriole; macula densa senses NaCl

### Critical Numbers
- Normal GFR: **125 mL/min**; renal plasma flow: **650 mL/min**; filtration fraction: **20%**
- Vital capacity: **4.8 L** (male); FEV1/FVC normal: **>80%**; TLC: **6 L**
- Resting membrane potential: **-70 mV** (nerve); **-90 mV** (cardiac muscle)
- **P50** (O2 dissociation): **26 mmHg**

### Classic Differentials
- **Obstructive** (FEV1/FVC <70%, TLC normal/↑, RV↑) vs **Restrictive** (FEV1/FVC >80%, TLC↓, RV↓)
- **Metabolic acidosis** with high anion gap (MUDPILES) vs normal anion gap (hyperchloremic — RTA, diarrhea)

### #1 Mnemonic
**MUDPILES** (high AG metabolic acidosis): Methanol, Uremia, DKA, Paraldehyde, Isoniazid/Iron, Lactic acidosis, Ethylene glycol, Salicylates

## Biochemistry

**#1 Concept: Enzyme deficiencies in storage disorders — direct one-liner questions**

### Must-Know One-Liners
- **Gaucher's**: glucocerebrosidase deficiency → Gaucher cells (crumpled paper cytoplasm); most common lysosomal storage disorder
- **Niemann-Pick**: sphingomyelinase deficiency → foam cells; cherry red spot
- **Tay-Sachs**: Hex-A deficiency → GM2 ganglioside accumulation; cherry red spot; NO hepatosplenomegaly
- **Phenylketonuria**: phenylalanine hydroxylase deficiency; musty odor; treat with phenylalanine-restricted diet
- **Alkaptonuria**: homogentisic acid oxidase deficiency → dark urine, ochronosis
- **HbA1c** measures glycemic control over **last 3 months** (RBC lifespan)
- **Rate-limiting enzyme of glycolysis**: Phosphofructokinase-1 (PFK-1)

### Critical Numbers
- **Normal blood glucose** (fasting): 70–100 mg/dL; **diabetes**: fasting ≥126 mg/dL, random ≥200 mg/dL
- **HbA1c** target in diabetics: **<7%**
- Cholesterol synthesis rate-limiting enzyme: **HMG-CoA reductase**
- **Km** = substrate concentration at half-maximal velocity (measure of enzyme affinity — LOW Km = HIGH affinity)

### Classic Differentials
- **Tay-Sachs** (no organomegaly, cherry red spot) vs **Niemann-Pick** (hepatosplenomegaly, cherry red spot) vs **Gaucher's** (no cherry red spot, bone crisis)
- **Competitive inhibition** (Km↑, Vmax same) vs **Non-competitive** (Km same, Vmax↓)

### #1 Mnemonic
**"Gaucher's has GUTS"**: Glucocerebrosidase, crUmpled paper cells, Type 1 most common, Splenomegaly dominant

## Pathology

**#1 Concept: Types of necrosis and their classic disease associations**

### Must-Know One-Liners
- **Coagulative necrosis**: ischemic infarcts (except brain) — cell outline preserved
- **Liquefactive necrosis**: brain infarcts, bacterial abscesses — cell outline lost
- **Caseous necrosis**: TB — cheese-like, granulomatous center
- **Reed-Sternberg cells** = classic Hodgkin's lymphoma; **owl-eye appearance**
- **Virchow's triad**: stasis + hypercoagulability + endothelial injury → DVT/PE
- **BRCA1**: breast + ovarian cancer (chromosome 17); **BRCA2**: breast + male breast (chromosome 13)
- **p53** = most commonly mutated tumor suppressor in human cancers; "guardian of genome"

### Critical Numbers
- **ESR** normal: male <15 mm/hr (Westergren), female <20 mm/hr
- **Amyloid** stains with **Congo red** → apple-green birefringence under polarized light
- **CML**: Philadelphia chromosome (9;22) → BCR-ABL; treated with **imatinib**
- **Down syndrome**: trisomy 21; risk ↑ with maternal age >35

### Classic Differentials
- **Hodgkin's** (RS cells, contiguous spread, good prognosis, B symptoms) vs **NHL** (non-contiguous, extranodal, varied prognosis)
- **Wet gangrene** (venous, infected, black, smelly, gas) vs **Dry gangrene** (arterial, mummified, clear margins)

### #1 Mnemonic
**"CATCH-22"** DiGeorge (Cardiac defects, Abnormal facies, T-cell deficit, Cleft palate, Hypocalcemia — chromosome 22 deletion)

## Pharmacology

**#1 Concept: Drug of choice (DOC) for major conditions — the single highest-yield pharm topic**

### Must-Know One-Liners
- **DOC malaria** (P. falciparum): Artemisinin-based combination therapy (ACT); **chloroquine** for P. vivax/ovale
- **DOC status epilepticus**: IV **lorazepam** (benzodiazepine first); then phenytoin/fosphenytoin
- **DOC hypertension in pregnancy**: **Methyldopa** (safe in all trimesters); labetalol/nifedipine alternatives
- **DOC TB**: HRZE × 2 months, then HR × 4 months (2HRZE/4HR)
- **Warfarin antidote**: Vitamin K (slow) + Fresh Frozen Plasma (immediate); **heparin antidote**: protamine sulfate
- **Neostigmine** = anticholinesterase → used to reverse non-depolarizing NMBs; **NOT effective** for succinylcholine
- **Black box warning**: fluoroquinolones — tendon rupture, QT prolongation, peripheral neuropathy

### Critical Numbers
- **Digoxin** toxic level: >2 ng/mL; therapeutic: 0.5–2 ng/mL
- **Lithium** therapeutic range: **0.6–1.2 mEq/L**; toxic >1.5 mEq/L
- **Aspirin** analgesic dose: 300–600 mg; antiplatelet: **75–150 mg/day**
- **Paracetamol** toxicity antidote: **N-acetylcysteine** (within 8 hours most effective)

### Classic Differentials
- **Succinylcholine** (depolarizing — fasciculations first, no reversal, hyperkalemia risk) vs **Rocuronium** (non-depolarizing — reversed by neostigmine/sugammadex)
- **Metformin** (no hypoglycemia, weight neutral, CI in renal failure eGFR<30) vs **Sulfonylureas** (hypoglycemia, weight gain)

### #1 Mnemonic
**"SHEMALE"** drugs causing SLE: Sulfonamides, Hydralazine, Ethosuximide, Methyldopa, Anti-TNF agents, Lithium, isoniazid (INH), Estrogens

## Microbiology

**#1 Concept: Vaccine-preventable diseases and their causative agents — NMC priority topic**

### Must-Know One-Liners
- **Mantoux test** positive: induration ≥10 mm (5 mm in immunocompromised/HIV); read at **48–72 hours**
- **VDRL** = screening for syphilis (non-treponemal); **FTA-ABS** = confirmatory (treponemal)
- **Weil-Felix test**: Rickettsial infections — OX-19 (typhus), OX-2 (spotted fever), OX-K (scrub typhus)
- **Negri bodies**: intracytoplasmic inclusions in rabies (hippocampus/Purkinje cells)
- **Koplik's spots**: measles (pathognomonic); appear before rash on buccal mucosa
- **ASO titre**: elevated in post-streptococcal glomerulonephritis; helps confirm rheumatic fever
- **Gram-positive cocci in clusters**: Staph; **chains**: Strep; **diplococci**: Pneumococcus/Neisseria

### Critical Numbers
- **Sputum AFB positivity**: need **5000–10,000 bacilli/mL** for smear positivity
- **CD4 count** <200/μL: AIDS-defining; start ART regardless of CD4 in all HIV+
- **Widal test**: O antigen titre >1:160 (or 4-fold rise) significant for typhoid
- **Incubation period rabies**: 1–3 months (range 10 days–7 years)

### Classic Differentials
- **Staphylococcus aureus** (golden colonies, coagulase+, protein A, PVL toxin) vs **Staph epidermidis** (coagulase−, biofilm, prosthetic infections)
- **Meningococcal meningitis** (petechial rash, Waterhouse-Friderichsen) vs **Pneumococcal** (lobar pneumonia, no rash, commonest bacterial meningitis in adults)

### #1 Mnemonic
**"Some Nasty Killers Have Pretty Rotten Insides"**: Sporothrix, Nocardia, Klebsiella, Histoplasma, Pseudomonas, Rhodotorula — causes of **opportunistic infections in immunocompromised**

## Forensic Medicine

**#1 Concept: Changes after death and their time correlations — directly tested in pattern**

### Must-Know One-Liners
- **Rigor mortis**: starts **1–2 hours** after death, complete by **6–12 hours**, disappears by **24–36 hours** (at 24°C)
- **Cadaveric lividity (livor mortis)**: appears **2–4 hours**, fixed by **6–12 hours** → used to confirm position of body
- **Putrefaction**: starts **24–48 hours**; first sign = greenish discoloration over right iliac fossa
- **Adipocere**: saponification of body fat in wet conditions; preserves body shape for months-years
- **Café coronary**: food bolus impaction in larynx → choking death; Heimlich maneuver
- **Brain death criteria**: absence of brainstem reflexes + apnea test positive; confirmed by 2 doctors
- **IPC 302**: murder; **IPC 304**: culpable homicide not amounting to murder; **IPC 304A**: rash/negligent act

### Critical Numbers
- **Alcohol**: legal driving limit in India: **30 mg/100 mL blood**; drunk = >100 mg/100 mL; lethal = >400 mg/100 mL
- **Vitreous potassium**: increases ~**14 mEq/L/day** after death → used to estimate time of death
- **Defensive wounds**: on **palms and forearms** (homicide); **hesitation cuts**: on wrists (suicide)
- Age of consent in India: **18 years** (POCSO Act)

### Classic Differentials
- **Homicidal hanging** (ligature mark above thyroid, oblique, non-continuous) vs **Suicidal hanging** (mark above thyroid, oblique, continuous, parchmentized)
- **Antemortem bruise** (vital reaction, inflammation, red/blue) vs **Postmortem bruise** (no vital reaction, dark red)

### #1 Mnemonic
**"ABCDE of Death Changes"**: Algor (cooling), Bloating (putrefaction), Cadaveric lividity, Decomposition, Eye changes (corneal clouding)

## Community Medicine / SPM

**#1 Concept: National Health Programs targets and vaccine schedule — highest yield in SPM**

### Must-Know One-Liners
- **NMR** (Neonatal Mortality Rate): deaths in first 28 days per 1000 live births; India target: <12 by 2025
- **MMR** (Maternal Mortality Ratio): maternal deaths per **100,000 live births**; India: ~97/100,000 (2018–20)
- **IMR** India: ~28/1000 live births (2020); **NMR** ~20/1000; **U5MR** ~34/1000
- **BCG at birth** (intradermal, 0.05–0.1 mL, right arm deltoid); **OPV birth dose** (bOPV, 2 drops oral)
- **DOTS**: Direct Observed Treatment Short-course; **Category I**: new smear+; **Category II**: retreatment
- **Herd immunity threshold**: polio >80–85%; measles **>92–95%** (highest)
- **Sensitivity** = TP/(TP+FN); **Specificity** = TN/(TN+FP); PPV depends on prevalence

### Critical Numbers
- **ASHA incentive** under JSY: Rs 600 rural, Rs 400 urban
- **Anganwadi**: one per **1000 population** (rural); **PHC**: one per **30,000** (plains), **20,000** (hilly)
- **NMR** components: early neonatal (0–7 days) + late neonatal (8–28 days)
- **Obesity**: BMI ≥30; **Overweight**: ≥25; Indian cutoff for obesity: **BMI ≥25**, central obesity waist **>90 cm** male, **>80 cm** female

### Classic Differentials
- **Case fatality rate** (deaths/cases of disease) vs **Mortality rate** (deaths/total population)
- **Period prevalence** (new + old cases over period) vs **Point prevalence** (cases at single point in time)

### #1 Mnemonic
**"SMART"** surveillance criteria: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound (also used for health program evaluation)

## General Medicine

**#1 Concept: Diagnostic criteria and staging systems — most directly tested in Medicine**

### Must-Know One-Liners
- **Wells score** DVT: clinical probability; PE: score >4 = high probability → CTA pulmonary angiography
- **CURB-65** pneumonia severity: Confusion, Urea >7, RR ≥30, BP <90/60, age ≥65; score ≥2 = hospital admission
- **Hypertensive emergency**: BP >180/120 with end-organ damage; lower by **25% in first hour** (not completely)
- **Child-Pugh score**: liver disease severity — Bilirubin, Albumin, PT, Ascites, Encephalopathy
- **ABCD2 score**: TIA stroke risk — Age, BP, Clinical features, Duration, Diabetes; score ≥4 = high risk
- **Waterfall sign** on chest X-ray: aortic dissection (widened mediastinum)
- **Osler's nodes**: painful (immune-complex, embolic) in SBE; **Janeway lesions**: painless (embolic) in SBE

### Critical Numbers
- **HbA1c** target: <7% (most diabetics); <8% (elderly, comorbid)
- **eGFR CKD stages**: G1 ≥90, G2 60–89, G3a 45–59, G3b 30–44, G4 15–29, G5 <15 mL/min/1.73m²
- **Troponin** rises at **3–6 hours** in STEMI, peaks **24 hours**, normalizes **7–14 days**
- **CHADS2 score** ≥2 in AF → anticoagulation indicated

### Classic Differentials
- **STEMI** (ST elevation in ≥2 contiguous leads, new LBBB, thrombus) vs **NSTEMI** (no ST elevation, troponin rise, subendocardial)
- **Type 1 DM** (young, thin, ketosis-prone, anti-GAD/IA-2 antibodies) vs **Type 2** (older, obese, insulin resistant, no antibodies)

### #1 Mnemonic
**"AEIOU TIPS"** causes of coma: Alcohol, Epilepsy, Insulin (hypo/hyperglycemia), Opiates, Uremia; Trauma, Infection, Psychiatric, Stroke/Structural

## Psychiatry

**#1 Concept: ICD-11/DSM-5 diagnostic criteria for major disorders — directly tested**

### Must-Know One-Liners
- **Schizophrenia**: ≥2 symptoms (delusions, hallucinations, disorganized speech, negative symptoms) for **≥6 months** (DSM-5)
- **Bipolar I**: at least one **manic episode** (≥7 days); Bipolar II: hypomania + major depression
- **Delirium**: acute, fluctuating, attention deficit, disturbed consciousness — key: **reversible**
- **Dementia (NCD)**: insidious onset, progressive, **no clouding of consciousness** — key difference from delirium
- **Clozapine**: gold standard for **treatment-resistant schizophrenia**; risk: **agranulocytosis** → weekly WBC monitoring
- **SSRI** = first-line for depression, OCD, panic disorder, PTSD, social anxiety
- **Electroconvulsive therapy (ECT)**: DOC for **severe depression with suicidal ideation**, catatonia, treatment-resistant depression

### Critical Numbers
- **Lithium**: therapeutic **0.6–1.2 mEq/L**; toxicity >1.5 mEq/L; signs: tremor, ataxia, confusion
- **Haloperidol** halftime: ~18–24 hours; **depot** (decanoate): q4 weeks
- **AUDIT score** ≥8: alcohol use disorder screening positive
- MMSE: **<24** suggests cognitive impairment; **<10**: severe dementia

### Classic Differentials
- **OCD** (ego-dystonic, patient resists) vs **OCPD** (ego-syntonic, no resistance, personality disorder)
- **Malingering** (conscious, external gain) vs **Factitious disorder** (conscious, no external gain — sick role) vs **Somatization** (unconscious)

### #1 Mnemonic
**"SIGECAPS"** for Major Depression: Sleep change, Interest loss, Guilt, Energy loss, Concentration difficulty, Appetite change, Psychomotor change, Suicidal ideation (≥5 for 2 weeks = MDD)

## Dermatology

**#1 Concept: Morphology-to-diagnosis mapping — most questions test lesion identification**

### Must-Know One-Liners
- **Auspitz sign**: pinpoint bleeding on removing psoriasis scale (due to dilated capillary loops)
- **Nikolsky sign**: positive in **pemphigus vulgaris** (intraepidermal), TEN, SSSS — lateral pressure → blister extension
- **Wickham's striae**: fine white lines on violaceous papules of **Lichen Planus** (4Ps: Purple, Polygonal, Pruritic, Papules)
- **Tzanck smear**: acantholytic cells (Tzanck cells) in pemphigus; multinucleated giant cells in herpes
- **Lepromin test (Mitsuda)**: positive in **tuberculoid leprosy** (TT); negative in lepromatous (LL)
- **Dog ear deformity**: lepromatous leprosy ears (leonine facies)
- **Erythema nodosum**: most common panniculitis; over shins; associated with streptococcal infection, TB, sarcoid, drugs

### Critical Numbers
- **Leprosy treatment**: PB (1–5 patches): **rifampicin + dapsone × 6 months**; MB (≥6 patches): **RFP + dapsone + clofazimine × 12 months**
- **PUVA** (psoralen + UVA): 320–400 nm; NBUVB: 311 nm — used for psoriasis, vitiligo
- **Isotretinoin** teratogenicity: iPLEDGE program; absolutely CI in pregnancy (category X)
- **Scabies** treatment: **Permethrin 5%** cream (preferred); benzyl benzoate 25% alternative

### Classic Differentials
- **Pemphigus vulgaris** (intraepidermal, acantholysis, IgG against desmoglein 3, Nikolsky+, oral mucosa first) vs **Bullous pemphigoid** (subepidermal, IgG against hemidesmosomes BP180/230, Nikolsky−, elderly)
- **Tinea capitis** (Microsporum — blue-green fluorescence Wood's lamp; Trichophyton — no fluorescence)

### #1 Mnemonic
**"ABCDE"** of melanoma: Asymmetry, Border irregularity, Color variation, Diameter >6 mm, Evolution

## General Surgery

**#1 Concept: Surgical emergencies and their immediate management — most tested in Surgery**

### Must-Know One-Liners
- **Courvoisier's law**: palpable gallbladder + painless jaundice → NOT gallstones → think periampullary carcinoma
- **Murphy's sign**: acute cholecystitis (arrest of inspiration on palpation of RUQ)
- **Charcot's triad**: fever + jaundice + RUQ pain = ascending cholangitis; add BP↓ + confusion = **Reynold's pentad**
- **Ranson's criteria**: acute pancreatitis severity; ≥3 = severe; look for: WBC >16,000, glucose >200, LDH >350, AST >250 on admission
- **Hartmann's procedure**: sigmoid colectomy + end colostomy (no anastomosis) — done in perforated sigmoid diverticulitis
- **Spigelian hernia**: lateral to rectus, through linea semilunaris — highest risk of strangulation
- **Sentinel lymph node biopsy**: first lymph node draining tumor — standard of care in early breast cancer

### Critical Numbers
- **Common bile duct** normal diameter: ≤6 mm (up to 8 mm post-cholecystectomy)
- **Appendix** blood supply: appendicular artery (branch of ileocolic artery, right branch of SMA)
- **Glasgow Coma Scale**: eye (4) + verbal (5) + motor (6) = maximum **15**; severe head injury: ≤8
- **AAA (Abdominal Aortic Aneurysm)** surgery indication: diameter **>5.5 cm** or rapid growth >1 cm/year

### Classic Differentials
- **Indirect inguinal hernia** (lateral to inferior epigastric, patent processus vaginalis, young males) vs **Direct** (medial to inferior epigastric, Hesselbach's triangle, older males, no sac)
- **Colicky pain** (intestinal obstruction — peristaltic waves visible) vs **Constant pain** (strangulation/peritonitis — rigid abdomen)

### #1 Mnemonic
**"OPQRST"** for surgical abdomen: Onset, Provocation, Quality, Radiation, Severity, Time; + **"FLAP"** for hernias prone to strangulation: Femoral, Littre's (Meckel's), Amyand's (appendix), Para-umbilical

## Orthopedics

**#1 Concept: Fracture eponyms and their mechanisms — most directly tested in Ortho**

### Must-Know One-Liners
- **Colles' fracture**: distal radius, **dinner fork deformity** (dorsal angulation); mechanism: FOOSH (fall on outstretched hand)
- **Scaphoid fracture**: tenderness in anatomical snuffbox; risk of **avascular necrosis** (blood supply from distal end)
- **Pott's fracture**: bimalleolar ankle fracture; **Pott's disease**: TB of spine (vertebral collapse → gibbus)
- **Looser's zones (Milkman's pseudofractures)**: osteomalacia pathognomonic finding on X-ray
- **Osgood-Schlatter disease**: tibial tuberosity apophysitis in adolescents (growth spurt + sports); tenderness over tibial tuberosity
- **FABER test**: Flexion ABduction External Rotation → hip pathology (SI joint)
- **Trendelenburg gait**: abductor weakness (gluteus medius); pelvis drops on opposite side when standing on affected leg

### Critical Numbers
- **Thomas test**: hip flexion deformity assessment; positive if lumbar lordosis disappears on flexing opposite hip
- **Bone healing**: cancellous bone: **6 weeks**; cortical: **3 months** (general rule); femoral shaft: **3–4 months**
- **Compartment syndrome** threshold: tissue pressure >30 mmHg (or within 30 mmHg of diastolic BP) → fasciotomy
- **Vitamin D** deficiency: 25-OH Vit D <20 ng/mL; severe <10 ng/mL

### Classic Differentials
- **Osteoporosis** (↓bone mass, normal mineralization, DEXA T-score ≤-2.5) vs **Osteomalacia** (↓mineralization, ↑ALP, ↓Ca, ↓PO4, Looser's zones)
- **Septic arthritis** (hot, swollen, restricted, WBC>50,000 in fluid, pus) vs **Transient synovitis** (afebrile, less restriction, WBC<15,000, self-limiting)

### #1 Mnemonic
**"SALTER-HARRIS"** growth plate fractures: I (slip), II (Above — metaphysis), III (Lower — epiphysis), IV (Through all layers), V (cRush — worst prognosis)

## Anesthesia

**#1 Concept: Rapid Sequence Intubation (RSI) drugs and sequence — most tested clinical scenario**

### Must-Know One-Liners
- **Mallampati classification**: I (full soft palate visible) to IV (only hard palate visible); grade III/IV = difficult airway
- **Succinylcholine**: depolarizing NMBA; onset 60 sec, duration 10–15 min; CI: hyperkalemia, burns, denervation injuries
- **Ketamine**: dissociative anesthetic; maintains airway reflexes + BP; **DOC in hemorrhagic shock/asthma** for induction
- **Propofol**: white emulsion; apnea + hypotension; antiemetic property; no analgesia; CI: egg/soy allergy
- **MAC (Minimum Alveolar Concentration)**: concentration of volatile agent to prevent response in 50% patients; **halothane MAC = 0.75%**, isoflurane = 1.15%
- **Malignant hyperthermia**: succinylcholine/volatile agents → ryanodine receptor mutation → hyperthermia, rigidity, acidosis; Rx: **dantrolene**
- **Epidural vs spinal**: epidural — larger dose, catheter, no dural puncture; spinal — smaller dose, single shot, into subarachnoid

### Critical Numbers
- **Cricoid pressure** (Sellick's maneuver): 44 N force applied to prevent aspiration during RSI
- **LMA (Laryngeal Mask Airway)**: does NOT protect against aspiration; contraindicated in full stomach
- **Thiopentone**: 3–5 mg/kg IV induction; **Propofol**: 1.5–2.5 mg/kg; **Ketamine**: 1–2 mg/kg IV
- **Pulse oximetry**: SpO2 <95% abnormal; detects oxyhemoglobin + deoxyhemoglobin only

### Classic Differentials
- **Spinal anesthesia** (into CSF, subarachnoid space, L3-L4 or L4-L5, fast onset, dense block) vs **Epidural** (outside dura, any level, slow onset, catheter for continuous)
- **Malignant hyperthermia** (genetic, rigidity, hyperthermia, rhabdomyolysis) vs **Neuroleptic malignant syndrome** (antipsychotic-induced, lead-pipe rigidity, hyperthermia, autonomic instability)

### #1 Mnemonic
**"SODA"** for RSI premedication: Suction ready, Oxygenate (preoxygenate 3 min), Drugs (induction + NMBA), Airway (ETT + confirm)

## Radiology

**#1 Concept: Pathognomonic X-ray/CT findings — highest yield in Radiology questions**

### Must-Know One-Liners
- **"Ground glass opacity"** on HRCT: pulmonary edema, PCP pneumonia, COVID-19, alveolar hemorrhage
- **"Eggshell calcification"** of lymph nodes: silicosis, sarcoidosis (rarely TB)
- **"Cannonball metastases"** on chest X-ray: renal cell carcinoma, choriocarcinoma, thyroid carcinoma
- **"String sign of Kantor"**: terminal ileum narrowing in Crohn's disease on barium follow-through
- **"Coffee bean sign"**: sigmoid volvulus on plain X-ray (inverted U-loop)
- **"Double bubble sign"**: duodenal atresia on plain X-ray (prenatal or newborn)
- **Radiation exposure**: CXR ~0.02 mSv; CT chest ~7 mSv; CT abdomen ~10 mSv

### Critical Numbers
- **CT head without contrast**: first investigation for suspected intracranial hemorrhage
- **MRI**: contraindicated in ferromagnetic implants, pacemakers, cochlear implants (older types)
- **"Golden S sign"**: right upper lobe collapse + hilar mass on CXR = **central bronchogenic carcinoma**
- **Ultrasound**: no radiation; used in pregnancy; operator-dependent; gold standard for gallstones

### Classic Differentials
- **Subdural hematoma** (crescent-shaped, crosses suture lines, veins torn) vs **Epidural** (biconvex/lenticular, does NOT cross suture lines, middle meningeal artery)
- **Consolidation** (air bronchograms, lobar, no volume loss) vs **Collapse** (volume loss, no air bronchogram, tracheal deviation toward)

### #1 Mnemonic
**"ABCDE"** CXR review: Airway (trachea midline), Bones, Cardiac (size <50% CTR), Diaphragm (right higher, angles sharp), Everything else (soft tissue, lines)

## OBG (Obstetrics and Gynecology)

**#1 Concept: Obstetric emergencies and their immediate management — highest yield**

### Must-Know One-Liners
- **Preeclampsia**: BP ≥140/90 + proteinuria (≥300 mg/24h) after 20 weeks; **severe**: BP ≥160/110, HELLP, eclampsia
- **Eclampsia Rx**: MgSO4 (Pritchard regimen: 4g IV loading + 5g IM each buttock; then 5g IM q4h); antidote: **calcium gluconate**
- **Placenta previa**: painless bleeding, placenta covers os; **Abruptio placentae**: painful bleeding, retroplacental clot
- **PPH definition**: blood loss >500 mL vaginal delivery, >1000 mL cesarean; most common cause: **uterine atony**
- **PPH management**: oxytocin 10 IU IM (first-line uterotonic); misoprostol 600 μg sublingual if no oxytocin
- **Bishop score**: favorable cervix for induction: score **≥8** (Dilation, Effacement, Station, Consistency, Position)
- **Down syndrome** screening: **nuchal translucency** (11–14 weeks); triple test (15–18 weeks): ↑AFP, ↑hCG, ↓estriol = **Edwards; ↓AFP, ↑hCG, ↓estriol = Down**

### Critical Numbers
- **GDM screening**: OGTT 75g at **24–28 weeks**; diagnosis: fasting ≥92, 1h ≥180, 2h ≥153 mg/dL (any one)
- **Normal AFI**: 5–25 cm; **oligohydramnios**: <5 cm; **polyhydramnios**: >25 cm (or deepest pool >8 cm)
- **Fetal heart rate**: normal **110–160 bpm**
- **Rhesus prophylaxis**: Anti-D 300 μg at **28 weeks** + within 72 hours of delivery in Rh-negative mothers

### Classic Differentials
- **Placenta previa** (painless, bright red, soft uterus, malpresentation, DO NOT do PV exam) vs **Abruptio** (painful, dark blood, woody hard uterus, fetal distress)
- **PCOS** (oligo-ovulation, hyperandrogenism, polycystic ovaries on USG — Rotterdam criteria: 2 of 3) vs **Hypothyroidism** (weight gain, oligo/amenorrhea, ↑TSH, ↓T4)

### #1 Mnemonic
**"HELLP"** syndrome: Hemolysis, Elevated Liver enzymes, Low Platelets — severe preeclampsia variant; Rx = delivery (definitive)

## Pediatrics

**#1 Concept: Developmental milestones and their age correlation — directly tested**

### Must-Know One-Liners
- **Social smile**: **6 weeks**; Neck holding: 3–4 months; Sits without support: **6 months**; Stands: 9–10 months; Walks: **12–15 months**
- **Single word with meaning**: 1 year; **2-word sentences**: 2 years; **complete sentences**: 3 years
- **Febrile seizures**: 6 months–6 years; complex if >15 min, focal, or recurs within 24h; **lumbar puncture** in <12 months mandatory
- **Kawasaki disease**: fever >5 days + 4/5: rash, conjunctivitis, oral changes, cervical lymphadenopathy, hand/foot swelling; Rx: **IVIG + aspirin**
- **Intussusception**: 3 months–3 years; currant jelly stool; sausage-shaped mass RUQ; reduce with **air/barium enema**
- **Pyloric stenosis**: 2–8 weeks; projectile non-bilious vomiting; olive-shaped mass; hypochloremic hypokalemic metabolic alkalosis; Rx: **Ramstedt's pyloromyotomy**
- **Nutritional marasmus**: wasting (weight-for-height <-3 SD), no edema vs **Kwashiorkor**: edema, fatty liver, flaky paint skin

### Critical Numbers
- **Birth weight** doubles by **5 months**, triples by **1 year**, quadruples by **2 years**
- **Normal birth weight**: 2.5–4 kg; **LBW** <2.5 kg; **VLBW** <1.5 kg; **ELBW** <1 kg
- **Anterior fontanelle** closes: **12–18 months**; posterior: 6–8 weeks
- **Hemoglobin** at birth: 14–20 g/dL; physiological nadir at **2–3 months**: ~10 g/dL (Fe2+ stores depleted)

### Classic Differentials
- **Croup (viral laryngotracheobronchitis)**: barking cough, stridor, steeple sign CXR; Rx: dexamethasone + nebulized epinephrine vs **Epiglottitis** (H. influenzae B, tripod position, thumb sign, no cough, drooling, toxic-looking)
- **Hirschsprung's** (failure to pass meconium in 48h, ganglionic cells absent in rectum, barium enema: transition zone) vs **Meconium ileus** (CF, first sign, soap-bubble appearance on X-ray)

### #1 Mnemonic
**"CHILD"** milestones: Chasing objects (3 mo), Holding head (4 mo), Independent sitting (6 mo), Locomotion walking (12 mo), Drawing (18 mo — scribbles)

## ENT (Ear, Nose & Throat)

**#1 Concept: Tuning fork tests and their interpretation — most directly tested ENT topic**

### Must-Know One-Liners
- **Rinne test**: AC>BC = Rinne positive (normal or SNHL); BC>AC = Rinne negative = **conductive hearing loss**
- **Weber test**: lateralizes to **better ear** in SNHL; to **worse ear (affected)** in conductive HL
- **Cholesteatoma**: acquired (pars flaccida perforation), squamous epithelium, bone erosion; Rx: surgery (mastoidectomy)
- **Otosclerosis**: conductive HL in young adults, females, pink blush of promontory (Schwartze sign); Rx: **stapedectomy**
- **Acoustic neuroma (vestibular schwannoma)**: SNHL + tinnitus + vertigo; **BPPV**: episodic vertigo, Dix-Hallpike positive, Epley maneuver
- **Meniere's disease**: triad — episodic vertigo + fluctuating SNHL + tinnitus + aural fullness; endolymphatic hydrops
- **Quinsy (peritonsillar abscess)**: uvular deviation away from affected side; Rx: aspiration or I&D + antibiotics

### Critical Numbers
- **Audiogram**: normal hearing <25 dB HL; mild loss 26–40; moderate 41–55; severe 56–70; profound >90 dB
- **Epistaxis**: 90% anterior (Kiesselbach's plexus, Little's area); 10% posterior (sphenopalatine artery)
- **Tracheostomy**: tube change first time at **48–72 hours** (tract forms); **emergency**: at bedside in 24–48h
- **Vocal cord polyp** → unilateral, associated with smoking; **nodule** → bilateral, professional voice users

### Classic Differentials
- **Sensorineural HL** (cochlear or retrocochlear, Rinne+, Weber to better ear, no TM perforation) vs **Conductive** (middle ear/ossicles, Rinne−, Weber to worse ear, TM abnormal)
- **Acute otitis media** (fever, bulging TM, pus behind drum) vs **Serous otitis media/Glue ear** (amber TM, air-fluid level, conductive HL, no fever)

### #1 Mnemonic
**"BPPV Epley"** positioning: Begin sitting, Position head 45° to affected side, lie back quickly, then turn to opposite side, sit up — maneuver to reposition otoliths

## Ophthalmology

**#1 Concept: Glaucoma types and their management — most tested Ophthalmology topic**

### Must-Know One-Liners
- **POAG (Primary Open Angle Glaucoma)**: painless, insidious, open angle, IOP>21, cupping C/D>0.6; Rx: topical **timolol** (beta-blocker first-line)
- **Acute angle closure glaucoma**: sudden severe eye pain + headache + nausea + haloes; mid-dilated fixed pupil; emergency — IV acetazolamide + pilocarpine; definitive: **laser peripheral iridotomy**
- **Diabetic retinopathy**: NPDR (microaneurysms, hard exudates, flame hemorrhages) → PDR (neovascularization); Rx PDR: **pan-retinal photocoagulation (PRP)**
- **Central retinal artery occlusion (CRAO)**: **cherry red spot** at fovea (surrounded by pale retina); painless sudden vision loss
- **Central retinal vein occlusion (CRVO)**: "blood and thunder" fundus — flame hemorrhages all 4 quadrants, disc edema
- **Cataract**: most common cause of **treatable blindness** in India; nuclear sclerosis = myopic shift
- **Trachoma**: Chlamydia trachomatis; most common cause of **preventable blindness** worldwide; SAFE strategy (Surgery, Antibiotics, Face washing, Environmental)

### Critical Numbers
- **IOP normal**: 10–21 mmHg; glaucoma suspect: >21 mmHg; treatment threshold: IOP + optic nerve changes
- **Visual field loss in glaucoma**: nasal step first → arcuate scotoma → tunnel vision → only temporal island remains
- **Snellen chart** distance: **6 meters** (20 feet); 6/6 = normal vision
- **Fluorescein angiography**: timing — arterial phase 10–15 sec, AV phase 15–20 sec, venous 20–25 sec

### Classic Differentials
- **CRAO** (cherry red spot, sudden, arterioles, whitish retina) vs **CRVO** (blood and thunder, veins, disc edema, poor prognosis)
- **Papilledema** (raised ICP, bilateral disc swelling, normal vision initially, no spontaneous venous pulsations) vs **Papillitis/Optic neuritis** (pain on eye movement, decreased vision, unilateral, RAPD present)

### #1 Mnemonic
**"MOAT"** glaucoma drugs: Miotics (pilocarpine), tOpical beta-blockers (timolol), Alpha agonists (brimonidine), Topical CAIs (dorzolamide) — in order of classic use

## Exam Day Strategy

### Time Management: 200 Questions in 210 Minutes
- **Total time**: 210 minutes = 1.05 min/question
- **Target pace**: complete 200 questions in **180 minutes** → 30 min buffer for review
- First pass: **~45 sec for confident questions** (do not overthink); mark uncertain ones
- Second pass: spend remaining time on marked questions — **2–3 min max per hard question**
- If behind pace: cut losses on calculations/complex reasoning; bank on one-liners

### When to Guess vs Skip (+4 / −1 Marking)
- **Attempt if**: you can eliminate ≥2 options → probability >50% → expected value positive
- **Skip only if**: completely no idea and cannot eliminate any option
- With +4/−1: **expected value neutral at 25% chance** (1 in 4 random); if you can eliminate 1 option → 33% → worth attempting
- **Never leave blanks** if you have any clue — statistical advantage favors attempting with partial knowledge
- **Never change first instinct** unless you recall a specific contradicting fact

### Image-Based Questions
- Look at the **question stem first** — it gives context before the image
- For X-rays: note laterality marker, scan for obvious abnormality, then check soft tissues and bones
- For histology: identify the **tissue type first**, then abnormal cells/patterns
- For clinical photos: note distribution (central vs peripheral, flexors vs extensors), morphology (vesicle vs pustule vs plaque)
- **Do not overthink** image questions — the image usually shows the classic textbook appearance

### Managing Anxiety and Mental Approach
- **Anchor thought**: "I have prepared — trust the preparation"
- If you hit a difficult question: take one deep breath, note it, move on — **do not let one question derail a block**
- **Physical**: sit upright, drink water between sections, blink consciously to reduce eye strain
- **Cognitive**: treat each question independently — one wrong answer does not affect the next
- If panic: **5-4-3-2-1 grounding** (name 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste)

## Last 30 Minutes Before Exam

### What to Review (Final 30 Minutes)
- **Drug of choice** quick list (most likely to be tested, highest ROI)
- **Diagnostic criteria numbers** — things that slip under stress (e.g., GFR stages, HbA1c targets)
- **Eponyms you tend to confuse** — write a 5-item personal list the night before
- Scan the **#1 mnemonic per subject** — anchor recall for each specialty
- Review **normal values** you blanked on during mocks

### Things to NOT Do the Night Before
- Do NOT start new topics — diminishing returns, increases anxiety
- Do NOT do a full mock test — fatigue the night before costs more than last-minute gains
- Do NOT stay up past midnight — REM sleep consolidates memory; sacrifice sleep = sacrifice recall
- Do NOT discuss "What did you study?" with friends — raises anxiety, introduces doubt about your preparation
- Do NOT rely on energy drinks — crash during exam; stick to your normal caffeine pattern
- **DO**: eat a light dinner, set two alarms, lay out your documents the night before, sleep by 10–11 PM
- **DO**: arrive at the centre 30 minutes early — reduces cortisol spike from rushing
