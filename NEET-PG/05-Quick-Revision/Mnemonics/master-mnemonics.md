## PHYSIOLOGY

### Lung Volumes — "IRV ERV TLC RV"
Normal values (approximate):
- **T**idal Volume (TV) = **5**00 mL → "TV channel 5"
- **I**nspiratory Reserve Volume (IRV) = **3**000 mL
- **E**xpiratory Reserve Volume (ERV) = **1**100 mL
- **R**esidual Volume (RV) = **1**200 mL

Capacities:
- **TLC** = TV + IRV + ERV + RV = 6000 mL → "**T**hink of **L**ung **C**apacity as a whole"
- **VC** = TV + IRV + ERV = 4800 mL
- **FRC** = ERV + RV = 2300 mL (the resting volume after normal expiration)
- **IC** = TV + IRV = 3500 mL

### Cardiac Cycle Phases — "ISOREIRS"
**I**sovolumetric **C**ontraction → **R**apid **E**jection → **S**low **E**jection → **I**sovolumetric **R**elaxation → **R**apid **F**illing → **S**low filling → **A**trial systole

### Acid-Base Mnemonics
**ROME** for direction of compensation:
- **R**espiratory **O**pposite: pH ↑ → pCO₂ ↓ (alkalosis); pH ↓ → pCO₂ ↑ (acidosis)
- **M**etabolic **E**qual: pH ↑ → HCO₃ ↑; pH ↓ → HCO₃ ↓

**"MUDPILES"** for high anion gap metabolic acidosis causes:
Methanol, Uremia, Diabetic ketoacidosis, Propylene glycol, Isoniazid/Iron, Lactic acidosis, Ethylene glycol, Salicylates

**"HARDUP"** for normal anion gap metabolic acidosis:
Hyperalimentation, Addison's, Renal tubular acidosis, Diarrhea, Ureteroenteric fistula, Pancreatic fistula

### Oxygen-Hb Curve Right Shift — "CADET, face RIGHT"
**C**O₂ ↑, **A**cid (↓ pH), **D**PG (2,3-DPG) ↑, **E**xercise (temperature ↑), **T**emperature ↑ → right shift

### Renal Tubular Reabsorption Sites — "PT is the Richest, Loop is the Hardest Worker"
- PCT: glucose, amino acids, Na⁺, K⁺, HCO₃, PO₄, uric acid (60–70% of filtrate)
- Loop of Henle (thick ascending): Na-K-2Cl (furosemide site); impermeable to water
- DCT: Na⁺ (thiazide site), Ca²⁺ (PTH)
- Collecting duct: water (ADH), Na⁺/K⁺ (aldosterone)

### Factors Affecting GFR
**"RAMP"** — GFR increased by:
- ↑Renal blood flow, ↑Afferent arteriolar dilation, ↑Mean arterial pressure (mild), ↑Plasma oncotic pressure decrease

### Vitamin Deficiencies
**B vitamins in order (B1–B12) mnemonics:**
- B1 (Thiamine) → **"The Brain Burns Glucose"** → Wernicke's (ataxia, ophthalmoplegia, confusion)

- B12 → **"12 months = 1 year = spinal cord"** → subacute combined degeneration (dorsal + lateral columns)

**Fat-soluble vitamins — "DEKA" (D, E, K, A)**

Vitamin A deficiency progression:
**"Night → Day → Keratomalacia"** — Night blindness → Xerophthalmia → Bitot's spots → Corneal ulceration → Keratomalacia

Vitamin D — **"D is for sun + kidney"**:
- Skin: cholecalciferol (D3) from 7-dehydrocholesterol + UV light
- Liver: 25-hydroxylation
- Kidney: 1α-hydroxylation (active calcitriol); PTH stimulates 1α-hydroxylase

### Glycogen Storage Diseases — "Very Poor Carbohydrate Metabolism"
I = Von Gierke (Glucose-6-Phosphatase)
II = Pompe (acid maltase — **"Pompe's heart PUMPS bad"**)
III = Cori (debranching enzyme)
IV = Anderson (branching enzyme)
V = McArdle (muscle phosphorylase)
VI = Hers (liver phosphorylase)

### Enzyme Inhibition
**"Competitive = Km up; Non-competitive = Vmax down"**
- Competitive: same Vmax, high Km (reversible with more substrate)
- Non-competitive: same Km, low Vmax (cannot overcome with substrate)
- Irreversible (like organophosphates): permanently reduces enzyme activity

### TCA Cycle Key Enzymes — "Clever Scientists Usually Forget Important Details"
Citrate synthase → Isocitrate dehydrogenase → α-Ketoglutarate dehydrogenase → Succinyl-CoA synthetase → Succinate dehydrogenase → Fumarase → Malate dehydrogenase → back to Citrate

Rate-limiting step: **Isocitrate dehydrogenase**

### Urea Cycle — "Ordinarily Careless Crappers Are Also Frivolous About Urination"
(not great — use the sites instead)
**COAL** → two enzymes in mitochondria: Carbamyl phosphate synthetase I + Ornithine transcarbamylase
Rest in cytoplasm: Argininosuccinate synthetase, Argininosuccinate lyase, Arginase

## SPM / COMMUNITY MEDICINE

### Sensitivity and Specificity — "SnNout and SpPin"
- **Sn**N**out**: High **Sn**ensitivity rules out disease (negative test in high sensitivity = disease unlikely)
- **Sp**P**in**: High **Sp**ecificity rules in disease (positive test in high specificity = disease likely)

### 2×2 Table — "A-B-C-D grid"
| | Disease + | Disease − |
|---|---|---|
| Test + | a (TP) | b (FP) |
| Test − | c (FN) | d (TN) |
- Sensitivity = a/(a+c); Specificity = d/(b+d); PPV = a/(a+b); NPV = d/(c+d)

### Study Design Hierarchy — "SMARCH"
(evidence hierarchy ascending):
**S**ystematic reviews, **M**eta-analysis → **R**andomized controlled trials → **C**ohort → **C**ase-control → **C**ross-sectional → **C**ase series → Expert opinion

Actually descending from top:
**Systematic review/Meta-analysis > RCT > Cohort > Case-control > Cross-sectional > Case series**

### Biases — "SLAP"
**S**election bias, **L**ead-time bias (screening), **A**scertainment bias, **P**erformance bias

### Route of Transmission — "Food, Feces, Fingers, Flies, Fomites"
The 5 Fs for feco-oral route (typhoid, cholera, hepatitis A, polio, amoebiasis)

## GENERAL MEDICINE

### Heart Failure Management — "ABCDE"
**A**CE inhibitors/ARBs, **B**eta-blockers, **C**ardiac resynchronization/aldosterone antagonists, **D**iuretics, **E**jection fraction (monitor)

### Cushing's Syndrome vs Cushing's Disease
- **Syndrome** = any cause of cortisol excess (including exogenous)
- **Disease** = pituitary ACTH-secreting adenoma specifically

### Criteria for DM — "FROG"
**F**asting glucose ≥126; **R**andom glucose ≥200 + symptoms; **O**GTT 2h ≥200; **G**lycated Hb (HbA1c) ≥6.5%

### Shock Classification — "CHORD"
**C**ardiogenic, **H**ypovolemic, **O**bstructive, **R**edistributive (distributive), **D**issociative (CO poisoning)

Hemodynamic patterns:
| Type | CO | PCWP | SVR |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cardiogenic | ↓ | ↑ | ↑ |
| Hypovolemic | ↓ | ↓ | ↑ |
| Distributive (septic) | ↑ (early) | ↓ | ↓ |

### Antibodies in Autoimmune Disease — "ANA in Everything, Specific ones Separate"
- ANA: sensitive for SLE but not specific

- Anti-CCP: RA (more specific than RF)

**C**HF, **H**ypertension, **A**ge ≥75 (×2), **D**iabetes, **S**troke/TIA history (×2), **V**ascular disease, **A**ge 65–74, **S**ex category (female)

### Burns — Rule of 9s (Adults)
**"9-9-9-18-18-1"**
- Head+neck = 9%
- Each arm = 9% (9×2 = 18%)
- Anterior trunk = 18%
- Posterior trunk = 18%

- A: confined to bowel wall (mucosa/submucosa)

## ENT

### Rinne-Weber Interpretation — "CHL: Weber to bad ear, Rinne negative"
**In Conductive Hearing Loss (CHL):**
- Rinne: negative (BC > AC) in affected ear
- Weber: lateralizes to affected (worse) ear — "bad ear hears better by bone"

**In SNHL:**
- Rinne: positive (AC > AC, but may be false negative in profound SNHL)
- Weber: lateralizes to unaffected (better) ear

**Mnemonic: "In CHL, the BAD ear WINS Weber (bone conduction enhanced)"**

### Cholesteatoma — "SAFE vs UNSAFE"
- Safe (tubotympanic) disease: large central perforation; mucopurulent discharge; no cholesteatoma; safe
- Unsafe (atticoantral) disease: attic/posterosuperior perforation; cholesteatoma; bone destruction; dangerous complications

### Causes of Vertigo — "BPPV is the most common"
- BPPV: brief episodes (<1 min), triggered by position change, Dix-Hallpike positive, no hearing loss
- Meniere's: triad = episodic vertigo + fluctuating SNHL + tinnitus; low-frequency hearing loss first
- Vestibular neuritis: sudden onset, prolonged (days–weeks), no hearing loss

## OPHTHALMOLOGY

### Visual Field Defects — "CHOP: Chiasm, Hemianopia, Optic tract, Partial lobe"
- Optic nerve → monocular blindness (before chiasm)
- Chiasm → bitemporal hemianopia (nasal fibers cross)
- Optic tract → contralateral homonymous hemianopia
- Temporal lobe (Meyer's loop) → upper quadrantanopia ("pie in the sky")
- Parietal lobe → lower quadrantanopia ("pie on the floor")
- Occipital → homonymous hemianopia with macular sparing

### Glaucoma — "OPEN = Painless, CLOSED = Painful"
- Open angle: insidious, painless, peripheral vision loss, cupped disc
- Closed angle (acute): sudden onset, painful red eye, halos around lights, fixed mid-dilated pupil

### Eye Movements and Cranial Nerves — "LR6, SO4, AO3"
- **L**ateral **R**ectus → CN **6** (Abducens)
- **S**uperior **O**blique → CN **4** (Trochlear)
- **A**ll **O**ther muscles → CN **3** (Oculomotor)

### Pupil Reactions — "RAPD = afferent defect"
- Relative Afferent Pupillary Defect (RAPD / Marcus Gunn pupil)
- Swinging flashlight test: affected eye shows paradoxical dilation
- Caused by optic nerve lesion or severe retinal disease

- **Cullen's sign**: periumbilical bruising in hemorrhagic pancreatitis
- **Grey Turner's sign**: flank bruising in hemorrhagic pancreatitis
- **Rovsing's sign**: appendicitis (LIF pressure causes RIF pain)
- **McBurney's sign**: tenderness at McBurney's point (appendicitis)

### Causes of Elevated AFP
**"HAG"** — Hepatocellular carcinoma, Ataxia telangiectasia, Germ cell tumors (yolk sac)
Also: neural tube defects (maternal AFP), multiple gestation

### Conditions Causing Clubbing — "CLUB"
**C**hronic lung disease (fibrosis, bronchiectasis, CF, empyema), **L**iver (cirrhosis), **U**C/Crohn's, **B**E (infective endocarditis), **A**BD (Abdominal causes: IBD), congenital **H**eart disease
Mnemonic: "CLUBBING = Cardiac, Lung, Ulcerative colitis, Biliary cirrhosis, Bronchiectasis, Infective IE, Neoplasia (lung CA), Gastrointestinal"
